New guidance and service from 4 August 2022

This new guidance and service replaces the interim guidance.

You should use this new guidance and service for new applications.

You should follow the interim guidance for any applications started before 4 August.

Chargepoints at commercial properties

This new service is not currently available for commercial properties.

If you’re applying for a chargepoint at a commercial property, you’ll need to follow the interim guidance.

Landlords can get grants to install electric vehicle (EV) chargepoints at their rental and leasehold properties.

About the grant

The EV chargepoint grant for landlords is for residential or commercial properties. You must be a landlord or other entity that rents, leases or manages properties in the UK.

The grant covers up to 75% of the cost to buy and install a chargepoint socket, limited to £350 per grant. Some chargepoints may have more than one socket.

Each financial year, landlords can receive up to:

  • 200 grants for residential properties
  • 100 grants for commercial properties

These can be across several properties and installations or for one property.

The Office for Zero Emission Vehicles (OZEV) provides this grant.

Who can apply?

You can apply for this grant if you are a:

  • landlord with property that you let
  • right to manage (RTM) company
  • residents’ management company (RMC)
  • company owning the freehold of a property
  • company owning a building’s common areas, including shareholders who are the property’s leaseholders
  • property factor in Scotland
  • private registered provider of social housing (PRP)
  • public authority, such as government departments and their agencies, the armed forces, local government, the NHS and emergency services
  • charity managing or owning residential property

OZEV will refer to you as the ‘landlord’ if you are any of these.

You must be a UK registered company, charity or public sector organisation to apply. You will need either your:

  • Companies House company registration number
  • VAT registration number

Using your company registration number will speed up the application process.

Linked enterprises

A linked enterprise is where one enterprise within a group of businesses or companies:

  • has a majority of the shareholders’ or members’ voting rights in another enterprise
  • has the right to appoint or remove a majority of the members of the administrative, management or supervisory body of another enterprise
  • has the right to exercise a dominant influence over another enterprise under a contract entered into with that enterprise or to a provision in its memorandum or articles of association
  • is a shareholder in or member of another enterprise, controlling alone, under an agreement with other shareholders in or members of that enterprise, a majority of shareholders or members’ voting rights in that enterprise

OZEV will treat any enterprise with these relationships as a single entity. The maximum grant limits apply to that single entity.

OZEV reserves the right to request further information or evidence from relevant parties to support any individual claim.

Franchises may or may not be a linked enterprise depending on the terms of your franchise agreement.

For more information about linked enterprises, read Article 3 of the European Commission’s User guide to the SME definition.

Does your property qualify?

Your property must be in the UK. Properties in the Channel Islands and Isle of Man are not included.

You can apply for:

  • single-unit residential properties, such as flats and houses
  • multi-unit residential properties, such as apartment blocks
  • commercially-let units

If your property is commercially let, the chargepoint must be for the sole use of your tenant’s staff or fleet vehicles. It cannot be for public use.

For each chargepoint socket installed, you must:

  • have a clearly-defined private parking space
  • own or have the sole legal right to each parking space

Your private parking space can be part of or separate from the property. You may have to show your installer your Land Registry title deeds to prove your legal right.

Some installations may need cables or parts to be placed across private or public land. You must get all legal rights and other permissions before work begins.

Your installer will assess if your parking space allows an EV to charge safely. OZEV will not intervene with an installer’s assessment.

You cannot apply if:

  • the grant is for a single-unit property that you live in
  • your property is a holiday rental
  • you rent the parking spaces from someone else

How the grant works for landlords

1) Contact one or more OZEV-authorised installers.

2) Get a quote and follow your chosen installer’s advice.

3) Check you are installing an OZEV-approved chargepoint model:

  • OZEV-approved residential chargepoint model list
  • OZEV-approved commercial chargepoint model list

4) Get your installer’s OZEV installer number, if you want to go ahead.

5) Create an account and apply for the grant. See the ‘How to apply’ section of this guidance to create or log into your landlord account.

6) Once OZEV approves the grant application, your installer can start the work.

7) Your installer has 90 days to do the installation. They can request extra time to complete the installation if it is needed. If the application expires, you must submit a new one.

8) When your installer finishes the installation, they will claim the grant on your behalf. To do this, they will upload a copy of your invoice and photos of the chargepoints installed. Your invoice will include a discount to cover the grant amount.

9) OZEV pays the grant to your installer.

Ask your installer if they are a member of the Electric Vehicle Consumer Code for Home Chargepoints (EVCC).

For technical details, read EV chargepoint grant for landlords: installer guidance.

Grant rules and restrictions

Landlords can claim up to 200 residential and 100 commercial grants per financial year.

You must:

  • use OZEV-authorised installers
  • only install OZEV-approved chargepoint models:
    • OZEV-approved residential chargepoint model list
    • OZEV-approved commercial chargepoint model list
  • use one address per application
  • use one installer per application – if you use more than one, you must submit a separate application for each one
  • provide one invoice per application
  • apply for residential and commercial properties separately

The approved grant amount is valid for 90 days from the application submission date. If necessary, your installer can extend this.

In considering eligibility, OZEV will use the public authorities referenced in:

  • the Local Government Act 2003, Section 33
  • the Freedom of Information Act, Schedule 1, parts 1-3

Chargepoints and sockets

You can apply for multiple chargepoint sockets at the same address in the same application.

A twin or double chargepoint counts as 2 chargepoint sockets.

The chargepoints you install must supply a minimum of 3kW to each chargepoint socket. This applies when all the sockets installed are being used for charging at the same time.

OZEV will reject your claim if your chargepoint model is not on the approved list. Chargepoints are listed at the request of their manufacturer. If a chargepoint is not listed, contact the manufacturer to confirm why not.

Chargepoint audits

OZEV may select your installation for an audit to ensure it meets the grant requirements. If your installation is selected, auditors will contact you directly on behalf of OZEV. If you do not allow a site visit, OZEV may revoke your registration.

Wider responsibilities of property owners and managers

The regulations say that, as the property owner or manager, you must:

  • assess potential fire risks
  • put in place adequate and proportionate fire protection measures to reduce the risk to life from fire

You must ensure that these measures are maintained.

You should update your risk assessment when installing EV chargepoints.

Grant misuse

OZEV reserves the right to take whatever action it thinks is appropriate if there is any evidence of:

  • grant requirements not being upheld
  • fraudulent activity
  • misuse of government funding by any party

Grant changes

The government reserves the right to end or change this grant scheme. If the grant ends or the grant amount changes, the government will:

  • aim to provide 4 weeks’ notice
  • honour claims made before any public announcement, subject to the grant criteria

How to apply

Before you apply, you must:

  • complete any upgrades to the building’s electrical supply that are needed to service its chargepoints
  • make arrangements for the maintenance of your chargepoints once they are installed
  • make arrangements - to pay for the electricity and other running costs of your chargepoints
  • consider the accessibility needs for EV owners with disabilities or mobility issues

To start an application, you must:

  • understand the grant rules and restrictions
  • agree with your installer what work they will carry out
  • have a landlord account

Create an account

To create a landlord account, you will need (one of the following):

  • company registration number
  • VAT registration number

You will also need:

  • access to an email address
  • a UK mobile phone with a signal as we will send you a security code

If you are a linked enterprise, you must apply using the company registration number of your controlling company.

Create an account or sign in to apply.

Chargepoints at commercial properties

There’s a different way to apply if you’re installing a chargepoint at a commercial property. Read the interim guidance to find out what you need to do.

Enquiries

You can email us if you have any queries about how to create an account or make an application.

You can also report any inconsistencies with the information supplied by an installer about a claim they have completed on your behalf.

Contact LandlordGrantEnq@dvla.gov.uk.

Other EV grants

There are other EV grants available for:

  • Workplace Charging Scheme (WCS) - businesses
  • Energy Saving Trust - domestic chargepoint funding in Scotland
  • EV chargepoint grant for flat owner-occupiers and people living in rented properties – for tenants only
