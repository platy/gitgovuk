Guidance

Selling coal for domestic use in England

Rules for coal merchants and retailers selling traditional house coal for use in domestic appliances in England.

From:

Coal merchants

To sell traditional house coal (also known as bituminous coal) you must be registered as a member of the Approved Coal Merchants scheme.

All sales of traditional house coal will be banned in England from 1 May 2023.

Delivering traditional house coal

You can only supply traditional house coal loose or in open bags directly to a consumer. Coal must not be bagged.

Advice to customers

You should tell your customers that traditional house coal will no longer be available from 1 May 2023.

You should advise your customers between 1 May 2021 and 1 May 2023 on the best manufactured solid fuel for them to switch to.

Retailers

Retail outlets in England including supermarkets and garage forecourts are not allowed to sell traditional house coal.

Low smoke (‘smokeless’) fuels

Coal merchants and retailers can continue to sell:

  • anthracite coal
  • semi-anthracite coal
  • low volatile steam coal

Smoke control areas

The rules also apply to the sale of traditional house coal in smoke control areas across England.

Only approved coal merchants can sell it, it must be sold directly to a consumer loose or in open bags and it can only be used in an appropriate exempt appliance.

After 1 May 2023 all sales of traditional house coal will no longer be allowed in England’s smoke control areas.

Read more about selling fuel in smoke control areas.

Enforcement checks

Enforcement officers will check that suppliers and retailers are complying with the rules.

They may check:

  • records at registered coal merchants to confirm that sales of traditional house coal in England are reducing between 1 May 2021 and 1 May 2023
  • records of the types of coal sold (for example, traditional house coal, anthracite, lignite)
  • delivery records to find out if deliveries are made in England that are within or outside smoke control areas

Fines

Coal merchants and retailers could be fined by local authority enforcement officers if unauthorised coal is sold to domestic household customers.

Depending on how severe the offence is this could be:

  • a £300 fixed penalty fine (FPN) issued by the local authority
  • a more substantial fine issued by the courts

Coal merchants could be fined if they sell unauthorised fuel to customers using ‘non-exempt appliances’ in smoke control areas. The amount of the fine will be decided by the court.

Challenging a fine

Read guidance on how to challenge or pay a FPN.

Exemptions

Traditional house coal extracted and sold from the Forest of Dean is exempt. It can continue to be sold locally.
