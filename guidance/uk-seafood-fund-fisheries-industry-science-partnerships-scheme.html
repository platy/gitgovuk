This guide is for people interested in applying for the Fisheries Industry Science Partnerships (FISP) scheme. Eligible applicants are:

  • sole traders who own or operate a UK fishing boat
  • organisations engaged in commercial or recreational fishing, processing or marine aquaculture in the UK
  • research organisations

The FISP scheme is part of the UK Seafood Fund.

About the FISP scheme

The FISP scheme will improve and share knowledge of fisheries and aquaculture by funding data collection and research to support sustainable fisheries management.

The FISP scheme and the Seafood Innovation Fund share the £24 million of funding allocated to the science and innovation part of the UK Seafood Fund.

All FISP projects must be a partnership between a member of the UK seafood industry and a research organisation.

To get funding, you must bid for a contract. Contracts are available for research projects through FISP part B. You can bid for projects:

  • up to £150,000 a year
  • from £150,000 to £500,000 a year

Contracts are available for a maximum of 2 years.

Applications for FISP part A grants (to develop a research proposal) are now closed.

When to apply

You can now apply for a contract through FISP until midday on 5 September 2022.

Defra plans to open a final round to bid for a contract through FISP from 21 November 2022 until midday on 5 January 2023. These dates may change.

How to apply

BRAVO is Defra’s online tendering system. You must use the FISP Invitation to Tender (ITT) on BRAVO to submit an application for a contract.

Read the ITT document on BRAVO carefully. This provides complete information about the tendering process, including eligibility criteria and how we will assess your application.

Register on BRAVO

  1. Go to the BRAVO website.

  2. Select ‘Register now’.

  3. Accept the conditions of the user agreement and select ‘Next’.

  4. Enter your organisation and user details.

  5. Make a note of the username you chose and select ‘Save’.

You’ll get an email with your unique password.

Contact the BRAVO helpdesk for any technical issues with the system:

Telephone: 0800 069 8630
Email: help@bravosolution.co.uk

Writing your application

Project partners should develop and write the application together. Proposals generally have a greater chance of success when both industry and research partners are involved throughout the whole process.

Find out about projects that have already been awarded FISP funding.

Getting help with your application

If you’ve already registered on BRAVO and started an application, you can submit a ‘clarification question’ if you need help. To do this, use the message function in the ITT on BRAVO. The FISP team in Defra will aim to respond to your question within 5 working days.

You can ask questions about the application process or submit a description of your project to check if it is eligible. You can also request a call back from the FISP team if you need help using the BRAVO system.

Attend a question and answer session

Defra is hosting 2 online question and answer sessions in August 2022. The FISP team will be available to answer your questions about the scheme.

Use the links below to join a session on your computer or mobile device:

  • 4 August 2022: FISP question and answer session 1 (2pm to 3pm)
  • 9 August 2022: FISP question and answer session 2 (11:30am to 12:30pm)

Outcome of your application

You’ll get an email with a link to BRAVO to find out if your application has been successful. We’ll aim to send this within 3 months of when the bidding round closes.

If your application is successful, we’ll issue a contract within 10 days of notifying you.

If your application is unsuccessful, you’ll get feedback to help you apply in the next round of FISP funding.
