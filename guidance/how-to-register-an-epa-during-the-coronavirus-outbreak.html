Can I register an EPA?

The government has published the ‘COVID-19 Response - Spring 2021’ setting out the roadmap out of the lockdown for England. This explains how restrictions will be eased over time. See specific guidance for Wales.

Some of the rules on what you can and cannot do changed on 29 March. However, many restrictions remain in place. Find out what you can and cannot do.

An enduring power of attorney (EPA) made and signed before 1 October 2007 can still be registered with OPG. You must register the EPA as soon as the donor starts to lose mental capacity.

You must also give notice to the donor, their family members and any other attorneys.

The EPA will usually be registered between 8 and 10 weeks after you gave the notice and sent the application form.

Give notice to the donor, their family members and other attorneys

Before applying to register an EPA, the attorneys must use form EP1PG to notify the donor, their family members and any attorneys who were appointed ‘jointly and severally’ but are not applying to register the EPA.

You must follow social distancing guidelines when giving notice.

Notice should be given by post if possible. If this is not possible, family members or other attorneys to be notified can give permission to accept the notice by email. They must reply to the email to confirm they have received the notice.

When notifying the donor, it may be appropriate for someone else to give notice to the donor on the attorneys’ behalf. For example, notice can be posted to care home staff to give to the donor.

If it’s not possible to give notice to the donor, the attorney will need to apply to the Court of Protection for permission to be excused from serving notice to the donor.

What if I cannot register the EPA?

If the donor has lost mental capacity and you cannot register the EPA, your authority to act as the attorney is suspended until you register the instrument with OPG.

If you need specific authority to make decisions for the person and you cannot register the EPA, you could consider seeking legal advice.

I’ve made an EPA, do I need to have it registered now?

If you’ve made an EPA but still have mental capacity, it does not need to be registered with OPG yet. Your attorney can continue to use the EPA to manage your financial affairs with your permission and instructions.

If you’re concerned about planning for your care and treatment in the short term, read our guidance on short-term options for health and welfare decisions.
