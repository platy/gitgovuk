Coronavirus (COVID-19) test kit: give feedback or report harm

How to give feedback about your COVID-19 rapid lateral flow, PCR or saliva test kit, including reporting harm or reactions you had when you did the test.

From:
UK Health Security Agency
Published
10 December 2021

Contents

  1. How to give feedback or report harm
  2. What happens after you report harm

You can give feedback about your NHS COVID-19 rapid lateral flow test kit, PCR test kit or saliva (spit) test kit.

If anything in your COVID-19 test kit is damaged or missing, do not use it. You’ll need to use another test kit.

If you’ve been harmed or had a reaction using the test kit, report it to us as soon as possible.

Your feedback will help us improve our COVID-19 testing services.

How to give feedback or report harm

You can give feedback or report harm online.

Rapid lateral flow test kit

Rapid lateral flow tests give a quick result. They are only for people who do not have symptoms of COVID-19.

Rapid lateral flow test kit: give feedback or report harm

PCR test kit

Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) tests are a test for COVID-19 that are sent to a lab.

PCR test kit: give feedback or report harm

Saliva (spit) test kit

Saliva tests, also known as a spit tests and loop-mediated isothermal amplification (LAMP) tests, are for people who do not have symptoms of COVID-19.

Saliva (spit) test kit: give feedback or report harm

Other ways to give feedback or report harm

You can call 119 (free from mobiles and landlines).

Lines are open every day 7am to 11pm. 119 provides supports in 200 languages.

SignVideo is a free online British Sign Language interpreter service for 119.

What happens after you report harm

The UK Health Security Agency, the test kit manufacturer or a medical specialist may investigate the problem depending on how serious it is. It’ll be recorded to help prevent similar incidents in future, even if it’s not investigated.

You’ll only be contacted if you need to provide more information.
