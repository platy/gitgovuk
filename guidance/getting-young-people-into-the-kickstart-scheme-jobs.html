Employer guidance

Applications to the Kickstart Scheme are closing soon.

After 17 December 2021 you will not be able to:

  • apply for a new Kickstart Scheme grant
  • add more jobs to an existing grant agreement

We will process applications submitted before this time or that are already in progress.

Before you can get a young person into a job

You need to:

  • successfully apply online or apply through a Kickstart gateway for a Kickstart Scheme grant before 17 December 2021
  • sign and return your Kickstart Scheme grant agreement to DWP or your Kickstart gateway
  • sign in to manage your Kickstart Scheme vacancies (only if you’ve applied online)

If you applied through a Kickstart gateway, you do not need to sign in to manage your vacancies. You will give your vacancy details to your gateway, who will share them with DWP on your behalf.

How to get the young people into the jobs

  1. Work coaches at Jobcentre Plus will use the vacancies you or your gateway submitted to match candidates to the jobs.

  2. You or your Kickstart gateway will receive a ‘referral’ email each time a young person is referred to the vacancy.

  3. Referred candidates may choose to apply for the job placement.

  4. You’ll then be able to interview the candidates who have applied.

  5. You or your Kickstart gateway will tell DWP when the young person has started so we can process the funding. You’ll be told how to do this in the ‘referral’ email.

You can advertise vacancies yourself, but all jobs must receive an introduction through a DWP work coach to receive full funding. DWP also uploads the approved vacancies to Find a Job on your behalf – so you do not have to do this. Young people can search this site for Kickstart vacancies themselves.

If a young person leaves the job early

You must email DWP as soon as possible if a young person either:

  • leaves their job before the end of the 6-month period
  • needs to temporarily leave their job (for example for special leave or COVID-19-related restrictions)

The email address is the same one you use to tell DWP about the young person’s start date. You’ll be told how to do this in the ‘referral’ email you get when a work coach refers a young person to your job vacancy.

If a young person leaves their job early, the next scheduled wage payment from DWP will be the last you receive for them.

DWP may be able to extend the funding period if the young person has to temporarily stop working.

You can replace the person who left early with another young person referred to the vacancy by a work coach.

Add more jobs to your grant agreement (grant variation)

You can add more jobs to your grant agreement until 17 December 2021 after it has been approved. This is known as a ‘grant variation’.

The additional jobs will be assessed to make sure they meet the Kickstart Scheme criteria.

If you applied directly, you can add more jobs online using the application service (until 17 December 2021).

If you applied through a Kickstart gateway, tell them you’d like to add more jobs before 17 December 2021. They will contact DWP to arrange this.

Get help and support with managing your Kickstart Scheme jobs

Contact your local or national Kickstart Scheme contact if you need help managing your Kickstart Scheme jobs.
