What HPTs do

Local HPTs lead the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA)’s response to all health-related incidents. They provide specialist support to prevent and reduce the impact of:

  • infectious diseases
  • chemical and radiation hazards
  • major emergencies

HPTs can help with:

  • local disease surveillance
  • maintaining alert systems
  • investigating and managing health protection incidents and outbreaks
  • implementing and monitoring national action plans for infectious diseases at local level

Use the find your local HPT in England postcode lookup if you’re a health professional needing expert local health protection advice.

Sending personal identifiable information (PII) and sensitive information

Take care when sending PII, and never put PII in the subject line of the email.

Sending PII to South London HPT

Please encrypt any personal identifiable information (PII) you send to slhpt.oncall@phe.gov.uk.

If you are sharing PII from another NHS.net email account, send this to phe.slhpt@nhs.net.

Sending PII to North East and North Central London HPT

Please encrypt any personal identifiable information (PII) sent to necl.team@phe.gov.uk.

If you are sending PII from an NHS.net email account, please send to phe.nenclhpt@nhs.net.

Sending PII and sensitive information Cheshire and Merseyside HPT

Please encrypt any personal identifiable information (PII) you send to candmhpu@phe.gov.uk.

If you are sharing PII from another NHS.net email account, send this to phe.candmhpu@nhs.net.

Contact the office if you do not know how to send emails securely.
