Guidance

Moving goods out of Great Britain through common transit countries

Find out what to do when you move goods out of Great Britain using transit.

From:
HM Revenue & Customs
Published
10 September 2021
Last updated
1 August 2022 — See all updates

Applies to England, Scotland and Wales

You should check if you can use common transit to move your goods and what you need to do to set up your business for transit movements.

Goods can start their journey from an authorised consignor premises or an office of departure.

If the goods are departing from an authorised consignor premises

Before the goods leave

  1. Plan your route.

  2. Get information about your haulier for your records.

  3. Make an export declaration.

  4. Load the goods, seal the doors (where appropriate), and note the seal number on the New Computerised Transit System (NCTS).

  5. Make a transit declaration.

  6. Wait for clearance from Border Force.

  7. Print the transit accompanying document (TAD) at your premises.

  8. Pass the TAD and other relevant information about your goods to your haulier.

  9. Tell your haulier to begin their journey.

The TAD will include your transit movement reference number which you need when using transit to move your goods.

If the goods are not cleared by Border Force to leave, you’ll need to keep the vehicle at your premises until Border Force arrive to inspect it.

While the goods are moving

  1. Check the movement of your goods in NCTS.

  2. Check and respond to any NCTS messages.

If an incident occurs on route, you or the haulier must contact the customs authority in that country where the incident happened.

When the goods arrive

  1. Check the movement has been ended on NCTS and your guarantee has been released.

  2. You can a receipt from your haulier.

  3. Keep records of your movement.

If the goods have not been delivered to customs at their destination, we will contact you within 7 days of the date when the goods should have arrived.

If the goods are departing from an office of departure

Before the goods leave

  1. Plan your route.

  2. Get information about your haulier for your records.

  3. Make an export declaration. If you’ve used entry in the declarant’s records for the export, you’ll need to make exit summary declaration separately.

  4. Load the goods, seal the doors, and note the seal number on NCTS.

  5. Make a transit declaration and include the export declaration reference number.

After you’ve submitted your transit declaration, you’ll also need to:

  • give the haulier details about the goods
  • give the haulier the local reference number (LRN), and either the export declaration references or a Master Unique Consignment Reference (MUCR), that you will have generated
  • tell the haulier to take the goods to either the inland border facility (IBF) or a port’s designated customs checking facility, or to an office of departure

If the goods are leaving from a location that needs your export declaration to be submitted as arrived

If the goods are leaving from a location that needs an export declaration to be submitted as ‘arrived’, you’ll need to:

  1. If you’re using Customs Handling of Import and Export Freight, either give all the export declaration references to the haulier so they can create a goods movement reference, or you can include them in a MUCR and give a paper copy to the haulier. If you’re using the Customs Declaration Service, you’ll need to give all the export declaration references to the haulier so they can create a goods movement reference.

  2. Submit the documents HMRC will tell you to send to the National Clearance Hub before the goods leave your premises, if the declaration has been chosen for a documentary check.

  3. Tell the haulier to take the goods to an IBF or a port’s designated customs facility to be checked, if the declaration has been chosen for an inspection.

While the goods are moving

  1. Check the movement of your goods in NCTS.

  2. Check and respond to any NCTS messages.

If an incident occurs, you or the haulier must contact the customs authority in that country.

When the goods arrive

  1. Check the movement has been ended on NCTS and your guarantee has been released.

  2. Request a receipt from your haulier.

  3. Keep records of your movement.

If the goods have not been delivered to customs at their destination, we will contact you within 7 days of the date when the goods should have arrived.

If you’re a haulier departing from an authorised consignor premises

Before the goods leave

  1. Get information about the goods from the exporter, and provide your details.

  2. Get your paper TAD which will include your transit movement reference number, this must stay with you throughout your journey.

  3. Get any other customs documentation including export declarations, licences and route information.

  4. Move to the port and exit the UK.

While the goods are moving

  1. Complete the exit and entry requirements at each customs border including the port your exiting in Great Britain.

  2. Move goods to each office of transit on your TAD.

  3. Present your TAD and goods at the office of transit.

When the goods arrive

  1. Present the TAD and goods to the office of destination or authorised consignee.

  2. Wait for customs authority to clear the goods.

  3. Send a receipt to the exporter.

If you’re a haulier departing from an office of departure

Before the goods leave

  1. Get information about the goods from the exporter, and provide your details.

  2. Get any other customs documentation including export declarations, licences and route information.

  3. Get your LRN (and a MUCR if there is one) from the exporter or the declarant.

  4. Take the goods to the designated office of departure.

At the office of departure

  1. Present the goods with the LRN to office of departure.

  2. Show either a goods movement reference or a MUCR, if there is one.

  3. Get your transit accompanying document which will include your transit MRN.

  4. Proceed to the port you are leaving from in Great Britain.

If the goods are leaving from a location that needs an export declaration to be submitted as arrived

If the goods are leaving from a location that needs an export declaration to be submitted as ‘arrived’, you’ll need to:

  1. Include all the export declaration references in a goods movement reference, or get a MUCR from the declarant in writing. This will need to be presented at the office of departure.

  2. Make sure there are no documentary checks needed on the export declarations. If there are, you may be turned away at the office of departure.

  3. Take the goods to an IBF or a port’s designated customs facility, if they are chosen for an inspection. You will need to tell the staff at the site that your goods have been chosen for inspection.

  4. Enter your transit movement reference number from the TAD in the goods movement reference, if you are given a MUCR by the declarant. You’ll need to do this before you arrive at the location the goods will be leaving Great Britain.

  5. Make sure you have a valid goods movement reference for leaving through any of the goods vehicle movement service locations. If you do not have one, you’ll not be able to leave.

While the goods are moving

  1. Complete the exit and entry requirements at each customs border including the port you are leaving in Great Britain.

  2. Move goods to the office of transit on your TAD.

  3. Present your TAD and goods at the office of transit.

When the goods arrive

  1. Present the TAD and goods to the office of destination or authorised consignee.

  2. Wait for customs authority to clear the goods.

  3. You can end a receipt or evidence of TAD discharge to the guarantor of the TAD.
