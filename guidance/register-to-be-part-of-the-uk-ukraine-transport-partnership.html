Guidance

Register to be part of the UK-Ukraine transport partnership

Register your organisation’s interest to support the reconstruction of critical transport infrastructure in Ukraine.

From:
Department for Transport
Published
25 August 2022

Contents

  1. Register your interest
  2. What happens next
  3. How we will handle your data

Ukraine needs support to start planning and rebuilding its transport network. Road, rail, airport and port infrastructure all need to be rebuilt or repaired.

The UK government is setting up the UK-Ukraine transport partnership to help with Ukrainian reconstruction. Private organisations and companies can register their interest in providing their specialist skills, knowledge and expertise.

Register your interest

To register your interest in joining the UK-Ukraine transport partnership, email the following information to UUTP@dft.gov.uk:

  • contact name
  • your role in the company or organisation
  • name of company or organisation
  • direct contact email address
  • transport sector expertise

Please advise if your organisation’s name can be published as a member of the UK-Ukraine transport partnership, or if you wish to remain anonymous.

Do not send any information apart from that asked for above. Read the section of this guidance on how we will handle your information.

What happens next

After we receive your email, we will contact you to confirm that your interest has been received and logged.

The department is working with the government of Ukraine to understand how best to facilitate collaboration and maximise the impact of UK industry. If any opportunities arise which match your transport sector expertise, we will contact you directly with further information.

How we will handle your data

The Department for Transport (DfT) is creating a register of organisations interested in the UK-Ukraine transport partnership. To register your interest, we’re asking for a contact name, job role and contact details. We will use these details to contact you if any opportunities arise which match your transport sector expertise.

You do not have to give us this personal information and it will only be processed with your consent.  If you do provide it, DfT will, under data protection law, be the controller for this data and will only use it for the purpose outlined above. You can withdraw your consent at any time using the Data Protection Officers contact details in our privacy policy linked below.

DfT’s privacy policy has more information about your rights in relation to your personal data, how to complain and how to contact the data protection officer.

Your personal data will be kept securely by the DfT and will not be shared with anyone else.  We will hold your data for 24 months of receipt or until you withdraw your consent.  After 24 months, we may contact you asking to refresh your consent to continue to hold your data.

If you give us permission to do so, we may list your organisation as a member of the UK-Ukraine transport partnership. Any published information will not include your name, job role or contact details.
