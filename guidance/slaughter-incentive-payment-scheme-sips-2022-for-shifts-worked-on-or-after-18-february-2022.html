Slaughter Incentive Payment Scheme (SIPS) 2022 - for shifts worked on or after 18 February 2022

Food business operators can claim payment for slaughtering additional pigs.

Changes made to the scheme from 18 February 2022 remove the requirement for pigmeat, from pigs slaughtered during a SIPS 2022 shift, to go for export or into the Private Storage Aid Scheme.

These changes apply to SIPS 2022 shifts worked on or after 18 February 2022.

Separate guidance Slaughter Incentive Payment Scheme 2022 applies to SIPS 2022 shifts worked before 18 February 2022.

Defra has been working closely with industry to understand how best to support them in response to the challenges caused by the pandemic, access to CO2 supplies, a temporary shortage of labour (specifically skilled butchers) and the loss of the Chinese market to several processing plants (as a result of the pandemic). This led to a growing number of pigs backing up on farm and impacted the capacity of processors to slaughter and process pigs. Pig prices have fallen and without continued intervention could rapidly fall very significantly further.

On 14 October 2021, Government announced a package of measures to support the pig industry and to seek to reduce the animal welfare implications of pigs backing up on farms. This package included measures to increase the throughput of pigs through processors.

The Slaughter Incentive Payment Scheme (SIPS) aim was to increase the throughput of pigs by contributing towards the extra costs involved in operating additional slaughter shifts at abattoirs. SIPS closed on 20 December 2021.

The revised SIP scheme (SIPS 2022) aims to incentivise processors to maximise the use of additional butchers from 14 January 2022 by putting on more shifts than has been possible at the end of 2021.

Following discussions with industry, changes made to the scheme from 18 February 2022 remove the requirement for pigmeat, from pigs slaughtered during a SIPS 2022 shift, to go for export or into the Private Storage Aid Scheme, which opened in November 2021.

The increase in the payment rate for eligible pigs slaughtered during the SIPS 2022 shifts from £3 to £10 per pig remains in place. The requirement for pigmeat entering Private Storage Aid to meet the relevant scheme rules also remains.

The scheme remains limited to 100,000 pigs.

SIPS 2022 will continue to be administered by the Rural Payments Agency (RPA).

Scheme dates

  • 14 January 2022 - scheme opens: first date additional shifts can be worked
  • 30 June 2022 - anticipated date that scheme closes - last date SIPS 2022 shifts can be worked (the scheme may be closed earlier if the limit of 100,000 pigs slaughtered is reached). The scheme was unavailable for SIPS shifts between 1 April and 6 April 2022 (inclusive).
  • 1 April 2022 - first claim window opens
  • 29 April 2022 - first claim window closes
  • 1 July 2022 – second claim window opens
  • 29 July 2022 – second claim window closes

Eligibility for the scheme

Pigs must be:

  • clean (have not been used for breeding)
  • a minimum deadweight of 25kg cold weight
  • slaughtered and processed in England

Shifts must be:

  • additional to shifts already agreed with the Food Standards Agency (FSA)
  • dedicated shifts for pigs to be slaughtered under SIPS 2022
  • standalone shifts – either on an additional day or with a distinct break between normal shifts and a SIPS 2022 shift
  • carried out between 14 January 2022 and 30 June 2022 (inclusive) and approved in advance by FSA

Claims must:

  • be one claim for each applicant (a single organisation, not one claim for each abattoir or site). A single organisation may submit a maximum of one claim for each claim window
  • contain all information requested in the claim form
  • be submitted between 1 April and 29 April 2022 and / or 1 July 2022 and 29 July 2022 (inclusive) - read about how to claim
  • be submitted by a person authorised to act on behalf of the business and with the correct permission levels in the Rural Payments service

How to register

Register in the Rural Payments service

You will need to be registered in the Rural Payments service so that we can pay you. To save time during the claim period, we recommend you do this in advance.

If you are not registered for the Rural Payments service, go to the Rural Payments page and follow the instructions about registering for Rural Payments.

If you’re not able to register online, call the RPA helpline on 03000 200 301 and they will help you to register.

Please state that you are a Trader and not a farmer, to make sure you get the correct registration with us.

Your bank details must be current to allow us to pay you without delay. If you are already registered with the Rural Payments service but you have changed your bank account details since we last paid you, please call us on 03000 200 301 and select the bank account option.

Register an expression of interest

If you’re interested in the scheme, you can complete a Slaughter Incentive Payment Scheme 2022 Expression of Interest form.

You do not need to complete the form to join the scheme, but this will inform RPA about participating abattoirs and assist the FSA with planning for additional veterinary cover.

How to claim

Following the extension of the scheme closing date, processors may choose to submit an initial claim before 30 April 2022 for SIPS 2022 shifts worked during the period up to and including 31 March 2022. Then, submit a second claim for SIPS 2022 shifts worked during the period 7 April until 30 June 2022 (inclusive). Or, submit a single claim covering SIPS 2022 shifts worked during the entire period when the claim window opens on 1 July 2022.

The claim windows open on 1 April 2022 and 1 July 2022 and you will need to download, complete and send us a Slaughter Incentive Payment Scheme 2022 (SIPS 2022) Claim for payment form by 29 July 2022 so that we can pay you.

We may ask for evidence so that we can validate your claim for example, traceability records.

Email your claim form and supporting evidence to trader@rpa.gov.uk.

Payment

The payment rate for the scheme is set at £10 for every eligible pig. Payment will be made within 28 days of a valid claim being received.

Recovery

If any of the following apply, we may take action to recover payments.

  • False or inaccurate statement(s) made by the applicant in the claim form
  • False or inaccurate evidence provided by the applicant in support of the claim
  • Any overpayment or erroneous payment made by RPA when processing the claim

Contact us

If you have any questions, or need help, contact RPA.

Email trader@rpa.gov.uk - write ‘Slaughter Incentive Payment Scheme 2022’ in the subject line.

Telephone: 03300 416 500 Opening times: Monday to Friday 8.30am to 5pm, except bank holidays.
