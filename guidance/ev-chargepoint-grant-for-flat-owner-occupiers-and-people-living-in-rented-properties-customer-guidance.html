New guidance from 4 August 2022

This new guidance replaces the interim guidance.

You should follow this new guidance for all new applications.

You should follow the interim guidance for any applications started before 4 August.

About the grant

The EV chargepoint grant for flat owner-occupiers and people living in rented properties is for anyone who:

  • owns and lives in a flat
  • rents any residential property

The grant covers up to 75% of the cost to buy and install a chargepoint socket, up to Â£350 per grant.

Your installer will claim the grant on your behalf.

The Office for Zero Emission Vehicles (OZEV) provides this grant.

Who can apply?

You can apply for this grant if:

  • you own and live in a flat, or you rent any residential property
  • your home has its own private off-street parking space
  • you own a qualifying vehicle

You can also apply if you are moving to an eligible property with an old chargepoint that you want to replace. You must not have received the grant before and you must meet all other criteria.

You should find out about the maintenance and running costs of a chargepoint before you apply.

You cannot apply if you:

  • have already received this grant or a grant under the Electric Vehicle Homecharge Scheme
  • are moving home
  • want to move an existing chargepoint to a new property
  • live in a house that you own

You should consult with an OZEV-authorised installer to find out if you qualify.

Does your property qualify?

Your property must be in the UK. Properties in the Channel Islands and Isle of Man are not included.

You can only apply for an existing residential property that you live in.

You cannot use it for new-build or unoccupied properties.

OZEV will check your address using your vehicle registration details. You may need to provide supporting evidence, such as a utility bill.

For the chargepoint socket installed, you must:

  • have a clearly-defined private off-street parking space
  • have access to the parking space at all times
  • own or have the sole legal right to the parking space

Your private parking space can be part of, or separate from, the property. You may have to show your installer your Land Registry title deeds to prove your legal right.

You must get all legal rights and permissions, such as from the freeholder of a block of flats.

You cannot place cables over public land, such as pavements, without permission from your local authority. This applies even if it is temporary.

Your installer will assess if your parking space allows an EV to charge safely. OZEV will not intervene with an installerâs assessment.

Does my vehicle qualify?

This grant is only for vehicles on the list of OZEV-approved electric vehicles.

You must provide evidence that you either:

  • are the registered keeper of one
  • have one on order
  • have leased one for at least 6 months, including via salary sacrifice
  • have used one as a work vehicle for at least 6 months

You cannot apply for this grant if your vehicle is not on the list of approved electric vehicles. Vehicles are listed at the request of their manufacturers. If your vehicle is not listed, contact the manufacturer to confirm why not.

Vehicle leases

If your vehicle lease is a bundle with a chargepoint, it must:

  • state that you own the chargepoint once payment is complete
  • itemise the cost of the chargepoint and installation, including VAT
  • ensure the chargepoint warranty meets the grant requirements
  • ensure the associated vehicle lease is for at least 6 months

You must show your installer proof that you own or lease your electric vehicle.

How to apply

  1. Contact an OZEV-authorised installer.
  2. Get a quote and follow your chosen installerâs advice.
  3. Check your vehicle is on the list of OZEV-approved electric vehicles.
  4. Check the chargepoint is on the list of OZEV-approved chargepoint models.
  5. Ask your installer to start your claim. They will initiate the process.
  6. When your installer starts your claim, you will receive an email with a link to an online form.
  7. Complete the online form to confirm your details.
  8. Contact your installer to start the installation.
  9. When your installer finishes the installation, they must upload proof. Evidence includes a copy of your invoice and photos of the chargepoint installed.
  10. Your invoice will show the total installation cost minus the grant amount.
  11. OZEV pays the grant to your installer.

Ask your installer if they are a member of the Electric Vehicle Consumer Code for Home Chargepoints (EVCC).

For technical details, read EV chargepoint grant for flat owner-occupiers and people living in rented properties: installer guidance.

Chargepoint audits

OZEV may select your installation for an audit to ensure it meets the grant requirements. If your installation is selected, auditors will contact you directly on behalf of OZEV.

Chargepoints and sockets

You should not install a chargepoint model that is not on the approved list. If you do, OZEV will reject your claim.

Chargepoints are listed at the request of their manufacturer. If a chargepoint is not listed, contact the manufacturer to confirm why not.

You should find out about the maintenance and running costs of a chargepoint before you install one.

Grant misuse

OZEV reserves the right to take whatever action it thinks is appropriate if there is any evidence of:

  • grant requirements not being upheld
  • fraudulent activity
  • misuse of government funding by any party

Grant changes

The government reserves the right to end or change this grant scheme at any time. If it ends or the grant amount changes, the government will:

  • aim to provide 4 weeksâ notice
  • honour claims made before any public announcement, subject to them meeting the grant criteria

Enquiries

You can contact OZEV with any queries at Chargepoint.Grants@ozev.gov.uk.
