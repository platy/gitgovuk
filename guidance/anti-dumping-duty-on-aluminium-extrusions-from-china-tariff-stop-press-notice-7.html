Anti-dumping duty on aluminium extrusions from China (Tariff stop press notice 7)

From 28 May 2022, anti-dumping duty on these products from China requires importers to provide HMRC with a bank guarantee until the provision expires.

Important information for importers of aluminium extrusions from China.

HMRC is introducing a provisional anti-dumping duty on aluminium extrusions from China. Importers of these products will need to provide HMRC with a bank guarantee while the provisional duty applies.

Changes introduced by the provision

From 28 May 2022, these changes will take effect.

  1. A provisional tariff will apply to goods from China falling within the commodity codes listed at the end of this notice. The provisional tariff is to be covered by the guarantee, and is not payable during the period of the provisional remedy.

  2. Duty rates between 9.5% and 22.35% will apply to imports from Chinese companies that have cooperated with the Trade Remedy Authorityâ€™s investigation are included in this notice.

  3. For all other Chinese exporters, a duty rate of 128.17% will apply. Importers of goods from the China must secure a bank guarantee equal to the estimated amount of provisional anti-dumping duty due on their imports.

  4. The guaranteed amount will only become payable if definitive measures are imposed. The Trade Remedy Authority regularly updates their public file with interim findings and case timelines. Interested parties can use this to increase their understanding of the process.
