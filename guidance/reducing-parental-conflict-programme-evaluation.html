Independent programme evaluation

The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) have commissioned IFF Research and the Tavistock Institute of Human Resources to conduct an independent evaluation of the Reducing Parental Conflict (RPC) programme.

Interventions being tested in Contract Package Areas (CPAs)

The evaluation will provide evidence on the implementation and delivery of 8 different interventions. This information will be used to help make informed, evidence-based decisions on which sorts of interventions should be commissioned in the future.

Practitioner training

The evaluation will examine whether the various types of practitioner training and support have changed practice on the ground in terms of:

  • identification
  • support provided
  • the extent to which practitioners feel skilled to deliver support and interventions to reduce parental conflict

It will also evaluate the perceived impact the training had on practitioners’ and professionals’ confidence and ability to provide support for parents in conflict.

Local integration

The evaluation will examine whether and how local authorities have integrated elements of parental conflict support into mainstream service for families.

Estimating the impact of the RPC interventions

Alongside the external evaluation, DWP is collecting data provided by parents at key points in their journey through the programme. This will help to estimate the impact of RPC interventions on reducing conflict and improving child outcomes.
