How to travel safely in airports and on aircraft during the coronavirus outbreak.

Because of coronavirus (COVID-19) there are rules that you need to follow when you travel abroad.

You should not travel if you have any COVID-19 symptoms.

Before booking a flight

Before you book an international flight, you should check what you need to do if you are:

  • travelling abroad from England
  • travelling to or from Northern Ireland
  • travelling to or from Scotland
  • travelling to or from Wales

Where possible, your airline will inform you on measures to minimise transmission of COVID-19.

In line with other medical conditions, airlines have the right to refuse travel to anyone they believe is not fit to fly.

Check what you will need to do if you will be returning to the UK.

NHS COVID Pass – show your COVID-19 vaccination status

An NHS COVID Pass shows your COVID-19 vaccination details or test results. This is your COVID-19 vaccination status.

You can use the NHS COVID Pass to prove your vaccination status when you enter another country while travelling abroad.

Find out:

  • about using the NHS COVID Pass to show your vaccination status
  • if the countries or territories you are travelling to accept proof of COVID-19 vaccination status as part of their entry requirements
  • how to prove your COVID-19 vaccination status if you are a resident of Northern Ireland, Scotland or Wales

NHS test and trace

You must not travel if you have been advised that you must self-isolate by the NHS test and trace service:

  • test and trace in Northern Ireland
  • test and protect in Scotland
  • test and trace in Wales

Online check-in

Where possible, check in online to avoid face-to-face contact at the airport.

You are strongly encouraged to check in baggage to the aircraft hold and minimise any hand baggage. This will speed up boarding and disembarking and minimise the risk of transmission.

Make sure any luggage you intend to check in does not contain any lithium batteries (in equipment such as laptops, or stand-alone batteries such as powerbanks), valuable or essential items (such as cash, credit cards, keys and medicines), or other prohibited items.

Arriving at the airport

Customers should follow the rules and guidance set by transport operators during all stages of their journey.

Follow the guidance on:

  • how to stay safe in England
  • how to stay safe in Northern Ireland
  • how to stay safe in Scotland
  • how to stay safe in Wales

At check-in

Where possible, avoid touching surfaces in the airport. Wash your hands for at least 20 seconds after using self-service check-in, luggage trollies and other often touched surfaces.

Depending on the airport you fly from and where you are flying to, you may need to have your temperature checked before flying.

At the security checkpoint

Follow advice in the airport to prepare for security checks.

On board the aircraft

Your airline will tell you about the measures in place for your flight.

In England, there is no legal requirement to wear a face covering. There may be different rules about wearing a face covering on board aircraft in Northern Ireland, Scotland and Wales.

Some transport operators may require you to wear a face covering as part of their conditions of carriage. Passengers should follow instructions and guidance from the crew.

During the flight, you should make the cabin crew aware if you become ill.

On arrival in the UK

When you arrive in the UK from abroad, you do not need to:

  • take any pre-departure or post-arrival COVID-19 tests
  • quarantine when you arrive

This applies whether you are vaccinated or not.

Transiting through England

Passing through England as part of an international journey is called ‘transiting’.

If you are transiting through England, you do not need to:

  • take any pre-departure or post-arrival COVID-19 tests
  • quarantine when you are in transit

This applies whether you are transiting ‘airside’ or ‘landside’.

Ireland, the UK, the Channel Islands and the Isle of Man

The Common Travel Area (CTA) is made up of Ireland, the UK (England, Northern Ireland, Scotland and Wales), the Channel Islands and the Isle of Man.

If you have not been outside the CTA

If you are travelling to England from somewhere within the CTA, you do not need to:

  • take any pre-departure or post-arrival COVID-19 tests
  • quarantine on arrival in England

Travelling on within the CTA after an international journey

If you will be travelling on from England to Ireland, Northern Ireland, Scotland, Wales, the Channel Island or the Isle of Man after an international journey, you must follow the rules for testing and quarantine for your final destination.

The Isle of Man has additional entry restrictions. Check the requirements for travelling to the Isle of Man.

Travelling within the UK

Because of COVID-19, there may be restrictions in place on what you can do, including restrictions on travel. For details, and for information on national and local restrictions, check:

  • how to stay safe and help prevent the spread of COVID-19 in England
  • safer travel guidance for Northern Ireland  
  • safer travel guidance for Scotland  
  • safer travel guidance for Wales 

Face coverings

In England, there is no legal requirement to wear a face covering. The government suggests that you wear a face-covering in crowded and enclosed spaces where you may come into contact with other people you do not normally meet.

There may be different rules about wearing a face covering on board commercial air flights in Northern Ireland, Scotland and Wales. Read more about:

  • face coverings in Northern Ireland
  • face coverings in Scotland
  • face coverings in Wales

Some transport operators may require you to wear a face covering as part of their conditions of carriage.

You should remove your face covering if asked to do so by police, border control or airport security.

Some international destinations may specify the face covering or mask you must wear – you should check before you travel.
