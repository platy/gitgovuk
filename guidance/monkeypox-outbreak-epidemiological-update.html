Background

Monkeypox is a re-emerging zoonotic infection, caused by the monkeypox virus, that occurs mostly in West and Central Africa. Previous cases in the UK had been either imported from countries where monkeypox is endemic or contacts with documented epidemiological links to imported cases. Between 2018 and 2021 there had been 7 cases of monkeypox in the UK, 4 of which were imported, 2 cases in household contacts, and 1 case in a health care worker involved in the care of an imported case. There was no documented community transmission in previous outbreaks.

Current epidemiological situation for data at 30 May 2022

Cases of monkeypox infection were confirmed in England from 6 May. The outbreak has mainly been affecting people without documented history of travel to endemic countries. Up to 30 May 2022 there were 190 laboratory confirmed cases in the UK.

Of the confirmed cases identified up to 30 May, 4 were in Scotland, 2 were in Northern Ireland, 1 was in Wales and 183 were in England. All further information is for cases among England residents only. A high proportion of cases were known to be London residents (86%, 132 out of 153 with reported home address), with a small number of cases in most other regions of England.

Where gender information was available, the majority were male (184), with only 2 female cases. Most monkeypox cases, 87%, occurred in people aged 20 to 49 years old. One hundred and eleven cases identified to be gay, bisexual, or other men who have sex with men (GBMSM). Recent foreign travel, within 21 days of symptom onset, was reported by 34 confirmed cases (18%), with 27 of these reporting travel within Europe.
