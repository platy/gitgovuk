This new guidance and service replaces the interim guidance.

You should use this new service for all new applications.

You should use the interim guidance and service for any applications started before 4 August.

About the grant

The EV chargepoint grant for flat owner-occupiers and people living in rented properties is for anyone who:

  • owns and lives in a flat
  • rents any residential property

The grant covers up to 75% of the cost to buy and install a chargepoint socket, up to £350 per grant.

You will claim the grant on behalf of your customer.

The Office for Zero Emission Vehicles (OZEV) provides this grant.

Who can apply?

Your customer can apply for this grant if:

  • they own and live in a flat or rent any residential property
  • their home has its own private off-street parking space
  • they own a qualifying vehicle

They can also apply if moving to a house with an old chargepoint that they want to replace. They must not have received the grant before and must meet all other criteria.

Your customer cannot apply if they:

  • have already received this grant or a grant under the Electric Vehicle Homecharge Scheme
  • are moving home
  • want to move an existing chargepoint to a new property
  • live in a house that they own

You must check if the customer meets the grant criteria.

Does the property qualify?

Your customer’s property must be in the UK. Properties in the Channel Islands and Isle of Man are not included.

They can only apply for an existing residential property that they live in.

They cannot use the grant for new-build or unoccupied properties.

For the chargepoint socket installed, the customer must:

  • have a clearly-defined private off-street parking space
  • have access to the parking space at all times
  • own or have the sole legal right to the parking space

A private parking space can be part of or separate from the property. You can ask your customer to show you their Land Registry title deeds to prove their legal right.

Your customer must get all legal rights and permissions, such as from the freeholder of a block of flats.

You cannot place cables over public land, such as pavements, without permission from your local authority. This applies even if it is temporary.

You will assess if each parking space allows an EV to charge safely. OZEV will not intervene with your assessment.

Does the vehicle qualify?

This grant is only for vehicles on the list of OZEV-approved electric vehicles.

Your customer must provide you with evidence that they either:

  • are the registered keeper of one
  • have one on order
  • have leased one for at least 6 months, including via salary sacrifice
  • have used one as a work vehicle for at least 6 months

They cannot apply for this grant if their vehicle is not on the list of approved electric vehicles.

Vehicles are listed at the request of their manufacturers. If a vehicle is not listed, contact the manufacturer to confirm why not.

Vehicle leases

If your customer’s vehicle lease is a bundle with a chargepoint, it must:

  • state that they own the chargepoint once payment is complete
  • itemise the cost of the chargepoint and installation, including VAT
  • ensure the chargepoint warranty meets the grant requirements
  • ensure the associated vehicle lease is for at least 6 months

Your customer must show you proof that they own or lease their electric vehicle.

How the grant works for installers

  1. Your customer contacts you as an OZEV-authorised installer.
  2. Check that your customer and the property qualify for the grant.
  3. Check that their vehicle is on the list of OZEV-approved electric vehicles.
  4. Check that you are installing an OZEV-approved chargepoint model.
  5. Survey the customer’s parking space and provide a quote.
  6. If they agree to proceed, log in to your account and create a new claim.
  7. Your customer will receive an online form to confirm the installation details.
  8. After completing the form, they will contact you to start the installation.
  9. When you finish the installation, you must upload proof. Evidence includes a copy of your invoice and photos of the chargepoint installed.
  10. Your customer’s invoice will include a discount to cover the grant amount. OZEV will pay you the grant.

Grant processing times

Your first claim may take up to 3 months for OZEV to process. This is to allow time for you to be set up on the payment system.

OZEV aims to process further claims within 30 working days.

Where possible, OZEV will contact you if there is a delay in your claim.

To enquire about the progress of a claim, email ChargePointGrantApps@dvla.gov.uk.

Please provide the customer’s name, address, postcode and claim reference number.

Chargepoint audits

OZEV may select your installation for an audit to ensure it meets the grant requirements. The auditors may contact you to conduct the audit. If you fail to respond, OZEV may remove you from the scheme. The auditors will also contact your customer directly on OZEV’s behalf.

Grant rules and restrictions

As an OZEV-authorised installer, you must:

  • help the customer to understand if they meet the grant criteria
  • only install OZEV-approved chargepoint models
  • test that all installed chargepoints work and meet safety requirements
  • provide and upload all documentation to submit the claim
  • not charge customers for the grant in advance of a claim
  • provide the customer with one invoice per application

Chargepoints and sockets

You must carry out chargepoint installations as per:

  • BS EN 61851-1:2019
  • the current edition of the IET wiring regulations – BS 7671:2018+A1:2020
  • the IET Code of Practice for Electric Vehicle Charging Equipment Installations (as amended)
  • the Electricity Safety, Quality and Continuity Regulations

The final installation must also meet electrical safety provisions under relevant building regulations.

You should install chargepoints to meet the needs of EV owners with disabilities or mobility issues.

The chargepoints you install must supply a minimum of 3kW to each chargepoint socket. This applies to sockets installed when they are being used for charging at the same time.

OZEV will reject your claim if your chargepoint model is not on the approved list. Chargepoints are listed at the request of their manufacturer. If a chargepoint is not listed, contact the manufacturer to confirm why not.

All chargepoints must have an on-site 3-year warranty on parts and installation. The landlord should contact you or the manufacturer if they have problems.

Grant misuse

OZEV reserves the right to take whatever action it thinks is appropriate if there is any evidence of:

  • grant requirements not being upheld
  • fraudulent activity
  • misuse of government funding by any party

Grant changes

The government reserves the right to end or change this grant scheme at any time. If it ends or the grant amount changes, the government will:

  • aim to provide 4 weeks’ notice
  • honour claims made before any public announcement, subject to them meeting the grant criteria

What you can claim for

You can claim for:

  • cost of unit
  • electrical components
  • civil engineering works
  • labour costs (for installation)
  • hardware costs
  • the VAT incurred by the customer
  • site survey works (when leading to a completed installation)

Some installations may need an extra smart meter or current clamp device. You can claim the capital cost of this if installed at the same time as the chargepoint.

You cannot claim for:

  • interest charges, bad debts, profits or entertaining
  • project management and reporting costs
  • administration costs
  • arbitrary inflation and contingency allowances for eligible costs
  • new land required for the proposed infrastructure
  • electricity provided by relevant infrastructure
  • ongoing back-office data provision
  • any other ongoing operating costs (for example, maintenance or communication)
  • any warranty extension beyond 3 years
  • transport costs
  • marketing costs
  • any other cost not listed as an eligible expenditure

Do not inflate or cross-subsidise costs across multiple installations.

How to claim

If you are an authorised installer, you can request a link to create a password and access this service for the first time.

You will need:

  • the email address you used to register with OZEV
  • your installer authorisation number

To get an installer authorisation number, you can apply to become an OZEV-authorised installer.

Once you have set up your account, you can sign in to view applications and submit claims.

Sign in to view applications and submit claims

Evidence needed to submit a claim

To submit a claim, you must provide:

  • details of the chargepoints you install
  • a copy of the invoice

For each chargepoint you install you must provide:

  • its make and model as listed on the OZEV approved equipment list
  • its serial number
  • a photo that shows the installed chargepoint - the photo must be clear enough for OZEV to verify the model installed

Where only one chargepoint is installed, you must also provide a photo showing a wide-angle view of the chargepoint in its parking area. Where possible its associated building should be in the background.

Where more than one chargepoint is installed, you must also provide photos showing all the chargepoints in place. These should clearly show all the chargepoints and all the parking spaces associated with them. Where possible, at least one photo should show the associated building in the background.

You must also upload a copy of your customer’s final invoice that shows the grant deduction.

OZEV will ask you to resubmit all evidence that is not suitable.

Enquiries

Contact installer.authorisations@dvla.gov.uk if you don’t know your OZEV approved installer number or for any other query relating to your installer authorisation.
