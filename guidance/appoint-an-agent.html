What an agent can do for you

You can appoint someone to deal with your business rates and the Valuation Office Agency (VOA) on your behalf, such as a:

  • rating agent
  • friend
  • relative

When you appoint them, they can add a property to your account. They can also tell the VOA that:

  • your property details need changing
  • you think your rateable value is too high

Choosing a rating agent

A set of standards describes what rating agents can and cannot do. Agents must follow the standards if they are members of the:

  • Rating Surveyors’ Association (RSA)
  • Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors
  • Institute of Revenues, Rating and Valuation

Read the rating agent standards on the RSA website.

Appointing an agent

Register for a business rates valuation account using your existing Government Gateway user ID. If you do not have a Government Gateway user ID, you’ll create one when you register.

Ask your agent for their agent code.

From your business rates valuation account, appoint the agent using their code.

Choose the properties you want the agent to manage.

You can have more than one agent acting for you on a property. The agents can all act for you at the same time.

Appointing a friend or relative

You can appoint a friend, relative or organisation as a ‘trusted helper’. They will:

  • register for a business rates valuation account
  • deal with the Valuation Office Agency (VOA) on your behalf

Appoint a trusted helper.

Viewing what your agent is doing

See what your agent (or trusted helper) is doing for you from your business rates valuation account.

The VOA will also send you a message when:

  • your agent does something for you
  • the VOA changes a status or makes a decision

You do not need to respond to the messages.

Removing an agent

You can stop an agent from acting for you on any of your properties.

You can remove an agent completely from your account so they can no longer do anything for you.
