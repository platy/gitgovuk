Recent government changes affect COVID-19 guidance in England, Wales and Scotland.

In England, the plan for Living with COVID-19 encourages safer behaviours through public health advice. We continue to follow government and public health guidance.

Coming to a court or tribunal

Our courts and tribunals in England, Scotland and Wales have some safety measures in place to reduce the risk of the virus spreading by:

  • recommending all users wear a face covering in crowded and enclosed parts of our buildings (unless they’re exempt)
  • using screens or barriers where you may come into close proximity with others you do not normally meet
  • assessing our estate for ventilation and continuing with our existing air quality standards, such as using carbon dioxide monitors
  • providing fluid resistant surgical masks in some areas such as custody suites or smaller jury deliberation rooms
  • encouraging you to take a lateral flow test before coming to our buildings.

Do not come to our buildings if you:

  • test positive for COVID-19
  • have COVID-19 symptoms
  • feel unwell.

Contact the court or tribunal immediately to let them know and make alternative arrangements.

Find out what to expect when coming to a court or tribunal building and when going through security.

If you’re coming to court as a juror, find our more about doing jury service.

Assessing and managing the COVID-19 risk

We ensure the safety of everyone who comes into our buildings through our court and tribunal COVID-19 safety controls, and by following government guidelines and public health advice.

Staff in our buildings work hard to ensure your safety. You can raise any issues in our buildings with local managers.

You can see our organisational risk assessment and assessment tool for more details.
