1. Understand how you’re accountable for your domain

Your .gov.uk domain is a critical digital asset and your organisation is responsible for its security.

Your .gov.uk domain shows that your emails and websites are coming from an official UK public sector organisation. Protecting your organisation’s digital identity and reputation is central to maintaining citizen trust in the UK public sector.

As the person who has applied for the .gov.uk domain, you are the domain name administrator and you are accountable for your domain. For large organisations, the administrator will usually be a public servant who is part of your domain management team. For smaller organisations like parish councils, the administrator will usually be the clerk or similar.

So you can understand what you need to do to properly manage your domain, you must read and follow the guidance on how you are accountable for protecting your .gov.uk domain.

The Domain Management team may suspend your domain name if your organisation does not manage your .gov.uk domain in line with the responsibilities or fails to keep domain name administrator contact details up to date.

2. Set up and keep your domain name secure

A domain name is an important digital asset and is not automatically protected. Once you start using your own .gov.uk domain your organisation must take responsibility for using and protecting the domain.
Make sure your IT teams and suppliers are securely managing your .gov.uk domain name by following the keeping your domain name secure guidance.

3. Manage any subdomains you create

If you’re a large organisation you are likely to create subdomains under your .gov.uk domain. You are responsible for making sure any subdomains you create are protected even if you have a supplier managing them on your behalf.

Poor management of your domain and subdomains could have significant financial, security and reputational consequences for your organisation and other government services.
We recommend that you and your supplier read the guidance on creating and managing .gov.uk subdomains.

4. What to do if you want to stop using your domain

When you no longer need to use your .gov.uk domain name, you must still protect it properly to avoid services being vulnerable to hijacking.

Do not simply stop paying your domain renewal fee when you want to stop using your domain. To secure it properly, you must follow the steps in the guidance on how to stop using your .gov.uk domain.

5. Contact the CDDO Domain Management team for any help

If you have any concerns about your domain name you can email

domainmanagement@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk or support@domains.gov.uk for help.

The CDDO Domain Management team may contact your organisation to help to make sure domain names are securely managed. The team may contact you to:

  • confirm that your contact details are up to date
  • let you know about any issues related to your domain name
  • ask about your usage and awareness of government services
