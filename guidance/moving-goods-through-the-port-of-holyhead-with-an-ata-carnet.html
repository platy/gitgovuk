Guidance

Moving goods through the Port of Holyhead

The Port of Holyhead will process Office of Transit, Office of Departure or Office of Destination and CITES checks, so you'll need to go to the Holyhead Interim Inland Border Facility Service when moving goods with an ATA Carnet.

Port of Holyhead

The Port of Holyhead will offer Office of Transit and CITES checks only. The Office of Transit function at Holyhead will be completed using the digital pre-lodgement model and any documentary or physical checks will also take place here.

Port of Holyhead address:

Port of Holyhead
Holyhead
LL65 1DJ

Holyhead Interim Inland Border Facility Service

Please note that Inland Border Facility Services provided at Holyhead Roadking Truckstop have closed.

The location for carrying out IBF checks will move to the Holyhead Interim Inland Border Facility Service:

Holyhead Interim Inland Border Facility Service
Parc Cybi,
Kingsland,
Holyhead

For satellite navigation use: 53.292313, -4.615258

From now until 7am 20 March 2021, hauliers can begin to use the new site to complete ATA Carnet services as no IT is required. However, to undertake Office of Destination and Office of Departure services, hauliers are required to exceptionally use the Port of Holyhead

(Hauliers will be directed to parking area upon stating their need to carry out the required checks at the entrance).

Transporting live animals under an ATA Carnet

If you are transporting live animals under an ATA Carnet, you will need to tell Border Force as this will help them to prioritise the processing of your ATA Carnet.

When your crossing is confirmed, you will need to let Border Force know when you expect to arrive at Roadking Truckstop. You must do this at least 24 hours before you’re due to arrive.

Email Border Force at: BFHolyhead@HomeOffice.gov.uk

If you’re a UK trader transporting live animals you’ll need to lodge a travel plan with Defra before you start your journey.
