Taking temporary responsibility for Plastic Packaging Tax in the event of death, incapacity or insolvency

What to do if someone registered for Plastic Packaging Tax dies, becomes incapacitated or insolvent.

You can temporarily take over responsibility for dealing with Plastic Packaging Tax on another person’s behalf if the person registered for the tax either:

  • dies
  • becomes incapacitated
  • becomes insolvent

How to inform HMRC

You need to notify HMRC within 21 days of temporarily taking over responsibility and provide evidence that you have authority to act in relation to the business.

How to report a death or incapacity

Use the Enquire about Plastic Packaging Tax online form.

You need to have a Government Gateway user ID and password to complete the form. If you do not have a user ID, you can create one before you start to fill it in.

You’ll also need to give details of the business and the registered person.

If the person has died, you will need to confirm you have a copy of their death certificate or other evidence of their date of death.

if the person is incapacitated, you will need to give:

  • details of how they became incapacitated
  • the date they have been incapacitated from

How to report an insolvency

Email: indirecttaxdesign.team@hmrc.gov.uk

You must give the:

  • date when the business became subject to the insolvency procedure
  • nature of the procedure if a business has become insolvent

You should also give their Plastic Packaging Tax reference number.

We will try to respond within 28 working days.

What happens after you have notified HMRC

Once the request has been accepted by HMRC the temporary treatment will last for a period of 6 months or until the business activities are settled and the temporary treatment ends.

Temporary treatment will end when either:

  • there is a new responsible person for the business activities
  • the person is no longer incapacitated and resumes their business activities
  • the insolvency of the business is settled
