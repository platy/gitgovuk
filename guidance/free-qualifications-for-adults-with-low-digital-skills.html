Our essential digital skills reforms provide learners with access to free, high-quality provision to equip them with the digital skills they need for life and work.

The statutory digital entitlement, introduced on 1 August 2020, provides free study for adults with low digital skills on our new essential digital skills qualification offer.

Who is eligible

You’re eligible for full funding under the entitlement if you:

  • are a UK resident
  • are aged 19 and over
  • do not have the essential digital skills required to operate effectively in work and day to day life

Education and training providers must carry out an initial assessment to determine your current skill level. This ensures that only adults who do not have essential digital skills will have learning fully funded under the entitlement. It also means individuals are enrolled on courses at the appropriate level.

You can find the full eligibility criteria in the funding rules for the ESFA funded adult education budget.

The Essential Digital Skills qualification offer

In April 2020 we published new national standards for essential digital skills which replaced out of date national ICT standards. The new standards set out the full range of essential digital skills that adults need to fully participate in life, to undertake most jobs, and to engage in further study.

Our new qualification offer available under the entitlement is assessed against these standards.

The qualifications available are:

Essential Digital Skills qualifications (EDSQs)

  • EDSQs are a new qualification type designed to meet the diverse needs of adults with no or low digital skills
  • EDSQs can have different objectives, reflecting different learning needs, motivations and starting points – the specific content of EDSQs will vary depending on their purpose

Digital Functional Skills qualifications (FSQs)

  • Digital FSQs will replace FSQs in Information and Communication Technology (ICT) from August 2023
  • Digital FSQs have standardised content and assessment, providing a benchmark of digital skills for employers

You can find out more about essential digital skills and other government-funded training opportunities that can help you gain skills for life.

Existing ICT and digital skills qualifications

We have removed funding approval from the following qualifications in the Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) funded qualification offers for adults:

  • all entry level and level 1 ICT User and related qualifications that are not EDSQs – from 1 August 2021
  • all level 2 ICT User and related qualifications which do not offer entry into a specific level 2 job role – from 1 August 2022
  • all level 2 ICT FSQs – from 1 August 2022
  • all entry level and level 1 ICT FSQs – from 1 August 2023

If you’ve already started on one or more of these qualifications before the funding approval end date(s), we will continue to fund it until you complete your qualification(s) or until the certification end date, whichever happens earlier.

You can find more information about funding approval for qualifications at Qualifications and public funding.

Find an Essential Digital Skills qualification

EDSQs are funded through the ESFA, Mayoral Combined Authority (MCA) or Greater London Authority (GLA) funded providers.

The National Careers Service can provide advice on digital skills providers in your local area. You can contact them by phone on 0800 100 900 or through their website.

To find out which Essential Digital Skills qualifications are already available, visit the list of qualifications approved for funding and search for essential digital skills.
