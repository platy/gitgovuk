Guidance

Take a COVID-19 rapid lateral flow test before your driving test

Find out when to get a coronavirus (COVID-19) rapid lateral flow test before your driving test, and what to do if you test positive.

From:
Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency

21 April 2021

Applies to England, Scotland and Wales

You should take a COVID-19 rapid lateral flow test before your driving test if you do not have COVID-19 symptoms.

If you have got COVID-19 symptoms, even if mild, get a polymerase chain reaction (PCR) test as soon as possible.

You can still take your driving test if you’re unable to take a rapid lateral flow test. You do not need to bring proof of your test result to your driving test.

Why you should get tested

About 1 in 3 people with COVID-19 do not have symptoms but can still infect others.

Rapid tests help to check if you have COVID-19. If people test positive and self-isolate, it helps stop the virus spreading.

When you take your driving test, you’ll be in an enclosed space with limited fresh air. This is a situation where you’re at a higher risk of passing on COVID-19.

Get a rapid lateral flow test

Rapid lateral flow tests are free and you get a result in 15 to 30 minutes after taking the test.

Find out how to:

  • order or collect rapid lateral flow test kits in England
  • order or collect rapid lateral flow test kits in Scotland
  • order or collect rapid lateral flow test kits in Wales

Take a test on the day of your driving test before you leave home.

If your rapid lateral flow test result is positive

In England, you should not go for your test if your rapid lateral flow test result is positive.

In Scotland and Wales, you must self-isolate immediately if your rapid lateral flow test result is positive.

Find out about:

  • self-isolating in England
  • self-isolating in Scotland
  • self-isolating in Wales

You will need to change your driving test appointment. To do this, email the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) with the subject title ‘COVID-19 short notice cancellation’.

Include at least 2 of these in your email:

  • your driving licence number
  • your theory test pass certificate number
  • your driving test booking reference

DVSA customer services
customerservices@dvsa.gov.uk

DVSA will contact you to help you rearrange your test. You will not have to pay again.

After you’ve changed your driving test date

You can email DVSA if you’d like them to try and find an earlier driving test appointment for you.

Use the email subject title ‘Lateral flow rebooking’ and include 2 of these 3 pieces of information:

  • your driving licence number
  • your theory test pass certificate number
  • your driving test booking reference number

DVSA customer services
customerservices@dvsa.gov.uk

You can only use this service if you had to change your appointment because of a positive COVID-19 test.
