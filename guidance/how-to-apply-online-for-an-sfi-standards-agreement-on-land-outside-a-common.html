How to apply online for an SFI standards agreement on land outside a common

Apply online in the Rural Payments service for a Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI) standards agreement on land outside a common.

Get ready to apply for an SFI standards agreement

Before starting your SFI application, read:

  • what you can do now to get ready to apply
  • the Sustainable Farming Incentive scheme information

Starting your SFI application

To start your SFI application, sign into the Rural Payments service using your existing sign-in details.

To create and submit an SFI application, make sure you have either:

  • Business Details ‘Full’ permission
  • BPS ‘Submit’ permission

You can also create an SFI application if you have BPS ‘Amend’ permission, but you will not be able to submit it.

You can check your permissions from your ‘Business overview’ screen in ‘Give people permission to act for this business’.

From your ‘Business overview’ screen, select ‘Sustainable Farming Incentive’ and then ‘Sustainable Farming Incentive Standards’. This takes you to the ‘SFI applications and agreements’ screen.

Select ‘Apply for SFI’ to create your SFI application. It may take a few moments for the application to appear.

How to apply for SFI

On the ‘Apply for SFI’ screen, you will see the steps that you need to complete to apply for an SFI standards agreement. As you complete each step you will be returned to this screen and the step will show as ‘Completed’.

The steps you need to complete are summarised in this table.

Application steps What you need to do
Step 1: Your land Check and confirm your land details and confirm that you will have ‘management control’ of the land you select for the standards
Step 2: Select your standards Select the standard(s) you want to apply for
Step 3: Choose the level of actions you agree to do Choose the level of actions in each standard and select available land parcels for each standard
Step 4: Review and submit your application Check your answers and submit your SFI application

Step 1: Your land

Check and confirm your land details

If your land details aren’t up to date, this will affect which land parcels you can select for the standards later in your SFI application.

You will only be able to select land parcels that:

  • are linked to your SBI
  • have an eligible land cover for the standard you select - read the information about eligible land covers in the SFI scheme information.

Before continuing with your SFI application, check that the details for the land parcels you will be entering into the standard(s) are up to date. Do this on your digital maps using the Land Viewer.

If the land details are up to date, select ‘Yes, my land details are correct’ and then ‘Continue’.

If the land details need to be updated, select ‘No, I need to update my land details’. Read the information about how to update your land details. You should not continue with your SFI application until we confirm that we have updated your land details.

Confirm you will have management control of the land

You must have ‘management control’ of the land parcels you select later in your SFI application to enter into the standards for the 3-year duration of your SFI agreement. If you will not have management control, you must not include that land in your SFI application. Read the information about management control.

Step 2: Select your standards

You’ll see the standards you can apply for based on the eligible land covers currently registered for the land parcels linked to your SBI.

Select all of the available standards you want to apply for by ticking the relevant boxes. You will then select eligible land parcels for each standard later in your application.

Select ‘Continue’.

Step 3: Choose the level of actions you agree to do

Choose the level of actions you agree to do

You have to answer questions to confirm the actions you will do on the land entered into the standard(s). This will determine if you are eligible to enter land into the introductory or intermediate levels.

If you have chosen more than one standard, you will need to answer the relevant questions for the actions in each standard.

Find out about the actions for the:

  • arable and horticultural soils standard
  • improved grassland soils standard
  • moorland standard

For each of the two soils standards, you must answer:

  • the questions for the actions in the introductory level, then select ‘Continue’
  • the question for the additional action in the intermediate level, then select ‘Continue’

How you answer the intermediate level question will impact what levels you’ll see when asked to select the available land parcels to enter into the standard. If you choose:

  • ‘Yes’, you’ll see both the introductory and the intermediate levels, but you can still select the introductory level for all the land parcels if you decide you don’t want to do the intermediate level action
  • ‘No’, you’ll only see the introductory level

For the moorland standard, you must answer the questions for the introductory level.

Select available land parcels for this standard

You’ll see the available land parcels for the first standard you have selected. Once you have selected land parcels to enter into that standard, you’ll do the same for any other standard(s) you have selected.

How to view your available land parcels

Your available land parcels for the standard are grouped together by the relevant Ordnance Survey (OS) Map Sheet Reference(s). These references are shown with two letters (for example, SY) and a number (for example, 6). This is how OS records mapping data.

To view the land parcels, either:

  • select the OS Map Sheet Reference link from the list
  • select the OS Map Sheet Reference on the map

You will then select a land parcel and level for the standard as explained below.

Once you have selected land parcels under an OS Map Sheet Reference, you will be able to view and select the land parcels under another OS Map Sheet Reference, where relevant.

How a land parcel’s available area is calculated

When you select a level for a land parcel, you will see its SFI ‘available area’. This is not the same as the BPS eligible area.

The SFI available area for each land parcel is automatically calculated by taking its total area and deducting the following areas, where relevant:

  • ineligible land cover(s) for the standard
  • ineligible Countryside Stewardship revenue options
  • ineligible Environmental Stewardship revenue options
  • any land already in an SFI pilot agreement

If we’ve automatically deducted any of the areas above, you will see a message immediately below the affected land parcel telling you why the available area has been reduced.

You must make sure that the total available area for the land parcel is also an eligible land type for the standard. For example, the whole available area must be improved grassland for the improved grassland soils standard. If only part of the available area is improved grassland, you cannot reduce its available area in your application so you must not select that land parcel. Read more information about eligible land covers and land types for each standard.

Select the level(s) in the standard for the available land parcels

You can now select the level in the standard for each land parcel under the relevant OS Map Sheet Reference.

The level(s) you see will depend on the answers you gave for the actions earlier in Step 3. For the soils standards, if you have answered ‘Yes’ to the intermediate level question, you’ll see both the introductory and intermediate levels.

To select all the available land parcels for a level, choose ‘Select all’ in the box under the relevant level.

Alternatively, you can select a level for each available land parcel. You can do this by ticking the relevant box for the level next to the land parcel, or select it on the map by clicking:

  • once for the introductory level
  • again for the intermediate level
  • again to deselect it

Once you have selected the level for the available land parcels under that OS Sheet Reference, select ‘Add to application’. You can then select available land parcels and levels under another OS Map Sheet Reference.

After you have selected all the land parcels and level(s) for that standard, select ‘Confirm selection’ to see ‘Your payment value summary for this standard’.

Select ‘Continue’ to return to the ‘Apply for SFI’ screen. If you selected another standard at Step 2, you now need to complete Step 3 for that standard.

Selecting a land parcel for a different standard

If a land parcel has a ‘land cover’ that’s eligible for more than one standard, you can only select it for one standard. For example, an improved temporary grassland land parcel with an ‘Arable’ land cover could be eligible for either:

  • the arable and horticultural soils standard
  • the improved grassland soils standard

You may have already selected this land parcel for the arable and horticultural soils standard but have now decided to enter it into the improved grassland soils standard instead. In this case, you can still select this land parcel for the improved grassland soils standard, but you will see a warning message to say it’s available but is already in another standard. You will need to confirm that you want to select the land parcel for the improved grassland soils standard instead.

Step 4: Review and submit your application

Check your answers

Once you have completed Step 3) for all the standards you want to apply for, you need to ‘Check your answers’ and if you believe the information shown is correct, select ‘Continue’. This will return you to the ‘Apply for SFI’ screen.

Submit your SFI application

Select ‘Submit your application’. Make sure you read the declaration on this screen. If you agree with that, select ‘I agree - submit my application’.

Once you submit your application, you cannot amend it. You can only withdraw it and start again.

After you have submitted your application, you can view your ‘application summary’ PDF which shows what you have applied for. You may want to print or save this application summary and keep it for your records.

We’ll tell you when your SFI standards agreement offer is available in the Rural Payments service for you to review. You can only accept or reject it. You cannot request changes to it. To find out how to accept or reject your agreement offer, read the information about how to review your agreement offer.
