A directory of legal aid ways of working and processes which have resumed operation following suspension during the coronavirus outbreak is set out below.

Guidance on how we are working with representative bodies to flex our operational approach to supporting the legal aid sector is available on our contingency response page

Operational processes resuming

Processes returning to operation, and the dates they come back into force are listed on this page. These updates have been shared with representative bodies. We will continue to work with stakeholders to restart business as usual operations.

Live measures

Website page Contingency area Date started Last updated Maintained until at least
1 Processing and payments Provider claims for private transport costs March 2020 10 December 2021 Removal of working from home guidance in England and Wales
2 Processing and payments 100% payment of Family Advocacy Scheme (FAS) July 2020 24 September 2021 30 September 2021 – reverting to pre-Covid process 1 October
3 Processing and payments Inquests and one-off billing of work in progress March 2020 16 March 2021 31 March 2022
4 Working with clients Tailored approach to means assessments (civil and crime) in line with the government-backed furlough and self-employment support schemes March 2020 24 September 2021 30 September 2021 – reverting to pre-Covid process 1 October
5 Working with clients Accept applications with digital signatures and guidance where no signature is obtained March 2020 15 June 2021 Retained until further notice
6 Working with clients Application evidence requirements: allow photographed or scanned evidence as well as no evidence where this is agreed with the Legal Aid Agency (LAA) March 2020 16 March 2021 Retained until further notice
7 Contract management and assurance Guidance for crime duty solicitor engagement and CRM12 duty rota March 2020 24 September 2021 30 September 2021 – reverting to pre-Covid process 1 October
8 Contract management and assurance Temporary COVID-19 Standards to apply in relation to where supervision and/or office requirements cannot be met due to COVID-19. March 2020 10 December 2021 Retained until 16 May 2022
9 Contract management and assurance Permit Pre-Quality Mark Audits as part of the Specialist Quality Mark (SQM) accreditation process March 2020 10 December 2021 Retained until 16 May 2022
10   Claim the police station fixed fee when providers have attended remotely rather than in person where the matter is dealt with under the agreed National Police Chiefs’ Council (NPCC)/Crown Prosecution Service (CPS)/defence interview protocol March 2020 4 October 2021 Presumption now for attendances to be face to face – in exceptional circumstances, they can still be conducted remotely, in line with protocol

Future changes

Wherever possible we will give 3 weeks’ notice of any other changes.

Stay up to date

If you have any questions about your contract, talk to your contract manager. We are monitoring the situation and will update any changes to operational guidance in the first instance on our digital channels:
