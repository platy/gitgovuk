Community Ownership Fund – further bids from reopening of first round announced

The Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities announces the first tranche of successful bidders from the reopening of the first round of the Community Ownership Fund.

From:
Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities
Published
24 March 2022

Contents

  1. Successful Bids
  2. Future Bidding Rounds

The Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities is delighted to announce the first tranche of successful bidders from the reopening of the first round of the Community Ownership Fund.

In December we announced the reopening of the fund for those applications which were eligible but unfunded in the first round. We have since contacted all eligible applicants and discussed whether they wished to reapply.

Successful Bids

At the Spending Review in October we announced 21 had been selected in this first bidding round with the sum of these bids being £5.3 million. Since this announcement one project (£250,000) withdrew from the fund due to a change in circumstances.

In December we also announced the successful £1 million bid by the Bury Supporters Society to purchase their historic stadium Gigg Lane. We are now delighted to announce a further 10 bids have been selected bringing the overall total of funding to £7.92 million and the overall number of projects to 31.

Future Bidding Rounds

The £150 million Community Ownership Fund delivers on the 2019 government commitment to strengthen community rights and support community groups to protect and take over assets of community value.

The funding we have announced so far has been for the first round of the fund, the fund will run over a total of four years until 2024/25.

In December 2021 we announced we would be delaying the start date of the fund to Spring 2022. We will announce further details regarding round two and its start date soon.

We would encourage any applicants who have been unsuccessful to apply again in a future round.
