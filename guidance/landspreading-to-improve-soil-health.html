Landspreading to improve soil health

How to meet the requirements of using mobile plant for landspreading waste to provide agricultural benefit or ecological improvement.

You can spread waste to land to improve soil health. This can help reduce the need to use manufactured fertilisers and quarried soil conditioners.

Spreading waste to land must:

  • be controlled by an environmental permit, regulatory position statement, low risk waste position or waste exemption
  • result in agricultural benefit or ecological improvement
  • be waste recovery, not waste disposal

Your activity must not:

  • endanger human health
  • harm the environment by causing risk to groundwater, surface water, air, soil, plants or animals
  • cause nuisance through noise or odours
  • adversely affect the countryside or places of special interest

Use this guidance to find out what you must do to meet the requirements.

You are breaking the law if you do not keep to the requirements.

Who this guidance is for

This guidance is mainly for holders of a relevant mobile plant landspreading permit and deployment.

You can also use it if you are:

  • a land manager or occupier
  • a farmer on whose land the waste is stored and spread

Or you are operating under (or plan to) a:

  • regulatory position statement
  • low risk waste position
  • registered waste exemption

When you do not need a permit

For some lower risk activities you do not need to apply for a permit. For example, if you can meet the requirements of a:

  • low risk waste position
  • relevant landspreading waste exemption

The following waste exemptions relate to landspreading:

  • U10 spreading waste to benefit agricultural land
  • U11 spreading waste on non-agricultural land
  • S3 storing sludge
  • D1 depositing waste from dredging inland waters
  • U12 using mulch
  • U13 spreading plant matter to provide benefits
  • U14 incorporating ash into soil
  • U15 pig and poultry ash

See the guide How to register or renew waste exemptions for the requirements.

Apply for a permit

You can apply for:

  • SR2010 No 4: mobile plant for landspreading
  • SR2010 No 5: mobile plant for reclamation, restoration or improvement of land
  • SR2010 No 6: mobile plant for landspreading of sewage sludge
  • a bespoke mobile plant permit for landspreading or land reclamation

The guide Waste: environmental permits explains how to apply for a:

  • standard rules permit
  • bespoke permit

How to comply with your permit

Landspreading: how to comply with your permit provides:

  • detailed information on the rules or conditions in your permit
  • the minimum standards we expect operators to follow to show they are complying with their permit

Apply to deploy mobile plant

You must:

  • apply for deployment using Landspreading: form LPD1 application for deployment (LPD1)
  • provide accurate and complete information
  • justify that your deployment will result in agricultural benefit or ecological improvement
  • provide all relevant supporting documents
  • include the correct payment

Where indicated you will need to use a person with appropriate technical expertise. For example, a Fertiliser advisers certification and training scheme (FACTS) qualified adviser.

We:

  • will assess the information you provide
  • may ask for more information if we need it to complete our assessment â€“ this may cause delays

You must not start any activities until you have received our written agreement. Allow at least 25 working days from the time your application is complete, for us to assess your application.

Giving the correct information

Landspreading: apply to deploy mobile plant provides the forms and guidance you need to make sure you give us the correct information.

Fill in LPD1 using the Landspreading: Form LPD1 guidance.

LPD1 requires a location map. We have provided an example location map with the type of information we expect to see.

You must produce a benefit statement that provides evidence of your landspreading activities resulting in agricultural benefit or ecological improvement. See:

  • Landspreading: produce a benefit statement, this includes a completed example
  • Landspreading: benefit statement template

Use the completed example and the template for the type of information we expect to see. You will need to provide site-specific information.

You must send the full analytical results for the waste and receiving soil. Follow the guidance given in Landspreading: provide a waste and soil analysis.

See Landspreading: benefits and risks of the waste types you can use for:

  • information about the waste types listed in the 3 mobile plant standard rules permits
  • potential benefits and risks of applying particular waste types and any operational activities to consider
  • references to other guidance and important information you need to be aware of

You can use Landspreading: how to manage soil health to help you:

  • identify and describe the soil
  • use a suitable sampling method and frequency

This guidance also describes how to manage:

  • the water content of the soil
  • the pH of the soil
  • the carbon to nitrogen ratio of the soil
  • soil and crop nutrients
  • contaminants
  • pests and diseases
  • salty whey and other high conductivity waste
  • organic manures
  • oil and fat trap waste

Complying with your agreed deployment

Your activities must meet the requirements of your permit and your agreed LPD1.

You must not make any changes to your deployment without our agreement.

Your agreed deployment form starts from the date we give our written agreement. It ends 12 months later. The deployment is part of your permit.

You must comply with your permit and agreed deployment. If you are concerned about not being able to comply, contact your local Environment Agency office.
