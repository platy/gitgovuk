The UK now has its own robust regulatory framework for goods. Manufacturers need to start using the UK conformity assessment marking (UKCA) to place goods on the market in Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales). The UKCA regime seeks to ensure consumers are protected from unsafe, and non-compliant goods.

There are different rules for Northern Ireland, and we will be running dedicated events to cover the requirements for placing goods on the market in Northern Ireland.

These webinars give an overview of the new responsibilities and regulations for businesses. Each product type may have specific requirements detailed in legislation and specific guidance which we cannot cover in detail during the session. See the A to Z of industry guidance.

There will be a chance to ask questions at the end of each seminar, but we are unable to provide advice on your specific circumstances.

Webinars for Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales)

These webinars are for:

  • businesses who have a role or involvement in the manufacturing or supply of CE or UKCA marked goods onto the Great British market
  • trade associations and organisations representing sectors which require the CE or UKCA marking

Economic operators and routes for conformity assessment

This webinar covers changes to the roles of economic operators (importers, distributors, manufacturers, and authorised representatives).

We will tell you about the roles and responsibilities of economic operators involved in the supply of goods requiring the UKCA marking to the Great British market and how roles may have changed following Brexit.

Select a date to register for this webinar:

  • Tuesday 14 June 2022 at 11am
  • Thursday 30 June 2022 at 3pm

Understanding the routes available for conformity assessment

This webinar covers:

  • the 2 different routes for conformity assessment (self-assessment and third-party conformity assessment)
  • how to find out which route is applicable for your product
  • how you can use these to meet UKCA requirements

Date to be confirmed.

Understanding marking and labelling

This webinar covers the labelling requirements for the UKCA marking.

The session will explain how to correctly label your product for the Great British market under the new regulatory regime. This will include use of the UKCA marking and importer labelling responsibilities as well as easements in place to support building the UKCA marking into the manufacturing process.

  • Tuesday 21 June 2022 at 11am
  • Tuesday 5 July 2022 at 3pm

Placing goods on the market in Great Britain

This webinar covers the process and requirements for placing goods on the market in Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales).

This session will cover the definition of placing on the market for Great Britain in detail, providing case studies and detailed scenarios to assist attendees understanding of placing on the market and its implications for economic operators.

Webinars for Northern Ireland

Placing goods on the market in Northern Ireland

This webinar covers the process and requirements for placing goods on the market in Northern Ireland.

The session will cover the new regulatory regime for goods in Northern Ireland which has different rules to the rest of the UK. It will cover:

  • placing goods on the market in Northern Ireland
  • the roles and responsibilities of economic operators
  • the specific rules relating to moving goods to and from Northern Ireland and Great Britain

Select a date to register for this webinar:

  • Thursday 14 July 2022 at 11am

Webinars for specific product areas

Understanding the new UK regulatory regime: Focus on construction products

This webinar covers the process and requirements for the new UK regime with a focus on construction.

The session will provide information on placing construction products on the Great British market, in relation to the Construction Products (Amendment etc.) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019.

This webinar is for:

  • businesses who have a role or involvement in the manufacturing or supply of CE or UKCA marked construction goods onto the Great British market
  • trade associations and organisations representing sectors which require the CE or UKCA marking

The regime in Northern Ireland will match the EU requirements for construction products (Regulation (EU) No 305/2011).

Date to be confirmed.
