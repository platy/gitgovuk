Guidance

Victim of crime abroad

This guidance sets out how the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) can help if you’re a victim of crime abroad.

When you are abroad, the support available and access to justice may vary depending on where you report the crime. This guidance explains what you need to do if you have become a victim of crime abroad, including theft, assault, rape or sexual assault.

How to report a crime

In many countries if you want a crime to be investigated and the police to obtain important evidence, you must report the crime to the local police before you leave the country.

You should then contact the nearest British embassy, high commission or consulate, or the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) in the UK, who will provide advice based on your circumstances.

When you return to the UK, you should also report it to your local UK police, even if you do not report the crime in the country where it happened. The UK police should send your information to the police in the country where the crime happened. However, foreign police forces are under no obligation to open a case and may not take action

You should report the crime to the UK police even if you do not want an investigation abroad. The UK police can offer you access to victim support in the UK. They may still provide some details of the crime to the police in the country where it happened, to protect vulnerable people, or to stop more crimes being committed.

Assault

If you have been assaulted, you should:

  • report the assault to the police in the country where it happened and ask for a police report
  • contact the nearest British embassy, high commission or consulate for help, especially if you have been admitted to hospital. We will provide support based on your circumstances

If you have been raped or sexually assaulted, read the separate guidance for victims of rape and sexual assault.

Theft

If your money, passport, traveller’s cheques, bank cards or anything else is stolen, or you have lost them, you should:

  • report it to the local police immediately
  • ask for a statement about the loss or a crime report (you will need this for your insurance claim)
  • for stolen traveller’s cheques or bank cards, contact the issuers to let them know. Your bank may be able to give you a code that allows you to withdraw cash from an ATM without the card. You may also want to ask your bank or a relative to send money to you using a reliable money transfer company. The nearest British embassy, high commission or consulate may be able to liaise with your bank or insurer to give you information about transferring money.  For further information see Financial assistance when travelling abroad
  • report a lost or stolen passport immediately. You can apply to the FCDO for an emergency travel document (sometimes called an ‘emergency passport’) if you’re abroad and cannot get a replacement passport in time for your planned travel

Other circumstances when you may need help

If you have been affected by any of these issues, read more specific advice:

  • admitted to hospital
  • detained or arrested
  • death of a British national abroad by murder or manslaughter
  • missing person abroad

Other sources of help

Travel insurance

If you’re travelling, you should buy appropriate travel insurance. You must check the insurance provides the cover you need.

Compensation

You may be able to apply for compensation if you are a victim of crime abroad. You usually need a police report to apply.

Not all countries have a compensation scheme.
