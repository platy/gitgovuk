Course summary

This course aims to introduce learners to the basics of user-centred design. It looks at why it is important to understand users and the needs they have before designing services through a combination of live online learning methods. These include group discussions and online activities, such as polls and quizzes.

This course explores different user research methods and how these methods can help teams understand users and the needs they have. It focuses on the role a User Researcher plays in an agile team and how to embed the users first principle within the team. This course also looks at how to remove bias and assumption from the process but also helps the whole team to be user-centred by getting them all involved in the research process.

This course is run online. Attendees will need access to a desktop or laptop computer with broadband access and be able to access Zoom video conferencing along with access to your microphone. You are welcome to use a personal device to join; all content is unclassified and there is no requirement to download any software or information.

Course aims and objectives

During this course you will learn:

  • why it’s important to understand users and their needs when developing user-centred services
  • how bias and assumptions affect the design process and how to reduce them
  • how user research works, including using different research methods for different purposes
  • the role of the user researcher in an agile team

Eligibility

Our courses are available to civil servants, local government employees, devolved administrations and other public servants.

You should not attend this course if you have attended the GDS Academy Digital and Agile Bitesize Basics course as this is covered during that course.

Duration

The course takes 90 minutes to complete, including an introduction to the online tools.
