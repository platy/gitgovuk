This guide explains how and when to pay your Wine or Cider Duty.

There is a separate guide for paying Beer Duty.

When to pay

Your return and payment are due by the 15th day of the month following your accounting period.

If you’re licensed for Wine Duty or registered for Cider Duty, you’ll automatically get a return. If you need a duplicate, contact HMRC.

Make sure you pay HMRC by the deadline, or you may have to pay a penalty.

What you’ll need

You’ll need your 12-digit duty reference number. You’ll find this on your return form.

Your payment may be delayed if you use the wrong number.

Pay now

How much time to allow

The time you need to allow depends on how you pay. You should also check your bank’s transaction limits and processing times before making a payment.

Payment method Time to allow
Approve a payment through your online bank account Same or next day
Online or telephone banking (Faster Payments) Same or next day
CHAPS Same or next day
Bacs 3 working days

If the deadline to pay the verification fee falls on a weekend or bank holiday, make sure your payment reaches us on the last working day before it, unless you’re paying by Faster Payments (online or telephone banking).

How to pay

Approve a payment through your online bank account

You can pay directly using your online or mobile bank account.

When you’re ready to pay, start your Wine or Cider Duty payment. Select the ‘pay by bank account’ option. You’ll then be directed to sign in to your online or mobile banking account to approve your payment.

The payment is usually instant but sometimes it takes up to 2 hours to show in your account.

You’ll need to have your online banking details ready to pay this way.

Online or telephone banking, CHAPS or Bacs

Use the following details to pay by Faster Payments, CHAPS or Bacs.

By cheque through the post

Send your return and cheque made payable to ‘HM Revenue and Customs only’. Write your 12 digit Cider or Wine Duty reference number on the back of the cheque.

Allow 3 working days for your payment to reach HMRC.

Nothing to pay

If you get a return and calculate that you have nothing to pay, or you’re due a repayment, you must still send it back to HMRC.
