Find a coronavirus (COVID-19) travel test provider if you're arriving in the UK

If you do not qualify as fully vaccinated, you need to get tested for COVID-19 when you arrive in the UK from abroad.

From 4am on Friday 18 March you will not need to take any COVID-19 tests when travelling to the UK from abroad.

Find a test provider

You can compare and choose a test provider based on cost and whether they are available in your region.

The government does not endorse or recommend any specific test provider. You should do your own research about test providers and their terms and conditions.

What you need to know

Booking an arrival PCR test

You must book and pay for an arrival PCR test (also known as a ‘day 2 test’) to take when you arrive in the UK.

You cannot use a free NHS test as your arrival PCR test.

You must include the booking reference from your arrival PCR test on your passenger locator form.

Booking a pre-departure test

You cannot use this service to book a pre-departure test if you’re travelling to the UK. You must get a test from a private provider in the country you’re travelling from instead.

Tests to travel abroad to another country

Check foreign travel advice to see what tests you need to take and any other requirements.

If you’ve ordered tests and there’s a problem

Contact your test provider to get help with the tests you’ve ordered. For example, if they have not arrived or you do not have your results.

You can make a complaint about a test provider. This will help improve the service.

The government cannot contact a test provider for you.
