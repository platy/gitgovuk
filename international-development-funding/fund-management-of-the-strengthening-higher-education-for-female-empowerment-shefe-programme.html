Overview

The Strengthening Higher Education for Female Empowerment programme (SHEFE) is designed to reform HE systems to deliver a better future for women and girls in sub-Saharan Africa and the Middle East and North Africa.

The programme will aim break down the barriers preventing women from accessing and thriving in the HE sector, including through promoting STEM degrees, inclusive digital education and greater safeguarding practices for women and girls.

Through partnering with the UK’s best universities and higher education institutions, SHEFE will leverage the world class expertise from the UK tertiary education sector to deliver the FCDO’s priority of empowering women and girls.

What will the fund achieve

The FCDO is looking for an experienced and dynamic fund manager to design the implementation of and manage the new SHEFE programme, an investment of up to £45m over the next 5 years, to fund:

  • partnerships that seek to address identified issues in the HE system, including: improving access, improving employment outcomes for girls and women to thrive in the labour market, including encouraging STEM-related learning, and targeted interventions to support safeguarding against sexual exploitation, abuse and harassment (SEAH)
  • attracting new types of players within the HE system, such as funding and quality assurance bodies and private sector partners (private providers and employers) to get a dynamic and multi‐disciplinary group of experts to focus on solutions to complex issues, such as employability
  • testing new models of delivery at scale, including online delivery
