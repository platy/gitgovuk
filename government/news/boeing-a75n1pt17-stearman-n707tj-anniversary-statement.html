Boeing A75N1(PT17) Stearman, N707TJ: Anniversary Statement

Boeing A75N1(PT17) Stearman loss of power during flying display and ditching off Sandbanks, Poole, Dorset, 4 September 2021.

From:
Air Accidents Investigation Branch
Published
1 September 2022

This statement provides an update on the AAIB investigation into an accident involving a Boeing A75N1(PT17) Stearman, registration N707TJ, in the sea off Sandbanks, Poole, Dorset, on 4 September 2021.

While performing an aerobatic wing walking display over the sea at Bournemouth Air Festival, the aircraft experienced a reduction in engine power.  The pilot stopped the display routine and flew the aircraft west, along the coast, with the intention of returning to Bournemouth Airport.   Although the aircraft was initially able to maintain height, the engine subsequently experienced a complete loss of power and the pilot ditched in the entrance to Poole Harbour.  The aircraft flipped over on contact with the water, but both occupants were able to exit the aircraft unaided.

The investigation is nearing completion and publication of the report is expected in late 2022.
