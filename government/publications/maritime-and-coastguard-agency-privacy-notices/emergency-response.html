Personal information gathered by our coastguard teams during an emergency response will be processed by the Maritime and Coastguard Agency (MCA) in order to facilitate and coordinate rescues, preserve the safety of life and to enable the MCA to carry out our public task. This information is shared with the Marine Accident Investigation Branch (MAIB), who are part of the Department for Transport, for them to carry out their legal duties under the provisions of the Merchant Shipping (Accident Reporting and Investigation) Regulations 2012. To see how the MAIB handle your personal data please see their Personal Information Charter on their website.

In certain rescue circumstances it may be necessary for us to share your data with global search and rescue authorities and those delegated competent authorities such as UK police and other UK 999 services and the Royal National Lifeboat Institution (RNLI) and any other declared rescue facility.

If you contact us directly to report an incident, we will ask you for your contact details which may be shared with our coastguard rescue officers/volunteers who will contact you directly, this may include relevant medical details at the time of the incident which will ensure appropriate rescue. Any information passed to us from another emergency service would be used and stored in the same way.

Our communications team may contact you to invite you to contribute to a news story or similar communications activity related to your experience. This is optional, and you can ask our communications team not to contact you again for this purpose.

How long we will retain your information

Your information will be kept securely and retained for 20 years after which time it will be reviewed and deleted unless required to support investigations and other reasons of public interest.
