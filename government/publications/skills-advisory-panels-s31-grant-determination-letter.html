Details

Since 2018, the 36 Skills Advisory Panels (SAPs) have been helping Mayoral Combined Authorities (MCAs), the Greater London Authority (GLA) and Local Enterprise Partnerships (LEPs), to fulfil their local leadership role in the skills system.

SAPs bring together employers, local authorities, universities, colleges, and other training providers, to help match training to the jobs available in the local area.

In the financial year 2021 to 2022, DfE funding supported SAPs to:

  • continue to build and increase analytical capability sustainably
  • maintain relationships to deliver the local skills agenda
  • refresh and publish their local skills report (originally published in March 2021)

In January 2021, DfE’s Skills for Jobs White Paper introduced a suite of reforms including Local Skills Improvement Plans (LSIPs) developed by Employer Representative Bodies (ERBs). These reforms aim to put employers more firmly at the heart of the skills system.

In 2021 to 2022, DfE is Trailblazing LSIPs in 8 areas of the country. DfE asked the corresponding SAPs in these 8 areas to support ERBs in the development of their Trailblazer LSIP. This included discretion for the SAP on whether to update their local skills report.

The Secretary of State for Education makes grant determinations under section 31 of the Local Government Act 2003.
