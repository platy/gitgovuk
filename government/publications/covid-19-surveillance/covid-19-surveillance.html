Robust population surveillance programmes are essential to understand the rate of SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) infection, and how the virus is spreading across the country. They help us to assess the impact of measures taken so far to contain the virus, to inform current and future actions, and to develop new tests and treatments.

The Surveillance and Immunity Directorate of the NHS Test and Trace’s Joint Biosecurity Centre (JBC) is taking forward Pillar 4 of the UK testing strategy. The strategy is to commission and manage a number of major studies to understand to what extent COVID-19 is present and affecting the wider population.

This also includes looking at how the immune system has responded to the virus and how this varies over time. We will use the data and insight generated from these studies to help strengthen our scientific understanding of COVID-19, inform government policy decisions and work across the testing programme.

We are working with a range of partners, including the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA, formerly Public Health England (PHE)), the Office for National Statistics (ONS), industry and academia, in order to deliver these studies.

Surveillance studies

COVID-19 Infection Survey – Office for National Statistics

April 2020 – ongoing

The COVID-19 Infection Survey (CIS) was started in April 2020 and is being delivered with the University of Oxford, IQVIA and other partners. It is funded by the Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC).

This study uses repeat household visits to establish the proportion of the general population who have COVID-19. Participants are tested whether they report having symptoms of COVID-19 or not. Blood samples are also taken from a proportion of participants to test for COVID-19 antibodies.

This study was originally set up to gather data from England, but has now been expanded to Wales, Northern Ireland and Scotland. The study tests 150,000 participants every fortnight.

REACT-1

April 2020 – ongoing

The REACT-1 study was launched in April 2020. It is a large population study that measures prevalence of COVID-19 in the general population and estimates how quickly the virus transmits between people (R value) across a 2-week period each month.

Over 125,000 unique participants take part each month and the results provide insights about COVID-19, and the prevalence of emerging variants.

REACT-1 monthly research findings

REACT-2

May 2020 – ongoing

The REACT-2 study was launched in May 2020 to estimate how many people have already had COVID-19 in England. Over 150,000 participants took an antibody finger prick test at home to provide useful insight about changes in antibody levels in the general population.

The study has also helped scientists learn more about the usability and accuracy of different antibody tests and how vaccines affect antibody levels in people.

COVID-19 symptom study app

This is a not-for-profit initiative started by health science company ZOE in collaboration with King’s College London in March 2020. It supports vital COVID-19 research and is one of the world’s largest ongoing studies of COVID-19 symptoms and facilitates hotspot detection. The ZOE app provides unique insight on asymptomatic and symptomatic information across the UK with over one million logging on a weekly basis, and is supported by a grant from DHSC to support its on-going data collection.

Vivaldi 1

May 2020 to June 2020

This study launched in May and focuses on understanding what proportion of care home healthcare workers and residents in England are positive for COVID-19, as well as the disease’s transmission and mitigation factors within care homes. As part of this study residents and carers receive swab testing, through the national care home testing programme, whilst care home managers take part in a national telephone survey.

Vivaldi 2

June 2020 – ongoing

This study was launched in June 2020 and builds on the findings of Vivaldi 1. The cohort study has expanded its initial set of 100 care homes in the Four Seasons Healthcare chain (FHSC) to cover approximately 340 homes in England and 15,000 participants.

Vivaldi 2 uses trained phlebotomists to draw blood from care-home residents and staff, which is then used to test for both antibody and cellular immune responses to COVID-19. This data is cross-referenced with DHSC-delivered swab testing in care homes to provide a detailed picture of prevalence, seroprevalence, transmission and immunity over time.

The study also provides data on the efficacy of the vaccine as the mass-vaccination programme is rolled out in care-homes nationwide.

In addition, samples of venous blood from care-home residents participating in Vivaldi 2 are undergoing immunological analyses to explore cellular immunity to COVID-19. This research, conducted by the University of Birmingham, is an integral part of the Vivaldi project and is sometimes known as ‘Vivaldi 3’.

Schools Infection Survey

October 2020 – ongoing

Commissioned by DHSC, the ONS collects data and conducts analysis, alongside UKHSA (formerly PHE) and the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine (LSHTM), to understand and assess COVID-19 infection and transmission within schools. The study involves repeated antibody and PCR testing of students and staff, drawn from up to 150 schools (both primary and secondary) in England.

sKIDs surveillance study – PHE

June 2020 to September 2020

At the beginning of June, PHE launched this pilot in partnership with the Department for Education through funding from DHSC.

The study focused on providing a better understanding of prevalence in pre and primary schools in the last half of the summer term after national lockdown measures began to ease. It was designed to provide the government with insights to inform the response to the virus and management of education settings in a safe and secure way.

This small pilot focused on blood and swab tests of pre-school, primary and staff from 130 schools from across England to better understand rates of transmission of COVID-19.

UK Biobank SARS-CoV-2 serology study

May 2020 – ongoing

This study was established in May 2020 by UK Biobank, in collaboration with the University of Oxford and funded through DHSC to establish the proportion of Biobank participants who have previously had the infection, the strength of different groups’ antibody responses and how this varies over time.

It uses a fixed cohort of 20,000 registered with UK Biobank and their families who self-administer finger prick blood samples once a month for 6 months.

PHE serology study

March 2020 to November 2020

This was a weekly serology testing of blood samples obtained from existing sources that provided baseline data of the population presenting with antibodies. Blood samples from the NHS Blood and Transplant service, Great Ormond Street Hospital and other paediatric hospitals were analysed.

Immunity studies

Sars-CoV-2 Immunity and REinfection EvaluatioN (SIREN) – UKHSA

June 2020 – ongoing

This is a major study testing blood samples and swabs obtained from healthcare workers who work in a clinical setting to see if infection with COVID-19 protects them from future episodes of infection. The study also assesses vaccine effectiveness and the effect of antibody protection against COVID-19 variants.

It was set up by PHE in April 2020 in response to the need to assess the risk of re-infection in those who have tested positive for antibodies.

Protective Immunity from T-Cells in Healthcare workers (PITCH) – Public Health England

March 2020 – ongoing

This study is a partnership between multiple top universities to understand the T-cell responses in healthcare workers. It focuses on the immunity that might come from having been previously infected and what this means.
