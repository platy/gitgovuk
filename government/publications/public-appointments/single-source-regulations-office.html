The Single Source Regulations Office (SSRO) is an important Executive Non-Departmental Public Body reporting directly to the Secretary of State for Defence under the terms of the Defence Reform Act 2014 which received Royal Assent in May 2014. The SSRO’s role is to be an independent regulator on MOD’s single source procurement and the custodian of the single source procurement framework, those contracts which are placed with defence suppliers without the usual competitive process. Its mission is to maintain a single source procurement framework that assures value for money for the UK taxpayer and allows a fair and reasonable price for single source suppliers.

It is responsible for:

  • regulating single source procurement in the UK defence industry
  • providing independent, evidence-based guidance and mediation on MOD single source procurement to the Prime Minister and Secretary of State for Defence
  • considering other specific procurement related issues as and when required

All appointments are remunerated at £500 per day, plus reasonable travel and subsistence expenses.

All appointments and re-appointments have been made in accordance with the Governance Code on Public Appointments.
