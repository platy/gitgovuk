The Royal Air Force (RAF) Museum is a world-class national museum, which is responsible for promoting the public’s understanding of the history, role and achievements of the RAF through the integrated and imaginative use of its exhibitions, collections and the knowledge and expertise of its staff.

Capturing the often amazing achievements of its people is central to telling the RAF’s story, both past and present. The arrival of a new and dynamic senior management team in 2014 to 15 was the start of a significant transformation programme aimed at ensuring that the RAF’s story is told in the most comprehensive, exciting and imaginative manner. The museum already has some ground breaking exhibitions, such as the Cold War exhibition at Cosford, but the current transformation programme, which is focused on celebrating the 100 year anniversary of the RAF in 2018, will take the museum to a new level and ensure that the RAF’s story endures and enriches the lives of future generations.

The first phase of the ambitious £23M transformation programme was the creation of a new World War 1 in the Air Exhibition, which was opened in December 2014 and recently received the Heritage Lottery Fund Best Heritage Project Award for 2015.

Besides improving its exhibitions and interpretation, the Museum’s already extensive education programme will be enhanced through greater STEM learning opportunities and facilities. Although the Museum naturally reflects on the past achievements of the RAF, new emphasis will be placed on what the RAF is doing today and how its preparing for the future.
