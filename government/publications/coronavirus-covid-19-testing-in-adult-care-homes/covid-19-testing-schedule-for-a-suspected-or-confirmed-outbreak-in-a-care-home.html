Definition of an outbreak

Care homes should follow a regular staff testing regime of weekly PCR testing and twice weekly lateral flow testing.

An outbreak is defined as 2 or more clinically suspected or confirmed positives (rapid lateral flow or PCR) among residents or staff detected in the same 14-day period.

However, one positive test result may be the first sign of an outbreak, so you should contact your local health protection team (HPT) for advice in this instance.

In the event of an outbreak

If you have one or more clinically suspected or confirmed case in staff or residents, you should contact your local HPT immediately for risk assessment and ask if outbreak testing is required.

If there is a delay in the HPT returning your call, you should:

  • start 7-day rapid response daily staff testing
  • ensure cases are isolated and contacts identified
  • follow current guidance on the management of staff and exposed patients and residents

Where it is unclear whether this is an outbreak, the HPT should err on the side of caution and recommend outbreak testing.

Rapid response daily staff testing

If your local HPT decides outbreak testing is not required but does advise testing, you should complete 7 days of daily staff testing using rapid lateral flow test (LFT) kits.

Any positive LFT result should be followed by a confirmatory PCR test.

If no further positive results are found within 7 days, you should stop the rapid response testing and return to the regular staff testing regime of weekly PCR testing and twice weekly lateral flow testing.

All confirmatory PCR tests (after an initial positive rapid LFT) must be negative too.

If further positive results are found, outbreak testing is required.

Outbreak testing

In an outbreak, staff and residents will follow this testing schedule.

Residents

  • on day 1 of the outbreak: one PCR test and one rapid LFT
  • on day 4, 5, 6 or 7 of the outbreak: one PCR test and one rapid LFT

Staff

All staff will take 2 PCR tests on:

  • day 1 of the outbreak
  • day 4, 5, 6 or 7 of the outbreak

Plus daily rapid LFTs.

You should start (or continue, depending on your circumstances) daily rapid lateral flow testing of all staff. Keep testing staff daily until you go for 5 consecutive days without a positive result.

Any positive LFT result from staff or residents should be followed by a confirmatory PCR test.

For PCR testing follow the usual care home testing process as laid out by the National Testing Programme. If a courier is not already booked, order a same day courier for your PCR tests.

PCR test any newly symptomatic people. Continue to isolate and ensure contact tracing is completed.

Once outbreak PCR and resident LFD testing is complete, continue with the regular testing cycle. Rapid response daily staff testing may continue in this scenario until no new cases have been identified for 5 days.

Determine the end of an outbreak

To determine the end of outbreak testing, all staff and residents should be retested again 28 days after the last resident or staff had a positive test result or showed coronavirus-like symptoms.

The regular resident testing cycle of 28 days can be delayed to coincide with outbreak recovery testing or end of outbreak PCR testing if the 2 test dates fall within a week of each other.

Any further positive cases after this point is a new suspected or confirmed outbreak. The care home must contact the HPT and immediately begin rapid response testing again.

If no further cases are identified at this point, the outbreak is considered to have ended. Resume the normal testing regime.

The 90-day window after a positive test

If someone has tested positive with a PCR test, they should not be tested using either PCR or rapid lateral flow tests for 90 days, unless they develop new symptoms during this time â in which case they should be retested immediately using PCR.

This 90-day period is from the initial onset of symptoms or, if asymptomatic when tested, their positive test result.

Newly symptomatic people

You should PCR test all newly symptomatic people, staff and resident. Also, continue to isolate and ensure contract tracing has been completed.

If you need more test kits

Contact 119 and ask for an urgent delivery if you are running out of the kits to do the necessary testing.

You should receive kits within 48 hours.
