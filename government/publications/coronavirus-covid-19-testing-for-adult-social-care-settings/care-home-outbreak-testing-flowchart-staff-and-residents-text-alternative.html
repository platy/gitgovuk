Step 1: Have there been 2 or more positive COVID-19 cases in a 14-day period?

NO

No positive tests

Continue regular asymptomatic staff testing. If any staff member or resident becomes symptomatic, they should take an LFD test on day 0 and then on day 2. If either of these are positive, follow the guidance in the IPC-COVID-19 supplement.

Positive case

If there is one positive case in the home, staff should conduct rapid response testing for 5 days. If there are further cases with the 5-day period:

1. Continue testing, but do not extend the rapid response testing period beyond 5 days. 2. Contact the HPT (or if applicable, your community infection prevention and control team, LA or CCG).

YES

Contact the HPT

An outbreak is 2 or more positive or suspected linked cases of COVID-19 that occur in the same setting within a 14-day period.

Contact the HPT (or if applicable, your community infection prevention and control team, LA or CCG) if you suspect an outbreak.

Outbreak testing

Day 0/1

All staff and residents take PCR (if an individual has tested positive for COVID-19 in the last 90 days, their testing may be different. Read the IPC-COVID-19 supplement for further information) and LFD tests.

Day 4 to 7

Staff and residents take one PCR (if an individual has tested positive for COVID-19 in the last 90 days, their testing may be different. Read the IPC-COVID-19 supplement for further information) and one LFD on one of these days.

Day 7 and after

Staff continue regular twice weekly symptomatic LFDs.

Step 2: Has it been 10 days since the last member of staff or resident tested positive for COVID-19 or experienced symptoms during the outbreak?

NO

Outbreak continues

Continue regular asymptomatic staff testing and continue LFD testing any newly symptomatic individuals.

YES

Outbreak recovery testing

All staff and residents conduct a PCR (if an individual has tested positive for COVID-19 in the last 90 days, their testing may be different. Read the IPC-COVID-19 supplement for further information) test.

Were any new positives found?

Yes

HPT (or if applicable, your community infection prevention and control team, Local Authority or CCG) will advise that the outbreak restrictions should continue until no further positives are found.

No

HPT (or if applicable, your community infection prevention and control team, Local Authority or CCG) will advise you can lift outbreak restrictions and follow normal testing.

Step 3: Have there been any positive cases in the home after outbreak recovery testing?

NO

Regular staff asymptomatic testing continues

If any staff or resident becomes newly symptomatic, they should take an LFD test when they become symptomatic. If this test is negative, take another test 48 hours later. If either of these are positive, follow the guidance in the IPC-COVID-19 supplement.

YES

This should be classed as a new potential outbreak and the care home should follow the process from step 1.
