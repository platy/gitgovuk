This is an overview of the key steps to take a lateral flow test (LFT) in adult social care (ASC).

It is very important that you read the step-by-step guide that comes with the test kit.

If you notice anything damaged, broken, missing or difficult to use in the kit, do not use it. Call the customer contact centre immediately on 119 to report the damaged, broken or missing contents. Lines open every day, 7am to 11pm.

Read more detailed guidance and resources for adult social care.

Key reminders

  1. Review the step-by-step instructions booklet in your kit.

  2. Complete a throat and nasal swab.

  3. Make sure you have your unique organisation number (UON) ready. If you do not know your UON, ask the person who provided you with the test.

  4. Wait the full 30 minutes before checking your result.

  5. Report your result online.

  6. Tests should be conducted at the frequency outlined by national policy for you.

Read your results

You will see the control line (C) begin to appear after about 4 minutes.

You must wait 30 minutes before your result is ready.

Do not leave the test to develop for longer than 30 minutes as this will make the result void.

If your test result is positive, you and your household must self-isolate following government guidelines.

One line next to C shows the test is negative.

Two lines, one next to C and one next to T even faint lines, shows the test is positive.

No lines or one line next to T shows the test is void.

Report every result – negatives and voids as well as positive results. This is important to help the NHS Test and Trace monitor the spread of the virus.

Report your results

Your result will be complete after 30 minutes.

You must report all rapid LFT results.

What you need to report your result

  • the test strip ID number on the test device (under the QR code)
  • the UON from the site where you received the test kit. If you do not know your UON, speak to the person who provided you with the test

Report online

When asked “Who was the test for?”, please select “A social care service”.

If you are an independent health provider (IHP), please select “It’s not listed”.

Report by phone

Call 119 (free from mobiles and landlines).

Guidance for a positive result

If you get a positive LFT result you must:

  • self-isolate immediately
  • take a confirmatory PCR test as soon as possible
  • inform your line manager, employer and/or the person who provided you with the test

If you were not provided with a PCR test, you can order one online.

Register your confirmatory PCR test online – you will need your UON.

Vaccine roll-out

You must continue to test, even if you have been vaccinated, as we do not yet know if the vaccine prevents the virus being passed on to others.

All staff still need to follow the workplace guidance, including wearing the correct personal protection equipment (PPE) and taking regular tests to protect themselves, the people they care for, family, friends and colleagues.

If you have any questions you can speak to your manager or call 119.
