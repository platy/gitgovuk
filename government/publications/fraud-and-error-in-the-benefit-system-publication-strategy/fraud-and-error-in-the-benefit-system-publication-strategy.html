Introduction

The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) publishes National Statistics about the levels of fraud and error in the DWP benefit system. They estimate the amount of money overpaid and underpaid to claimants and are an important metric that is included in the department’s annual report and accounts.

This document explains how we plan to release these statistics in future, and how further welfare reforms will be included.

Consultation

Following a consultation in summer 2018, the department moved to an annual release (previously they were every 6 months). The Financial Year Ending (FYE) 2019 estimates were published in May 2019, and were the first to follow the new annual timetable.

These statistics will be used in the department’s annual report and accounts and will be the statistics that the National Audit Office comment on. Management Information, data and analysis has been regularly provided to officials within the department since the May 2019 publication, allowing trends to be identified and issues addressed, ensuring a continued focus on the issue of fraud and error.

Read the full response to the consultation.

Future reporting

For FYE 2023, we are measuring the following benefits for fraud and error:

  • Universal Credit
  • Housing Benefit (working age, non-passported cases)
  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Pension Credit
  • State Pension
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

This will see us re-introduce the measurement of PIP, which was last measured in FYE 2020, after it was paused due to the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic.

We intend to publish our FYE 2023 estimates in May 2023.
