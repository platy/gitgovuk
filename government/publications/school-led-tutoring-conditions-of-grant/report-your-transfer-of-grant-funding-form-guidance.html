Introduction

As part of the Department for Education’s (DfE)’s education recovery plan, the Secretary of State for Education has provided financial assistance to eligible local authorities, for their maintained schools, and academy trusts. This was in accordance with section 14 of the Education Act 2002, in the form of the school-led tutoring grant for the 2021 to 2022 academic year.

This grant gave schools and academy trusts the flexibility to determine how best to provide tutoring intervention, to support catch-up for lost education due to the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic.

These instructions provide guidance so local authorities can use this service to report where they have passed school-led tutoring grant funding on to independent special schools.

About the fund

This grant funding is only for expenditure on school-led tutoring, and cannot be combined with other premiums.

It should entail staff costs of delivering tuition only and cannot be used to cover costs such as:

  • diagnostic tools
  • room hire
  • equipment
  • laptops
  • transport
  • stationery
  • record keeping

You can read more about how to use the grant in the school-led tutoring guidance.

This funding cannot be used for the non-subsidised element of the National Tutoring Programme (NTP) including costs related to support provided by NTP tuition partners and academic mentors.

When to complete your submission

The deadline to complete this form is 5:00pm on Friday, 9 September 2022.

Accessing the form

This form is for local authorities only.

Access the school-led tutoring: report transfer of grant funding form to confirm transfer of grant funding to independent special schools.

Before you start

Make sure you have:

  • read this guidance document
  • a DfE Sign-in account
  • the names of each independent special school, including their unique identifier (UID) that you have passed school-led tutoring funding to, and the funding amounts transferred

Accessing the form

Sign in to your DfE Sign-in account:

  1. Access the School-led tutoring: report transfer of grant funding form.
  2. Sign in to DfE Sign-in using your account information (username and password).
  3. Choose the organisation for which you are completing the form.
  4. The next page will display the details we hold about the organisation you selected when you signed in. This will show the name, address, and unique reference number (URN) or UK Provider Reference Number (UKPRN) of the organisation, as well as the name and email address of the person who signed in via DfE Sign-in.
  5. If these details are correct, select ‘continue’ to move to the next page.
  6. If you believe the details are incorrect, or you are experiencing issues accessing the form, contact us via the ESFA help centre and we will investigate.
  7. Complete the form as instructed in the following sections.

Your account must be connected to the organisation you are claiming funding for.

You do not need to add any services in DfE Sign-in. It is used for authentication only, and the form will not appear in your list of services.

If your DfE Sign-in account is associated with more than one organisation, you must select the individual setting from the list presented on the ‘Select your organisation’ page, which will then allow access to the form.

If the individual organisation is not displayed, you would need to find out who within your organisation has approver permissions for DfE Sign-in and they can add or remove a user.

If you cannot find who has approver access, your chief executive officer (CEO)/chief financial officer (CFO) or business manager can raise an incident via the DfE Sign-in Service Desk.

If you do not have a DfE Sign-in account

You will need to create one:

  1. Access DfE Sign-in.
  2. Select the ‘create account’ button.
  3. Enter your name and email address (it is recommended that you use your work email address, or one that is clearly associated with your organisation).
  4. We will send you an email to verify your email address. Copy the code from this email and paste it into the box on the verification page.
  5. Create a memorable password of 8 characters, which must include 2 upper case characters, 2 lower case characters and 2 numbers.
  6. Sign in using your new credentials, select ‘organisations’ and then ‘request an organisation’, enter the URN or name of your establishment. The request will be sent to the DfE Sign-in approver for the establishment, to approve your request.

You might have noticed the term ‘approver’ when creating your account. An approver is someone at your organisation responsible for controlling who has access to DfE Sign-in and the services within the system. It’s usually a senior person, such as an administrator or a manager.

Once your request to access an organisation has been approved, you can follow the steps outlined in sign into your DfE Sign-in account to complete the form.

For security reasons, you will need the approval of one of your organisation’s approvers before you can access the form.

 If you need further assistance

If you are having problems using the DfE Sign-in service, use our DfE Sign-in help service. You will be able to request further assistance if required. When raising a support request, select ‘other.’

If you believe you are eligible to claim but have problems accessing the form, contact us via the ESFA help centre.

Completing the form

Selecting ‘continue’ on each page will take you through the form.

You will also be advised what will happen next once you have submitted the form.

Conditions for accessing the form

You must be a local authority to confirm transfer of your grant funding to independent special schools. If you are an ineligible setting, you will see the following message:

‘You cannot access this form because your organisation is not a local authority.’

If you think this is incorrect, contact us via the ESFA help centre.

 Your details

This page will display the details we hold about the organisation you selected when you logged into DfE Sign-in.

If any of your details are incorrect, you should let us know using the ESFA help centre. You can still make a submission.

How many independent special schools have you transferred school-led tutoring funding to?

You will be asked to enter the number of independent special schools you have transferred school-led tutoring to.

Schools details

You must report the details of each independent special school that you have transferred school-led tutoring funding to.

You can use the Get Information about Schools service to help you to find the correct school name and URN.

You will be able to add another school later.

Amount of funding transferred

You must enter the amount of you have transferred to the independent special school you selected on previous page.

The amount must be more than zero in value.

School contact details

Report the business contact details including full name and business email address of your designated contact person at the independent special school that you have transferred the school-led tutoring grant to. This will be the person who can be contacted about the grant.

On the next page, you will then be asked if you would like to add another school you have transferred funding to.

Declarations

Once you have completed your submission, you’ll be asked to make the mandatory declarations.

You will not be able to submit your form without agreeing to the declarations.

After completing the declarations, you should select the ‘continue’ button.

You will then see the submission confirmation page.

Summary

The final page gives you summary of your submission relating to the school-led tutoring: report transfer of grant funding. After checking your answers, you should select the ‘confirm and send’ button which will submit your form.

 Confirmation

The final page confirms your submission relating to the school-led tutoring: report transfer of grant funding.

You will receive an email which includes your claim reference and a PDF of your submission.

 Amending your submission

If your submission is incorrect, you can change your answers and resubmit this form up until 5:00pm on Friday, 9 September 2022, when the form window closes. To do this, you will need to sign in and update the relevant information.

If you make an amendment, we will take the details of the most recently submitted form and disregard previous versions.

Claims can be submitted, or resubmitted until the deadline by selecting the link to the claim form. It will not be possible to grant any extensions to the deadline.

You will receive a new unique submission reference for an amended claim.

Enquiries

If you have read this guidance and have any questions about the school-led tutoring: report transfer of grant funding, contact us via the ESFA help centre.

When completing the enquiry form, select ‘other’ as your query type.
