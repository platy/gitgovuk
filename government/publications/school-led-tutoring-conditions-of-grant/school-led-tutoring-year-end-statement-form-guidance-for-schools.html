Introduction

As part of the Department for Education’s (DfE’s) education recovery plan, the Secretary of State for Education has provided financial assistance to eligible local authorities for their maintained schools and academy trusts. This was in accordance with section 14 of the Education Act 2002 and took the form of the school-led tutoring grant for the 2021 to 2022 academic year.

This grant gave schools and academy trusts the flexibility to determine how best to provide tutoring intervention to support catch-up for lost education due to the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic.

About the year-end statement

The Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) has released the school-led tutoring year-end statement form, and all schools, academies and independent special schools that have received funding will be expected to complete and return the statement detailing how much tuition has been delivered.

This is a mandatory return.

Where the funding has not been used fully (or at all) by schools and academies, ESFA will treat this as an overpayment. ESFA will offset this against future payments.

Where the local authority has passed funding on to an independent special school and the school has not utilised it fully or at all, ESFA will recover this from the independent special school and not the local authority.

The funding will be recovered via an invoice from ESFA to the independent special school. We will use the information provided in the year-end statement to calculate this.

Refer to the ‘Calculating overpayments’ section in the school-led tutoring guidance for further information, including detailed examples which set out how overpayment will be calculated in different scenarios.

When to complete your submission

The deadline to complete this form is 6:00pm on Friday, 16 September 2022.

Accessing the form

This form is for schools, academies and independent special schools only.

Access the school-led tutoring: year-end statement form to submit your year-end statement to ESFA.

Before you start

Make sure you have:

  • read the instructions on this page and the year-end statement guidance for schools, academies and independent special schools
  • a DfE Sign-in account
  • pupil hours and funding spent information

 Accessing the form

Sign in to your DfE Sign-in account:

  1. Access the school-led tutoring: year-end statement form.
  2. Sign in to DfE Sign-in using your account information (username and password).
  3. Choose the organisation for which you are completing the form.
  4. The next page will display the details we hold about the organisation you selected when you logged in. This will show the name, address, and unique reference number (URN) or UK Provider Reference Number (UKPRN) of the organisation, as well as the name and email address of the person who signed in via DfE Sign-in.
  5. If these details are correct, select ‘continue’ to move to the next page.
  6. If you believe they are incorrect or you are experiencing issues accessing the form, contact us via the ESFA help centre and we will investigate.
  7. Complete the form as instructed in the following sections.

Your account must be connected to the organisation you are making the statement for.

You do not need to add any services in DfE Sign-in. It is used for authentication only, and the form will not appear in your list of services.

If your DfE Sign-in account is associated with more than one organisation, you must select the individual setting from the list presented on the ‘Select your organisation’ page, which will then provide you with access to the digital form.

If the individual organisation is not displayed, you would need to find out who within your organisation has approver permissions for DfE Sign-in and they can add or remove a user.

If you cannot find who has approver access, your chief executive officer (CEO)/chief financial officer (CFO) or business manager can contact us to raise an incident.

If you do not have a DfE Sign-in account

You will need to create one:

  1. Access DfE Sign-in.
  2. Select the ‘create account’ button.
  3. Enter your name and email address (it is recommended to use your work email address or one that is clearly associated with your organisation).
  4. We will send you an email to verify your email address. Copy the code from this email and paste it into the box on the verification page.
  5. Create a memorable password of 8 characters, which must include 2 upper case characters, 2 lower case characters and 2 numbers.
  6. Sign in using your new credentials, select ‘organisations’ and then ‘request an organisation’, enter the URN or name of your establishment. The request will be sent to the DfE Sign-in approver for the establishment, to approve your request.

You might have noticed the term ‘approver’ when creating your account. An approver is someone at your organisation responsible for controlling who has access to DfE Sign-in and the services within the system. It is usually a senior person, such as an administrator or a manager.

Once your request to access an organisation has been approved, you can follow the steps outlined in sign in to your DfE Sign-in account to complete the form.

For security reasons, you will need the approval of one of your organisation’s approvers before you can access the form.

 If you need further assistance

If you are having problems using the DfE Sign-in service, contact us via DfE Sign-in Help. You will be able to request further assistance if required. When raising a support request, select ‘other.’

If you believe you are eligible to claim but have problems accessing the form, contact us via the ESFA help centre.

 Completing the form

Selecting ‘continue’ on each page will take you through the form.

You will also be advised what will happen next once you have submitted the form.

Conditions for accessing the form

You must be a school, academy or an independent special school to make year-end statement. If you are an ineligible setting, you will see the following message:

‘Sorry, you are not allowed to access this form.

Contact us if you need more information.’

 Your details

This page displays the details we hold about the organisation you selected when you logged into DfE Sign-in.

If any of your details are incorrect, you should let us know via the ESFA help centre. You can still make a submission.

Tuition complete

You must answer this question according to the school-led tuition delivered for the academic year 2021 to 2022.

You should only complete this form once all your tuition delivery is complete.

Amount of funding spent on school-led tutoring

You must enter the amount of funding you have used on school-led tutoring. The amount must be more than 0 in value.

Time spent on school-led tutoring

You must enter the amount of time (as a whole number) you have spent giving tuition using the school-led tutoring grant. The amount must be more than 0 in value.

Number of pupils

In this section you need to confirm pupil numbers (as a whole number) that you have given tuition to using the school-led tutoring grant. The amount must be more than 0 in value.

Declarations

Once you have completed your submission, you will be asked to make the mandatory declarations.

You will not be able to submit your form without agreeing to the declarations.

After completing the declarations, you should select the ‘continue’ button.

You will then see the submission summary page.

Summary

This page gives you a summary of your submission relating to the school-led tutoring: year-end statement. After checking your answers, you should select the ‘confirm and send’ button which will submit your form.

 Confirmation

The final page confirms your submission relating to the school-led tutoring: year-end statement.

You will receive an email which includes your submission reference.

 Amending your submission

If your submission is incorrect, you can change your answers and resubmit this form up until 6:00pm on Friday, 16 September 2022 when the claim window closes. To do this, you will need to log back in and update the relevant information.

If you make an amendment, we’ll take the details of the most recently submitted form and disregard previous versions.

Claims can be submitted, or resubmitted until the deadline by selecting the link to the claim form. It will not be possible to grant any extensions to the deadline.

You will receive a new unique submission reference for an amended claim.

Enquiries

If you have read this guidance and have any questions about the school-led tutoring: year-end statement, contact us via the ESFA help centre.

When completing the enquiry form, select ‘other’ as your query type.
