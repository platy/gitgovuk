1. Introduction

The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) is legally required to manage its customer records to ensure it complies with data protection legislation, including the Data Protection Act (DPA) 2018 and General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The legislation requires that any personal data must be:

  • processed lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner
  • collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes
  • adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary
  • accurate and kept up to date
  • kept for no longer than is necessary
  • processed securely

Customer records are defined as any piece of information that relates to our claimants or customers regardless of its content and format.

Compliance with these instructions is the responsibility of all staff.

2. Retention of customer records

2.1 Classification of records

DWP has 2 classifications of customer records:

  1. ‘supporting’
  2. ‘non-supporting/ephemeral’

The definitions are as follows.

1. Supporting records

Supporting records are documents or data that support a benefit or child maintenance outcome decision, and contains information on which that decision is based. It is generally information that would be supplied in the event of an appeal or fraud investigation, or that affects the amount of benefit paid or maintenance assessment. See section 3 for general guidance on retention of customer documents.

2. Non-supporting/ephemeral records

Non-supporting/ephemeral records are documents or data that do not fit into the definition of supporting documents. These are of a general nature and do not affect the claim in any way. For example, general enquiries, internal templates, front cover sheets.

2.2 How long do I keep records?

Telephone call recording

Specific retention periods apply for telephone calls recorded in DWP operations. These are as follows:

  • supporting calls: 14 months from date of call and then deleted
  • non-supporting or ephemeral calls: 4 weeks from date of call and then deleted

Where a service line receives both supporting and non-supporting / ephemeral calls, the retention period will align with supporting calls.

All directorates must comply with the above guidelines. There can be exceptions where there is a departmental interest. See section 4 for further details of those exceptions.

Where an interest applies the relevant call(s) can be retained for longer that the standard telephone retention period. An exception cannot be applied indefinitely and the retention reason should be reviewed to ensure continued compliance.

Non-telephone records

The following retention policy is applied to all non-telephone records in DWP:

  • supporting records: generally records classified as supporting are retained for 14 months after DWP’s live interest in the claim has ended. As with telephone records, exceptions can apply to this. See section 4 for further details of those exceptions

  • non-supporting or ephemeral records: These should be retained locally for 4 weeks and then paper records must be destroyed via confidential waste.

The retention period is ‘triggered’ when a case is closed or the end of DWP interest.

2.3 Destruction of benefit records

When destroying benefit documents/data, we are applying DWP information management policy and not the Data Protection Act (DPA). The DPA does not specify retention periods.

The following standard responses must be used when members of the public make enquiries about the destruction of records:

  • when documents/data have been destroyed in line with the information management policy, use standard letter 1

  • when documents/data have not been destroyed in line with the information management policy, including any that may have been destroyed in error (either partially or fully), use standard letter 2

If benefit records are held by an external organisation, a contractor’s declaration must be completed and sent to the Departmental Records Officer when the records are destroyed.

Destruction embargoes

Destruction embargoes may be applied to our records from time to time based on business need. This can be applied to records irrespective of format. Please contact the Information and Records Management team for details of any such embargoes.

3. General retention principles

Users must:

  • only retain information required to support a claim by applying the principles of what is ‘supporting’ and what is ‘ephemeral’

  • apply retention periods to all claim documents or data whether the claim is successful or not

  • destroy all paper documents that are scanned into electronic systems within 4 weeks of being scanned

  • retain clerical benefits, including Armed Forces Independence Payment locally, apart from Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit (IIDB) claims which should be sent to remote stores

  • photocopy documents identified as valuable. The original should be returned to the claimant and the photocopy sent to remote stores

  • save customer information in the right place for the right length of time

Users must not:

  • print any documents relating to a benefit that are uploaded onto the Document Repository System (DRS) unless they are required to support an appeal or prosecution where access to DRS is not available

  • print information stored on electronic systems, including Universal Credit live service or Universal Credit full service and CMS2012, unless it is required to support an appeal or prosecution, and access to these systems is not available

  • retain duplicate information under any circumstances

  • retain customer records ‘just in case’ they may be needed at some point in the future

  • retain supporting records locally for longer than 4 weeks after they have been uploaded to the DRS. Once a supporting document has been uploaded to DRS it can be destroyed

  • send ephemeral records to remote stores under any circumstances

  • send non-associated post to remote stores if it does not have a parent file (a live original claim) there already

4. Guidance for specific customer records

The standard retention period for telephone recordings and non-telephone records is set out at section 2.2

Non-standard retention periods customer and benefit records are listed below.

Applicable retention periods are applied to customer records, whether they are, telephony, paper based or uploaded onto DRS.

If the case has the following departmental interest, the customer or benefit record must be retained until end of interest, then the normal retention period applies. DWP interest is defined as:

  • fraud

  • debt management including: overpayments, compensation recovery, civil proceedings and recovery from estates

  • appeals including: mandatory reconsiderations

  • customer feedback or complaints being dealt with by Independent Case Examiner (ICE) cases and Parliamentary Health and Service Ombudsman (PHSO) Cases only

  • criminal cases review commission cases

  • cases subject to a performance measurement check

The following benefits have the standard (non-telephone) 14 month retention period for supporting records:

  • Access to Work

  • appeals – do not send duplicate documents or highlighted and reported decisions to stores

  • Attendance Allowance

  • bereavement benefits

  • Carer’s Allowance

  • debt management (including compensation recovery, arrears and enforcements)

  • Disability Living Allowance

  • Income Support (IS)

  • Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit (IIDB)

  • Jobseekers Allowance (JSA)

  • Pension Credit

  • Social Fund

  • State Pension

  • Universal Credit

The following benefits do not have the standard 14 month retention period for non-telephone supporting records. For more details please contact data.protectionofficer@dwp.gov.uk:

  • Child Maintenance Group

  • Claimant Commitments

  • Complaints and Redress Team documents

  • Employer Adviser Support documents

  • Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

  • European Social Fund (ESF)

  • European Refugee Fund Claims for claims to:
    • JSA
    • ESA
    • IS

  • finance records (including financial checks, payments, journals, Flexible Support Fund, New Enterprise Allowance, FF100 and Special Payments). See Managing Finance and Procurement Records

  • fraud

  • Incapacity Benefit

  • Independent Case Examiner (ICE) cases

  • Internal Process Review documents

  • MAPPA J forms

  • NINO Allocation

  • Pension Sharing on Divorce documents

  • Personal Independence Payments (PIP)

  • RVU type B cases

  • State Pension Deferred Lump Sum Payments

  • Suicide and self-harm documents

  • Support for Mortgage Interest (SMI) loan documents

  • Warm Home Discount Scheme

  • Winter Fuel Payments

  • Work and Health Programme (WHP)
