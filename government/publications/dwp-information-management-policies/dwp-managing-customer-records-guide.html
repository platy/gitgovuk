1. Introduction

The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) is legally required to manage its customer records to ensure it complies with data protection legislation, including the Data Protection Act (DPA) 2018 and General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The legislation requires that any personal data must be:

  • processed lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner
  • collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes
  • adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary
  • accurate and kept up to date
  • kept for no longer than is necessary
  • processed securely

a. Customer records are defined as any piece of information that relates to our claimants or customers regardless of its content and format, for example, the retention policy is ‘format neutral’.

What is a record?

A record is the information (data) we hold on citizens/customers/claimants, there are a few formats including; electronic (system), paper and telephony.

1.1. Compliance

1.1.1. Compliance with these instructions is the responsibility of all staff.

2. Retention of customer records

2.1 Classification of records

2.1.1. DWP has 2 classifications of customer records: Supporting or Non-supporting/Ephemeral.

The definitions are as follows.

2.1.2. Supporting records: documents or data that support a benefit or child maintenance outcome decision, and contains information on how that decision was made. It is generally information that would be supplied in the event of an appeal or fraud investigation, or that affects the amount of benefit paid or child maintenance assessment. See Section 3.1.1 for the standard retention of customer documents.

2.1.3. Non-supporting or Ephemeral records: documents or data that do not fit into the definition of supporting documents. These are of a general nature and do not affect the claim in any way. For example, general enquiries, internal templates, front cover sheets.

3. How long do I keep records?

3.1. Supporting Records

3.1.1. Supporting records: Standard retention is 14 months, see section 6 supporting records are retained for 14 months after DWP’s live interest in the claim has ended see 5.2.

3.1.2. There are some non-standard retention periods see section 7

What is ‘end of live interest’?

The retention period is ‘triggered’ when a case is closed or at the end of DWP interest. DWP interest includes:

  • fraud investigation has ended,
  • overpayment has been repaid

3.2. Non-Supporting or Ephemeral records

3.2.1. Non-supporting or Ephemeral records: These should be retained locally for 4 weeks and then electronic records should be deleted and paper records must be destroyed via confidential waste.

3.3. Telephone call recording

Specific retention periods apply for telephone calls recorded in DWP operations. These are as follows:

3.3.1. Supporting calls: 14 months from date of call and then deleted.

3.3.2. All Directorates must comply with the above guidelines.

3.3.3. If there is a need to retain telephony records for longer than the standard retention period. A change request must be applied for.

3.4. SMS text messages

The full content of the SMS, including the mobile number to which the SMS was sent, to be retained for 90 days. See the DWP SMS Text Policy for more information.

3.5. Destruction of benefit records

3.5.1. When destroying benefit documents / data, we are applying DWP Information Management Policy (IMP) and not the UK General Data Protection Act (UK GDPR). The UK GDPR does not specify retention periods.

3.5.2. The following standard responses must be used when members of the public make enquiries about the destruction of records.

3.5.3. When documents / data have been destroyed in line with the IMP, use Standard Letter 1.doc.

3.5.4. When documents / data have not been destroyed in line with the IMP, including any that may have been destroyed in error (either partially or fully), use Standard Letter 2.doc.

3.6. Held by external organisations

3.6.1. If benefit records are held by an external organisation, a Contractor’s Declaration must be completed and sent to the Departmental Records Officer when the records are destroyed.

3.7. Destruction embargoes

3.7.1. Destruction embargoes or easements to current retention periods may be applied to customer records from time to time based on business need. These can be applied to records irrespective of format.

To apply for an embargo or an easement please complete this request form and return it to the Information and Records Management team.

4. General retention principles

4.1. Users must

4.1.1. Only retain information required to support a claim by applying the principles of what is ‘supporting’ and what is ‘ephemeral’.

4.1.2. Apply retention periods to all claim documents or data whether the claim is successful or not.

4.1.3. Destroy all paper documents that are scanned into electronic systems within 4 weeks of being scanned.

4.1.4. Retain clerical benefits, including Armed Forces Independence Payment (AFIP) locally, apart from IIDB claims which should be sent to Remote Stores.

4.1.5. Photocopy documents identified as valuable. The original should be returned to the claimant and the photocopy sent to Remote Stores.

4.1.6. Save customer information in the right place for the right length of time.

4.2. Users must not

4.2.1. Print any documents relating to a benefit that are uploaded onto the Document Repository System (DRS) unless they are required to support an appeal or prosecution where access to DRS is not available.

4.2.2. Print information stored on electronic systems, including UC Live or UC Full Service and CMS2012, unless it is required to support an appeal or prosecution, and access to these systems is not available.

4.2.3. Retain duplicate information under any circumstances.

4.2.4. Retain customer records ‘just in case’ they may be needed at some point in the future.

4.2.5. Retain supporting records locally for longer than 8 weeks after they have been uploaded to the Document Repository System (DRS) (link is external). Once supporting documents have been uploaded to DRS they can be destroyed.

4.2.6. Send non supporting/ephemeral records to Remote Stores under any circumstances.

4.2.7. Send Non-Associated Post (NAP) to Remote Stores if it does not have a parent file (a live original claim) there already.

5. Guidance for specific customer records

5.1. General records

5.1.1. The standard retention period applies to all supporting records in these formats; electronic, paper and telephony in section 6.

5.1.2. Non-standard retention periods for supporting records are listed in section 7.

5.1.3. Applicable retention periods are applied to customer records, whether they are, telephony, paper based or uploaded onto DRS.

5.1.4. If the case has the following departmental interest, the customer or benefit record must be retained until end of interest, then the normal retention period applies. DWP Interest is defined in 5.2 onwards:

5.2. Ongoing action

5.2.1. Debt Management including: overpayments, Compensation Recovery (CRU), civil proceedings and Recovery from Estates.

5.2.2. Appeals including: Mandatory Reconsiderations.

5.2.3. Customer feedback or complaints being dealt with by Independent Case Examiner (ICE) cases & Parliamentary Health and Service Ombudsman (PHSO) Cases only.

5.2.4. Criminal Cases Review Commission cases.

5.2.5. Cases subject to a Performance Measurement check.

6. Standard Retention 14 month retention (Electronic, paper and telephony)

The following benefits have the standard 14 month retention period for supporting records.

a) Access to Work

b) Appeals – do not send duplicate documents or highlighted / reported decisions to Stores

c) Attendance Allowance

d) Bereavement Benefits

e) Carer’s Allowance

f) Debt Management (including CRU, arrears and enforcements)

g) Disability Living Allowance

h) Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit (IIDB)

i) Jobseekers Allowance

j) Social Fund

k) State Pension

l) Universal Credit
