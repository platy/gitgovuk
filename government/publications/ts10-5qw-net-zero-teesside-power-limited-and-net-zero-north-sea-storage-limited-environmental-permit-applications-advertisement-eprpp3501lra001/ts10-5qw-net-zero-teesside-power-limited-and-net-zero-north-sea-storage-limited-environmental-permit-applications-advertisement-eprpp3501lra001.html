Details of the applications

The Environment Agency has received new bespoke applications for environmental permits under the Environmental Permitting (England and Wales) Regulations 2016 from Net Zero Teesside Power Limited and Net Zero North Sea Storage Limited.

Application numbers

EPR/PP3501LR/A001 and EPR/FP3143QN/A001

Regulated facility types

Section 1.1 Part A(1) (a): Burning of any fuel in an appliance with a rated thermal input of 50MW or more.(EPR/PP3501LR/A001)

Section 6.10 Part A(1): Carbon capture and storage. (EPR/PP3501LR/A001)

Directly Associated Activity to Section 6.10 Part A(1): Carbon capture and storage. (EPR/FP3143QN/A001)

Regulated facility address

Net Zero Teesside CCUS Project
Redcar
Cleveland
TS10 5QW

The applications include:

  • the completed application form
  • a non-technical summary
  • a summary of the management system
  • an environmental risk assessment
  • proposed operating techniques, including an assessment of best available techniques
  • management plans to address any key risks
  • modelling and monitoring of any significant emissions to air, land or water
  • a report on the condition of the site

How to view and comment on the applications

If you have any comments on the applications send these by 30 September 2022.

You can comment:

Online: view and comment on the application using Citizen Space

Via email: pscpublicresponse@environment-agency.gov.uk

For advice about how to make a representation or if you are unable to make a representation via e-mail please call 03708 506 506.

The Environment Agency will normally put any comments it receives on the public register. This includes your name but not your personal contact details. Please tell us if you do not want your response to be public.

The privacy notice for permit applicants/operators can be viewed at Privacy Notice

The proposals in this application are also the subject of a separate environmental impact assessment carried out as part of a Nationally Significant Infrastructure Project. You can get further information on this from the National Infrastructure Planning Portal
