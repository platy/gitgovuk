The Family Public Law online service allows local authorities to complete and submit online applications for care and supervision orders online.

1. Important statistics

Since 2019 the Family Public Law service has processed 12,000 applications.

2. Main outcomes

The Family Public Law service aims to make the process of applying for care and supervision orders faster and more effective for children and local authorities.

We’ve achieved several main outcomes so far, including:

  • local authorities can add their legal representatives to the case so that they can access documents and evidence whilst preparing for a hearing
  • files and bundles can be accessed remotely and without delay for all professionals involved in a case
  • automatic notifications alert CAFCASS to new cases as soon as applications are submitted
  • local authorities say that applying online is up to 50% quicker than applying on paper

3. How the Family Public Law service makes applying for care and supervision orders faster and more effective

The service is used by local authorities in England and Wales. It notifies everyone involved as the application progresses. This makes sure that actions are taken quickly, and children do not face unnecessary delays in having their future agreed.

The service also helps the judiciary make faster decisions for children, by allowing them to see all the information about a case in one place.

We use MyHMCTS to make court processes in Family Public Law more efficient, by making sure that courts, parties and their representatives have access to the right information at the right time.

4. Looking ahead in 2022

By the end of 2022 the Family Public Law service will:

  • allow local authorities to issue placement applications in care proceedings
  • allow local authorities to issue a secure accommodation application
  • allow local authorities to issue a child assessment application
  • allow professional users to issue a discharge of care/supervision application
