1. Welcome message from Baroness Barran

In my new role as the minister with responsibility for governance, I am delighted to be able to introduce myself and welcome you to the November governance update. As I write to you we are celebrating Trustees’ Week, when we recognise those who volunteer their time and expertise in governance roles.

From my previous experience as minister responsible for charities, and my own personal experience as a charity trustee, I have huge respect for the role you play as governors and trustees and I look forward to learning more about the contributions you make to our schools and trusts.

Effective governance underpins our continuing efforts to raise standards in our schools and your passion and dedication is vital to securing the best future for our children and young people. Thank you for all you do and I look forward to being the champion of this incredible volunteer army.

2. Understanding your data: a guide for school governors and academy trustees updated

We have updated Understanding your data: a guide for school governors and academy trustees. This resource brings together a range of data to help boards analyse, discuss, and challenge the educational and financial performance of their school or academy trust.

We have:

  • updated and improved all sections to provide new information and to reflect changes in policy
  • included new subsections on cyber security, additional support for pupils with special educational needs or disabilities and governing body composition
  • included links to additional sources of information, such as the ‘View my financial insights’ (VMFI) tool

3. National Leaders of Governance: eligibility for funded support and new designations

Working with the National Governance Association (NGA) we have designated over 60 new National Leaders of Governance (NLGs) to provide fully funded governance support to trusts and schools. All NLGs have completed a rigorous assessment process and meet the new NLG Standards.

NLGs undertake funded external reviews of governance and support improvement of governance in trusts and schools that meet the eligibility criteria:

  • trusts that are eligible for the Trust and School Improvement Offer (TSI)
  • maintained schools that have a current single RI Ofsted judgement, with RI in Leadership and Management and nominated by the diocese or local authority

In addition, regional school commissioner teams may offer NLG support to trusts that would most benefit from support to improve governance.

Local authorities and dioceses can nominate eligible maintained schools by contacting NGA.

4. Free services for finding skilled volunteers to become school governors and academy trustees

The Inspiring Governance online service provided by Employers and Education is available for maintained school governing bodies and local governing bodies of academy trusts to:

  • search for skilled volunteers by skills, experience or location
  • contact potential candidates directly
  • advertise vacancies to registered volunteers in the local area

Academy Ambassadors provided by New Schools Network is a free, bespoke service matching business people and professionals with academy trusts looking to strengthen their boards through the addition of the particular skills such individuals can bring to bear.

6. The View my financial insights (VMFI) tool has been updated with your latest data (academy trusts only)

The View my financial insights (VMFI) tool has now been updated with the latest 2021 Budget Forecast Return (BFR) data. It provides a forward look of the trust’s position for the next 3 years. The metrics in VMFI uses the trust’s BFR data to provide valuable contextual information and, where relevant, suggest actions to help with forward planning.

Visit View your education data to access the refreshed metrics and download the updated Summary of Finances.

What is new?

  • the forecasts and risks section now includes BFR 2021
    • additional 3 years of forecast data
    • updated budget forecast metrics
  • updated summary of finances

Find out more about VMFI on our User guide: view my financial insights guidance page.

7. Update your information: Get Information about Schools (GIAS)

Get Information about Schools (GIAS) website is where school and trust governance details are stored. It is essential that these details are kept up to date, including email addresses. The GIAS guidance page provides further details.

Academy trusts must notify the Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) about the appointment or vacating of governance roles within their trust. This is a requirement in the Academies Trust Handbook.

Contact your governance professional or clerk to add or update information in the governance section of GIAS using the school or trust’s DfE Sign-in account.

The GIAS service help pages may be useful.
