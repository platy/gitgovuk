Industrial action - temporary variations to grant rates

The department recognises that as a result of the recent and potential further rail strikes as well as the risk of continued significant industrial action, the unit costs of transporting freight by rail are likely to increase in the short term.  At the forthcoming bid round (submission period 8-15 July 2022) the department will consider proposals for temporary variations to grant rates for flows currently supported by the grant.

The department will also ensure sufficient resources are available for the grant to ensure any higher payment rates for existing flows are not at the expense of new proposals in this round (including flows that were considered in the last bid round but not funded if they are bid for again).

A temporary variation in rates could include rail period 4 (26 June – 23 July 2022).  The department will examine whether the temporary grant rates should be adjusted for other rail periods if there is protracted industrial action, in a way similar to the approach taken in 2020 during the initial periods affected by Coronavirus-related issues.

Freight grants guidance

There are 2 steps to apply for and receive funding from the:

  • Mode shift revenue support scheme (MSRS) for rail and inland waterways (PDF, 633KB)
  • Waterborne Freight Grant (WFG) for coastal and short sea shipping (PDF, 327KB)

Companies must:

  1. Originally register, to be verified by the Department for Transport (DfT) as suitable to receive journey grant funding.
  2. Make individual claims for individual journeys, once registered and verified.

Applying for freight grants

To bid for the different freight grant schemes, complete the:

  • MSRS Intermodal registration form
  • MSRS Bulk and Waterways registration form
  • Waterborne Freight Grant registration form

Applications are prioritised on the basis of value for money using the benefit cost ratio (BCR). Applications need to have a minimum BCR of 2:1 and a value of £5,000.

Application closing dates

Applications can be made for financial year 2022 to 2023 funding. The closing dates for applications are:

  • 12 February 2022, rail period 1 (1 April 2022)
  • 8 July 2022, rail period 5 (24 July 2022)
  • 23 September 2022, rail period 8 (16 October 2022)
  • 12 December 2022, rail period 11 (8 January 2023)

Claiming for payment

Following an award, you can make a freight grant scheme claim by emailing freightgrants@dft.gov.uk with the relevant completed form. The form you need to use is (one of the following):

  • the MSRS Intermodal journey grant application form (ODS)
  • the MSRS Bulk and Waterways journey grant application form (ODS)
  • the Waterborne Freight Grant journey grant application form (ODS)

Grants awarded

Recent grant awards are expected to remove the need for 1,239,710 lorry journeys and reduce carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions by 79,936 tonnes. The environmental BCR is expected to be 7.24:1.

A listing of all awards since December 2014 is available. Awards prior to December 2014 are available on the National Archives website.
