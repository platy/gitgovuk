Notice given under Schedule 2 to the Land Registration Rules 2003

Interpretation

1.In this Notice:

(a) expressions used have, unless the contrary intention appears, the meaning which they bear in the Land Registration Rules 2003 (SI 2003/1417), as amended from time to time,

(b) “AP1 application” means an application for which form AP1 is prescribed by the Rules,

(c) “Digital Registration service” is the service to enable certain applications to HM Land Registry to be made electronically by means of the portal using the digital application form, with such electronic documents and scanned copies of deeds and documents attached as are required to support the application(s).

(d) “development scheme” or “scheme title” means a registration arrangement made with the registrar to incorporate several parcels of land into a single registered title as they are acquired, as described in HM Land Registry’s Practice Guide 72 – Development Schemes,

(e) “digital application form” means a digital form that can be used when making certain applications using the Digital Registration service,

(f) “portal” means the secure website platform used to access HM Land Registry’s Business e-services,

(g) “the Rules” means the Land Registration Rules 2003,

2.Reference to a form other than the digital application form means the form of that description prescribed by Schedule 1 to the Rules or designated by a direction of the registrar made under section 100(4) of the Land Registration Act 2002.

Currency of notice

3.This Notice is made under rule 14 of, and Schedule 2 to, the Rules and shall be current for the purposes of Schedule 2 on and after 06:30 hours on 8 December 2021. It replaces on that date Notice 25 – Digital Application Form dated 18 August 2021.

The delivery of applications electronically

4.Arrangements have been made for a public beta to enable making:

(a) certain AP1 applications without submitting a form AP1, and

(b) applications for which the use of one or more of the forms in the Schedule to this Notice is prescribed by the Rules.

5.The use of the digital application form through the Digital Registration service replaces the form AP1 where it would otherwise be an AP1 application, and its use is subject to the provisions of paragraphs 6 to 22.

6.The use of the digital application form does not obviate the need to provide other forms in support of the application(s) made where their use is prescribed by the Rules or designated by a direction of the registrar made under section 100(4) of the Land Registration Act 2002.

When applications may be delivered

7.Subject to the availability of the service, applications may be delivered between 0630 hours and 2300 hours every day.

Who may deliver applications

8.Applications may be delivered only by a firm which already uses HM Land Registry’s electronic Document Registration Service and which has been approved and given access by HM Land Registry to use the public beta referred to in paragraph 4.

Applications for which the digital application form can be used

9.Subject to paragraph 10, an application made using the digital application form must be:

(i) an AP1 application, and for the purpose of the current release of the public beta, that includes an application to register:

  • a transfer for valuable consideration
  • a transfer not for valuable consideration
  • a transfer of a share
  • a transfer subject to a charge
  • a transfer under a power of sale
  • an assent

(ii) Charge Applications

  • a discharge of a charge
  • a charge
  • an obligation to make further advances

(iii) an application for which a form set out in the Schedule to this Notice is prescribed by the Rules, and be of a type that is capable of being satisfactorily identified by selecting an option in the drop-down list provided by the box ‘Transaction – select from list’ in the ‘Select transaction’ screen in the Digital Registration service.

or

(iv) an application of the types detailed in 9 (i) (ii) and (iii) above, in which one or more of the parties is acting by an attorney under an appropriate power.

Application types for which the digital application form cannot be used

10.The digital application form cannot yet be used for any application:

(a) to register:

  • a transfer of part
  • a lease
  • an assent of part
  • a transfer by operation of law
  • a release from a charge (form DS3)
  • any other application, apart from those referred to in paragraph 9(i), where, if the application were made in paper form, confirmation and evidence of identity would be required in panels 12 to 14 inclusive of form AP1, but, for these purposes, disregarding the exceptions set out in paragraph 2(1) of Direction 2 made by the registrar on 29 April 2020

(b) for first registration,

(c) that relates to an individual register of title that is not held in electronic form,

(d) that relates to a development scheme or scheme title.

11.If an application is made using a digital application form that is not an application for which the digital application form can be used, the registrar may treat it as an application made using the electronic Document Registration Service through portal, pursuant to Notice 20 given by the registrar under Schedule 2 to the Rules, but disregarding paragraph 3 of that Notice, or as an application made in paper form using form AP1 and/or one or more of the forms set out in the Schedule to this Notice.

General conditions and limitations

12.If more than one application is delivered together, the applicant must list the applications in priority order when prompted.

13.The applicant must provide such particulars as are requested during the course of completing a digital application form.

14.When the applicant completes the box ‘Transaction – Select from list’ the applicant must select from the drop-down list the text which satisfactorily identifies the application type being made.

15.Any requisitions arising from any application made using a digital application form will be delivered to the applicant electronically, and all replies to requisitions and further documents must be lodged through the Reply to Requisition service in HM Land Registry’s portal.

16.Any acknowledgment, certificate, vectorised plan or other result of an application will be issued by the registrar electronically. If for any reason the registrar considers that it cannot be conveniently issued in electronic form, he may issue it in paper form.

Limitations on format of documents that will be accepted as attachments

17.Electronic attachments to applications, including scanned plans, must:

(a) be either in Tagged Image File/Tagged Image File Format (TIFF), Portable Document Format (PDF), Graphical Interchange File Format (GIF) or a file complying with Joint Photographic Expert Group standards (JPEG).

(b) not exceed 20 megabytes in size for each single TIFF, PDF, GIF or JPEG document.

18.When a plan is included in the application it must be scanned:

(a) in no more than two parts, at a maximum size (per part) of A3, but where the plan is scanned in more than one part, a third scanned image must also be lodged with the application showing the full extent of the plan on one page,

(b) in full colour,

(c) at a resolution not less than 200dpi and not more than 600dpi.

19.If evidence of compliance with Stamp Duty Land Tax or Land Transaction Tax legislation is required in respect of an application, an SDLT submission receipt in PDF, TIFF, GIF or JPEG may be attached to the application in the case of SDLT. Otherwise a scanned copy of an SDLT5 certificate or a Land Transaction Tax certificate should be attached.

General particulars and documents to be provided

20.The applicant must attach to the digital application form electronic versions or scanned copies of such forms and documents as will be required by the registrar to enable the registrar to complete the application(s) applied for.

21.Subject to paragraph 18 (if applicable) each form and document attached to the digital application form must be scanned as a separate document or attached separately and be supported by one of the following certificates:

  • I/We certify this attachment is a true copy of the original document.
  • I/We certify this attachment is a true copy of a document which is certified by a conveyancer to be a true copy of the original.
  • This attachment is an uncertified copy.

22.Each electronic attachment, including scanned attachments, must be clearly legible and of sufficient quality to be reproduced as an official copy by the registrar.
