The regulator expects registered providers to identify problems and take effective action to resolve them. If a registered provider takes responsibility for self-improvement and we conclude that it has the capacity and capability to respond to the problems, we will work with it to achieve the necessary corrective actions. However, in circumstances where self-improvement has not succeeded, or where a registered provider is unable or unwilling to respond positively, or where the regulator concludes that such an approach is not appropriate (for instance, where urgent action is necessary or there is an immediate and significant risk of serious detriment to a registered provider’s tenants in relation to consumer matters), we may need to consider the use of our regulatory, enforcement and general powers.

The guidance sets out :

  • Approach to intervention and enforcement
  • Approach to use of powers
  • Principles underpinning the approach: what registered providers can expect
  • How the regulator will engage with registered providers
  • Factors that may lead to the use of powers
  • Checks and balances on the use of our powers
  • Duty to minimise interference
  • Regulators’ Code
  • Consideration before exercising a power
  • Voluntary undertakings
