As a children’s social care provider, you must pay an annual fee to remain registered.

Establishment or agency Annual fee
Children’s home[footnote 1] with 3 or fewer places £2,836 flat fee
Children’s home with 4 to 26 places £2,836 flat fee, plus £283 for each place from 4 up to and including 26
Children’s home with 27 or more places £9,436 flat fee
Multi-build children’s home with 3 or fewer places £4,254 flat fee
Multi-build children’s home with 4 to 6 places £4,254 flat fee, plus £283 for each place from 4 up to and including 6
Residential family centre with 3 or fewer places £1,889 flat fee
Residential family centre with 4 or more places £1,913 flat fee
Boarding school with 3 or fewer places £926 flat fee
Boarding school with 4 to 12 places £926 flat fee, plus £56 for each place from 4 up to and including 12
Boarding school with 13 to 50 places £1,455 flat fee
Boarding school with 51 to 500 places £2,036 flat fee
Boarding school with over 500 places £2,445 flat fee
Residential college with 3 or fewer places £1,233 flat fee
Residential college with 4 to 9 places £1,233 flat fee, plus £75 for each place from 4 up to and including 9
Residential college with 10 places £1,757 flat fee
Residential college with 11 or more places £1,819 flat fee
Residential special school with 3 or fewer places £2,267 flat fee
Residential special school with 4 to 17 places £2,267 flat fee, plus £226 for each place from 4 up to and including 17
Residential special school with 18 or more places £5,457 flat fee
Independent fostering agency £3,113
Adoption support agency £1,750
Adoption support agency: small £985
Voluntary adoption agency £1,858 for the principal office, plus £1,858 for each branch and £985 for each small branch
Voluntary adoption agency with a small principal office £985 for the principal office, plus £1,858 for each branch plus £985 for each small branch
Local authority adoption service £2,059
Local authority fostering service £3,216
Holiday schemes for disabled children £500

Fees and legislation

Annual fees are set out in legislation:

  • Her Majesty’s Chief Inspector of Education, Children’s Services and Skills (Fees and Frequency of Inspections) (Children’s Homes etc.) Regulations 2018
  • Her Majesty’s Chief Inspector of Education, Children’s Services and Skills (Fees and Frequency of Inspections) (Children’s Homes etc.) (Amendment) Regulations 2022

  1. The term ‘children’s home’ in this document also covers secure children’s homes providing accommodation for the purpose of restricting liberty, schools registering as children’s homes, and refuges registering as children’s homes. ↩
