Background Information

As of the 31 January 2022, Nuclear Waste Services has been set up to combine all of the NDA Waste activities, giving greater flexibility and driving improvements and synergies across the NDA Group. This brings together LLW Repository Limited, Radioactive Waste Management Limited and the NDA Integrated Waste Management Programme (each with their own IT teams, infrastructure, and systems).

Role Description

The Project Engineering Manager (PEM) will be a member of Nuclear Waste Services, Engineering Team embedded within the Repository Asset Care Enhancement and Remediation Programme (RACER). Reporting to the Lead Project Engineering Manager, the PEM will be matrixed into the RACER project management team, acting as line manager for Engineering Team members within the RACER team.

The role of the PEM is to organise and coordinate multi-discipline design engineering teams, including the supply chain designers, responsible for delivering engineering design work (specifications, calculations, designs, analysis, verification or assessment) to deliver fit for purpose engineering solutions that consider construction, commissioning and operational requirements, meeting LLWR’s company legal, regulatory and project requirements. The PEM will be the primary interface between Engineering and the RACER team.

Duties

Engineering delivery support to Project / Programme Managers for:

  • Leading the development of the engineering elements of Project baseline scope, schedule and budget
  • Ensuring the design engineering aspects of RACER projects and tasks are delivered and controlled efficiently and effectively to meet agreed specification, cost, and schedule
  • Ensuring design solutions deliver the business case, functional specification, and execution plan
  • Ensuring engineering solutions meet agreed performance requirements and deliverables define and preserve the design intent
  • Ensuring status and performance of engineering work is accurately and timely reported and corrective/recovery actions are implemented *Ensuring all necessary documentation is generated to meet safety and legal requirements that enable receipt on nuclear licensed site, installation, commissioning and taking into service of plant and equipment
  • Establishing design delivery strategies for RACER projects and tasks

Engineering delivery and Intelligent Customer support to Lead Project Engineering Manager:

  • Ensuring appropriate and adequate design standards and practices are used on RACER projects and tasks and deliverables and solutions generated are safe, legal and adequately underpinned
  • Ensuring periodic forecasting of resources is accurate and timely
  • Ensuring subject matter experts are appropriately and adequately engaged in a timely manner and Learning from Experience is captured, disseminated, and utilised
  • Supporting the development of effective design strategies and plans in line with the overall function and RACER strategies and plans
  • Specifying the level of verification and validation necessary to underpin design solutions that meet the safety, engineering and operational needs (including reviews)
  • Identification and management of engineering and technical risks that affect delivery of the projects and tasks and the longer-term performance of the engineering in service through to end of life
  • Serve as Intelligent Customer for the specification and acquisition of bought-in engineering services. Specifying, overseeing and reviewing engineering work performed by supply chain contractors - including ensuring that the supply chain is suitably qualified and experienced.
  • Ensuring clarity and accuracy of functional and technical specifications and scopes of work provided to supply chain organisations
  • Inputting to and reviewing work packaging, tender selection, and tender submissions for procurement of engineering services
  • Establishing engineering work approval strategies within the project and task scope
  • Accepting engineering work performed by supply chain organisations on behalf of RACER
  • Ensuring awareness of the Environmental Safety Case (ESC) taking account of the associated implications for engineering and maintenance activities
  • Reinforcing the importance of environmental requirements and the environmental nature of the Nuclear Waste Services site and incorporating them in the delivery of engineering project work

Essential Skills/Qualifications

  • Engineering graduate (min 2:2) from an accredited institution in a relevant discipline or equivalent appropriate professional qualification
  • Relevant experience serving in a multi-discipline design team with responsibility for the delivery of design engineering packages including drawings, specifications, calculations, reports and engineering substantiation.
  • Experience of working to the CDM Regulations as designer or Principal Designer
  • Experience of working in project teams during multiple project phases
  • Knowledge of project management principles
  • Experience of supporting project management activities with respect to design delivery activities
  • Ability to effectively manage supply chain contractors during design and execution phases
  • Ability to deliver high quality schedules and cost estimates
  • Good verbal and written communication skills
  • Knowledge of typical forms of design engineering contracts

Desirable Skills/Qualifications

  • Chartered Engineer
  • Experience of managing multi-discipline design teams
  • Experience of serving with an Intelligent Customer and Design Authority role
  • Certified by the Association of Project Managers (APM)
  • Knowledge / experience of NEC 3/NEC 4 contracts
  • Experience of delivering design engineering packages in a highly regulated environment
  • Experience of substantiating that design engineering packages meet regulatory requirements (e.g. nuclear or environmental safety case requirements)
