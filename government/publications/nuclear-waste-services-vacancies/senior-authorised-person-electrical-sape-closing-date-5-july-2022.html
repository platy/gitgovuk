Background Information

As of the 31 January 2022, Nuclear Waste Services has been set up to combine all of the NDA Waste activities, giving greater flexibility and driving improvements and synergies across the NDA Group. This brings together LLW Repository Limited, Radioactive Waste Management Limited and the NDA Integrated Waste Management Programme (each with their own IT teams, infrastructure, and systems).

Role Description

  • To act as Senior Authorised Person (electrical) (SAP(e)).

  • To act as process owner for RSG 1.15_02 (Repository Site Guidance - Safe Use of Generators on the LLWR Site) and RSP 1.15.04 (Repository Site Process - Portable Appliance Testing).

  • To act as intelligent customer for Control, Electrical & Instrumentation (C,E&I) Goods & Services.

  • To act as SME (Subject Matter Expert), DAP (Duty Authorised Person) secondary for electrical safety.

  • To provide support to the System Engineering capability and management of system health

Duties

  • Set, improve, and maintain standards and expectations on electrical safety.

  • Recommend, assess and appoint (where appropriate) Suitably Qualified and Experienced Personnel as Nominated Persons (electrical) (NP(e)) and Authorised Persons (electrical) (AP(e)).

  • Assist the Process Owner for Electrical Safety to maintain the electrical safety processes and procedures.

  • Audit electrical safety including:

    • Compliance with RSM 1.15, RSG 1.15_01 and the associated suite of RSPs/RSGs.

    • Required and implemented Examination, Inspection, Maintenance and Testing (EIM&T).

    • Compliance with electrical standards.

  • Provide professional advice, guidance, information, clarification coaching and mentoring on:

    • All aspects electrical safety including the Electricity at Work Regulations 1989, RSM 1.15, RSG 1.15_01 and the associated suite of RSPs/RSGs.

    • EIM&T and operation of electrical equipment and systems.

    • The safe use of generators as detailed in RSG 1.15_02.

  • Support maintenance activities by:

    • Assisting in the preparation of discipline specific work packages and determination of post-maintenance test requirements.

    • Assisting with the planning of complex maintenance activities based on insight and system experience.

  • Support the Asset Management Programme by:

    • Providing expert advice on ageing of Electrical Distribution Equipment.

    • Proactively support LLWR’s Asset Management Programme implementation including providing technical input to the production of Asset Management Plans and related programme outputs.

    • Providing System Engineers with the relevant data relating to electrical equipment and systems to allow them to produce system health reports and asset management plans.

  • Share learning from LLW Repository, the NDA estate and wider industry to drive best practice in the EIM&T, operations and safety of electrical equipment and systems incorporate learning from operating experience feedback (OEF).

  • Where required, liaise with regulators including the Office for Nuclear Regulation (ONR) for issues or events relating to electrical systems

Essential Skills/Qualifications

  • BEng with significant electrical engineering content (minimum), or equivalent

  • Strong general understanding of high voltage power networks and be experience of low voltage and high voltage environments (up to 11kV)

  • Practical understanding of electrical safety legislation

  • Practical understanding of safely operating low voltage and high voltage distribution systems (up to 11kV)

Desirable Skills/Qualifications

  • Chartered Engineer (or demonstrably equivalent qualifications and experience)

  • MEng with significant electrical engineering content or equivalent

  • Experience of Asset Management / System Engineering practices and processes

  • Proven experience working in a nuclear regulated environment
