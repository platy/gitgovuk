Background Information

As of the 31 January 2022, Nuclear Waste Services has been set up to combine all of the NDA Waste activities, giving greater flexibility and driving improvements and synergies across the NDA Group. This brings together LLW Repository Limited, Radioactive Waste Management Limited and the NDA Integrated Waste Management Programme (each with their own IT teams, infrastructure, and systems).

Role Description

To provide Health and Safety support to the LLWR (including construction projects and contractors carrying out any work on behalf of the LLWR), so the LLWR business remains compliant with LLWR policy, statutory and Site Licence requirements

Duties

  • To ensure that effective and quality advice is provided to the LLWR (including contractors) in all health and safety matters. To be aware of and abide by the company’s Standards and Expectations and to challenge others where it is noted that these Standards and Expectations are not being upheld.
  • To provide advice on compliance with all aspects of health and safety Legislation
  • To provide support to the LLWR management system, ensuring that any documents ‘owned’ are reviewed and amended as required and in line with the timeframes stipulated.
  • To take an active role in the assurance and audit activities required as part of the overall assurance framework for the LLWR.
  • To lead by example on the Standards and Expectations in safety behaviour, ensuring that high standards of safety behaviour are achieved at all levels of the organisation, through promotion, motivation and encouragement of others to adopt positive safety behaviours to prevent human error.
  • To monitor safety performance, identify shortcomings and take an active role in driving safety improvement activities including the production and analysis of project safety metrics.
  • To keep up to date with developments in H&S legislation and H&S good practice and advise the relevant Process Owner(s) with respect to the incorporation into LLWR procedures and working practices as appropriate.
  • To support the planned work programmes of the respective areas/projects and ensure timely preparation of documents and other required information/advice.
  • To coordinate and consult with Contractor and Sub-Contractor Safety Advisors on health and safety matters
  • To liaise with Safety Representatives on matters of health and safety that are relevant to the workforce and to encourage the involvement of the Safety Representatives in the development of safety improvements and initiatives, inspections and investigations and all other issues pertinent to their role and responsibilities.

Essential Skills/Qualifications

  • A minimum of NEBOSH Diploma
  • Relevant experience working as a Safety Advisor
  • A sound understanding of Construction activities
  • Be able to show previous experience of producing PUWER Risk Assessments
  • Previous experience of producing COSHH Assessments;
  • Be able demonstrate a sound understanding of Confined Space work and provide evidence of advice given to support work.

Desirable Skills/Qualifications

  • Hold P405 Management of asbestos in buildings
  • NEBOSH Fire Certificate
