Background Information

As of the 31 January 2022, Nuclear Waste Services has been set up to combine all of the NDA Waste activities, giving greater flexibility and driving improvements and synergies across the NDA Group. This brings together LLW Repository Limited, Radioactive Waste Management Limited and the NDA Integrated Waste Management Programme (each with their own IT teams, infrastructure, and systems).

Role Description

The Commercial and Contract Management Delivery team are responsible for the commercial management of several frameworks and contracts within LLW Repository Ltd (LLWR).

As a result of secondments and internal/external moves, there is a requirement for additional personnel to manage the recently placed Facilities Management Contract.

NWS believe that this would be best suited via the provision of a Quantity Surveyor to support the LLWR Commercial Delivery Manager.

The role will be primarily the deliverance of the Mitie Contract by managing monthly applications, change requests, pain/gain. Scope gap analysis, day to day updating and maintaining portfolio of contract administration spreadsheets to ensure the supplier is performing to contract requirements and target cost.

Duties

As Quantity Surveyor your duties will include, but not be limited to, the following:

  • The place of work would usually be the LLW Repository Site, or potentially at Pelham House at times

  • The Consultant will be expected to engage with all departments at Nuclear Waste Services, depending on the goods, services or works

  • Maintain an awareness of supplier’s capability and financial stability in order to support the needs of the business

  • Have a knowledge base of contract terms and conditions (NEC/CCS) to ensure that contract management can be delivered effectively

  • Work collaboratively with suppliers to implement best practice and innovation

  • Ensure all post contract documentation is documented and recorded

  • Manage change in accordance with the contract

  • Estimate work packages in advance of formal issue through the contract

  • Actively manage contract risk

  • Actively manage supplier key performance indicators

  • Day to Day updating and maintaining portfolio of contract administration spreadsheets

  • Management of the payment application / invoicing process for the assigned contracts

  • Review and approval of contractor quotations

  • Provide monthly/quarterly reporting data as required

Essential Skills/Qualifications

  • MCIPS or RICS Chartered Surveyor

  • Proven ability to develop and communicate commercial strategy, as well as overseeing its implementation

  • Defining contract strategies and structures using NEC/CCS suite of contracts

  • Working knowledge of the Public Contract Regulations

  • Excellent collaborative communication skills (written and oral)

  • Proactive and self-motivated

  • Ability to deal with people across a wide range of management and technical expertise.

Desirable Skills/Qualifications

  • Complex service contract mobilisation & management

  • A full UK driving licence
