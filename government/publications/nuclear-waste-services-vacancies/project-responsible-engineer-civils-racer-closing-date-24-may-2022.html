Background Information

As of the 31 January 2022, Nuclear Waste Services has been set up to combine all of the NDA Waste activities, giving greater flexibility and driving improvements and synergies across the NDA Group. This brings together LLW Repository Limited, Radioactive Waste Management Limited and the NDA Integrated Waste Management Programme (each with their own IT teams, infrastructure, and systems).

Role Description

Nuclear Waste Services understands performance of the site infrastructure is key to the safety and success of the facility. The Repository Asset Care Enhancement and Remediation Programme (RACER) is the Nuclear Waste Services programme to maintain, improve and renovate infrastructure for the continued contribution to the safe and efficient management of the site.

To lead delivery of civil engineering work (specification, calculation, analysis, design, verification or assessment) for the Repository Asset Care Enhancement and Remediation Programme (RACER) on behalf of the Programme Manager and the Head of Engineering. To support commissioning engineering activities as required.

Duties

  • Act as CS&A lead for the Repository Infrastructure Programme
  • Day-to-day management of all aspects of CS&A disciplined design work, production, selection, checking, development and delivery of optimised fit for purpose engineering and design solutions balancing aspects of safety and environmental performance, operational effectiveness and constructability.
  • Provide support to the CS&A Responsible Engineer and Nuclear Waste Services Design Authority as required. Providing the specification of discipline engineering design requirements; ensuring clarity and accuracy of functional and technical specifications and scopes of work provided to supply chain organisations.
  • Serve as the Project Responsible Engineer, having formal delegated authority as Design Authority for infrastructure projects and tasks.
  • Generating, contributing, and managing the generation of design deliverables including specifications, calculations, technical reports, drawings and 3-D models etc.
  • Ensure that all civil engineering related contractors are suitability qualified and experienced (SQEP) in producing engineering deliverables.
  • Ownership of project-related CS&A engineering assurance activities
  • Inputting to and reviewing work packages, tender selection, and tender submissions for procurement of discipline related engineering services.
  • Identification and management of design, engineering and technical risks that affect delivery of tasks and projects.
  • Management of discipline related drawings/models and drawing/model quality; supporting the development and maintenance of configuration baselines and design bases within RIP
  • Carrying out plant inspections as required.
  • Ensuring all work is carried out in accordance with the Engineering Design Manual and supporting management system documents.
  • Identifying appropriate engineering standards and practices to be used for the delivery of engineering work.
  • Maintaining technical competence and currency with respect to modern standards and methodologies relevant to civil engineering.
  • Participation in or facilitation of engineering study meetings, design reviews, HAZOPs, HAZCONs, Design Authority Technical Reviews, Document Review Committees, as required.
  • Act as Design Review Chair as required
  • Attend team meetings and provide regular updates on work delivered/supported to the Project Engineering Manager, and weekly updates at Programme schedule/programme reviews.

Essential Skills/Qualifications

  • Engineering graduate (min 2:2) from an accredited institution in a relevant civil engineering discipline or equivalent appropriate professional qualification.
  • Relevant experience in an engineering leadership role, managing staff and contractors.
  • Relevant experience serving in an engineering or design capacity with responsibility for the production, checking and approval of design and engineering deliverables including drawings, specifications, calculations, and reports.
  • Accredited by an appropriate professional institution (i.e., Incorporated/Chartered Engineer) or capable of achieving Chartership and working toward that objective.
  • Broad knowledge of industry consensus standards, processes, procedures and methods relevant to the civil engineering areas with a full understanding of underlying principles.
  • Proven ability to manage and prioritise own and team workload against multiple stakeholders and competing priorities.
  • Can demonstrate having the ability to engage and impact on others and work effectively as part of a wider team.
  • Ability to ensure minimum standards and fundamental process requirements are maintained when under pressure to deliver and ability to challenge others at all levels of the organisation.
  • Excellent verbal and written communication and presentation skills.
  • Proven experience in the production of high-quality technical reports, specifications and engineering documentation.

Desirable Skills/Qualifications

  • Experience in general project management areas, work planning and schedule/programme management.
  • Experience and training in the commissioning of systems and equipment and all safety and plant isolation aspects of commissioning.
