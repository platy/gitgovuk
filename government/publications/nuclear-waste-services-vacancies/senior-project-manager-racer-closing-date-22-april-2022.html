Background Information

As of the 31 January 2022, Nuclear Waste Services has been set up to combine all of the NDA Waste activities, giving greater flexibility and driving improvements and synergies across the NDA Group. This brings together LLW Repository Limited, Radioactive Waste Management Limited and the NDA Integrated Waste Management Programme (each with their own IT teams, infrastructure, and systems).

Role Description

The Project, Programme and Portfolio Management (PPM) capability at Nuclear Waste Services is looking to create an exciting new role to join the Repository Asset Care Enhancement and Remediation (RACER) Programme in the capacity of a Senior Project Manager. The role is to be the Senior Project Manager responsible for their own Tasks and Projects as well as the Team that delivers Tasks and Projects within the However, other Tasks and Projects maybe assigned at the discretion of the RACER Programme Manager.

RACER ensures the Nuclear Waste Service site assets are renewed, refurbished, upgraded and improved where appropriate. Working closely with the Sites main stakeholders the Programme delivers Tasks and Projects ranging from £50k to £25 million and above. The Tasks and Projects could cover a wide range of complexity and can include study work through to significant design packages and construction of new plant or building.

Typical annual Spend is estimated at £5-7 million per annum (including internal costs), however this will vary depending on the pipeline of tasks and projects. The Programme is in scope until closure of the site in 2130. The role will be to have overall day to day responsibility and accountability for the work and the RACER Project Team (mixture of Project Managers, Sub Project Managers and other Supply Chain staff) to ensure delivery in accordance with the Life Time Plan (LTP).

The role will oversee the full project lifecycle including initiation, definition, planning, construction, commissioning and handover. The successful candidate will report to the Programme Manager who reports directly to the Site Director and be responsible for managing a multi-discipline team assigned to the individual Tasks and Projects identified above within the Programme. The role will ensure the appropriate level on interaction with other Senior Project Managers to ensure business continuity across Nuclear Waste Services as a whole.

The successful candidate will have an opportunity to become a nominated deputy for the RACER Programme Manager. Projects will be delivered and expedited in accordance with Site’s Project Management Procedures which includes production of Business Cases, Life Time Plan Scope and Detailed Volumes, Project Execution Plans, Quality Plans, Risk Registers, Sourcing strategies, etc. The role will also involve a significant amount of sub-contract management though Nuclear Waste Services Repository Infrastructure Framework (RIF) using the site’s process and procedures for execution with certainty.

Duties

Requirements of the Role:

Safety:

  • Involvement in the preparation of a comprehensive ES&H Plan.
  • Ensures that the Plans are proactively monitored, and requirements are enforced.
  • Conduct regular Safety walkdowns and lead on maintaining a strong safety culture in the team.

Leadership:

  • Lead and coach the delivery team outlining a clear understanding of the roles, responsibilities, authority and accountabilities, ensuring that the company practices are implemented and complied with.
  • Control scope and meet the specified project functional criteria.
  • Establish escalation routes.
  • Maximise the use of Learning from Experience and disseminating new LFE to the relevant functions
  • Ability to demonstrate strong interpersonal skills (e.g. conflict resolution, team builder).
  • A clear understanding of the roles, responsibilities, authority and accountabilities of the team.
  • To draw upon the Company’s resources in order to successfully find and implement solutions to project execution issues.

Project Risk:

  • Develop, maintain and communicate the “Management of Project Risk Plan” as a live document.
  • Develop and manage the project contingency (budget and time).
  • Seek and implement opportunities. Communication Skills:
  • Ability to demonstrate consistent, effective and efficient written and oral communication skills at the project level (client, internal resources, suppliers, subcontractors, consultants, etc.).
  • To maintain consistent communication with senior management on key project issues, potential client issues and approach to resolutions.
  • Ability to demonstrate a highly professional, competent and persuasive presentation style.

Quality:

  • To ensure quality standards are met in compliance with health, safety, security and environmental codes and standards (e.g. LLWR Project Execution Procedures and RSPs,
  • ISO/ASQ certification, and OSHA requirements).

Execution Planning:

  • To have an understanding and ability to execute the project, employing the Project Execution Plan developed in the initiation phase and expands the Project Execution Plan for subsequent phases (Project Initiation, Project Execution, Construction, etc.) and communicate and coordinate the implementation of the enhancements with the Project team.

Schedule Performance:

  • To ensures that a firm scope-based schedule baseline is established and resourced.
  • To demonstrate command of the schedule goals, key milestones, and approach to the work embodied in the schedule.
  • To manage performance and progress against the plan and ensures accurate forecasts with commensurate action plans.

Financial Performance:

  • To demonstrate strong scope control skills.
  • To manage performance and progress against the plan and ensures accurate forecasts with commensurate action plans for cost, schedule, and resource utilisation.
  • To establish annual operating budgets in cooperation with customer, Business Manager or financial department.

Commercial, Contracts and Supply:

  • To lead the preparation of contract strategies working closely with the Commercial teams.
  • To monitor Supply Chain schedule performance ensuring contract compliance and delivery of Lifetime Quality Records.
  • An Understanding of the scope of work, technical requirements, and commercial terms of the project contract for their area of responsibility.
  • To monitor and ensure contract compliance with the supply chain.

Stakeholder Engagement and Communication:

  • Ability to demonstrate consistent, effective and efficient written and oral communication skills at the project level (client, internal resources, suppliers, contractors, consultants, etc.) including the production and maintaining progress dashboards.
  • To build client confidence and nurture positive client relationships.
  • To maintain consistent communication with senior management and regulators on project progress.
  • Ability to demonstrate a highly professional, competent, and persuasive presentation style.

Essential Skills/Qualifications

  • Accredited Project Manager certification (APM Project Management Qualification) or relevant experience in a collaborative project delivery environment.
  • Experience of initiating, developing, and leading an Engineering biased collaborative team consisting of direct reports, various internal support functions and Supply Chain resources.
  • Exhibits demonstrable qualities in providing vision, motivation and inspiration, setting high standards and expectations, leading by example and holding the Project Team to account.
  • Experience in working within relevant project delivery environments e.g. engineering, construction, or technical biased projects.

Desirable Skills/Qualifications

  • Graduate in a relevant discipline with relevant experience, of which have been in the nuclear industry or other highly regulated industry.
  • Has a record of proven performance as a Project/Delivery Manager on previous Projects and demonstrable evidence of the management of Task and Project change.
  • Already holds the role of Senior Project Manager.
  • Knowledge in expediting NEC 3/4 forms of Contract.

Supervises

Line management responsibilities for 2-3 Project Managers who are in turn responsible for 1-2 Sub Project Managers plus Supply Chain Framework Staff and possible day to day mentoring and management of 2 x Apprentice Project Managers
