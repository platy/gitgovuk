Background Information

As of the 31 January 2022, Nuclear Waste Services has been set up to combine all of the NDA Waste activities, giving greater flexibility and driving improvements and synergies across the NDA Group. This brings together LLW Repository Limited, Radioactive Waste Management Limited and the NDA Integrated Waste Management Programme (each with their own IT teams, infrastructure, and systems).

We are seeking to deliver a highly capable information security service to Nuclear Waste Services, encouraging collaborative working and the use of new technologies; there’s opportunity to do things differently, to improve our business and to show cyber security as a critical business enabling function. Our vision is to put partnership first, encourage new thinking and work to remove barriers and creating cyber communities, with the ambition to deliver solutions hand in hand with our stakeholders, that meet business and security needs.

The existing cyber security team is a small, high performing, dynamic team enabling our business to transform, grow and adopt new technologies securely, within a highly regulated environment. The role is broad, with opportunities to develop into specialisms as Nuclear Waste Services grows and matures.

Role Description

The Information Security Officer will develop and strengthen relationships within the business by providing timely, technical and procedural advice throughout the system and information asset lifecycles, from concept, operations, archival and through to deletion. Ensuring that key technical partners and business stakeholders are well informed and professionally guided through technical processes and assessments.

The role holder is expected to lead in one of the following portfolios of work: Security architecture and engineering – The portfolio holder ensures that security is by design and by default, developing secure system engineering practices and advising on secure architectures that provide a layered approach to defence.

Security and risk management – The portfolio holder completes the information risk assessment process by measuring high and low level designs (IT OT and SaaS) against established risk methodologies, advising and working with the system owner in order to close out work improvement plans to the point of Authority to Operate.

Security assurance, testing, compliance and monitoring – The portfolio holder plans and implements the information assurance schedule, using a variety of technical tools and audit techniques, measuring compliance against implemented high and low level designs, ISMS and industry standards.

Business relationships (Capability, Culture & Comms, Supply Chain) – The portfolio holder is key to maintaining and building positive relationships thought the business, our key delivery partners the Digital and IT teams to create a more collaborative approach to deliver and security assurance, Commercial and Procurement teams, Physical Security Team, Project Offices and our Supply Chain.

Cyber resilience – The portfolio holder will be responsible for advising and assisting the business-on-business continuity planning, disaster recovery, cyber exercising as well as being the focal point for security event management and investigation.

Duties

  • The role is multifaceted and will see the holder contribute to all elements of cyber and information security:
  • Assist with the production and implementation of the Cyber Security Strategy and Cyber Security and Information Assurance Plan.
  • Lead on innovation and engage with business stakeholders to ensure security is built into the design stages of projects.
  • Work with Digital and IT teams through delivery of technology and data programmes providing secure architecture advice and assurance throughout design, implementation and testing.
  • Create, review, monitor and evaluate documents within the Information Security Management System (ISMS) ensuring that it is fit for purpose.
  • To assist in the development of robust business continuity plans and cyber incident response plans.
  • Conduct cyber and information risk assessments on new IT and OT (Operational Technology) projects to ensure that information risks are minimised to an acceptable level
  • Take the lead and/or assist to Initiate investigations into information security incidents and factor lessons learnt from incidents into the Information security policies and processes.
  • Promote an information security aware culture across all levels of the business using a variety of methods.
  • Represent the business with delegated authority at internal and external working groups.
  • To deputise the CISO role when the CISO is not around.

Essential Skills/Qualifications

  • Degree (min 2.2) in a relevant discipline or, demonstrable relevant experience in the cyber security industry and 3 relevant industry certifications such as:

  • Certificate in Information Security Principles (CISMP)
  • ISO 27001 Lead Auditor or Implementor
  • Certified Incident Handler
  • Certified Network Defender
  • Certified Ethical Hacker
  • Cloud Security Essentials (SANS or equivalent)
  • Public Cloud Security (SANS or equivalent)
  • Certified Security Risk Manager (CSRM) or Certificate in Risk and Information Control
  • ICS/SCADA Security Essentials (SANS or equivalent)
  • Equivalents or other relevant certifications will be considered.
  • Good level of knowledge of procedures and methods relevant to the field together with a full understanding of underlying principles.
  • Good written and verbal communication skills.

And

  • Understanding of management frameworks directly relevant to Information Security such as ISO 27001, HMG Security Policy Framework, Government Security Standard 007, NIST 800-53
  • Awareness of security legislation and regulatory objectives and requirements relevant to Information Security e.g., Data Protection Act, Computer Misuse Act and Freedom of Information Act, Nuclear Industry Security Regulations, ONR Security Assessment Principles.
  • Understanding of key concepts such as threats, threat actors, vulnerabilities, likelihood, business impacts, aggregation, information assets, asset values, risk appetite, risk tolerance etc.

Desirable Skills/Qualifications

  • Professional qualification in the following:
  • Certified Information Security Security Professional (CISSP)
  • CISM (Certified Information Security Manager)
  • Nuclear industry cyber security experience
  • Operation Technology in a regulated industry experience
