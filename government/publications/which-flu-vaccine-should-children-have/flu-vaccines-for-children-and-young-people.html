Flu vaccines

There are 3 types of flu vaccine available for children in 2022 to 2023:

  1. Live attenuated influenza vaccine (LAIV) nasal spray suspension.

  2. Injected inactivated Quadrivalent Influenza Vaccine, egg-grown (QIVe).

  3. Injected inactivated Cell-based Quadrivalent Influenza Vaccine (QIVc).

Vaccine type by age group is shown below.

Under 6 months of age

Children under 6 months of age are too young to have the flu vaccine. This is why it is important that expectant mothers have a flu vaccination; pregnant women can have the flu vaccine at any stage of their pregnancy.

Aged 6 months to under 2 years of age [footnote 1]

Question 1: Are they in a clinical risk group?

If no - they are not eligible for the flu vaccine.

If yes - they should have the injected inactivated QIVe. For egg allergic children offer QIVc ‘off-label’. Children in this age group who have never had a flu vaccination will need 2 doses 4 weeks apart.

Aged from 2 years [footnote 1] to less than 18 years of age in eligible year groups [footnote 2] or in a clinical risk group

Question 2: Is the LAIV nasal spray contraindicated or otherwise unsuitable? [footnote 3]

If no contraindications to LAIV nasal spray they should have the LAIV nasal spray vaccine. Children aged up to 9 years in a clinical risk group who have never had a flu vaccination will need 2 doses 4 weeks apart.

If LAIV contraindicated or otherwise unsuitable [footnote 3] they should have the injected QIVc. Children less than 9 years of age in a clinical risk group who have never had a flu vaccination will need 2 doses 4 weeks apart.
