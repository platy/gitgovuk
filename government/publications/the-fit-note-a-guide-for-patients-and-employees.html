Details

This guide explains to patients and employees what different sections of the fit note mean and how they can use it to talk with their healthcare professional and employer about returning to work.

Published 1 March 2013
Last updated 1 July 2022 + show all updates
  1. 1 July 2022

    Fit notes can now be certified by doctors, nurses, occupational therapists, pharmacists and physiotherapists.

  2. 6 April 2022

    Updated guidance to introduce a new version of the fit note.

  3. 27 January 2022

    The temporary change to the rules on when you need to provide a fit note has ended. From 27 January 2022 you will need to provide a fit note after 7 days of sickness to claim benefits or claim Statutory Sick Pay from your employer.

  4. 17 December 2021

    Added a message about not having to provide fit notes (sick notes) until 27 January 2022 to give GPs more time to work on the coronavirus vaccination boosters.

  5. 16 September 2021

    Removed mentions of the Fit for Work service, which has now closed. Updated the sample Statement of Fitness for Work document.

  6. 14 December 2016

    Added this note to the page – A new version of the fit note is now available but old ones can still be used. The advisory notes now include information about Fit for Work and the collection of anonymous fit note data. We will revise the guidance to reflect the changes.

  7. 29 September 2015

    Published updated guidance.

  8. 27 August 2015

    We've removed the guidance while we update it.

  9. 30 January 2015

    Updated English version to include information about the new Fit for Work service.

  10. 1 March 2013

    First published.
