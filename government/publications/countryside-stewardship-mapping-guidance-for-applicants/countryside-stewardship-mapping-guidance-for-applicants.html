Introduction

Before you apply for Countryside Stewardship (CS) 2022, you should check all land parcels you intend to include on your CS application. If you need to make changes to your land parcels, it is important that you do this as soon as possible so that the most up to date information can be included on your application.

Before applying you should also read:

  • Guidance on agri-environment agreements (CS and ES) and Basic Payment Scheme (BPS) (which is mainly focused on existing agreement but includes information about land cover and non-agricultural land which is relevant for all applicants)
  • Rural Land Entitlement (RLE1) guidance (2022 version)
  • Countryside Stewardship: How to apply
  • Guidance on woodland grant schemes and BPS: operations note 42

What to check on your digital maps before applying

You should check all land parcels you intend to include on your CS application on your digital maps in the Rural Payments service. To do this:

  1. Go to the Rural Payments Service
  2. From the ‘Business overview’ screen, click ‘View land’ link
  3. To view individual land parcel details, select the land parcel you need. The ‘Parcel details’ screen shows you a digital map and some land parcel details, for example land cover and total area.

You need to make sure that:

  • all the land parcels being included in your CS application are registered and linked to your Single Business Identifier (SBI). This means the land parcels need to show on your digital maps in the Rural Payments service. All agricultural and non-agricultural land parcels which are 0.0100 hectares or above must be registered and linked to your holding (SBI). Non-agricultural land covers such as buildings and farmyards on which you want to apply for management options or capital items can be mapped as non-agricultural land covers within larger parcels. For example:
    • Options HS1 or HS8 for maintenance or weatherproof traditional farm buildings
    • any capital works in a farmyard, such as those for water and air quality improvements.
  • the permanent land parcel boundaries are shown correctly. This includes the following:
    • Hedges you’re intending to use for options BE3 (management of hedgerows)
    • BN5 (hedgerow laying)
    • BN6 (hedgerow coppicing) in your CS application.

You can view permanent land parcel boundaries on your digital maps in the Rural Payments service. Tick ‘Hedges’ on the relevant map on ‘View land’. For more information read the RLE1 guidance.

  • the ‘land cover’ is shown correctly. Agricultural and non-agricultural land must have an appropriate land cover.

Note: land cover is not the same as land use. We classify land by identifying the physical nature of the land. These categories are referred to as land covers. We then look at the activities the land is used for, these sub-categories are referred to as land uses:

Land cover categories Examples of land use
Arable land Temporary grassland, fallow land, winter wheat, spring barley
Permanent grassland Permanent grassland
Permanent crops Permanent crops
Non-agricultural area Woodland, farm building, ungrazeable scrub, reedbed

We only map the land cover, not the land use. For a CS application being submitted in 2022, the land cover shown on your digital maps must reflect the land use declared for that land parcel as of 16 May 2022 (the year before the CS agreement starts).

For more information read CS applications and land uses below.

The management required under some CS options means the land becomes non-agricultural. The land cover for non-agricultural land that is managed (or intended to be managed) under an agri-environment scheme needs to show correctly on your digital maps in the Rural Payments service.

For more information read Registering or updating land cover below.

How to update your digital maps

Linking a registered land parcel to your SBI

If a land parcel is already registered in our Rural Payment Service (Land Parcel Identification System (LPIS)) but is not shown as linked to your SBI, you may need to:

  • Ask the previous occupier to transfer it to you. This can be done online using the Rural Payments service if the land parcel shows as 100% owned. If the Holding type shows as ‘Tenant’ or it’s less than 100% owned, it cannot be transferred online. The Change land tenure form can be used to update the land tenure if it’s incorrect. Once RPA have made this change it should then be possible to transfer that land online.
  • If it’s not possible to transfer the land online, you should submit an RLE1 form to us and ask that it’s added to your holding (SBI). The effective date for transfer must be when the land was legally transferred to you and must be before the CS agreement start date.
  • If you wish to maintain a dual link with the existing occupier, use the ‘Add land’ email route. This adds a link between the land parcel and your SBI, but it keeps any existing link between the land parcel and another SBI. To remove that existing link, an RLE1 form should be submitted by the existing occupier. For more information, read the RLE guidance

Registering a land parcel for the first time

If a land parcel is not already mapped in our Rural Payments service (LPIS), you will need to submit an RLE1 form and sketch map to request it is mapped for the first time. This may include land that is not eligible for the Basic Payment Scheme (BPS) such as farmyards.

Read the RLE1 guidance for more details, in particular ‘Part D: Register a land parcel to your holding’.

The ‘Effective date of change’ on the RLE1 form (column D5 in Part D) should be the date that you took legal responsibility for the land parcel. If the date you took responsibility was 1 January 2022 fill in the effective date of change as 31 December 2021 (the day before the agreement start date).

Updating permanent land parcel boundaries

To update a permanent land parcel boundary, including adding hedges particularly where you intend to use for BE3 (Management of hedgerows), BN5 (Hedgerow laying) or BN6 (Hedgerow coppicing), submit an RLE1 form and sketch map.

Read the RLE1 guidance for guidance on updating permanent land parcel boundaries and what to include on a sketch map.

Registering or updating ‘land cover’

To register or update the land cover, fill in an RLE1 form and provide a sketch map.

Read the RLE1 guidance for more details, in particular ‘Part D: Add a permanent non-agricultural area or feature’ or ‘Change land cover’.

Write ‘Land cover change’ at the top of your sketch map. Clearly label the land parcel with the new land cover (arable, permanent grassland, permanent crops or non-agricultural). If the land cover is non-agricultural, write the relevant land use code to identify the appropriate type of non-agricultural land cover for the land parcel(s). For example:

  1. WO12 (woodland)
  2. WO25 (ungrazeable scrub)
  3. IW05 (shingle)
  4. IW07 (bog)
  5. MW02 (tidal area)
  6. MW03 (reed bed)
  7. MW04 (intertidal habitat)
  8. MW05 (saline habitat)
  9. CF02 (vegetated shingle)
  10. CF03 (sand dunes)

For CS Higher Tier, Mid Tier or Wildlife Offer agreements with a start date of 1 January 2023, the ‘Effective date of change’ on the RLE1 form (column D5 in Part D) should be 31 December 2022 (the day before the agreement start date).

For CS Woodland Creation, if the agreement start date will be after the BPS application deadline (16 May in 2022), the ‘Effective date change’ on the RLE1 form should be 1 January 2023. If the agreement start date is before the BPS application deadline, the ‘Effective date of change’ should be the actual agreement start date.

CS applications and land use

When you include land parcels in a CS Higher Tier, Mid Tier or Wildlife Offer application that has been used to claim BPS in the same year, we will check that the CS management option applied for is compatible with the BPS land use declared for that land parcel. This should be the land use for that land parcel as of 16 May 2022. It is the ‘land use’ declared in the year you apply that we will check for your CS application, even though the CS agreement start date is 1 January 2023.

If the land use is changed after 16 May 2022, you do not need to change the land use declaration for your 2022 BPS application but you may need to change the land use for future BPS applications.

CS Wildlife Offers starting 1 January 2023 can only be located on agricultural land declared on the BPS 2022 application with an arable or permanent grassland use.

For a CS Higher Tier, Mid Tier or Wildlife Offer application being submitted in 2022 (with an agreement start date of 1 January 2023), the land cover shown on your digital maps must reflect the land use declared on the BPS 2022 application. This should be the land cover for that land parcel as of 16 May 2022 (the year before the CS agreement starts on 1 January 2023).

Where the land cover is changed after 16 May 2022, the land cover on your digital maps must also be changed by submitting an RLE1 form. If the same land is used to claim BPS payment in 2022, the ‘Effective date of change’ on the RLE1 form (column D5 in part D) should be 31 December 2022 (the day before the agreement start date).

When including land parcels in your CS application (for a 1 January 2023 start date), you should note that:

  • some CS grassland options can only be located on land declared with a land use of permanent grassland on the BPS 2022 application (with a corresponding ‘land cover’ shown on your digital maps)
  • you can usually only locate CS arable options on land declared with a land use of temporary grassland or other arable land use (matching the option requirements) on the BPS 2022 application (with the corresponding arable ‘land cover’ shown on your digital maps).

You may need to submit an RLE1 form and sketch map to request the ‘land cover’ is updated to correspond with the land use declared on the BPS 2022 application.

Completing the RLE1 form

The electronic RLE1 form can be completed and emailed to us at ruralpayments@defra.gov.uk. Please make sure that your RLE1 form and any associated sketch maps are sent in a separate email to any other documents you need to send us, using the subject heading ‘RLE1 Land Change [SBI]’.

Read the RLE1 guidance about completing the RLE1 form. For additional information read ‘How to use the electronic RLE1 form’.

Important: make sure you submit the RLE1 form to the RPA before you submit your CS application. Try to do this do this as early as possible, or at least one month before the CS application window closes. This will allow RPA to make the requested changes to your digital maps so your application will have the most up to date information.

Make sure you keep a copy of all completed/ submitted RLE1 forms and sketch maps for your records.
