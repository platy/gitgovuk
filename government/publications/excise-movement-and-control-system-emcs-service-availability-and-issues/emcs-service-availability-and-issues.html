End of EU Exit transition on 31 December 2020

As a result of EU Exit transition ending on 31 December 2020, there are some changes to how goods can move under Excise Duty Suspension and changes have been made to the EMCS system to reflect the changed situation.

You can find more information on:

  • importing excise goods to the UK from the EU
  • exporting excise goods to the EU
  • moving excise goods as freight under the Northern Ireland Protocol from 1 January 2021

The excise notices and other related guidance on GOV.UK have also been updated.

Service issues

EMCS rejected exports matching issue

This issue affects EMCS users who dispatch export movements under EMCS — since 9 February 2022, no export entry matching has taken place with eAD movements on EMCS.

EMCS issues export rejection messages 30 days after the eAD movement date of dispatch when no export matching has taken place.

EMCS will be issuing export rejections for all EMCS export movements with a date of dispatch of 9 February 2022 onwards until a fix can be implemented.

The evidence of export procedures in Notice 197, section 13.10, gives guidance on the evidence needed when emailing the EMCS Helpdesk to request the manual finalisation of these movements.

HMRC are urgently investigating the situation for a resolution and apologise for the inconvenience this will cause.

Northern Ireland traders exporting goods from Northern Ireland warehouses

When an XI-ARC is declared in Box 40 of the CHIEF export entry, the following error code message will be displayed: E3392 – ‘ARC Ref 3rd – 4th chars do not identify an EU Member State’

If you use an export agent, make them aware of the workaround below.

Enter the LRN (Local Reference Number) in Box 40 of the customs declaration as if the EMCS movement had been dispatched under the fallback procedure.

For example, Z (first field)/FAD (second field)/LRN (third field). The LRN Local Reference Number should be as submitted on the EMCS movement.

Then declare the EMCS XI-ARC number in Box 44 (the additional information field).

For example, ‘EMCS ARC number; 21XI00000000012345678’

Finally, in Box 49, declare the premises Excise Reference Number (ERN) in the GB-format, not XI.

If you use the XI ERN the following error code message will be displayed: E1799 – Premises authorising country must be within EU.

For example, XI00001234567 should be entered GB00001234567.

Note, as a result of this issue, CHIEF will not discharge the XI-ARC movement on EMCS. Therefore, once the consignment has been exported and the CHIEF Export Entry has reached departed status, submit evidence of export to the EMCS Helpdesk by emailing emcs.helpdesk@hmrc.gov.uk, to request the closure of the undischarged ARC, as per the existing procedures in Notice 197, Section (13.10).
