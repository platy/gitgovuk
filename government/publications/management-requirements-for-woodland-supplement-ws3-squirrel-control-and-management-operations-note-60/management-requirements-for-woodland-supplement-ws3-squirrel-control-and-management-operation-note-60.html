1. Purpose

This operations note is to be used to determine the appropriate works within Woodland Supplement WS3 – Squirrel Control and Management. WS3 is only eligible to be used in conjunction (on the same area) with Countryside Stewardship (CS) Woodland Improvement Option WD2 and will be used to support the increased effort to control grey squirrels. Effective use of supplement will increase resilience within the woodland, support and help protect biodiversity and structure of the site. Wider environmental benefits are likely to occur beyond the site boundary because of this option.

2. What you need to do

Guidance for each step of the process is provided for each of the mandatory requirements which are:

  • demonstrable increases or maintenance of acceptable levels of grey squirrel control from the start of the CS agreement
  • draft Squirrel Control Plan (SCP) to be submitted and agreed with the Woodland Officer (WO) at the time of application. Use the Squirrel Control Plan guidance.
  • full SCP agreed with the WO by the end of year 1
  • completion and submission of cull data annually to the Forestry Commission, that includes a record of squirrels culled, and time spent culling (effort)
  • an impact/activity survey to be completed and submitted to the Forestry Commission at the end of Year 1, 3 and 5. Use the Activity and Impact Methodology document for guidance

Applicant Supporting information

  • annual training and awareness days will be available in all FC areas to provide support with management plan writing and the completion of monitoring requirements. Your local Forestry Commission office will be able to provide details

3. Sources of further advice

Comprehensive guidance on controlling grey squirrels can be found on the Forest Research website.

Information on red squirrel conservation is available through the UK Squirrel Accord.

The Grants and Regulations website contains all the information you need to apply for grant support. Alternatively, you can request this information from your local Forestry Commission office.
