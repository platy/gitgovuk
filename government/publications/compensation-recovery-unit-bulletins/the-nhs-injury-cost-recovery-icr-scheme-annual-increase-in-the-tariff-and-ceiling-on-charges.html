1. The NHS Injury Cost Recovery (ICR) Scheme – Annual Increase in the Tariff and Ceiling on Charges

The CRU, on behalf of the Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC), currently administers the above scheme, which allows for the recovery of National Health Service (NHS) costs following injuries where people claim and receive personal injury compensation.

The tariff and ceiling on charges (cap) payable by Compensators for the recovery of NHS treatment and ambulance charges under the NHS ICR scheme will increase from 1 April 2018. The increases will apply only to injuries sustained on or after this date.

This uplift in the level of charges is an annual event to reflect Hospital and Community Health Services inflation.

Scotland will also lay regulations to increase the tariff and ceiling of charges in line with England and Wales both in terms of amounts and implementation date.

2. Jersey Motor Traffic Costs Recovery

Jersey Health and Social Services Division (Jersey HSSD) will also increase their tariff and ceiling of cap charges, in line with the DHSC NHS ICR scheme.
