This privacy notice covers:

  • the online service, previously available at www.gov.uk/coronavirus-shielding-support
  • the earlier version of the online service, available at www.gov.uk/coronavirus-extremely-vulnerable and the automated telephone service (both of which took registrations up to 17 July 2020)

About this service

The service to support people who are clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus (COVID-19) is now closed.

This means we no longer:

  • collect personal information through the service
  • receive personal information from the NHS
  • share personal information with other organisations that supported the shielding programme, for example local authorities and supermarkets

The Cabinet Office is the data controller for the information.

This page explains what personal information we collected before the service closed and how we’re protecting it.

Personal information

The NHS provided us with the following personal information about individuals who are clinically extremely vulnerable to coronavirus:

  • name
  • address
  • date of birth
  • phone numbers
  • NHS number
  • gender
  • the condition a patient had which made them eligible for shielding support
  • name of the individual’s GP practice

We collected the following information through the service:

  • name
  • address
  • date of birth
  • email address
  • contact details
  • NHS number
  • details of the support you needed (for example, care needs or help getting essential supplies)

Lawful basis for processing your personal information

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we are processing this information on the basis that we’re carrying out a task in the public interest.

Some of the information we hold is sensitive health and care information. This is classed as ‘special category’ personal data within the meaning of Schedule 1 to the Data Protection Act 2018. This means we must state a further lawful basis for processing it. We’re relying on substantial public interest and governmental purpose to process this special category personal data.

What we do with your information

The service is closed to new registrations, but we’ll continue to hold personal information we previously collected through registrations on the service.

How long we keep your information

We and the organisations we share your information with will only keep your personal information for as long as we need it to help with the overall government response to coronavirus.

We keep these arrangements under review to make sure they remain appropriate and proportionate.

Where your information is processed and stored

Your personal information may be transferred outside the UK while being processed. If this happens, we’ll make sure your information is given the same level of technical and legal protection as it has within the UK. For example, by making sure a party we transfer the information to is contractually required to provide the same level of protection.

How we protect your information

We’re committed to doing all that we can to keep your information secure. We have set up systems and processes to prevent unauthorised access to or disclosure of the data we collect about you. For example, we protect your data using varying levels of encryption.

All third parties that process personal information as part of delivering the service are required to keep that information secure. Our data processor is Amazon Web Services, who provide cloud hosting services.

NHS login

While the service was open for new registrations, users had the option of creating an NHS login to confirm their identity when they used the service online.

By creating and using an NHS login, you will have provided personal information to NHS Digital. NHS Digital is the data controller for any personal information you provide to create or access your NHS login. Our role is as a processor of this information - meaning that we act according to NHS Digital’s instructions when processing this information.

See the NHS login privacy notice for details.

Your rights

In certain circumstances you have the right to ask for your information to be deleted by contacting us. We may need to refuse your request if the information is needed as part of the emergency response to coronavirus.

You also have the right to request:

  • information about how your personal information is processed, and to request a copy of that personal data
  • that any inaccuracies in your personal information are rectified without delay
  • that any incomplete personal information is updated - you can include the missing information in your request
  • that the processing of your personal information is restricted in certain circumstances - for example, where accuracy is contested

You also have the right to object to the processing of your data.

If you want to make any of these requests, contact us using the details below.

Changes to this notice

We may change this privacy notice. When we make changes to this notice, the ‘last updated’ date at the top of this page will also change. Any changes to this privacy notice will apply to you and your information immediately. If these changes affect how your personal information is processed, the Cabinet Office will take reasonable steps to make sure you know.
