DE 1/8 Signature/ Authentication (Box 54: Place and Date, Signature and Name of the Declarant/ Representative)

Declaration Categories Field format No. of occurrences at header level No. of occurrences at item level
FSD an..35 1x NA

All Declaration Categories:

Where, as a result of declaration processing, it is determined that customs will accept an FSD without the need for the presentation of a paper copy, the user identity used to lodge the FSD with CDS must be declared in DE 3/18 and will replace the hand-written signature as the authentication credential for that declaration.

The CDS role identified by the user identity will determine the liability for all data transmitted in that FSD and acts as an electronic signature. The user identity used to transmit the FSD to CDS must be an Authorised Signatory as defined below

Individual liability will rest with the signatory within the company for the use of that credential (for instance, the user identity). If there is to be a change of person who will act as signatory, this must be notified immediately to HMRC in writing, whereupon its records will be updated.

Where the party declared in DE 3/18 is not the holder of the authorisation:

  • DE 3/20 must be completed with the EORI number of the holder of the authorisation.
  • DE 3/21 must be completed with code 2.

Notes:

Authorised Signatories:

The FSD must be signed, by means of CDS user credentials, according to the following rules. If the holder of the authorisation is:

  • An individual, by that individual or an employee who has been authorised in writing
  • A partnership, by one of the partners, or one of their employees authorised in writing by a partner
  • A company incorporated in the UK under the companies acts, by a director or the company secretary, or an employee of the company authorised in writing by a director or the company secretary
  • A corporation incorporated in the UK by statute, by a person authorised by law to sign for the corporation, or an employee of the corporation authorised in writing by such a person
  • A foreign firm or company, or a corporation incorporated abroad by statute, by a person authorised by the law of the country in which the firm or company, is established

Alternatively, the FSD may be signed on behalf of the holder of the authorisation by any firm, company or individual to whom they have given the authority to act as a declarant /representative for customs purposes. The manner of authorisation is a matter of arrangement between the holder of the authorisation and their declarant/ representative but, in giving authority to a declarant/ representative, the holder of the authorisation will be assumed to have given authority to the clerks and servants authorised by the agent to carry out all of their customs business. Customs may at any time require evidence that a declarant/ representative has been authorised by them to sign FSDs on their behalf.

DE 1/10 Procedure (Box 37: Procedure)

Declaration Categories Field format No. of occurrences at header level No. of occurrences at item level
FSD an4 Requested procedure code: an2 + Previous procedure code: an2 NA 1x

This data element is mandatory.

All Declaration Categories:

Enter the (4-digit) Procedure Code detailed in Appendix 1: DE 1/10: Requested and Previous Procedure Codes.

The use of a Procedure Code in this data element constitutes a formal declaration that the conditions of the relevant regulations (including but not restricted to):

  • Council Regulation (EU) 952/2013;
  • Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2015/2446;
  • Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2015/2447;
  • Taxation (Cross-Border Trade) Act 2018, Schedule 1.

Will be complied with and binds the legal declarant accordingly.

Representatives/Agents must have prior written approval from the legal declarant (holder of the authorisation) to enter goods to a specific procedure on their behalf and must ensure a copy of the FSD is returned to the holder of the procedure.

Notes:

The Procedure Code may only be declared with the specified Declaration Category, as defined in the table below.

Declaration Category Declaration Title Allowable Requested Procedure Codes
FSD Final Supplementary Declaration Reports 0090

DE 1/11 Additional Procedure Code (Box 37: Procedure)

Declaration Categories Field format No. of occurrences at header level No. of occurrences at item level
FSD an3 Union codes: a1 + an2 OR National codes: n1 + an2 NA 1x

This data element is mandatory.

All Declaration Categories:

Enter the (3 digit) Additional Procedure Code from Appendix 2: DE 1/11: Additional Procedure Codes, using the completion notes to help you complete the FSD.

The use of the Additional Procedure Code in this data element constitutes a formal declaration that the conditions of the relevant regulations (including but not restricted to):

  • Council Regulation (EU) No. 952/2013
  • Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) No. 2015/2446 and
  • Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No. 2015/2447
  • Taxation (Cross-Border Trade) Act 2018, Schedule 1.

Will be complied with and binds the legal declarant accordingly.

Representatives/ Agents must have prior written approval from the legal declarant (holder of the authorisation) to enter goods to a specific procedure on their behalf and must ensure a copy of the FSD is returned to the holder of the authorisation.
