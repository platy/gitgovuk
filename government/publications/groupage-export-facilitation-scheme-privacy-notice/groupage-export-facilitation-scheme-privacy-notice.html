How we will use your data, and your rights when applying on behalf of a company to join the groupage export facilitation scheme (GEFS).

Who is collecting your personal data

The data controller is the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra). See Defra’s Personal Information Charter.

You can contact Defra’s Data Protection Officer by email at: data.protection@defra.gov.uk.

Any questions about how we are using your personal data and your associated rights should be sent to Defra’s Data Protection Officer.

The Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA), a Defra executive agency, will have access to this data for the purposes of administration of the scheme.

What data we collect

The personal data we collect from you is your:

  • name and the company you represent
  • telephone number
  • email address

Why we need your data

You are applying for the company that you represent to join the GEFS. We need your contact details to process the application and administer your company’s membership of the scheme.

Our legal basis for processing your data

As you are voluntarily applying to join the GEFS, the legal basis for our processing your data in relation to the scheme is that you have consented for us to do so.

Can you withdraw consent

You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. You can do this by emailing the following information to: GEFSteam@apha.gov.uk

  • your name
  • name of the exporting company
  • GEFS membership number
  • reason for withdrawing your consent

What will happen if you opt out

If you ask for your application form to be deleted and membership of the scheme to cease, then your company will no longer be a part of the GEFS. However, if you change your name and contact details that you provided with your application, we can make this change and your company can remain part of the scheme.

What we do with your data

We will use your data in the following ways:

  • to verify eligibility for GEFS membership which provides certifying officers (COs) and competent authorities with a sufficient level of confidence in the accuracy of the support attestations used within the groupage guidance
  • compliance with the terms and conditions of the GEFS membership

The data we collect will be shared with APHA and may be shared with other government departments, agencies and public bodies such as Trading Standards, External Officials and Local Authorities to ensure compliance with the terms and conditions of the GEFS membership

We will not:

  • sell or rent your data to third parties
  • share your data with third parties for marketing purposes

We will share your data if we are required to do so by law - for example, by court order, or to prevent fraud or other crime.

How long do we keep your data

We will only keep your personal data for as long as:

  • you are the authorised signee for the exporting company
  • the GEFS is operational

Your rights

See a list of your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018)

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the ICO (the data protection supervisory authority) at any time. Should you wish to exercise that right full details are available on the ICO’s website.
