Assessment framework

The UK government will assess bids from across the UK against a common assessment framework.

Applications will be scored using this framework, and final decisions on funding will be made by the Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities (DLUHC).

Assessment criteria

Applications will be assessed on the following 4 criteria:

1. Strategic case: applicants should show that without intervention the asset would be lost to the community, the impacts that this would have and support they have from the community and other partners.

The strategic case will account for 30% of the total assessment.

2. Management case: applicants should show the objectives and deliverability of the project and how the asset will be run sustainably.

The management case will account for 30% of the total assessment.

3. Potential to deliver community benefit: applicants should show how the asset will deliver community benefits under community ownership.

The potential to deliver community benefit will account for 30% of the total assessment. 

4. Added value of the community asset based on community need: using local data and information on community need, applicants should be able to show the added value of the asset to the community.

The added value of the community asset based on community need will account for 10% of the total assessment.

Scoring and weighting

Each criterion has a minimum score that must be met to be considered for shortlisting. Failure to meet this score on any single section will mean your application is not shortlisted.

Additionally, the combined score of sub-criteria 2.1, 2.2 and 2.3 must total 12 or higher to be considered for shortlisting. This is to ensure your proposal has a viable and robust business case, financial management plan and the necessary skills to deliver.

To arrive at the overall score, the assessment scores for each criterion will be weighted according to the importance of that criteria. Total scores will be given as a percentage.

Table 1: scoring and weighting breakdown by assessment criteria

Assessment criteria Minimum score for shortlisting Maximum score available Weighting for overall assessment
Strategic case 11 18 30%
Management case 15 25 30%
Potential to deliver community benefit 9 15 30%
Added value of the community asset based on community need 2 5 10%

1. Strategic case

Total score and weighting

The total score available for this section is 18. The minimum score to be considered for shortlisting is 11. This will be weighted to account for 30% of the overall assessment.

1.1. Benefits

This will be scored out of 5.

Assessment and guidance

In order for your project to be eligible for funding from COF your asset must be at risk of loss, or already be lost, to the community. It is important that you set out a strong case for intervention. Your case for community intervention should include:

  • The current, or former, use of the asset and who benefits from it
  • How the loss of the asset will, or already has, affected the people in the community
  • Why the asset will be lost without community intervention

1.2. Engagement

This will be scored out of 103.

Assessment and guidance

It is important that you are able to show that the community have been involved in the formulation of your plans and there is strong support locally for them. You should be able to provide evidence of support you have for your plans to save the community asset.

This might include:

  • How you have engaged with the community and how this engagement has shaped your project plans
  • Describing any fundraising you have done in the community
  • How you have involved local people in the project

You should also be able to show you have built local partnerships around your plans, and the role your proposals could play within other local plans. If you are working closely with local businesses or in conjunction with the council it’s important to give us details.

This might include:

  • Describing whether your project plans fit into any wider local plans or partnerships and the role your project could play in these
  • Telling us about the support in the community for your project and how you gathered it

1.3. Environmental sustainability

We would like you to give details on how your project supports the journey to Net Zero and incorporate low or zero carbon approaches. We are also interested to hear if your project considers and mitigates its impact on the natural environment.

This question will be scored out of 3.

2. Management case

Total score and weighting

The total score available for this section is 25. The minimum score to be considered for shortlisting is 15. This will be weighted to account for 30% of the overall assessment.

You will need to submit a business plan, and any other supporting documents, when prompted at the end of your application. We will also ask for the key figures or details in this section, and where we can find this in your business plan. The business plan will be the key document we use to assess the management case.

Additionally, the combined score of 2.1, 2.2 and 2.3 must total 12 or higher to be considered for shortlisting. This is to ensure your proposal has a viable and robust business plan and financial management plan.

2.1. Funding breakdown

This will be scored out of 5.

Assessment and guidance

You will need to include an overview of funding information relative to your project. We want to fully understand the total costs of your project, what the COF funding will be used for, what your match funding will be used for, what your sources of match are and when you expect to have this match funding available.

You will be expected to provide a breakdown of the following in your business plan:

  • Project costs
  • Capital costs
  • Revenue costs
  • Total request for funding (capital + revenue costs)
  • Match funding details
  • Funding you have already secured
  • Funding you still need to secure
  • Timescales for securing the outstanding match funding
  • Independent valuation of the asset (if you intend to buy it)

The Community Ownership Fund will provide up to a maximum of 50% of the capital costs to purchase, (and renovate and or repair) the community asset. You should demonstrate other sources of funding that you have secured or will be securing for the capital costs of your project.

Funding from the Community Ownership Fund may be committed ‘in principle,’ before you have secured other grants or loans. Applicants are reminded that as part of a Grant Funding Agreement a legal charge against the asset may be required. However, we will need evidence that you will be able to secure all match funding for your community asset and drawn down capital within 12 months of the date indicated on your offer letter. We expect that most applicants will have made significant progress on this front within six months.

Applicants should note that any costs incurred before the date of the offer letter provided to successful projects will not be considered as eligible match funding.

2.2. Financial and risk forecasts

This will be scored out of 10.

Assessment and guidance

This section is about demonstrating the viability of your project and business. We want to be able to see that you have extensive financial plans to ensure that your project is sustainable in the long term and that you have considered some of the problems you may face and have strategies to overcome them.

The financial and management forecasts in your business plan should include:

  • Completed or planned feasibility studies
  • Planned activities or services that will take place in the community asset
  • Financial forecasts, including - Income sources, costs with an evidenced set of assumptions, and explanation of the use and need of revenue funding and cashflow.
  • Planned skills or resources needed to manage the asset
  • Risk and mitigations considered

2.3. Skills and resources

This will be scored out of 5.

Assessment and guidance

This is about demonstrating capabilities to deliver your project and manage the asset in the long-term. We want to see that you have a board and management structure that is able to ensure your project is successful.

The skills and resources information in your business plan should include:

  • Relevant experience of delivering projects similar to the one you are seeking funding for
  • Any relevant project management expertise you, or others in your organisation, may have
  • Roles you plan to recruit
  • Governance and membership structures of your organisation
  • Members of your board, including their role and main responsibilities

2.4. Representation, inclusiveness and integration

This will be scored out of 5.

Assessment and guidance

It is important that you are able to show us that you answer to your local community and that you have plans to ensure the asset is inclusive and accessible. You should demonstrate you are accountable to the community you represent.

You can do this by:

  • Explaining how you will involve the community in the running of the asset
  • Including information on how the community will be involved in decision making and how you will measure public support for your decisions
  • Describing anything that might prevent people from using the asset or participating in its running
  • Tell us how you will try to make your project accessible and inclusive to everyone in the community
  • Describing how your project will bring people together from all over the community

It is important that you explain how the community asset will be accessible and inclusive for all members of the community, including the involvement of local underrepresented groups.

3. Potential to deliver community benefits

Total score and weighting

The total score available for this section is 15. The minimum score to be considered for shortlisting is 9. This will be weighted to account for 30% of the overall assessment.

Assessment and guidance

In order to secure funding, we want to know how your community stands to benefit from the protection of your asset. You should demonstrate the community benefits of the asset, and how these will be sustained and improved through community ownership.

You will be asked to explain:

  • What community benefits you expect to deliver with this project
  • How you will measure the benefits your asset will bring to the community
  • How you will deliver and sustain benefits over time
  • How the asset will be inclusive and benefit the wider community, for example how you intend to reach different parts of the local community

4. Added value based on community need

Total score and weighting

The total score available for this section is 5. The minimum score to be considered for shortlisting is 2. This will be weighted to account for 10% of the overall assessment.

Assessment and guidance

Its important that your organisation understands the local community and we want to see that you have a deep understanding of your local area and how your project can help to counter some of the challenges you may face locally. We are looking for any additional details or information about your community that demonstrates the need for this asset.

You will need to:

  • Tell us about your local community as a whole
  • Include details of any needs your community has and how your project will look to support these
  • If available, include any data you have on protected characteristics and equality impacts
  • Describe any specific challenges your community faces and how your asset will address these
