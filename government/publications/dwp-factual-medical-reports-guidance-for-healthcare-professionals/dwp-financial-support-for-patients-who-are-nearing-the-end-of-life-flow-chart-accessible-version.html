This is an accessible version of the flow chart for clinicians to explain when to use the DS1500 or SR1 form following improvements to the special rules process.

  1. Patient is identified as nearing the end of their life.

  2. Does your patient have an estimated prognosis of less than 6 months to live?

    If yes, complete a DS1500 form which can be accepted for all benefits.

    If no, does your patient have an estimated prognosis of less than 12 months to live?

  3. If your patient has an estimated prognosis of less than 12 months to live, are they aged 18 to State Pension age and considering or claiming Universal Credit or Employment and Support Allowance?
    (There are some exceptions if your patient is 16 or 17 years old.)

    If yes, complete a SR1 form.

    If no, please complete a DS1500 form when you consider your patient has an estimated prognosis of less than 6 months to live. This can be used for all benefits.

Completing a DS1500 form

  • online version via NHS Spine portal
  • electronic version returned via email: pip.e-ds1500@dwp.gov.uk
  • paper version – sent to your nearest jobcentre for Employment and Support Allowance and Universal Credit claims or the address on the back of the form for other benefit claims

If you need to get an electronic copy of a DS1500 form, please email a request to
pip.e-ds1500@dwp.gov.uk

Completing a SR1 form

  • electronic version returned via email: form.e-SR1@dwp.gov.uk
  • paper version sent to your nearest jobcentre

If you need to get an electronic copy of a SR1 form, please email a request to
form.e-SR1@dwp.gov.uk

Our preferred method of return for the SR1 form is via email.

Booklet of paper copies

To get a booklet of paper copies of either form please go to: The ‘Special Rules’: how the benefit system supports people nearing the end of life and see section ‘How to get blank SR1 and DS1500 forms’.
