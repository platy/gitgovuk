Overall Objective for the Qualifications

This handbook relates to the following qualification:

  • Level 3 Award in Operating a Communication System.

The qualification is designed to prepare learners to enter the workplace with the relevant and sufficient skills to establish, use and maintain a communication system where the focus is on communication using radios and antennae.

Pre-entry Requirements

There are no pre-requisites for this qualification.

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of 2 mandatory units. To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 7 credits as shown in the table below.

Mandatory Units

Unit Reference Number Unit Title GLH TQT RQF Level Credit Value
T/618/1174 Preparing and maintaining a communication system 22 30 3 3
A/618/1175 Operating a communication system 30 40 3 4
  Total 52 70 3 7

Age Restriction

This qualification is available to learners aged 18 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

This qualification creates a number of opportunities for progression onto higher level qualifications such as the City and Guild’s Level 4 Diploma in ICT Professional Competence.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified for this qualification.

Credit Transfer

Credits from similar regulated units which have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred.

Learning Outcomes

Learning outcomes - the learner will: Assessment Criteria - the learner can:
1. Be able to prepare a communication system 1.1 apply techniques to install radio components
  1.2 apply techniques to install antenna systems and hardware
  1.3 apply techniques to power a communication system
  1.4 show how to configure a communication system
2. Be able to maintain a communication system 2.1 apply techniques to conduct communication system maintenance
  2.2 show how to protect communication system equipment
Additional information about this unit
Unit aim (s) The overarching aim of this unit is to develop the fundamental skills and knowledge which learners require to effectively establish and maintain a communication system within a workplace context. Learners will be able to apply specific and appropriate techniques in line with relevant organisational and regulatory requirements to configure antenna and radio systems whilst implementing the relevant processes for maintaining such systems.

Level 3
Credit Value 4

Total Qualification Time 40

Learning Outcomes

Learning outcomes - the learner will: Assessment Criteria - the learner can:
1. Be able to maintain constant communications 1.1 apply effective communication procedures
  1.2 demonstrate effective communication in context
2. Be able to conduct communications 2.1 apply techniques to effectively process information
  2.2 demonstrate effective management of information systems in context
Additional information about this unit
Unit aim (s) The overarching aim of this unit is to develop the fundamental skills and knowledge which learners require to effectively use a communication system within a workplace context. Learners will be able to operate to, and within, organisational requirements and restrictions which control how information is communicated Learner will also become aware of how barriers to effective communication are recognised and addressed, and engage in the processes and procedures which ensure an organisation is able to communicate internally and externally.
