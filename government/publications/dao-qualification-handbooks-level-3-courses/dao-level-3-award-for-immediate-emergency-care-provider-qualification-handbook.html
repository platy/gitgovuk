The Qualification

Overall Objective for the Qualifications

This handbook relates to the following qualification:

  • DAO Level 3 Award for Immediate Emergency Care Provider

The purpose of the course is to provide formal training in Immediate Emergency Care ensuring candidates are competent to deliver Immediate Emergency Care in both their peacetime and operational roles.

Pre-entry Requirements

Candidates should be 3 years post completion of one of the following courses: Individual and Team Skills or Immediate Emergency Care

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of 3 mandatory units. To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 3 credits as shown in the table below.

This Level 3 Award which is current for three (3) years after which re-qualification is required.

Age Restriction

This qualification is available to learners aged 16 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

This qualification contributes to personal career development.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified for this qualification.

Credit Transfer

Credits from similar regulated units which have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred.

Qualification Units

Title: First Person on Scene’s initial management of an incident

Unit Number H/617/1966
Title First Person on Scene’s initial management of an incident
Level 3
Credit Value 1
Guided Learning Hours 9
Total Qualification Time 10

Learning Outcomes and Assessment Criteria

1. The learner will: Evaluate the scene

       The learner can:

  • Describe the use of the acronym “METHANE” during scene assessment

2. The learner will: Describe the precautions that minimise the risk of infection

       The learner can:

  • Describe how personal risk can be reduced
  • State how to deal with spillages

3. The learner will: Describe the post-incident procedures

       The learner can:

  • Explain the post-incident procedures

4. The learner will: Describe First Person on Scene activation

       The learner can:

  • State the conditions which are critical in the first five minutes after an incident
  • State the forms of communication
  • State the communication procedures

5. The learner will: List the equipment used by the First Person on the scene

       The learner can:

  • Describe the checks required on equipment to be used by the first person on scene
  • List the equipment and optional items required
  • Identify the clothing to be worn

6. The learner will: Explain communicating with a casualty

       The learner can:

  • Discuss the communication cycle
  • Discuss what makes a good communicator
  • State the steps taken when dealing with casualties who have language or communication barriers
  • State how to simplify communication
  • State the requirement for respect and confidentiality

7. The learner will: State the requirement for casualty consent

       The learner can:

  • Describe casualty consent
  • State when consent is not needed

8. The learner will: Undertake examination and assessment

       The learner can:

  • Describe how the history of the incident can be useful
  • State what is to be considered when dealing with sudden illness
  • Describe the mechanisms of injury
  • Demonstrate casualty and assessment
  • List the five Primary Survey components

9. The learner will: Describe Triage at the scene of an incident

       The learner can:

  • State the meaning of Triage
  • Describe what rapid assessment determines
  • State how prioritising casualties is achieved

10. The learner will: Describe the provision of an adequate oxygen supply

       The learner can:

  • State how oxygen is supplied to the tissues
  • Identify what normal air contains
  • Identify how the environment can cause problems

11. The learner will: Demonstrate the handling of a casualty with a blocked airway

       The learner can:

  • Describe the procedure for assessing the airway
  • State the common cause of a blocked airway
  • State the additional causes of obstruction
  • Identify a complete and incomplete airway obstruction
  • Demonstrate how the initial assessment is performed
  • Describe a secondary brain injury

12. The learner will: Demonstrate opening and maintaining a clear airway

       The learner can:

  • Demonstrate opening and inspecting the mouth
  • Demonstrate head tilt and chin lift
  • Demonstrate the actions to be taken for suspected neck and spinal cord injury
  • Demonstrate a Jaw Thrust manoeuvre
  • Describe how to deal with a casualty with broken / false teeth

13. The learner will: Manage a choking casualty

       The learner can:

  • State how choking can occur (foreign object)
  • Demonstrate the initial action to be taken with the casualty
  • Describe the action to be taken when the casualty suffers a loss of consciousness
  • State how to deal with a choking child

14. The learner will: Demonstrate the management of respiratory problems

       The learner can:

  • Describe normal breathing
  • Recognise signs of breathing problems
  • State the general principles of caring for a casualty with breathing difficulties
  • Describe respiratory conditions
  • Describe the treatment for chest conditions
  • Demonstrate breathing assessment using rise and fall

15. The learner will: Demonstrate what is necessary for Basic Life Support

       The learner can:

  • Describe effective life support
  • Describe how to manage the casualty who is conscious, breathing and with a pulse
  • Demonstrate how to manage the casualty who is conscious but breathing
  • Demonstrate the management of an airway obstruction

16. The learner will: State the additional advice on basic life support techniques

       The learner can:

  • Describe the technique for rescue breathing
  • Describe mouth to nose ventilation
  • Describe mouth to tracheostomy ventilation
  • Demonstrate rescue breathing
  • State when to stop resuscitation
  • Describe effective oxygenation
  • Demonstrate the preparation of a bag – valve mask
  • Demonstrate the sizing and fitting of a mask
  • State the difficulties in placing and using a bag – valve mask
  • State best ventilation practice
  • Demonstrate the two operator technique

17. The learner will: Demonstrate putting a casualty into the recovery position

       The learner can:

  • State the advantages of the recovery position
  • Demonstrate preparation of the casualty before movement
  • Demonstrate placing the casualty in the recovery position
  • State the procedure for minimising pressure sores

18. The learner will: Describe the use of Suction Equipment

       The learner can:

  • State the principles of suction
  • State the care to be taken when using suction equipment
  • Explain and demonstrate the use of suction
  • Explain solid / fluid obstructions

19. The learner will: Describe the Chain of Survival in relation to Automated External Defibrillators (AED)

       The learner can:

  • State the chain of survival
  • State the interventions known as the chain of survival
  • Describe a non-shockable rhythm
  • State the three interventions that affect casualty survival
  • State how Cardio – Pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) can be enhanced
  • Recognise that despite all treatment protocols being correctly carried out, some casualties will not survive

20. The learner will: Demonstrate Automated External Defibrillation (AED)

       The learner can:

  • State the normal / abnormal heart rhythms
  • Describe the normal workings of the heart
  • Describe abnormal heart rhythms

21. The learner will: Demonstrate using an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) for cardiac arrest

       The learner can:

  • Demonstrate CPR
  • Demonstrate the use of an AED
  • Describe the use of an AED on children

22. The learner will: Demonstrate the management of a casualty with poor circulation and shock

       The learner can:

  • Assess casualty’s circulation
  • Define a pulse
  • State the signs checked for in a pulse
  • Demonstrate locating and taking of the radial pulse
  • Demonstrate locating and taking of the carotid pulse
  • Demonstrate the control of external bleeding
  • State the signs and symptoms of internal bleeding
  • Describe the management of internal bleeding
  • Describe the initial management of injury to chest, abdomen pelvis and femur

23. The learner will: Demonstrate the Control of catastrophic Haemorrhage

       The learner can:

  • Describe the use of Haemostatic dressings
  • Demonstrate the use of Haemostatic dressings
  • Describe the use of the Combat Application Tourniquet (CAT)
  • State the potential hazards of the CAT
  • Demonstrate the application of the CAT
  • Demonstrate the use of the emergency bandage
  • State under what circumstances two CAT’s may need to be applied
  • Recognise and treat a high lower limb traumatic amputation as a result of a blast injury

24. The learner will: Complete the handover to the ambulance crew

       The learner can:

  • State the information required by the ambulance crew (MIST)
  • Complete an incident form

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) On completion of this unit, candidates will be able to demonstrate their ability to react as the first person on the scene of an incident. They will assess and manage casualties. They will demonstrate how to carry out basic life support protocols including use of Automatic External Defibrillation and the application of Combat Application Tourniquets and haemostatic agents.

Title: Management of Medical Emergencies

Unit Number K/617/1967
Title Management of Medical Emergencies
Level 3
Credit Value 1
Guided Learning Hours 9
Total Qualification Time 10

Learning Outcomes and Assessment Criteria

1. The learner will: Describe the management the angina/heart attack

       The learner can:

  • Describe the circulatory system of the heart
  • Define angina
  • Define myocardial infarction
  • State the signs and symptoms of Angina / Myocardial Infarction
  • Describe the management of Angina / Myocardial Infarction

2. The learner will: Describe the management of a diabetic casualty

       The learner can:

  • State the two main types of diabetes
  • State the signs of hypoglycaemia
  • State the signs of hyperglycaemia
  • Describe the recognition of a diabetic casualty
  • State the initial treatment of a diabetic casualty

3. The learner will: Describe the management of a stroke casualty

       The learner can:

  • State the causes of a stroke
  • State the signs and symptoms of a stroke
  • Describe the initial treatment of a stroke casualty

4. The learner will: Describe the management of a fitting casualty

       The learner can:

  • State the signs and symptoms of a fitting casualty
  • Describe the initial management of a seizure

5. The learner will: Describe the management of a casualty that has fainted

       The learner can:

  • State the cause of fainting
  • Discuss the management of a casualty who has fainted

6. The learner will: Describe the management of a casualty with a loss of consciousness

       The learner can:

  • State the causes of unconsciousness
  • Discuss the management of an unconscious casualty

7. The learner will: Describe the management of an asthma casualty

       The learner can:

  • State the signs and symptoms of an asthma casualty
  • Discuss the management of an asthma casualty

8. The learner will: Describe the management of the casualty suffering from anaphylaxis/allergic reaction

       The learner can:

  • Define anaphylaxis
  • State the common causes of anaphylaxis
  • Describe the signs associated with severe anaphylaxis and allergic reaction
  • Describe the management of anaphylaxis and allergic reaction

9. The learner will: Demonstrate the removal of crash helmets

       The learner can:

  • State the factors that should be considered when removing a crash helmet
  • Demonstrate the procedure for helmet removal by two people

10. The learner will: Demonstrate the use of an appropriate basic airway adjunct

       The learner can:

  • Recognise airway obstruction by the tongue
  • Describe the shape and use of an OPA
  • Describe the indications for use of an OPA
  • Describe the contra - indications for use of an OPA
  • Demonstrate the insertion of an OPA
  • Demonstrate the Oro – pharyngeal airway and its use
  • Demonstrate the Naso – pharyngeal airway and its use

11. The learner will: State the complications involved with an Oro-Pharyngeal Airway (OPA)

       The learner can:

  • Describe how an airway blockage can be caused by an OPA
  • Describe the damage to soft tissue caused by an OPA
  • Describe the damage to teeth or dental fillings caused by incorrect insertion of an OPA
  • Describe the stimulation of the gag reflex by an OPA

12. The learner will: Describe oxygen therapy

       The learner can:

  • Describe breathing and oxygen therapy
  • Identify types of oxygen mask / device
  • Explain how to recognise a non-breathing / inadequately breathing casualty
  • Describe the mask to face seal
  • Explain the approximate concentration of oxygen in the lungs
  • Describe the administration of oxygen
  • Describe the indications for oxygen
  • Explain the contra - indications for oxygen
  • Describe the methods of providing oxygen to a spontaneously breathing casualty

13. The learner will: Demonstrate airway management using oxygen

       The learner can:

  • Describe oxygen safety
  • Discuss handling oxygen cylinders
  • Describe the use of a regulator (flow valve)
  • Demonstrate assembling the equipment
  • Explain oxygen therapy

14. The learner will: Demonstrate the use of supraglottic airway

       The learner can:

  • Describe the use of the Single use supraglottic airway
  • Demonstrate the insertion of Single use supraglottic airway
  • Describe the possible problems encountered

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) On completion of this unit, candidates will be able to demonstrate their ability to recognise and manage a range of medical emergencies. They will also describe the preparation and use of medical oxygen and the use of the Oro-Pharyngeal airway

Title: Manage trauma related emergencies

Unit Number M/617/1968
Title Manage trauma related emergencies
Level 3
Credit Value 1
Guided Learning Hours 9
Total Qualification Time 11

Learning Outcomes and Assessment Criteria

1. The learner will: Recognise and manage the initial care of injuries to bone and soft tissue

       The learner can:

  • Describe the signs and symptoms of skeletal and soft tissue injury
  • Demonstrate the initial management of injuries to bones and soft tissue
  • Demonstrate head to toe survey (secondary survey)
  • Describe exposure
  • Explain the mnemonic AVPU
  • Describe pupil assessment
  • Recognise changes in pupil size and possible causes

2. The learner will: Recognise and manage the initial care of burns and scalds

       The learner can:

  • Describe the main types of burns
  • Describe the main dangers to a casualty from burns
  • Explain the classification of depth and area
  • Describe the key risks associated with an area of burns
  • Describe the initial management of burns and scalds
  • Describe the initial management of chemical burns

3. The learner will: Recognise and manage the initial care of other trauma related injuries

       The learner can:

  • State the information required by the ambulance crew (MIST)
  • Demonstrate the application of a cervical collar
  • Demonstrate the application of a stump dressing
  • Demonstrate the application of a pelvic sling

4. The learner will: Provide assistance to the Paramedic

       The learner can:

  • Discuss providing assistance
  • Discuss managing the scene
  • Discuss moving and positioning the casualty

5. The learner will: Demonstrate moving and handling casualties

       The learner can:

  • State the general principles of moving and handling casualties
  • Describe manual handling and the law
  • Explain risks to own spine

6. The learner will: Assess the need to move the casualty

       The learner can:

  • Describe conducting the assessment (TILE)
  • Describe other factors that influence casualty handling
  • Demonstrate the use of transfer boards
  • State the importance of correct posture

7. The learner will: Demonstrate the management of the falling and fallen casualty

       The learner can:

  • Demonstrate the control of the falling casualty
  • Demonstrate assisting the fallen casualty off the floor

8. The learner will: Demonstrate moving a casualty in extremis

       The learner can:

  • State the three instances where movement of a casualty may be necessary
  • Demonstrate the techniques used for a casualty transfer
  • Demonstrate the techniques used for transferring a casualty from the bed to the floor

9. The learner will: Describe the extrication of a trapped casualty

       The learner can:

  • State the two types of entrapped casualty
  • Describe the priorities in entrapment
  • Discuss the assessment and management of a trapped casualty
  • Describe the time critical injuries in the entrapped casualty

10. The learner will: Understand the factors to be considered with trapped casualties

       The learner can:

  • Explain the management and extrication of trapped casualties
  • Describe the priorities in entrapment
  • Discuss the assessment and management of a trapped casualty
  • Describe the time critical injuries in the entrapped casualty
  • Describe the casualty and vehicle assessment
  • State the two main approaches to casualty release
  • State the three methods of extraction from a motor vehicle
  • Demonstrate the use of the long board in extrication
  • Demonstrate cervical spine control

11. The learner will: Describe the five different types of shock

       The learner can:

  • Define shock
  • Describe hypo-volaemic shock
  • Describe anaphylactic shock
  • Describe cardio-genic shock
  • Describe septic (or toxic) shock
  • Describe neuro-genic shock

12. The learner will: Describe the body’s compensatory mechanisms

       The learner can:

  • Describe the compensatory mechanisms controlled by the sympathetic nervous system

13. The learner will: Explain the classes of shock

       The learner can:

  • State the signs and symptoms of developing shock due to blood loss
  • State the signs and symptoms of Class 1 shock
  • State the signs and symptoms of Class 2 shock
  • State the signs and symptoms of Class 3 shock
  • State the signs and symptoms of Class 4 shock

14. The learner will: Describe appropriate cannula and circulatory support in accordance with local protocols

       The learner can:

  • State the three indications for intravenous cannulation
  • State when cannulation is most likely performed
  • List the equipment required for cannulation
  • State the two factors influencing cannulation selection

15. The learner will: Select an appropriate venepuncture site

       The learner can:

  • State the factors that influence the choice of vein
  • State the preferred veins and where they are situated

16. The learner will: Demonstrate the steps taken when performing cannulation

       The learner can:

  • Show the steps taken when performing cannulation
  • Describe the hazards and complications that can occur when performing cannulation

17. The learner will: Prepare for intravenous fluid replacement

       The learner can:

  • Explain the time limitations of intravenous fluid replacement prior to hospital admission
  • State the checks made on all fluid containers before use
  • Prepare and connect the giving set

18. The learner will: Describe intravenous fluids

       The learner can:

  • State the two types of fluids used for infusion and their indications
  • Describe the hazards and complications of infusion
  • Describe the signs, symptoms and management of an air embolism
  • State the signs, symptoms and cause of circulatory overload
  • State the causes and remedial action taken when fluid fails to run
  • Identify how infection can be avoided

19. The learner will: Demonstrate the management of a simulated casualty suffering from musculo-skeletal trauma including extrication

       The learner can:

  • Describe the movement of the vertebral column
  • Explain the movement of the vertebral column
  • State the functions of the vertebral column
  • Describe the spinal column

20. The learner will: State the mechanisms of spinal injury

       The learner can:

  • State the causes of spinal injury
  • State the mechanism of spinal movement causing trauma
  • State the cause of axial loading compression
  • State the effects of hyper-flexion, hyper-extension and hyper-rotation
  • Describe how a distraction injury is caused
  • State the signs that would most likely indicate that a casualty had a spinal injury (secondary survey)
  • State the casualties most susceptible to spinal injury (localised spinal injury)

21. The learner will: Identify emergency drugs and pharmacology

       The learner can:

  • State the indications for emergency drug use
  • State the contra indications for emergency drug use
  • State the therapeutic effects of emergency drugs
  • State the side effects of emergency drugs
  • State the administration methods and dosage of emergency drugs
  • State the usage for Epinephrine (Adrenaline) for an adult
  • State the usage for glucagon
  • State the usage for Glycerol Trinitrate Spray
  • State the correct usage for Naloxane (Narcan)
  • State the usage for Normal Saline 9%
  • State the usage for Salbutamol (Ventolin)

22. The learner will: State the implications, procedures and precautions to be observed when administering Opiate Analgesia

       The learner can:

  • State the actions to be taken when administering Opiate Analgesia
  • State the indications for the administration of Opiate Analgesia
  • State the contra - indications for the administration of Opiate Analgesia
  • State the side effects of the administration of Opiate Analgesia
  • State the administration and dosage of Opiate Analgesia
  • Demonstrate preparation and administration of the opiate analgesia

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) On completion of this unit, candidates will be able to recognise and treat a range of trauma related injuries to bones, muscles, spinal and other injuries. They will describe when and how a casualty could be moved from the scene of injury. Candidates will demonstrate the process of cannulation and precautions to be taken when administering intravenous fluids. They will know a range of therapeutic drugs and state their uses. They will be able to state the procedures and precautions to be taken when administering Opiate Analgesia
