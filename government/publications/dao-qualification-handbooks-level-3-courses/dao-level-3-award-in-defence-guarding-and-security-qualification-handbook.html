The Qualification

Overall Objective for the Qualifications

This handbook relates to the following qualification:

• Level 3 Award in Defence Guarding and Security

The main purpose for this Qualification is to provide the learners with the necessary learning in which to fulfil roles within the defence, guarding and security industry.

Pre-entry Requirements

There are no pre-entry requirements for this qualification.

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of 3 mandatory units. To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 4 credits as shown in the table below.

Mandatory Units

Unit Reference Number Unit Title GLH TQT RQF Level Credit Value
L/617/1136 Guardroom and Control Room Procedures 10 20 3 2
R/617/1137 Effective Search Procedures 5 10 3 1
Y/617/1138 Effective Patrol Procedures 3 10 3 1
  Totals 18 40 3 4

Age Restriction

This qualification is available to learners aged 18 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

This qualification creates a number of opportunities for progression to further higher education and to increase career prospects within the security industry.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified for this qualification.

Credit Transfer

Credits from similar regulated units which have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred.

Qualification Units

Title: Guardroom and Control Room Procedures

Unit Number L/617/1136
Title Guardroom and Control Room Procedures
Level 3
Credit Value 2
Guided Learning Hours 10
Total Qualification Time 20

Learning outcomes and assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Understand guardroom and control room procedures 1.1 Explain guardroom and control room Procedures
  1.2 Apply procedures for accurate keeping of local registers
  1.3 Apply surveillance equipment procedures
2. Know how to approach incidents and threats 2.1 Explain the meaning of the terms “incidents and threats”
  2.2 Demonstrate effective use of local emergency procedures
  2.3 Formulate incident and threat reports
  2.4 Outline effective procedures for all incidents and threats
3. Know how to use communication equipment 3.1 Explain the types of communication equipment
  3.2 Demonstrate appropriate use of communication equipment
  3.3 Demonstrate appropriate call-sign procedures
  3.4 Demonstrate appropriate behaviour for radio use

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) Is to provide the learner with the understanding of the working environment of a guardroom/control room. The unit also guides the learner on how to deal with situations, incidents and threats using the correct procedures.
  This unit also equips the learner with the standard communication techniques and how to use the equipment supplied.
Assessment requirements specified by a sector or regulatory body (if appropriate) None
Details of the relationship of the unit and relevant National Occupational Standards The NOS standards used in this unit are as follows;
  • ESKISP6075.01 Lead incident management
  • PPLAOG95 Operate control room equipment to meet operational requirements in the aviation industry
  • PPLAOG95 Operate control room equipment to meet operational requirements in the aviation industry
  • Identify and assess emerging or potential threats to government, security, law and order
  • SFSSLP4 Control security incidents
Name of the organisation submitting the unit Defence Awarding Organisation

Title: Effective Search Procedures

Unit Number R/617/1137
Title Effective Search Procedures
Level 3
Credit Value 1
Guided Learning Hours 5
Total Qualification Time 10

Learning outcomes and assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Understand building and vehicle search procedures 1.1 Describe the effective procedures for building and vehicle searches
  1.2 Apply effective procedures for building and vehicle searches
  1.3 Justify reasons for building and vehicle searches
  1.4 Apply appropriate procedures for any refusal of building or vehicle searches
  1.5 Identify search equipment required
  1.6 Complete search documentation
2. Understand personal search procedures 2.1 Describe the effective procedures for personal search
  2.2 Apply personal search procedures
  2.3 Justify reasons for personal searches
  2.4 Apply appropriate procedures for any refusal of personal searches

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) Is to provide the learner with the knowledge to perform searches effectively, using standard procedures.
  It also shows the learner the standard techniques and how to use the equipment supplied, also an understanding of all the regulations covering searches.
Details of the relationship of the unit and relevant National Occupational Standards The NOS standards used in this unit are as follows:
  • SFSSSH2 Carry out systematic Security Search procedures
  • SFSSLP27 Carry out searches of vehicles
  • SFJCK4 Conduct searches
Name of the organisation submitting the unit Defence Awarding Organisation

Title: Effective Patrol Procedures

Unit Number Y/617/1138
Title Effective Patrol Procedures
Level 3
Credit Value 1
Guided Learning Hours 3
Total Qualification Time 10

Learning outcomes and assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Undertake effective patrol procedures 1.1 Explain effective patrol procedures
  1.2 Apply effective patrol procedures
  1.3 Produce reports of breaches of security
  1.4 Evaluate the merits of effective patrol procedures

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) The aim of the unit is to provide the learner with the skills and knowledge of how to conduct effective patrols in line with organisations procedures.
   
Details of the relationship of the unit and relevant National Occupational Standards The NOS standards used in this unit are as follows:
  • SFSSLP6 Maintain the security of property and premises through observation
  • SKAC228 Maintain site security
Name of the organisation submitting the unit Defence Awarding Organisation
