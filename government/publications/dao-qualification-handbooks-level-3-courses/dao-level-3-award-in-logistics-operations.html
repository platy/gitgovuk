The Qualification

Overall Objective for the Qualifications

This handbook relates to the following qualification:

• Level 3 Award in Logistics Operations

This qualification provides an introductory route into the knowledge and understanding required by those who are working in, or have ambitions to work in the logistics sector.

Pre-entry Requirements

There are no pre-entry requirements.

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of one mandatory unit. To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 4 credits as shown in the table below. ### Mandatory Units

URN Unit Title Level Credit Value GLH TQT
D/618/2481 Logistics operations 3 4 30 40

Age Restriction

This qualification is available to learners aged 18 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

This qualification creates a number of opportunities for progression in logistics operations.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified.

Credit Transfer

Credits from similar regulated units which have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred.

Qualification Unit

Title: Logistics Operations

Unit Number D/618/2481
Level 3
Credit Value 4
Guided Learning Hours 30
Total Qualification Time 40

Learning outcomes and Assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Understand how to operate a logistics function 1.1 discuss the roles and responsibilities of a logistics operation in context
  1.2 explain the role of other stakeholders in a logistics operation
  1.3 discuss how the working environment influences the behaviour of others in a logistics context
2.Know how to maintain accurate stock records and levels 2.1 explain types and categories of discrepancy reports
  2.2 carry out relevant processes and procedures in context
  2.3 show how to control stock orders
  2.4 outline relevant supply chain processes in context
3. Know how to maintain safe and secure stock holdings 3.1 outline relevant stock holding policies in context
  3.2 explain relevant stock holding procedures in context

Additional information about the unit

The learner will The learner can
Unit aims The aim of this unit is to provide learners with the fundamental knowledge to be able to undertake a range of tasks within logistics operations. These tasks include stock control processes and maintaining stock in a serviceable condition.
Assessment requirements specified by a sector or regulatory body (if appropriate) None
Details of the relationship of the unit and relevant National Occupational Standards This unit relates to the following national occupational standards;
  SFL15 – Maintain health, safety and security in logistics operations.
  SFLWS22 – Maintain working relationships with colleagues in logistics operations.
  SFLWS33 – Monitor the receipt, storage and despatch of goods in logistic operations.
  SFLWS34 – Undertake stock checks and record the results in logistic facilities.
