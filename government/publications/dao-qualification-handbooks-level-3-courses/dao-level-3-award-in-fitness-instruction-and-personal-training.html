The Qualification

Overall Objectives for the Qualifications

This handbook relates to the following qualifications:

  • Level 3 Diploma in Fitness Instruction and Personal Training

On successful completion of this qualification learners will have the skills and knowledge to effectively operate as a fitness instructor where the planning and delivery of fitness activities and fitness assessment are key components of the course. These components are augmented by an appreciation of anatomy, diet, and nutrition. Learners will also become aware of industry and organisational requirements which inform the roles and responsibilities of an instructor/trainer.

Pre-entry Requirements

There are no formal pre-entry requirements.

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of 4 mandatory units.

To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 44 credits as shown in the tables below.

Mandatory Units

Unit Reference Number Unit Title GLH TQT RQF Level Credit Value
J/618/6184 Roles and Responsibilities in the Fitness Industry 80 100 3 10
L/618/6185 Leading Fitness Activities 130 150 3 15
R/618/6186 Conducting Fitness Assessments 130 150 3 15
Y/618/6187 Anatomy, Diet and Nutrition 34 40 3 4
  Total 374 440 3 44

The detailed content of each of the units are included in Section 5 of this handbook.

Age Restriction

These qualifications are available to learners aged 16 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

All qualifications provide opportunities to progress onto qualifications such as the Active IQ Level 4 Certificate in Advanced Personal Training.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified.

Credit Transfer

Credits from similar regulated units which have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred into these qualifications.

Qualification Units

Title: Roles and Responsibilities in the Fitness Industry

Unit Number J/618/6184
Level 3
Credit Value 10
Guided Learning Hours 80
Total Qualification Time 100

Learning outcomes and Assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Understand an organisation’s position in the fitness industry 1.1 Explain a health and fitness-based organisation’s purpose and structure.
  1.2 Explain the aim of a health and fitness-based organisation.
  1.3 Apply techniques to produce a research-based report focused on the fitness industry.
2. Know about the responsibilities of a fitness instructor 2.1 Explain the responsibilities of a fitness instructor.
  2.2 Demonstrate the responsibilities of a fitness instructor in context.
  2.3 Explain the conduct and personal qualities expected during training.
  2.4 Demonstrate effective management of organisational assets.
  2.5 Demonstrate effective advertising of sporting activities.
3. Be able to comply with organisational requirements as a fitness instructor 3.1 Explain the requirements for formal training management and administration.
  3.2 Explain the requirements for supporting customers.
  3.3 Explain the requirement for continued professional development in context.
  3.4 Apply organisational policies and procedures in context.

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) The aim of this unit is to expose learners to key responsibilities within the fitness industry. On completion of this until, learners will be able to locate their role within a fitness-based organisation where compliance with organisational policies and procedures are key considerations.

Title: Anatomy, Diet and Nutrition

Unit Number Y/618/6187
Level 3
Credit Value 4
Guided Learning Hours 34
Total Qualification Time 40

Learning outcomes and Assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Know about the human anatomy 1.1 Describe the components and functions of the skeleton.
  1.2 Describe muscle types and function.
  1.3 Describe the components of movement.
  1.4 Explain the principles of core stability.
2. Know about body systems 2.1 Describe the components and function of the circulatory system.
  2.2 Describe the components and function of the respiratory system.
  2.3 Describe conditions associated with the cardio-pulmonary system.
  2.4 Describe the components and function of the digestive system.
  2.5 Describe the components and function of the digestive system.
  2.6 Describe the components and function of the nervous system.
  2.7 Describe the endocrine system.
3. Know about diet and nutrition 3.1 Describe the energy required for muscle function.
  3.2 Explain the effects of diet and lifestyle on the human body.
  3.3 Explain the basic principles of nutrition and weight management.
  3.4 Explain healthy diet and the use of supplements and fluids within sport.
  3.5 Explain the purpose and function of vitamins and minerals.

Additional information about this unit

Unit aim (s) The aim of this unit is to introduce learners to features of the human body and how diet and nutrition affects the performance of the human body in a fitness context. One completion of this unit learners will be able to advise clients on how to improve diet and nutrition, so they are fitter and healthier.
