1. About this service

The Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) provides this service as part of its responsibilities to ensure that motor vehicles are roadworthy. The MOT history service allows individuals, organisations and the wider public to view the MOT status and history of vehicles to establish their background.

The service also provides a link to vehicle recall information supplied from a third-party data source.

The service is a subset of the MOT testing service database.

The data controller for DVSA is DfT - a data controller determines the reasons and how personal data is processed. For more information see the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) Data Protection Public Register. DfT’s registration number is Z7122992.

2. What data we need

The data we collect from you will include:

  • vehicle registration mark – the focus of which is on the vehicle and not the individual, so we do not class this as personal data
  • anonymous data including details of which version of the web browser you used, information on how you used the site, user cookies, IP address and page tagging techniques to improve user experience

The personal data we collect from you when using MOT history trade application programme interface (API) from you will include:

  • business name and address
  • email address
  • phone number
  • IP addresses you use to access the site
  • questions, queries of feedback you give

We will also collect personal data about you from any complaints we receive from learner drivers or other members of the public.

The legal basis for processing this data is public task.

We have no statutory obligation to provide MOT history service. It does contribute to improved road safety by identifying and prompting the need for vehicle roadworthiness.

All personal data entered for MOT history trade API application is volunteered by the user on account creation and if not registered, will not receive the services.

3. Why we need it

We need the personal data we collect from you to:

  • provide MOT history service
  • respond to your request for MOT history trade API
  • provide MOT history trade API (where approved)
  • report and fix issues with the MOT history trade API

4. What we do with it

We collect, use and store the data you give us for the reasons set out in this policy.

We will not:

  • sell or rent your data to third parties
  • share your data with third parties for marketing purposes

We use an external company called SmartSurvey to host the MOT history API request form and the data you provide.

We will share your data if required to do so by law – for example, by court order, or to prevent fraud or other crime.

5. How long we keep your data

We’ll only keep your personal data for as long as it is needed for the reasons set out in this policy or as long as is required by law.

We will hold data:

  • until we’ve been notified the vehicle has been scrapped – this data is not considered personal data as the focus is on the vehicle
  • for 50 months for anonymous user data
  • for MOT history trade API until you stop your account
  • for 6 years following subscription

6. Where it might go

Reminder data is held on cloud services, hosted in the European Union and as such meets security safeguards equivalent to those required by Data Protection Legislation.

SmartSurvey is hosted in the UK. You can read their privacy notice.

7. Protecting your data and your rights

The DVSA personal information charter sets out what steps are taken to protect your data, and the rights you have over your data.

8. Automated decision making

Your data is not subject to automated decision making or profiling as defined in data protection legislation.

9. Changes to this notice

We may change this privacy notice at its discretion at any time.

When we change this notice, the date on the page will be updated. Any changes to this privacy notice will be applied to you and your data as of the revision date.

We encourage you to periodically review this privacy notice to be informed about how your data is protected.
