Direction given under section 100(4) of the Land Registration Act 2002

Applications by post, document exchange and personal delivery

1.I direct, under section 100(4) of the Land Registration Act 2002, that the addresses of the offices of HM Land Registry listed in Schedule 1 for the receipt of applications and correspondence by:

a. post,

b. document exchange, or

c. personal delivery,

are specified in Schedule 2 to this direction.

Addresses

2.Columns 2 to 4 of the table in Schedule 2 specify HM Land Registry addresses depending on the:

a. method of delivery (post, document exchange or personal delivery), and

b. type of customer specified in Column 1.

Receipt of applications

3.Applications for registration or correspondence in connection with an application must not be marked “Private and confidential” or “Private” or “Confidential”. Any applications or correspondence so marked and sent to HM Land Registry will be redirected from the address in Schedule 2 to an office listed in Schedule 1 and will be either:

a. inspected, and if found to be an application for registration, or a document connected with an application for registration, returned to the address indicated in Schedule 2 for scanning, or

b. returned to the customer, for the application or correspondence to be sent to HM Land Registry without such marking.

The application will not be received for the purpose of rule 15 of the Land Registration Rules 2003 until received at the HM Land Registry address specified in Schedule 2 for the second time as a result of step 3(a), or until it is received without the markings following step 3(b) above.

4.Any applications sent to an HM Land Registry office address other than as specified in the table in Schedule 2:

a. will be redirected by the registrar to an address specified in the table in Schedule 2, and

b. will not be received for the purpose of rule 15 of the Land Registration Rules 2003 until received at such redirected address.

5.The office name to be inserted after the words “HM Land Registry” in any address shown in Schedule 2 to this direction is:

a. the name of the office where the customer team of the customer (if any) is located, or

b. if the customer does not have a customer team, the name of the office closest to the location of the customer, in either case being one of the offices listed in Schedule 1.

6.If the name of any office listed in Schedule 1 is omitted from the address where indicated by “XXXXXX Office” after the words “HM Land Registry” in Schedule 2, the application will be received for the purposes of rule 15 of the Land Registration Rules 2003 when it is received at the appropriate address shown in Schedule 2.

7.The provisions of this direction also apply to the definition of “appropriate office” in rule 19 of the Land Registration Rules 2003.

8.This direction has effect on and after 4 February 2019, and replaces the Direction - Addresses of the proper offices for the receipt of applications under the Land Registration (Proper Office) Order 2013, made on 3 April 2018.

Mike Harlow
Acting Chief Executive and Chief Land Registrar
