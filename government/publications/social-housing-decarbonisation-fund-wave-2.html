Details

The Social Housing Decarbonisation Fund (SHDF) will upgrade a significant amount of the social housing stock currently below Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) C up to that standard. It will support the installation of energy performance measures in social homes in England, and help:

  • deliver warm, energy-efficient homes
  • reduce carbon emissions
  • tackle fuel poverty
  • support green jobs
  • develop the retrofit sector
  • improve the comfort, health and well-being of social housing tenants

The Wave 2.1 competition will allocate up to £800 million of funding to support the installation of energy performance measures in social homes in England.

The competition is due to launch in September 2022. All grant funding for Wave 2.1 projects must be transferred to the grant recipient and spent by 31 March 2025. Projects may opt to deliver until 30 September 2025, where:

  • the scale and ambition of the project requires this
  • only co-funding is used in the final 6 months of delivery

Update: 18 August 2022

We have expanded the eligibility criteria for the Wave 2.1 competition:

  • registered charities that own social housing can now apply directly to the SHDF Wave 2.1 competition, either as a single bidder or as the lead of a consortium
  • if registered charities plan to apply, we recommend they submit their self assessment in the Social Housing Retrofit Accelerator as early as possible to access support for their bid

Draft guidance / Grant Funding Agreement, Data Sharing Agreement and Privacy Notice

The clarification period has ended so we will amend the documents and publish the final versions at competition launch, clarifying where the changes are. We don’t expect any updates to result in significant changes to policy design.

We published draft versions of the Wave 2.1 Grant Funding Agreement, the Data Sharing Agreement and the Privacy Notice, so that social housing landlords can familiarise themselves with the terms and conditions before signing the agreements, should they be awarded grant funding.

Sections that are yet to be clarified, or that are specific to each individual agreement, are highlighted in green.

Terms and conditions may still change: if you are awarded a grant, we will provide final versions of the agreements at that point, indicating any changes.

Eligibility

Bidders

Open to:

  • local authorities
  • combined authorities
  • registered providers of social housing (including housing associations and arms-length management organisations (ALMOs) that are registered providers)
  • registered charities that own social housing

All eligible organisations can apply directly to Wave 2.1 either as single bidders or as the lead of a consortium.

The above organisations, along with ALMOs that are not registered providers, can apply as part of a consortium led by an organisation that is eligible to lead a bid.

Eligible properties

All existing social housing as defined by the Housing and Regeneration Act 2008 (sections 68-70), below EPC C, regardless of archetype, is eligible.

How to apply for Wave 2.1

The draft application form is available.

The competition is due to open in September 2022.

The submission of applications will open at least 5 working days before the close of the competition.

We will confirm details of the submission process by early to mid October 2022.

See the indicative timeline for more details of competition dates

We encourage you to start preparing and building partnerships now. For support with bid development, visit the Social Housing Retrofit Accelerator and complete a self-assessment. This also provides access to useful resources and events on retrofit in general and the SHDF application process in particular.

Contact us

If you have any enquiries about SHDF Wave 2.1, please email SHDFWave2.Applications@BEIS.gov.uk.

Background

The fund follows on from:

  • SHDF Wave 1, which will see energy performance improvements in up to 20,000 social housing properties, reducing bills and carbon emissions
  • the SHDF Demonstrator, where up to 2,000 homes are being improved to at least EPC Band C and around 1,200 local jobs supported
