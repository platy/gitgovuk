Scope

The reconciliation thresholds apply to Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) AEB adult skills, including non-formula funded Community Learning and 19 to 24 traineeships, and advanced learner loans bursary (ALLB) providers.

In areas where the AEB has been devolved, Mayoral Combined Authorities or the Greater London Authority are responsible for considering any provider flexibilities in their areas.

Over-delivery

We have increased the over-delivery threshold for ESFA grant and contract funded AEB skills for the academic year 2021 to 2022 from 103% to 110%. This is in recognition of providers’ efforts to focus on delivery following the coronavirus pandemic and accommodating additional eligible learners recently resettled from other countries including Ukraine and Afghanistan.

This brings the over-delivery threshold for the academic year 2021 to 2022 in line with the thresholds for 19 to 24 traineeships and the level 3 Free Courses for Jobs offer.

This increase in the over-delivery threshold is for one year only (2021 to 2022). We will confirm the over-delivery threshold for the academic year 2022 to 2023 in due course and any changes will be subject to affordability.

Under-delivery

We recognise the challenges providers have faced because of the pandemic. But we also know that many providers have been able to continue deliver successfully and have adapted the way that they teach. We consider the time is now right to return to the pre-pandemic reconciliation threshold.

Under-delivery thresholds for the academic year 2021 to 2022 for ESFA AEB grant-funded providers remain as published.

Funding Under-delivery threshold
AEB (adult skills and 19 to 24 traineeships) 97%
ALLB 100%
