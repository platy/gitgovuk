Introduction

This document provides clinical guidance for workplaces using rapid lateral flow tests to identify positive cases of coronavirus (COVID-19).

You must read and agree to this guidance to qualify for free workplace testing.

Why testing is important

Rapid lateral flow tests are a fast and simple way to find people who do not have symptoms of COVID-19, but who may still be spreading the virus.

Testing:

  • identifies staff who currently have COVID-19 so they can self-isolate
  • protects unvaccinated and vulnerable people from infection
  • prevents and controls the spread of the virus by identifying asymptomatic cases

Using rapid lateral flow tests

Rapid lateral flow tests use either nose or throat and nose swabbing to help identify positive cases of COVID-19. They deliver accurate results within 30 minutes.

The testing method and time to deliver results depends on which test type is being used. Manufacturers’ instructions for use must always be followed.

Find detailed guidance and testing information on how to do a rapid lateral flow test for coronavirus.

Storage of rapid lateral flow tests

Kits can be stored at room temperature:

  • store extraction solution at 2°C to 30°C
  • store the test cartridge at 2°C to 30°C

Organisation requirements

When taking part in workplace testing, your organisation is expected to:

  • set up and market your test site to participants
  • staff your site according to the requirements of your testing option
  • provide PPE and waste disposal facilities to your site operatives
  • register test sites and make sure results are reported

Workplace testing options

There are 2 types of workplace testing available. Each method uses rapid lateral flow tests to deliver accurate results within minutes.

Option 1: Workplace Collect

Your organisation sets up a collection point at your workplace. Employees can collect rapid lateral flow tests to do at home and self-report their results.

Workplace Collect workforce requirements

You need at least one Collection Point Assistant to run your workplace collection point. More roles may be required, such as a Queue Coordinator, depending on how you run Workplace Collect.

All staff will also need to complete online training modules. A quality lead should be in place to make sure all training is done, and regular competency assessments are carried out.

Level Role Responsible for
Operative Collection point assistant • greeting participants
• explaining testing process
• storing tests and managing stock levels
• regular cleaning of collection desk
Operative Queue coordinator • crowd control and social distancing
• supporting test site set up and signage

Workplace Collect set up

The size and location of your rapid lateral flow test collection point will vary depending on the space available and the testing needs of your workplace.

Additional requirements may help you decide on a location for your collection point.

These include:

  • space for social distancing
  • easy to clean floors and surfaces
  • storage for your rapid lateral flow tests
  • making it accessible to all participants
  • complying with health, safety and fire regulations

The following safety steps should also be followed:

1. Signage to help with social distancing and queue flow.

2. Hand sanitiser stations on arrival.

3. A location away from your main area of business.

4. Access only for operatives and participants.

5. Good airflow and ventilation.

6. Storage temperature of tests based on manufacturers’ requirements.

Workplace Collect testing and reporting results

Volunteers for testing will be given rapid lateral flow tests. They will then need to complete the following steps:

1. Self swab to obtain a sample.

2. Analyse the sample and interpret the results.

3. Record the result online.

Option 2: Asymptomatic Test Sites (ATS)

This allows you to set up a test site at your workplace. Employees volunteer for regular on-site testing using rapid lateral flow tests in a controlled environment. This option requires space to set up a site and staff to help run it.

ATS workforce requirements

The number of people required to run your site will scale with the size of your ATS and the number of people being tested.

Several ATS roles can be performed by one person, but you are required to have at least 3 full time operatives running an ATS site. This is so they can provide a dedicated and consistent service.

All staff will also need to complete online training modules. A quality lead should be in place to make sure all training is done, and regular competency assessments are carried out.

Level Role Responsible for
Manager Team leader • running test site operations
• managing operatives
• receiving and managing test stock
• site health and safety
Site operative Queue coordinator • crowd control and social distancing
• supporting test site set up and signage
Site operative Registration assistant • greeting participants
• explaining the testing process
• assisting with registration
Site operative Test assistant • directing participants to testing stations
• regular cleaning of testing stations
• answering participant questions
Testing operative Processing operative • processing test samples
• reading and recording results on each rapid lateral flow device
Testing operative Results recorder • collating and uploading test site results

ATS set up

Asymptomatic test sites are designed to be modular and scalable. This is so they can be set up in different sized workplaces.

You must follow legal, safety and operational requirements for setting up an ATS.

These include:

  • making your ATS accessible to all participants
  • adequate lighting and an internet connection for reporting results
  • a legal agreement to allow testing if you do not own your testing space
  • health and safety and security sign off
  • a one way system to manage the flow of participants
  • easy to clean floors and surfaces
  • good airflow and ventilation
  • space away from your normal day to day workplace

How you set up and configure your test site will depend on the size of your space and number of people being tested.

However, you will need 4 essential components:

1. Arrival area: where participants are welcomed and given test registration cards.

2. Testing area: for participants to swab themselves and give their samples to a Testing Operative.

3. Processing area: where Testing Operatives process samples and record results.

4. Exit area: for participants to leave your test site.

ATS testing and reporting results

Volunteers for testing will be given a barcode with their rapid lateral flow test. They will then need to register their test on the GOV.UK register a test service, either before or after they take their swab. This will link their personal details with their test record so they can receive results.

After swabbing, workforce staff will need to complete the following steps:

1. Samples will be analysed on site by the Processing Operative.

2. Results will be recorded by the Results Recorder.

3. These will then upload to the log site COVID-19 results service.

The individual will then receive their results directly.
