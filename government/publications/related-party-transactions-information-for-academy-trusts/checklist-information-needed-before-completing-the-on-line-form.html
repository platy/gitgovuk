This checklist is to help you prepare the evidence needed when seeking approval for a related party transaction. Documents can be uploaded or provided using a hyperlink to the relevant page on the academy trust website. You must have this information to hand before starting to complete the online form. It isn’t possible to save the form to submit later.

Item Requirement Information and documents needed
1 The transaction is reported before it is confirmed with the supplier The start date of the transaction, contract or agreement should not be a retrospective date.

Academy Trust Handbook, part 5: Delegated authorities 		 
2 A competitive tendering process is in place and has been applied Documents relating to the procurement process undertaken which demonstrate a competitive tendering process was in place and applied, and the procurement rules and thresholds in the Public Contracts Regulations 2015 and find a tender service were observed.

It is best practice for academy trusts to reflect buying procedures and procurement law for schools, buying for schools and Crown Commercial Service public procurement in their procurement policy.

Deals for schools can make buying simpler

Academy Trust Handbook, sections 2.27 to 2.29 		 
3 Procurement was fair and open The supply of goods or services is properly procured through an open and fair process.

Evidence to demonstrate this might include the academy trust’s procurement policy and quotations from suppliers. The number of quotations should be in line with procurement policy, but a minimum of three is advised.

Academy Trust Handbook, sections 2.27 to 2.29 		 
4 Board oversight of related party transactions In agreeing the transaction with a related party, the academy trust must ensure:

• internal delegation levels exist and are applied

• that the board meeting was quorate where applicable and sufficient records are kept to show the transaction has been conducted in accordance with the high standards of accountability and transparency required within the public sector.

Evidence to demonstrate this might include minutes from the relevant board meetings where the transaction was approved, or evidence of relevant approvals in line with the academy trust’s scheme of delegation.

Academy Trust Handbook sections 2.28 and 5.36 to 5.40 		 
5 Identify and manage conflicts of interest Trustees have statutory duties as company directors to avoid conflicts of interest. Guidance on how to manage a conflict of interest is available.

Evidence to demonstrate this might include:

• the register of interest is in place and maintained

• relevant business and pecuniary interests of members, trustees, local governors and accounting officers are published on the academy trust’s website and the minutes of any meeting or decision in which the related party transaction or agreement has been discussed.

Academy Trust Handbook sections 5.35, 5.45 to 5.48 		 
6 Transaction is in line with the “at cost” policy Evidence to demonstrate the transaction is in line with the “at cost” policy.

Evidence to support this includes a statement of assurance and a contract with the supplier on the basis of an open book agreement.

Academy Trust Handbook sections 5.49 and 5.59 		 

Supplementary check for church academy trusts

Item Requirement Information and documents needed
7 Services that can only be delivered by the diocese, which provide essential functions fundamental to the religious character and ethos of the academy trust are regarded as meeting the “at cost” requirement. An agreement of the levy or voluntary contribution paid to the diocese, relating to services for the purposes of maintaining the religious ethos or character of the academy trust.

This should not include any traded services.

A traded service is any service which does not directly relate to maintaining the religious ethos or character of the academy trust.
