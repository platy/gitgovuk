1. Introduction

The online form is for academy trusts to declare or seek approval for an agreement or contract with a related party, before it’s confirmed with the supplier.

Summary guidance explains the reporting requirement for related party transactions and should be read in addition to this guidance.

Have all of your information ready before starting to complete the form, as you cannot save an unfinished entry and return to it later. A checklist is available to help you prepare.

You need an Identity and Access Management System (IDAMS) account to use the form. You can register for an IDAMS account and set up other users to report related party transactions.

3. Completing the online form

3.1 Homepage

There are 3 options on the homepage. You can return to this page at any time using the ‘home’ button at the top of the screen.

Submit transaction or agreement - declare or seek approval for a related party transaction.

Transaction or agreement status - check the status of cases for the academy trust using the transaction list.

Transaction status Description
Declared only A proposed agreement or transaction is declared and approval is not required.
Awaiting decision A new agreement or transaction submitted for which approval is required. We aim to respond within 10 working days.
Information requested More information is needed. You will receive an email when the status of your case changes. Access your IDAMS account for further details.
Rejected The related party transaction is not approved. You will receive an email when the status of your case changes. Access your IDAMS account for further details.
Approved Based on the evidence provided, the related party transaction is approved. You will receive an email when the status of your case changes.

Your suppliers - a list of related parties or suppliers previously recorded in the online form.

3.2 Supplier information

Where the supplier is a limited company, you must provide the company number. You can get information about companies from Companies House. For all suppliers you need to provide an address.

Tell us about the person or organisation providing goods or services, selecting from the descriptions outlined in the Academy Trust Handbook, section 5.49.

If none of the available options apply, you will need to select ‘other’ and enter a description of the relationship in the free text box.

Using the checkboxes, you will be asked to confirm:

  • the supplier is listed in the academy trust register of business interests
  • a signed statement of assurance has been received from the supplier
  • an open book agreement is in place with the supplier
  • whether you have received a donation from the supplier in the past

If you have received donations from the supplier in the past, tell us which type of donation it was, by selecting from the list. If you select 'other' you must also complete a free text box.

If a formal contract is proposed, you need to select 'create a contract'. This includes new agreements and existing ones which are being renewed.

If details of an existing supplier have changed, contact us using the ESFA enquiry form.

3.3 Contract or agreement

This page only appears if you indicated that a formal contract is proposed.

Where a contract will be delivered across more than one financial year, the full contract value should be reflected in the first financial year of the contract period. For example, an agreement value of £25,000 comprising an estimated £5,000 of transactions per year over 5 years should be declared as £25,000 in the first year rather than £5,000.

The table sets out information you will need to provide about the contract.

Form item Description
Name A name to describe the agreement, for your reference
Break clause Tell us if your contract has an option to break or terminate early.
Exit clause Tell us if your contract has an option to exit without penalty.
Extension clause Tell us if your contract has an option to be extended
Provide terms and conditions If a break or extension clause is in place, state the terms and conditions of the agreement.
Start and end dates Complete this section if you know the dates. You will still be able to complete the form if you don’t have this information.

3.4 Transaction information

You need to provide the expected date, type and value of the transaction, contract or agreement.

The proposed date of transaction should be the expected start date of the agreement, or the date from which it will be renewed. This should be a future date as academy trusts are required to seek approval before confirming or renewing an agreement with a related party. For transactions made across more than one financial year, provide the transaction start date with further details of the contract duration or end date in the contract or agreement section of the form.

Enter a description of the goods or services to be provided and include the proposed cost of the transaction. If the academy trust intends to recover VAT, the proposed cost should not include VAT.

The total value of transactions to date is calculated automatically. Where the cumulative total value of transactions with a single supplier exceeds £20,000, the transaction will be referred to ESFA for approval.

The total value of transactions refers to all transactions with the same supplier or related party within a financial year, including any that are being considered for approval.

4. Managing conflict of interest

You must confirm whether the related party was involved in the decision-making process. You must provide a summary of how the conflict of interest was resolved, or how their involvement was managed.

5. Upload evidence

This section is only visible if the related party transaction requires approval from the ESFA. The checklist sets out the type of documents to upload, to provide evidence:

  • of how you agreed to the related party transaction. This may include minutes from meetings where the related party transaction was discussed and agreed, and records of any relevant discussions.
  • that your academy trust followed its procurement policy and, if applicable, any results or scores from a tendering process.
  • that your academy trust tested the market before making a decision.
  • to show how you have managed any conflict of interest.
  • that a statement of assurance is in place.

You will also need to include a copy of the agreement or proposed contract.

This is not an exhaustive list

Accepted formats include PDF files and Microsoft Word, Excel and PowerPoint documents.

5.1 Check your answers

The summary page shows all the information you have entered into the form. You can amend any of the information by using the 'back' button on the page to return to a section. You cannot amend the form once you've clicked 'save and submit'.

5.2 Academy trust details and declaration

Review the contact information for the academy trust. If the details have changed or are incorrect, please contact ESFA enquiries, selecting 'related party transactions' from the options menu.

You must make declarations about the process followed to agree the proposed transaction. The declarations are that, on behalf of the academy trust:

  • I declare that conflicts of interest in the tendering of goods and services with the related party have been managed in line with policy guidelines
  • I declare that the transaction complies with the “at cost” policy
  • I declare that the transaction is an ’arm’s length transaction’

Important

The academy trust Accounting Officer is responsible for the accuracy of these declarations, including where these are made by others under delegated authority. The form must be completed by a representative of the academy trust who has authority to do so.

6. Submitting the completed online form

After submitting the form, you’ll receive an email confirming receipt and providing a reference number for the transaction.

We aim to decide on requests for approving related party transactions within 10 working days, subject to receiving all relevant information and evidence. It is important that you submit requests for approval in sufficient time to allow ESFA to assess them.

You’ll receive an email confirming receipt and providing a reference number for the transaction.

7. After submitting the form

If you've made a mistake on the form, information has changed, or you've submitted the form by mistake, you will need to contact us. Use the ESFA enquiry form stating the transaction reference number and the reason it needs to be removed or amended.

We will confirm with you when a submitted transaction did not need to be reported. The transaction will then be removed from your IDAMS account.

Transactions are given a status, as described at section 3.1. When a transaction status changes, an alert email is issued to prompt you to review the details on IDAMS.

We’ll send an email alert if we need further information about a transaction. Your IDAMS account will have details of the information we need to continue the assessment.
