ASEAN

  • founded in 1967, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) is the deepest regional grouping in Asia and is responsible for the development of an extended regional architecture. It brings together ten countries in Southeast Asia: Brunei Darussalam, Cambodia, Indonesia, Lao PDR, Malaysia, Myanmar, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand and Vietnam

  • ASEAN is structured into three Communities: political-security; economic; and socio-cultural

  • in 2020, the total combined Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of the ten ASEAN Member States was US$3.0 trillion, resulting in ASEAN becoming the fifth largest economy in the world, after the United States (US$ 20.9 trillion), China (US$ 14.7 trillion), Japan (US$ 5.0 trillion), and Germany (US$ 3.8 trillion)

  • by 2025, ASEAN aims to integrate further and narrow the development gap, utilising technology to raise productivity

Current UK institutional relationship with ASEAN

  • on 5 August 2021, former Foreign Secretary, Dominic Raab, joined the conferment ceremony with ASEAN Foreign Ministers and representatives where they welcomed the UK as an ASEAN Dialogue Partner

  • the UK has been a party to the Treaty of Amity and Cooperation (TAC) in Southeast Asia since 2012

  • the UK has Embassies or High Commissions in all ten ASEAN Member States

  • in November 2019, we opened the UK Mission to ASEAN and welcomed the first dedicated UK Ambassador to ASEAN

  • the UK held its second dialogue with ASEAN Foreign Ministers in April 2021 and had its first formal meeting with ASEAN Economic Ministers (1st UK-AEM Meeting) in September 2021

  • the UK’s National Crime Agency gained ASEAN Chiefs of National Police (ASEANAPOL) Dialogue Partner status in September 2019

  • the UK currently has a secondee in the ASEAN-Singapore Cyber Security Centre of Excellence

  • since 2018, the UK has supported the ASEAN Economic Reform Programme, worth £19.7 million ($24.67 million) and the ASEAN Low Carbon Energy Programme, worth £15 million ($18.8 million)

Security

  • the UK works across the region with ASEAN partners: sharing experiences and building capability for Southeast Asia law enforcement in areas of national, regional and international joint interest, including with relevant human rights content and with gender sensitivities

  • the UK has National Crime Agency offices based in Thailand, the Philippines and Vietnam. We work alongside ASEAN law enforcement partners on a wide range of issues, such as forensics, money laundering, modern slavery and child sexual exploitation

Defence*

  • through a network of Defence Attachés and Defence Advisers, the UK maintains a bilateral defence relationship with seven ASEAN Member States (Brunei Darussalam, Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand and Vietnam)

  • defence co-operation is coordinated by a regional hub (British Defence Staff Southeast Asia) based in Singapore

  • at the request of His Majesty The Sultan, the UK maintains a military Garrison in Brunei Darussalam

  • the UK is a member of the Five Power Defence Arrangements (FPDA) – Southeast Asia’s only collective security arrangement. The FPDA’s other members are Australia, Malaysia, New Zealand and Singapore

  • over the last two years, the Royal Navy has maintained an almost-unbroken presence in the region. Most recently, the UK Carrier Strike Group, led by HMS Queen Elizabeth visited Brunei, Cambodia, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand and Vietnam

  • UK defence engages with ASEAN Member States across a broad range of areas, from preparatory support to operational deployments with Singapore and Vietnam, through to developing regional collaboration via the jointly sponsored Regional Jungle Warfare Symposium in Brunei Darussalam. UK defence also continues to offer a wide range of UK-based and regionally run courses, and development activities to support capacity building and knowledge sharing across the region

*The UK has suspended all defence engagement and training with the military in Myanmar since 2017.

UK-ASEAN trade and investment

  • total trade in goods and services between the UK and ASEAN Member States was £36.2 billion in the four quarters to the end of Q3 2021

  • UK exports to ASEAN Member States in the four quarters to the end of Q3 2021 amounted to £17.7 billion (50.5% goods; 49.5% services). UK imports totalled £18.5 billion (74.4% goods; 25.6% services) over the same period

  • the stock of UK inward FDI from ASEAN was at least £15.2 billion in 2020. This accounts for at least 0.8% of the total UK inward FDI stock

Development and infrastructure

  • the UK is a major development partner for Southeast Asia, investing more than £3.5 billion of total UK Official Development Assistance (ODA) in ASEAN over the past decade

  • the UK has committed to double its International Climate Finance (ICF) from £5.8 billion to £11.6 billion for the period of April 2021 to March 2026. The fund includes support to countries in Southeast Asia to help mitigate and adapt to the impact of climate charge, to reduce deforestation and to pursue clean, sustainable economic growth

  • at COP26 in November 2021, the UK announced up to £110 million for the ASEAN Catalytic Green Finance Facility (ACGF) to support the development of sustainable and resilient infrastructure, such as renewable energy, clean transportation or urban infrastructure

  • the UK is one of the founding members of the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB) and is the joint-largest contributor to the AIIB’s $128 million technical assistance Special Fund

  • the UK’s Intellectual Property Office (IPO) attachés in Southeast Asia support ASEAN governments, international development partners, and industries to build a rules-based Intellectual Property system

  • the UK’s Better Health Programme (BHP) has been delivering in four ASEAN countries (Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand and Vietnam) since the end of 2019 to strengthen healthcare systems by addressing the burden of non-communicable diseases and increasing access to quality healthcare

  • the UK has stood by Southeast Asia during times of humanitarian need. The UK delivered support for the tragic events surrounding the Sulawesi earthquake and tsunami in September 2018. Following storms and floods in 2020, the UK provided £1.25 million through the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) to assist 24,000 affected people in Vietnam and 10,000 in the Philippines with access to health, water and livelihoods support. The UK also provided £750, 000 to the International Federation of the Red Cross (IFRC)’s response to Typhoon Rai

  • the UK is a leading donor to the humanitarian response in Myanmar. Since February 2021, the UK has provided £18 million in humanitarian assistance in Myanmar

  • the UK contributed £1 million to the COVID-19 ASEAN Response Fund in 2021

Tourism and education

  • more than 120,000 British nationals live in Southeast Asia and over 3 million British nationals visited each year before the pandemic

  • there are over 40,000 ASEAN students studying in the UK and over 125,000 students in ASEAN Member States studying for British higher education qualifications overseas

  • the UK’s Chevening Scholarship programme fully funds approximately 200 students a year from ASEAN Member States to study for postgraduates degrees in the UK

  • the British Council is contributing to education reform and development across Southeast Asia, having trained over 30,000 English teachers over the past five years. It also teaches English to over 6 million face-to-face and online learners in Southeast Asia across all ten ASEAN Member States

  • the British Council has led a consortium to deliver an £8.5 million (€10 million) project to support harmonisation of higher education in the ASEAN region

Science and innovation

  • the UK’s Newton Fund will have invested up to £106 million ($132.5 million) by 2021 to support international science and innovation collaborations in Southeast Asia

  • the UK is investing £21 million ($2.84 million) in the Fleming Fund for projects to tackle Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) in Indonesia, Lao PDR and Vietnam. The Fleming Fund also supported AMR projects in Myanmar with the value of £3 million ($4 million), which have now ended

  • the UK Space Agency has supported projects in Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines and Vietnam under the 5 year £152 million ($190 million) International Partnership Programme, which uses the UK space sector’s research and innovation strengths to deliver a sustainable economic or societal benefit to emerging and developing economies
