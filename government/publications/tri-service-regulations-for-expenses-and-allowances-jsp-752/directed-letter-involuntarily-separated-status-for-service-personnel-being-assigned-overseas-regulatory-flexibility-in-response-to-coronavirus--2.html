Directed Letter - involuntarily separated status for service personnel being assigned overseas. Regulatory flexibility in response to coronavirus.

Dated: 15 February 2021

Originally, exceptional authority was granted on 23 Oct 20 to enable any Service Person (SP) who was due to be assigned from the UK to an accompanied assignment overseas and is either a CEA claimant or will have children [footnote 1] accompanying them, to serve unaccompanied. This exceptional authority to serve Involuntary Separated (INVOLSEP) was initially granted up to the end of April 2021 with a review date of February 2021. A review has been carried out and exceptional authority has been granted to extend the original period from the end of Apr 21 to the end of August 2021.

This exceptional authority acknowledges the impact of COVID-19 and associated travel restrictions between the UK and overseas locations and/or the potential imposition of mandatory periods of quarantine or isolation by either the UK government or the governments of host nations overseas. The situation will be reviewed prior to the end of June 2021 at which point the exceptional authority will either be extended or withdrawn on the authority of Armed Forces Remuneration (Allowances).

Those that decide to take up the offer to serve unaccompanied will be entitled to INVOLSEP rates of LSA, unaccompanied rates of LOA, GYH(O) and retain eligibility for movement of Personal Effects (PE) at public expense [footnote 2] and the payment of Disturbance Expense [footnote 3] (DE) for a delayed move in accordance with JSP 752. The immediate family will retain authority to be moved at public expense to the overseas location without the need to submit PACCC casework.

CEA claimants must inform the Unit HR at their future overseas station that they will be serving INVOLSEP, the Unit HR must then inform PACCC assurance immediately for audit purposes.

When this exceptional authority is withdrawn, any SP who has opted to serve INVOLSEP, will be expected to be accompanied by their immediate family at the earliest opportunity, but the SP will retain INVOLSEP status for 3 months from the date of this authority being removed or until the SP is accompanied, whichever is sooner.

Any CEA claimant who is not accompanied by their spouse/civil partner by the commencement of the Spring term 22 will be considered Voluntary Separated (VOLSEP) unless they have submitted casework to the PACCC to remain INVOLSEP.

Due to the vast array of overseas locations, the accommodation options and provision for any SP taking up this INVOLSEP status should be considered on a case by case basis and advice sought if required. Retention of their current SFA will be managed in line with JSP 464, Vol 1, Part 1, para 0725 (f). In the event that personnel are living in Tied Ex-Officio SFA, they will be required to vacate and move to alternative SFA, in line with JSP 464, Vol 1, Part 1, para 0725 (k), entitlement to PE and DE is authorised [footnote 4]. Additionally a review of the accommodation requirements in the overseas location will need to be considered as the unaccompanied SP may no longer have entitlement to SFA whilst concurrently retaining SFA in the UK.

Service personnel who have already claimed DE for the overseas move into public accommodation [footnote 5] may retain eligibility as long as the immediate family joins the Service person at the duty station within the timeframe stipulated within this DL.

This exceptional authority is not automatically available to those already assigned overseas and accompanied by their spouse/civil partner as different packages are already in place to support them. Any retrospective requests to serve unaccompanied should be staffed through Unit HR to the PACCC [footnote 6].

Single Service enquiries should be directed initially to Unit HR staffs who, if necessary, should seek further guidance from their single Service allowance policy staff.
