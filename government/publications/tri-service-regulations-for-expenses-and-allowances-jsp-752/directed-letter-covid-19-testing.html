12 January 2021

In parallel with a recent announcement on COVID-19 testing[footnote 1], [footnote 2], am directed to inform you of the following interim arrangements for reimbursement for COVID-19 testing requirements. This Directed Letter replaces version 1, released on 10 December 2020. Amendments are written in blue. Service personnel (SP) and their immediate families may be eligible to reimburse costs associated with COVID-19 tests in the following circumstances: the mandatory requirement to to carry out a COVID-19 test prior to returning to the UK; the Test to Release scheme; or those that for exceptional reasons, due to a Service requirement, have had to pay for testing. The definition of ‘immediate families’ [footnote 3] can be found at the reference but, for ease, has also been included in the annex. These arrangements are to be applied with an effective date of 1 December 2020 and will remain in place until 30 April 2021 or until rescinded. In some cases, the eligibility period for previous exceptional authority described in v1 of this Directed Letter has been reduced in response to the latest government restrictions.

SP returning to the UK – Mandatory COVID-19 Testing

With effect from 14 January 2021, passengers arriving from all international destinations will be required to present a negative COVID-19 test result before departing for England[footnote 4] to help protect against new strains of coronavirus circulating internationally. This is in addition to the mandatory 10 day self-isolation for arrivals.

SP and their immediate family who are assigned overseas and are required to travel to the UK on duty [footnote 5] can reclaim the costs of one private COVID-19 test per duty journey. SP must demonstrate to the chain of command that they were unable to arrange for a test through Service sources [footnote 6] or a free testing facility provided by the host nation.

Method of claim. If approval is granted, costs are to be reclaimed via JPA using ‘Miscellaneous’ expense type then selecting ‘Medical, Dental, Opticians and Prescription fees’ from the dropdown menu. SP should record “Return to UK COVID-19 Test DAN 18” in the justification box.

SP returning to the UK – Test to Release

The Test to Release Scheme offers individuals an opportunity to reduce the selfisolation period on arrival in the UK. Travellers arriving from a destination not on the Travel Corridor list can pay for a private COVID-19 test after 5 full days of self-isolation. Further details can be found in Defence Advice Note (DAN) 18[footnote 1].

SP Assigned Overseas

SP and their immediate family who are assigned overseas and are required to travel to the UK on duty[footnote 7], and required to isolate on arrival, can reclaim the costs of one private COVID-19 test per duty journey. This exceptional authority is effective between 1 December 2020 and 30 April 2021. Defence will not reimburse testing costs for SP returning to the UK on leave, unless the trip took place between 1 December 2020 and 31 January 2021.

SP returning from temporary duty overseas

SP who are assigned within the UK who, for duty reasons, are required to temporarily travel overseas and on their return to the UK are required to isolate on arrival, can reclaim the costs of one private COVID-19 test per duty journey. This exceptional authority is effective between 1 December 2020 and when the policy is reviewed on 30 April 2021. Defence will not cover test & release costs for UK-based SP who go overseas on holiday.

Non-British passport holding SP

Non-British passport holding SP, and immediate families that permanently reside with them in the UK, that travel on leave to their country of origin between 1 December 2020 and 31 January 2021[footnote 8], can reclaim the cost of one private COVID-19 test per person on their return to the UK.

Method of claim

Costs are to be reclaimed via JPA using ‘Miscellaneous’ expense type then selecting ‘Medical, Dental, Opticians and Prescription fees’ from the dropdown menu[footnote 9]. SP should record “COVID-19 Test & Release DAN 18” in the justification box. Should the need to isolate be removed, Defence will no longer cover the costs of testing.

Service requirement to conduct COVID-19 testing at cost

There have been a small number of occasions whereby SP have been unable for Service reasons to arrange for a test through Service sources[footnote 10] or a free testing facility provided by the host nation, and have been required to pay for COVID-19 tests.

Approval for reimbursements of COVID-19 tests in this instance should be given on a case-by-case basis. TLBs are to satisfy themselves of the host-nation testing requirements[footnote 11] and the Service need for such a test to take place. If approval is granted, costs are to be reclaimed via JPA using ‘Miscellaneous’ expense type then selecting ‘Medical, Dental, Opticians and Prescription fees’ from the dropdown menu. SP should record “TLB approved COVID-19 test” in the justification box.

The terms of this Directed Letter will remain in place until rescinded or replaced by AF Rem Allowances.

JSP 752 definition of ‘immediate family’

In accordance with JSP 752 Chapter 2 Section 1 para 02.0123: The following persons are to be regarded as members of the immediate family of a Service person:

a. Their spouse/civil partner.

b. Any dependant child under the age of 18 years (but see paragraph 02.0109).

c. With the approval of the DBS MIL PERS-MIL OPS PACCC, any dependant son or daughter over 18 and under 21 years not undergoing further education.

d. A son or daughter of 18 years and over who is normally resident in the home of the Service person and who is certified by a medical authority to be suffering from physical or mental infirmity.

e. An unmarried son or daughter, or one who is not in a civil partnership, over the age of 18 years who is normally resident in the home of the Service person provided that for compassionate reasons (e.g. the death or chronic ill health of the spouse/civil partner) it is necessary for that son or daughter to live with the Service person and act as the housekeeper.
