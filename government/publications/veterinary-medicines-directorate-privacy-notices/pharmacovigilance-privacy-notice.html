2. What of my data is being collected and how is it used -What is the legal basis for the processing

Personal data we collect is names, contact details and in rare cases of human adverse events, we may collect medical records. Processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest and in the exercise of official authority vested in the data controller by the Veterinary Medicines Regulations 2013 (SI 2033). This to identify new issues and trends in the existing pattern of adverse events and dealing with them appropriately, to ensure that all currently authorised veterinary medicine products are safe and efficacious to use. We also use the personal data to ensure we can identify and remove duplicate reports from our system.

3. Who will my data be shared with

We will not pass on your information to anyone outside the VMD without your permission. We ask for your contact details so we can get in touch if we need more information about the adverse event. If you do not want us to contact you can say so when you report the adverse event.

We do not share any personal information. We code any health events that occurred following exposure to a veterinary medicine, this may include information from medical records. We then transmit this in an anonymised format; only the individual’s initials and first 2 digits of postcode, to the manufacturer of the product and to the European Medicines Agency central database. They then use the data to carry out statistical analyses to try to identify any emerging trends with a particular product.

4. If you are relying on my consent to process my data, can I withdraw my consent

You have the right to withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on your consent.

5. How long will my data be held for

We will hold this data for at least the lifetime of the product concerned plus a further 20 years.

6. What will happen if I don’t provide the data

Failure to provide this information will mean that we are less able to identify new issues and trends in the existing pattern of adverse events and deal with them appropriately, to ensure that all currently authorised veterinary medicine products are safe and efficacious to use.

7. Will my data be used for automated decision-making or profiling

The provision of the information you provide is not connected with individual decision making; making a decision solely by automated means without any human involvement, or profiling; automated processing of personal data to evaluate certain things about an individual.

8. Will my data be transferred outside of the EEA - If it will, how will it be protected

The data you provide will not be transferred outside the European Economic Area.

9. What are my rights

A list of your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation, the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018), is accessible on the Information Commissioners Office (ICO).

10. How do I complain

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the ICO, the supervisory authority, at any time. Should you wish to exercise that right full details are available on the ICO website.
