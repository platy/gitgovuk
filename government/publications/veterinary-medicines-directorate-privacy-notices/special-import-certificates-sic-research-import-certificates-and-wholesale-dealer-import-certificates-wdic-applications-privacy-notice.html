2. What of my data is being collected and how is it used? What is the legal basis for the processing?

Personal data we collect are Certificate holder name, RCVS membership number and employers address. In some cases we collect the animal owners name and address. Processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest and in the exercise of official authority vested in the data controller by the Veterinary Medicines Regulations 2013 (SI 2033) to authorise Special Import Certificates (SIC), Research Import Certificates and Wholesale Dealer Import Certificates (WDIC).

3. Who will my data be shared with

We share this information with other teams in Defra, other government departments (Home Office and MHRA) and the Devolved Administrations so we can inform these departments about unauthorised products that are imported into their regions

4. If you are relying on my consent to process my data, can I withdraw my consent?

You have the right to withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on your consent.

5. How long will my data be held for

We will hold this data for a minimum of one year. We then archive it.

6. What will happen if I don’t provide the data

Failure to provide this information will mean that we are less able to authorise Special Import Certificates (SIC), Wholesale Dealer Import Certificates (WDIC) and Research Import certificates (RIC).

7. Will my data be used for automated decision-making or profiling

The provision of the information you provide is not connected with individual decision making (making a decision solely by automated means without any human involvement) or profiling (automated processing of personal data to evaluate certain things about an individual).

8. Will my data be transferred outside of the EEA - If it will, how will it be protected

The data you provide will not be transferred outside the European Economic Area.

9. What are my rights

A list of your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation, the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018), is accessible at the Information Commissioners Office (ICO).

10. How do I complain

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the ICO, the supervisory authority, at any time. Should you wish to exercise that right full details are available their website.

Our Personal information charter explains more about how we treat your personal information.
