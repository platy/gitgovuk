1. Before you start

This guide is for respondent solicitors. Before you can use financial remedy services on MyHMCTS, you and/or your organisation must have completed other steps:

  1. Set up a HMCTS Payment by Account (so that you can pay any relevant fees)
  2. Registered your organisation with MyHMCTS
  3. Set up your MyHMCTS user account

The MyHMCTS guidance has further information and instructions. If your organisation has an account already, contact the administrator to arrange for your account creation.

Once you have a MyHMCTS account, you can sign in using your email address and password.

If you need additional help with MyHMCTS email MyHMCTSsupport@justice.gov.uk. We aim to respond within 5 working days.

Before you can manage a case, your firm’s case administrator must first assign the case to you. See our Accept a case and assign it to a solicitor guide for the steps they need to take.

If you are nominated as the respondent solicitor but your case administrator cannot find the case, it means either:

  • the case has not yet been served on you, or
  • the applicant was not able to add your details to the case to give you digital access to the file

In these circumstances you will need to continue to correspond with the court by paper. You will receive updates on your case, orders and letters by post.

2. Find your cases and update your details

Find a case

1. Once you have signed in, you will see a case list with all the cases that have been assigned to you.

2. To find the case you want to manage, use the case list filters and then open the case by selecting the link in the ‘Case Reference’ column.

3. Alternatively, select ‘Find case’ to search using information from the case, such as the applicant or respondent details, or case/solicitor references.

4. Select the appropriate jurisdiction and case type, add the relevant details to the field you want to search and select ‘Apply’. Then open the case by selecting the link in the ‘Case Reference’ column.

5. You will then see the case dashboard – several different tabs that show information about the case. The dashboard will open on the ‘History’ tab, which lists the actions already taken on the case.

Update your details

1. If needed, you can update your email address associated to the case. To do this, go up to ‘Next step’ and select ‘Update Respondent Details’ from the dropdown menu. Then select ‘Go’.

2. On the ‘Update Respondent Details’ screen, enter the updated email address then select ‘Yes’ and ‘Continue’.

3. You will then be asked to check your answers. If you need to amend anything before you submit, select ‘Change’. This will take you back to the previous screen to allow you to change your answers. Once you are happy with your updates, select ‘Submit’.

4. You will then be taken back to the history tab in the case dashboard, where you can see the case has now been updated with your details.

5. You can see the respondent’s and your details in the ‘Respondent’ tab.

3. Upload case files for a hearing

1. When the case is listed for the first hearing you will get a notification by email. This notification will explain that you can find the notice of first appointment in the ‘Case documents’ tab. The notice will explain what to do next, including which documents you must file with the court and serve on the other party. There will be contact information if you need help.

2. You will also find forms A, C and G in the ‘Case documents’ tab.

3. To upload your documents in preparation for the hearing, select ‘Upload Case Files’ from the ‘Next step’ dropdown list and then select ‘Go’.

4. To add a document to the case file, select ‘Add New’.

5. A document upload function will now appear on the screen. Select browse and find the file you want to upload. Then choose the document type from the dropdown list.

6. Select that the evidence is provided on behalf of the respondent and whether it should be kept confidential. If you mark the document as confidential, the applicant solicitor will not be able to see this even after you have shared your documents. Then select ‘Continue’.

7. Repeat the last 3 steps to add more documents. You can add as many documents as you wish.

8. You will then be asked to check your answers. If you need to change any documents you have uploaded, select ‘Change’. This will take you back to the previous screen to allow you to change your documents. Once you are happy with your updates, select ‘Submit’.

9. You will then be taken back to the case dashboard, where you can see the uploaded documents listed in the ‘Respondent Documents’ tab.

10. You will be able to see the documents the applicant has shared in the ‘shared documents tab.

4. Share documents with the applicant’s solicitor

1. The applicant’s solicitor will not be able to see your documents listed in the ‘Respondents Documents’ tab. Any documents marked as confidential cannot be shared with the other party but the judge and caseworker can see them.

2. Contact the court if you need to remove a document from the file.

3. To share documents with the applicant’s solicitor, select ‘Share documents with applicant’ from the ‘Next step’ dropdown list.

4. On the next 2 screens select ‘Yes’ and ‘Continue’, then ‘Submit’.

5. Your shared documents will now be visible in the ‘Shared documents’ tab.

6. If you upload further documents, repeat steps 3 and 4 to share these with the applicant’s solicitor.

5. Upload the hearing bundle

1. To upload the hearing bundle, select ‘Manage Hearing Bundles’ from the next step dropdown. Then select ‘Go’.

2. Select ‘Add new’ and enter the date to which the bundle relates.

3. Choose whether it is a financial dispute resolution hearing.

4. Select ‘Choose file’ and browse to find the bundle document.

5. Add a description, if relevant. Then select ‘Continue’.

6. To update the bundle, follow the same steps and upload the updated bundle or enter a new hearing date if incorrect.

7. To remove a bundle, select and open the right one from those filed. Then select ‘Remove’ against the bundle you want to remove.

8. To confirm you are sure you want to remove the bundle, select ‘Remove’ on the pop-up screen.

6. Upload orders to the system after a hearing

1. If the court decides that you need to draft an order, you will get a notification by email. This notification will explain what happens next and include contact information if you need help.

2. To upload your draft order, select ‘Upload Draft Order’ from the ‘Next step’ dropdown list in the case dashboard and then select ‘Go’.

3. Select ‘Add new’ and then choose ‘Draft order’ from the ‘Purpose of Document’ dropdown list.

4. Browse for and select the file. Then select ‘Continue’ then ‘Submit’.

5. If the judge does not approve the order, you may need to resubmit it – the court will notify you. You will be able to view the judge’s reason in the ‘Orders’ tab.

6. Repeat steps 2 to 4 to resubmit the order. At step 3, select ‘Resubmitted Draft Order’ from the ‘Purpose of Document’ dropdown list.

7. When the draft order is approved, the sealed version can be found in the orders tab.

8. The notice of hearing can be found in the scheduling and listing screen.

7. Create a general application within proceedings

This should be used for general applications only – for example, an application to adjourn a hearing.

1. Select ‘Create General Application’ from the ‘Next step’ dropdown and then select ‘Go’.

2. Select ‘Respondent’ from the ‘Application received from’ options. Complete the other fiends as appropriate and then select ‘Continue’.

3. Upload a PDF copy of the application form (for example the D11 form). Select ‘Continue’.

4. If required, upload a draft order to accompany the application. Select ‘Continue’.

5. Check your answers and then select ‘Submit’.

The court may contact you to arrange for payment of the application fee if applicable. Once a decision is made on the application, the application outcome (for example, an order and a copy of the application and draft order) will be posted to you.

8. Submit a notice of change

You can submit a notice of change (or acting) on MyHMCTS for cases issued online or when a case is issued on paper but then uploaded to MyHMCTS by the case worker.

Use a notice of change through MyHMCTS to:

  • change the solicitor or firm on the case, or
  • add a solicitor where the respondent was previously unrepresented

If you wish to stop representing a client, you need to make a request to the local financial remedy centre.

If a solicitor being added is not registered on MyHMCTS, they will not be able to access the case.

See the case administration guide for how to submit a notice of change or acting.

9. Get help with MyHMCTS

Manage case and manage organisation queries

The MyHMCTS service is supported by the My HMCTS support team. They will assist if you are having difficulties or errors with things such as logging in, or assigning cases. Contact them at MyHMCTSsupport@justice.gov.uk.

Queries about cases lodged on the portal

If you have are a party to a case, or intend to lodge a consent case on the portal and require assistance, please contact the CTSC at ContactFinancialRemedy@justice.gov.uk or 0300 303 0642

Queries about a contested financial remedy application

Contact the financial remedy court managing the case. Contacts can be found at HMCTS Financial Remedy Centres.

Queries on the financial remedy portal

If you are seeing an error message in the service that cannot be resolved, or if you have feedback on how we can improve, contact the HMCTS Financial Remedy Service Team at HMCTSFinancialRemedy@justice.gov.uk.
