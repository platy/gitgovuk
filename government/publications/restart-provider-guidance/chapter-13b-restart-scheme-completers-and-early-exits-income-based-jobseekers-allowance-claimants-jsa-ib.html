Introduction

13.01. This chapter sets out the steps that you and Jobcentre Plus will take to ensure that all Restart Scheme Participants exit the scheme at the appropriate stage in their journey by either:

  • Completing their allotted time
  • Achieving an employed or self-employed Job Outcome
  • Deemed to be an “Early Exit” due to a change of your Participants personal circumstances

13.02. A Participant is deemed as completing the Restart Scheme (a Completer) in either of the following circumstances:

  • Participant reaches end of allotted time having not achieved an employed or self-employed Job Outcome
  • Participant achieves employed or self-employed Job Outcome

Restart Scheme completers where Participant reaches end of allotted time (365 calendar days) without having achieved an employed / self-employed job outcome

13.03. Your Participant will have completed their time on the scheme where they have reached the end of their allotted time and have not achieved either an employed or self-employed Job Outcome. For further information on relating to employed or self-employed Job Outcome validation requirements. See Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 17 – Funding Model and Claim Process.

Actions

13.04. DWP Provider Referral and Payment System (PRaP) will automatically calculate the end date for your Restart Participant for 365 calendar days based on the start date you input following the initial meeting. The line will automatically close and therefore become an exit on PRaP on day 366.

13.05. You will be responsible for recording and updating any of your internal systems as appropriate.

Restart Scheme completers where Participant achieves an Employed Job Outcome

13.06. Your Participant will have completed their time on the Restart Scheme where they have achieved the earnings threshold applied to an Employed Job Outcome.

  • Your Participant meets or exceeds the pre-set earnings threshold as outlined in Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 17 – Funding Model and Claim Process
  • The Employed Job Outcome payment is generated and made to you the provider via PRaP
  • PRaP automatically generates your Participants exit reason and end date

Restart Scheme Completers where Participant achieves a Self Employed Job Outcome

13.07. Your Participant will have completed their time on the Restart Scheme where they have achieved the durational threshold applied to a Self-Employed Job Outcome.

  • Your Participant achieves 6 months of Self-Employment
  • You submit Self-Employment Job Outcome claim on PRaP
  • Self-Employment Job Outcome payment is generated via PRaP
  • Self-Employment Job Outcome payment generates your Participants exit reason and date on PRaP

Action

13.08. You will be notified via PRaP where your Participant has achieved the earnings threshold applied to the employed Job Outcome or the durational threshold applicable to the self-employed Job Outcome. The provision line will automatically close in PRaP when the final outcome payment has been made to you the provider via PRaP.

13.09. You will only be responsible for recording and updating any appropriate tracking activity you undertake on your internal systems as required.

Please Note: There is no requirement for you to deliver any support to your Participant beyond their allotted time on the Restart Scheme. However, regular contact with your Participant throughout the contracted tracking period could enable timely, tailored and beneficial intervention e.g. where your Participant may be at risk of falling out of employment.

See Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 17 – Funding Model and Claim Process for further detailed guidance relating to Participant tracking periods and potential outcome validations.

Early Exits due to Change of Circumstances

13.10. There is no responsibility for you to action any change of circumstance notified directly by your Participant, however, you should encourage your Participant to use the JSA Helpline or inform their reporting Jobcentre Plus in writing, of any change of circumstance which may impact their entitlement to benefit.

13.11. You are required to take appropriate PRaP activity should you receive a CEPD1 from Jobcentre Plus relating to your Participant prior to their 365 days allotted time on the Restart Scheme being achieved due to the following:

  • your Participant is deceased
  • your Participant moves outside England / Wales for period equal to or longer than remainder of allotted time
  • your Participant is in prison for period equal to or longer than remainder of allotted time
  • your Participant has terminal illness and chooses to leave the Restart programme

13.12. The Restart Scheme should be delivered as prescribed until such time that Jobcentre Plus advises you that your Participants change of circumstance has been notified, accepted and any subsequent conditionality impact has been updated.

Actions

13.13. You must only action the exit on PRaP upon receipt of a CEPD1 from Jobcentre Plus. The form must clearly state one of the early exit reasons listed above. Should it fail to do so, contact your Single Point of Contact (SPOC) in Jobcentre Plus.

Please note a PRaP 14 is not necessary for early exits. You will be responsible for manual input to PRaP of any notified change of your Participant’s circumstance as follows:

Participant is Deceased

13.14. You are required to take appropriate PRaP activity should you receive a CEPD1 from Jobcentre Plus relating to this change of circumstance as follows:

  • You should input the outcome ‘Deceased’ using Leaver Code 43 with the exit date as date of death (It is not input automatically or by Jobcentre Plus)

Participant moves outside England / Wales for period equal to or longer than remainder of allotted time on the Restart Scheme

13.15. You are required to take appropriate PRaP activity should you receive a CEPD1 from Jobcentre Plus relating to this change of circumstance as follows:

  • You should input the outcome ‘Exceptional Circumstances’ using Leaver Code 46 with the exit date as leaving England/Wales (It is not input automatically or by Jobcentre Plus)

Participant is in prison for a period equal to or longer than the remainder of their allotted time on the Restart Scheme

13.16. You are required to take appropriate PRaP activity should you receive a CEDP1 from Jobcentre Plus relating to this change of circumstance as follows.

  • You should input the outcome ‘Exceptional Circumstances’ using Leaver Code 46 with the exit date as date of imprisonment (It is not input automatically or by Jobcentre Plus)

Participant has a terminal illness and chooses to leave the Restart Scheme

13.17. You are required to take appropriate PRaP activity should you receive a CEPD1 change of circumstance from Jobcentre Plus detailing your Participants decision to exit the Restart programme as follows:

  • You should input the outcome ‘Exceptional Circumstances’ using Leaver Code 46 with the exit date as date of decision to exit made by your Participant (It is not input automatically or by Jobcentre Plus)

Please Note: Please refer to Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 7B – Change of Circumstances for action should you receive a notification directly from your Participant or your Participants representative prior to receiving a CEPD1 from Jobcentre Plus.
