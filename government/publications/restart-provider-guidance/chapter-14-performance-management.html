Overview

14.01. This chapter covers how we will manage performance of the Restart Scheme. To do this, we will apply our Performance Management Intervention Regime (PMIR) - a structured and escalating course, over four levels, of interaction and intervention, focusing on both a qualitative and quantitative assessment of your performance.

14.02. Working with you, this regime aims to ensure the quality delivery of provision, looking closely at customer service, job outcomes and achieving value for money for the taxpayer, all as outlined in your contract.

14.03. As part of the PMIR you will need to have in place a Provider Action Plan which should clearly focus on your top priority areas for improvement, including appropriate time-bound actions to address and resolve identified performance deficiencies. An example of a standard Provider Action Plan along with guidance for completion is included at Annex 4 and Annex 5 respectively.

Please Note: You must have digital solutions in place for circumstances whereby Face to Face meetings in person are unable to take place, for example. COVID-19, Local Restrictions. The requirement to adopt this digital solution will be advised by or made in consultation with DWP.

Further information on the PMIR is in Annex 1.

Working Together

Contract Performance Reviews

14.04. An integral part of the PMIR is the Contract Performance Review (CPR). These meetings take place on a regular basis, usually each month, and are chaired by your DWP Performance Manager (PM). Local colleagues from Jobcentre Plus may also attend these meetings.

14.05. At least five calendar days prior to each CPR, you will need to upload your Provider Action Plan to the Jaggaer system. Information from your Monthly Performance Return, your Action Plan and our Management Information will form a key basis for the discussion. Your performance will be viewed against the performance metrics and your obligations, as detailed in your contract.

14.06. If you have sub-contractors within your delivery model for this contract, your responsibility and how you manage their delivery will also form part of the discussions, including, for example, how you plan to address any poor performance. You will need to ensure that all systems and processes used for the monitoring and recording of performance are robust and provide a clear audit trail of evidence providing DWP with the assurance that you and your supply chain are delivering the contract as required.

14.07. More detailed information about CPRs can be found in Annex 2.

Performance Compliance Officers (PCOs) and their checks

14.08. Performance Compliance Officers (PCOs) work closely with the PM and undertake regular evidence-based reviews to check your performance against Customer Service Standards 2-7 and provide assurance that you, as a provider, are adhering to the delivery model set out in your contract.

14.09. PCOs will check a sample of Restart Scheme Participant records held by you, to determine what required activities have been undertaken, and when. The scope of these compliance checks will be shared with you.

14.10. The checking sample will be selected randomly using DWPâs Provider Referral and Payments (PRaP) system. The frequency, periodicity and size of the samples may change throughout the life of the contract and, for example, where a Provider holds two Restart Scheme contracts, a single sample may be drawn from across both contracts.

14.11. Details of the sample to be checked, will be uploaded to the Jaggaer system no less than five working days prior to the checks being carried out, see example at Annex 3a. We expect that you will undertake your own compliance checks on the sample and upload the completed sample to the Jaggaer system at least 24 hours in advance of the agreed meeting date. This will allow time for the PCO to review your findings and ensure that you and the PCO can have a fully informed discussion as the checks are undertaken.

14.12. Our checks may be conducted through the use of digital channels or by face to face means, or a combination of both, and we will let you know the logistics in advance. For example, digital checks may be conducted using Microsoft Teams, where you will be expected to share your screen with the PCO completing the evidence checks. If the checks are face to face, these may be centralised in a DWP location.

14.13. All evidence must be available on your IT system for inspection and you should utilise equipment which can be used remotely and which complies with the relevant DWP policies for face to face and digital use, as defined in your contractâs Terms and Conditions. In the case of face to face checks, if the location to be used is a DWP location, you should bring the necessary equipment to enable the PCO to carry out centralised checks of your systems - this includes the provision of any necessary electronic evidence that is required by the PCO to undertake the checks effectively.

14.14. The findings from the checks will be shared with you and relevant DWP stakeholders on the Summary Report. An example of a completed Summary Report can be found at Annex 3b. This will usually be within 12 working days of the checks being completed. CPRs will usually include a discussion about the PCO checks, especially any issues that have been identified and the actions that you are undertaking to resolve these. These actions should be included on your Action Plan. An example of an Action Plan is included at Annex 4, with guidance on the completion of this shown at Annex 5.

14.18. Your PM will provide you with a summary of the findings from these activities, and they will discuss with you at your next CPR or other appropriate forum. Any remedial action you plan to do is agreed and added to your action plan.

Performance and the Performance Metrics

14.24. From the start of your contract, we expect you to achieve each of the Performance Metrics. These are the:

  • Delivery Fee Standards
  • Customer Service Standards (CSSs)
  • Performance Indicator (PI)
  • Minimum Performance Level
  • Tender Performance Level

Performance Delivery and Expectations

14.25. Throughout the life of the contract, we will review your performance in each of the above areas. To do this, we will use our available management information, whether from our systems and/or from the PCO checks, the Management Information that you provide, and with the approach as detailed in our PMIR.

14.26. If any of the above Metrics are not achieved, whether thatâs on an in-month, rolling basis, cohort, or cumulative basis, we may consider using an appropriate contract lever as detailed within your contract. We may also consider recovering any costs we incur as a result of our performance improvement activity with you, again as detailed in your contract.

Delivery Fee Standards

14.27. For the first year of your contract, the Delivery Fee that you receive will be dependent on your staffing levels covered in the next two paragraphs and also the achievement of CSSs covered in para 14.30-14.36, and if appropriate this includes those of your supply chain partners.

14.28. On the first working day each month, you will need to provide DWP, via the Jaggaer system, with detailed information about the staff Full Time Equivalent (FTE), in a management or direct delivery role, who are employed on your contract. We will provide a standardised template for this purpose. We will compare this information against the agreed monthly staffing levels that you detailed in your contract.

14.29. If any monthly return does not show the numbers, or level of staff FTE that are expected to be working on this contract, we will consider reducing the monthly Delivery Fee should the % of staff FTE fall below 95%. Further information about forfeiture of the monthly Delivery Fee can be found in the Restart Specification Section 4 paras 4.47-4.51.

Customer Service Standards (CSSs)

14.30. There are eight CSSs, that we expect you to deliver against, within this contract. These are shown in Annex 6.

14.31. We will use a mixture of Management Information held on our systems, findings from our PCO checks, and from regular customer-focused surveys, to review your performance against the CSSs.

14.32. The CSSs focus on key elements in the participantâs journey on this provision, with the CSS1 looking at the time taken from the point of the referral from Jobcentre Plus to completing the initial face to face meeting â performance here will be determined by the management information derived from the PRaP system.

14.33. The remaining CSSs, with the exception of the one focusing on Customer Satisfaction (CSS8), will be measured through structured and periodic checks conducted by our PCOs. These checks, of a sample of cases and usually each month, will look to assure you are delivering your service in accordance with your delivery model and, as a minimum the monthly sample of checks are compliant in at least 95% of cases. Where more than one of these checks is marked as a âfailâ, we will look to undertake further work, which may include more checks.

14.34. The final CSS8 is focused on Customer Satisfaction rates and will be measured through a survey of a number of your participants. We expect to see a high degree of customer satisfaction and your performance here will be assessed, on a relative basis, against the performance in each of the provisionâs CPAs. The surveys will be carried out by an independent third party who is contracted to DWP for this purpose.

14.35. Performance against each CSS will form a key part of your CPR discussion with your PM each month.

14.36. Each CSS will be treated independently, therefore if your performance against any of the CSSs falls below the level that we expect, we will consider whether the amount of your monthly Delivery Fee is deferred, forfeited or whether other contractual action is appropriate. Further information about the deferral or forfeiture of part of the monthly Delivery Fee in relation to CSS 1-7 can be found within Restart Specification Chapter 4 paras 4.52-4.56.

Performance Indicator (PI)

14.37. As one of the Performance Metrics, the Earnings Performance Indicator (PI) provides an early insight on the number of job outcomes you may achieve in the coming months.

14.38. This PI will show the number of participants, each month, that need to start work and receive their first earnings so that the relevant Required Number of Outcomes (RNOs), is achieved. We will regularly review the PI and if we see that the level is not being achieved or we believe that without further intervention, the PI will fall to a level that means the RNOs wonât be achieved, then we may issue a Performance Indicator Course of Action Notice (PICAN). Further details on this Notice can be found in your contract.

Tender Performance Level (TPL) and Minimum Performance Level (MPL)

14.39. As part of your contract, we expect that you will achieve your Tender Performance Level (TPL) and accordingly, our Minimum Performance Level (MPL). The TPL is part of the offer you made within your successful bid and is the percentage of starts you believe will move into employment and achieve the Earnings Threshold. This threshold is calculated at 16 hours per week for 26 weeks at the National Living Wage (NLW).

14.40. The MPL is set as our minimum expectation that 25% of starts will achieve the Earnings Threshold.

14.41. For each of the TPL and MPL, we will apply a performance curve, or expectation for the month in which we expect the threshold to be reached for a given percentage of the participants who have entered employment. This helps to calculate the RNOs in any given month. Further detail on how this calculation is made can be found in Annex 8 of the Specification.

14.42. If the RNOs expected for the MPL or TPL is not achieved in any given period, we will consider intervention action as outlined in our PMIR and/or contractual action as detailed in your contract.

Relative Performance

14.43. Across all of the Performance Metrics, and not just for CSS8, we will review performance on a relative basis.

14.44. We will use ranking tables to monitor performance across CPAs and Providers, to drive continuous improvement within this provision.

RNOs, cohorts, and Management Information

14.45. As outlined in earlier paragraphs within this chapter, achieving the RNOs is how we will determine if you are meeting the MPL and TPL for this provision.

14.46. As well as the performance curve, a percentage of the MPL and TPL that is to be achieved increases year on year, is also part of the calculation of RNOs. This percentage starts at 85% of the MPL and TPL to be achieved in the first year, rising to 90% for year 2, 100% for year 3 and 115% in years 4 and 5. It is important that you ensure that as one year moves into the next, that sufficient outcomes are being achieved to prevent any possible shortfall in the coming year.

More detailed information about this calculation, can be found in Annex 8 of the Specification.

14.47. Throughout the life of your contract, we will measure your RNO performance against the MPL and TPL on a cohort basis, viewed in-month, on a rolling 3-month, rolling 12-month, and cumulative basis from day one of the contract.

14.48. Performance data will be taken from HMRC data for employed participants, our PRaP system and, in the case of self-employed participants, from outcome claims submitted by you.

14.49. Each month, we will produce a Management Information Pack detailing performance against the PI, MPL, TPL, CSS1 and CSS8 which we will share with you. You must treat this information, and any other information for this provision that you have access to, as confidential and for your sole use.

Annex 1: Performance Management Intervention Regime (PMIR)

We operate a robust Performance Management Regime (PMIR), the aim to provide support, and hold you accountable for achievement of the performance metrics stipulated in your contract.

You are expected to meet as a minimum, the performance levels attached to each of the performance metrics as stipulated in your contract, and we consider formal action where such performance levels are not met.

You will need to provide us with a named contact who will work with our DWP PM to ensure the performance metrics at CPA level are achieved.

There are four levels of the PMIR which can run independently or concurrently and we will be able to take action at any level, at any time for example we will be entitled to take formal action at Level 4 whether or not it has taken all of the steps outlined at levels 1, 2 and/or 3.

The PMIR framework will be used to address under performance, however nothing in the PMIR will prejudice the remedies available to DWP for failure to meet any Performance Metrics.

  • Level 1: Standard Action: DWP Performance Manager Led
  • Level 2: Enhanced action: DWP Performance Manager/Senior Performance Manager Led
  • Level 3: Informal action: DWP Senior Management Led, DWP National Provision Lead
  • Level 4: Formal action: DWP Senior Management Led, DWP National Provision Lead and DWP Commercial Lead

Your CPA must have in place a provider action plan submitted by your Contract Operational Lead to the PM regardless of what intervention level you are in. The provider action plan is required to be in place immediately following the contract implementation period (from contract go live).

The purpose of the Provider Action Plan is to provide us with assurance that you have identified the top priority areas for improvement. All actions in the plan should be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic and Time-bound (SMART) and any longer term actions should incorporate SMART milestone targets. It may also include incremental performance and pipeline data that may lead to outcomes to improve the achievement of the performance metrics in line with the Contract.

An example of a standard Provider Action Plan along with guidance for completion is included at Annex 3 and Annex 4 respectfully. Although we strongly encourage use of the standard Action Plan, you may use your own format provided it covers as a minimum all elements of the standard Action Plan.

At each level of intervention, your PM will continue to work closely with you with, the frequency of meetings, scope and types of activity increasing where improvements are not seen, examples of these activities are listed within Intervention Level 1 below.

Your PM will hold regular CPRs with you at CPA level focussing on the performance against quantitative and qualitative aspects of the contract including, but not limited to in-month, rolling 3-months, rolling 12-month cohort or cumulative basis from Day 1 of the contract. More detail on CPRs is covered in Annex 2.

The CPRs will be arranged by your PM, Jobcentre Plus representatives and other DWP staff may also attend these meetings, for example PCO or SPM Manager. Your first CPR will be arranged during the contract implementation period.

Level 1: Standard action: DWP Performance Management led

Your PM will hold regular CPR meetings with you which will focus on achieving the contractual Performance Metrics. This will include for example

  • Analysis of the DWP Management Information Pack;
  • Analysis of the MPR which is submitted by you on a monthly basis;
  • Provider Action Plan;
  • Feedback from Jobcentre Plus;
  • PM Record Checks (participant record checks);
  • PCO checks/reports;
  • Go-Look-See/Deep Dives) and Unannounced Provider Visits

Whilst at Level 1, if it is determined that you may not achieve the performance levels expected, your PM may undertake informal action to escalate you to Level 2 intervention and you will be notified of this in writing.

You will be required to enhance your Provider Action Plan, to capture all agreed actions for performance improvement. You will also be required to provide a flightpath including incremental performance and pipeline data that may lead to outcomes to improve the achievement of the performance levels and CSSs in line with the Contract with the format to be agreed with your PM.

Some of the Key activities which may be utilised in level 2 are as listed within level 1 intervention.

Level 3: Informal Action: DWP Senior Management Led â DWP National Provision Lead

If the remedial actions at Level 2 were unable to lift your performance in order to meet the performance levels and CSSs in the Contract, the DWP SPM can propose escalation to Level 3.

You will be invited to a meeting to discuss your performance with Senior Managers and you will receive a Management letter requesting that your performance improves. At this stage you will be informed that formal action could be taken in the event that performance doesnât improve.

Some of the key activities which may be utilised are as listed in Level 1 intervention.

Level 4: Formal Action: DWP Senior Management Led â DWP National Provision Lead and DWP Commercial Lead

If you fail to meet the required standards at Level 3, DWP EAD and Commercial Directorate will take formal action to address the performance issues, considering and applying, where applicable, all the remedies available to DWP under the contract.

Introduction

This guidance has been produced to support the completion and application of the standard Provider Action Plan (AP). You are encouraged to use of the standard AP. An AP should be in place unless you are notified otherwise.

General principles for completion

The AP should be succinct and SMART (Specific; Measureable; Achievable; Realistic; Time-bound) focussing on activities to improve performance, delivery and address key risk areas at CPA level, it should be focussed avoiding lengthy narratives.

Your PM will review your completed AP to ensure that it addresses areas of concern, that the content is fit for purposes and focusses on appropriate actions to address and resolve identified performance deficiencies.

The AP should be a living document which is reviewed monthly, with completed actions removed and new actions added as risks/issues arise. Actions should only be removed or added by mutual consent of the Provider Accountable Lead and DWP PM (usually at the CPR). A new version should be forwarded by the provider each month, and previous versions archived.

You should upload your updated monthly AP to the Jaggaer system five working days prior to the CPR.

Header Table completion

You should complete the header table with details specific to the contract: i.e. Date, Provider name, CPA, Provider Accountable Lead, PM and SPM.

The âdate updatedâ box should initially show the date of first completion and then be amended to reflect the date of each subsequent monthly update.

The âProvider Accountable Leadâ named on the AP should have overall accountability regardless of who is undertaking each action on the plan within your organisation. This should normally be your contract operational lead who attends CPR meetings. They will have overall responsibility for delivering the plan and reporting progress against actions and milestones on a monthly basis.

Monthly Contract Update completion

Each month you should complete a narrative giving an overview of current contract performance and activity, highlighting any changes since the previous monthâs update and reasons why. This may include an explanation of any variance from the flight path (if applicable), an account of what went well and not so well (and reasons for it) and an assessment of any emerging risks and opportunities identified. You should additionally highlight any action being taken to rectify issues and mitigate risks.

Specific Areas Requiring Action

You should identify all areas of contract concern impacting performance and delivery. Where you are subject to an intervention regime higher than Level 1, AP should include actions specifically to address the reasons you have been placed in the regime level.

The AP template provides a tab for each area identified which should be numbered sequentially. You should add further tabs as required as new areas are identified. It should be clear what the area is to be addressed and what the outcome objective is, i.e. the specific performance uplift expected.

Every area identified should then have corrective action(s). It is suggested that the AP should contain a maximum of 5 top priority areas for improvement/corrective actions, e.g. job starts, RNOs, staffing, caseload sizes (this list is not exhaustive). Each action should be clear and SMART, showing how they are to be achieved. If someone other than the Provider Accountable Lead will be responsible for undertaking an action, the Provider may wish to provide a name, however, the Provider Accountable Lead shall have ultimate responsibility for delivering on the actions and providing progress updates.

Avoid lengthy documents being created by long, narrative filled columns, there is a column to the right of the actions for the provider to specify a target date for completion of each action.

Each month you should complete an update on progress made against the action(s). Once an Action is completed, this will be removed the following month.

Annex 7: Electronic Signature Information

You may record a Restart Scheme Participantâs signature electronically in the circumstances as specified within your Restart Scheme Contracts:

  • At the Initial Face to Face meeting following the Participant being issued with a hard-copy induction pack

  • Following completion of the Participantâs Initial SMART Action Plan

  • Following updates to the Participantâs SMART Action Plan

Restart Scheme Providers may determine themselves which method of obtaining an electronic signature they utilise. There are several options listed below, however this list is not exhaustive, and other digital technologies may become available, providing viable alternatives to the options listed:

  • A Participantâs wet signature and date could be recorded on a paper copy of the document. This could then be scanned, creating an electronic version of the wet signature; the paper version would then be destroyed. This could be further substantiated through records of corresponding appointment dates and times, etc.

  • A Participantâs electronic signature could be recorded via a tablet or other touchscreen device; signing their name on the touchscreen, and therefore directly onto the document requiring a signature, the Participantâs electronic signature would be captured. The time and date that the document was amended would be recorded on the document and this could be further substantiated through corresponding appointment dates and times, etc.

  • A Participantâs electronic signature could be recorded through an e-signature app where document templates can be created, uploaded, and then signed digitally. An embedded audit trail is created offering an added layer of eSignature security providing proof of who signed the document, and when

It is the responsibility of the Provider to ensure that their proposed method is in line with their Restart Contract and Security Plan. The Security Plan should be updated to reflect these new arrangements and must incorporate specific policy around the processing of this type of data, if not previously included.

Providers will be required to provide assurance (for PCO/PAT and audit purposes) that their proposed method can demonstrate that the signature is bona fide and that the date/ time the electronic signature was obtained can be verified as a minimum, to the same standard their wet signature confirmation process would have demonstrated.

Provider Action Required

Prior to utilising the option of recording a Participantâs signature electronically Providers are asked to inform DWP of their chosen method. Informing us of your chosen method will help to avoid confusion during PCO checks etc.

To do this Providers should complete the Electronic Signature template and return to restart.providerenquiries@dwp.gov.uk

Providers are asked to await email confirmation from DWP that the completed template has been received, before implementing their chosen method.

A copy of the completed template will be retained by DWP.
