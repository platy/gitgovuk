Introduction

9.1. The purpose of this chapter is to provide guidance to enable you to raise a compliance doubt when:

  • a Potential Participant has failed to attend their Initial Face to Face Meeting which you have mandated them to, or
  • a Participant has failed to undertake an activity during their time on the Restart Scheme which you have mandated them to

9.2. The process for raising a compliance doubt in these two instances are the same. For the purposes of this chapter, references to raising a compliance doubt where a Participant fails to comply with an activity includes where a Potential Participant fails to attend the Initial Face to Face Meeting.

9.3. This chapter only applies to activities which you have mandated the Participant or Potential Participant to undertake and they have failed to comply. You are not required to raise a compliance doubt for voluntary activities which the Participant or Potential Participant has failed to undertake.

9.4. This chapter must be read in conjunction with Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 8A – Mandating a Participant to undertake activity and Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 10 – Participant compliance.

Sanctions

9.5. A sanction is a financial reduction that is imposed on a Universal Credit claimant who doesn’t comply with their requirements without good reason.

9.6. The types of activity you may mandate your Restart Scheme Participant to undertake attracts a low level sanction, where they fail to comply without good reason.

9.7. A low level sanction comprises two elements – an open-ended sanction which is in place until the Participant has met the compliance condition, plus a fixed period of 7, 14 or 28 calendar days.

9.8. Further information regarding compliance conditions can be found in Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 8A – Mandating Participants to undertake activity.

9.9. Further information regarding low level sanctions can be found on GOV.UK.

When to raise a compliance doubt

9.10. Where your Participant is mandated to undertake an activity and they fail to comply without good reason, this is a sanctionable failure. This may be where they have failed to attend an activity or fully take part, dependant on the requirement.

9.11. You are required to notify us of the sanctionable failure after it has occurred, this is a compliance doubt.

9.12. You must raise a compliance doubt for every sanctionable failure without exception.

9.13. There may be instances where your Participant contacts you after the date of the mandatory activity to explain their reasons and you feel it is understandable why they did not comply. However, you must still raise a compliance doubt. This is because accepting your Participant’s reasons for the failure constitutes the making of a decision on good reason, and you do not have the authority to do this. Decisions on good reason can only be made by the Secretary of State, or Universal Credit Decision Makers (DM) on their behalf.

9.14. You must raise a compliance doubt promptly following your Participant’s failure to comply. Whilst there is no minimum or maximum timeframe for this, it is important that you raise a compliance doubt as quickly as possible after the sanctionable failure. Delays in doing so may mean a sanction is not imposed, for example where too much time has passed for a Participant to reasonably recall the reasons for their failure.

9.15. To support you in raising a compliance doubt as quickly as possible, you are not required to undertake any preliminary activities first, such as making your Participant aware that you will be notifying DWP.

9.16. If your Participant meets their compliance condition before you have raised the compliance doubt, you must still raise the doubt and notify DWP of compliance at the same time. This will enable DWP to bring the open-ended element of a sanction to an end quickly, where a sanction is imposed. Further information regarding compliance can be found in Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 10 – Participant compliance.

9.17. DWP will monitor compliance doubt referrals on an ongoing basis and information will be available to inform discussions with your DWP Performance Manager.

How to raise a compliance doubt

9.18. You must raise a compliance doubt by completing a UCD603 ‘Failure to participate in the Restart Scheme’ form.

9.19. The UCD603 form will provide the Universal Credit Decision Maker (DM) with information to enable them to progress the compliance doubt to the right outcome.

9.20. It is important you complete all relevant sections of the UCD603 form and take care to complete it accurately. Any UCD603 forms which are incomplete or incorrectly populated will be cancelled and returned to you, as we will be unable to progress them.

9.21. You will be required to confirm you made attempts to encourage your Participant to undertake the activity voluntarily before utilising mandation. You are not required to provide additional detail or supply evidence to demonstrate this, but irrespective of whether or not the individual goes on to start the Restart Scheme, you should keep records on your internal systems, in the event of appeal.

9.22. For Restart Scheme Participants who have complex needs requiring advanced support, you will be required to confirm you are satisfied there were no known challenges which affected their ability to understand the requirement or consequences of failing to comply, or their ability to undertake the activity. You are not required to provide additional detail or supply evidence to demonstrate this, but you should keep records in the event of appeal.

9.23. There is the opportunity within the UCD603 form for you to provide any supporting evidence which may be helpful for the Universal Credit DM to know, for example any reasons for the failure provided by your Participant.

9.24. There is no requirement to send a copy of the Mandatory Activity Notification (MAN) or any other supporting evidence with the compliance doubt.

9.25. Once completed you must send the UCD603 form to the District Restart Triage inbox by unencrypted email and include only the CPA reference number in the e-mail subject box. i.e. Subject: CPA 1a. You will have been notified of the email addresses for these inboxes during implementation activity

9.26. Finally, you must record your actions on your internal systems to enable any MI to be gathered and to remind you of the need to notify DWP of compliance at the earliest opportunity. Further information regarding compliance can be found in Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 10 – Participant compliance.

9.27. There is no requirement to wait for the outcome of the compliance doubt before continuing to attempt engagement with your Participant. It is in your best interests to continue trying to engage as quickly as possible to make the best of your Participant’s time on the Restart Scheme, and to give them the earliest opportunity to comply and bring any sanction imposed to an end quickly.

The decision-making process

9.28. On receipt of the compliance doubt DWP will undertake a quality check to ensure only referrals which are complete and correct will be progressed for decision-making. Those which are incomplete or incorrect will be cancelled at this point and returned to you. The compliance doubt must be resubmitted once errors are rectified, but this will create nugatory work for you and DWP and avoidable delays to the decision-making process.

9.29. Your Participant will then be contacted by DWP to provide their reasons for failing to undertake the mandated activity. This will happen even if you were aware of their reasons and included this information in the compliance doubt.

9.30. If good reason applies and a simple decision can be made, you will receive a UC136 from the Participant’s Jobcentre Plus work coach notifying you of the outcome of the compliance doubt.

9.31. If the course of action described above is not appropriate, the compliance doubt will be referred to a Universal Credit Decision Maker.

9.32. The Universal Credit Decision Maker will take into account all of the circumstances and evidence presented and will determine whether or not there was good reason for the failure to comply.

9.33. You may receive evidence from your Participant during the decision-making process. Where this happens, you must advise your Participant to share this with the Universal Credit Decision Maker via their journal.

Outcome of the compliance doubt

9.34. You will receive a UC136 from the Universal Credit Decision Maker notifying you of the outcome of the compliance doubt.

9.35. Where a sanction is not imposed the notification will include the reasons for this, for example where there has been a change in circumstances. Further information regarding change of circumstances can be found in Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 7A – Change of circumstances.

9.36. Where a sanction is imposed, you must update your internal systems to enable MI to be collected and to remain aware that you will need to notify DWP as quickly as possible when your Participant has complied. Further information regarding compliance can be found in Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 10 – Participant compliance.

9.37. This information will also help inform future engagement including the use of mandation, as you will not be able to mandate your Participant further to the type of activity they have been sanctioned for, until they have met the compliance condition. Further information regarding mandation can be found in Restart Scheme Provider Guidance Chapter 8A – Mandating Participants to undertake activity.

Recoverable Hardship Payments

9.38. Recoverable Hardship Payments are available to claimants who have their Universal Credit payments reduced because of a sanction.

9.39. A Participant on the Restart Scheme may apply for a Recoverable Hardship Payment from DWP if they are unable to meet their household’s basic, immediate and essential needs for housing, food, heating and hygiene costs only.

9.40. If a Participant asks about or requests information about Recoverable Hardship Payments, you must direct them to their Jobcentre Plus office as soon as possible.
