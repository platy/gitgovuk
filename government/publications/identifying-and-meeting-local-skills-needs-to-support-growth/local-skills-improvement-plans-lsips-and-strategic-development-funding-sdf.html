The skills for jobs white paper set out the government’s blueprint for reshaping the technical skills system to better meet the needs of employers and the wider economy.

As part of this new approach, the Department for Education has introduced local skills improvement plans (LSIPs) and a strategic development fund (SDF).

Local skills improvement plans

LSIPs will provide an agreed set of actionable priorities that employers, providers and stakeholders in a local area can get behind to drive change.

LSIPs will:

  • place employers at the heart of local skills systems
  • facilitate direct and dynamic working arrangements between employers and providers

We are in the process of rolling out LSIPs and employer representative bodies have been designated to lead the development of LSIPs from 5 September 2022.

Once the programme is fully rolled out, there will be 38 LSIPs covering the whole of England.

The role of designated ERBs

Designated ERBs will work closely with employers, providers and key stakeholders to develop evidence-based, credible, and actionable LSIPs. Each plan will set out the key changes needed in a local area to make technical skills training more responsive to employers’ needs.

The plans will help people to develop the skills they need to get good jobs and increase prospects.

It is our expectation that by being employer-led, LSIPs will be uniquely placed to highlight the skills employers need most in the workplace but may be struggling to find locally. Following their publication, LSIPs will be reviewed regularly to ensure they remain up to date and relevant.

Further information on the process for developing and reviewing a plan is set out in our statutory guidance on local skills improvement plans.

DfE will run a redesignation process and review the geographies for LSIPs periodically.

LSIP trailblazers

In the 2021 to 2022 financial year we ran 8 LSIP trailblazer pilots that enabled us to test how employers, providers and local stakeholders can work together effectively to develop a LSIP.

The ongoing independent evaluation of the trailblazers, alongside wider stakeholder engagement and evidence, has informed the development of the LSIP statutory guidance and our approach to national rollout.

Getting involved with your LSIP

Having a single designated ERB leading the development of a LSIP for each specified area in the country will:

  • make it easier for employers to navigate the system,
  • help drive up engagement
  • enable employers to build stronger relationships with their local providers

We encourage businesses to contact the designated ERB for their area if they would like to be involved.

Milestones for LSIP rollout

Autumn 2022: the statutory guidance on local skills improvement plans will be updated.

Summer 2023: we expect most of the country will have a LSIP developed and approved by the Secretary of State.

March 2025: plans will be drawn up for an initial period up to March 2025. ERBs will work with partners to regularly review and update their LSIP to ensure it remains relevant.

Strategic development fund

The strategic development fund provides capital and programme funding to enable FE providers in an area to:

  • support changes in local teaching and training facilities and provision so as to better meet the needs of employers, as set out in LSIPs
  • support local innovation and economic growth
  • develop a more efficient overall delivery infrastructure

What the funding is for

The purpose of the fund is to:

  • support providers across an area to upgrade their facilities, equipment and curriculum to better meet the needs of their local economy
  • enable colleges and other FE providers to build their overall capacity to meet local skills priorities, and to drive more effective and efficient use of funds through a more coordinated FE offer
  • support ongoing FE provider quality improvement through FE workforce training, developing sector-led approaches to peer-to-peer support, sharing good practice, including two-way industry exchanges with FE providers
  • stimulate employer demand for and investment in skills leading to more sustainable provision, including by raising awareness about new technologies and driving the adoption of innovation in local businesses, especially in small and medium-sized enterprises

Funding awarded

In 2021 the strategic development fund was trialled in 18 areas across England.

In spring 2022 the strategic development fund (SDF) was opened to collaborations of further education (FE) providers across England.

All areas of England applied for and received funding in the second strategic development fund round, with a total of £92 million funding awarded in the 2022 to 2023 financial year.

Strategic development funding arrangements for the 2023 to 2024 financial year will be confirmed in due course.
