Details

Future publications

We will no longer publish a new list each month. The data will not be refreshed to include amendments or repayments.

We will only update the list to remove any historical data that has been published for 12 months.

When the data is updated to remove historical data that has been published for 12 months, if there are employer details but no amount is shown in a column, this is because there was either:

  • no claim in that month
  • an adjustment to zero for that month

Available data

We published the latest available data for claim periods between December 2020 and September 2021.

We used the employer name attached to the qualifying PAYE scheme. Where the employer is a company, we:

  • used their registered name where possible
  • included a Company Registration Number where possible

We included an indication of the amount claimed, within a banded range, for all listed employers.

The banded ranges are:

  • £1 to £10,000
  • £10,001 to £25,000
  • £25,001 to £50,000
  • £50,001 to 100,000
  • £100,001 to £250,000
  • £250,001 to £500,000
  • £500,001 to £1,000,000
  • £1,000,001 to £2,500,000
  • £2,500,001 to £5,000,000
  • £5,000,001 to £10,000,000
  • £10,000,001 to £25,000,000
  • £25,000,001 to £50,000,000
  • £50,000,001 to £100,000,000
  • £100,000,001 and above

If a company has more than one PAYE scheme the banded amount will be the total amount claimed across all of their PAYE schemes.

For each published month, the:

  • banded amount is the total amount claimed
  • data includes any reduction made against a claim for overpayments in earlier periods
  • data does not include a reduction for a claim made, where an overpayment is declared against claims for later months

This data does not include employers:

  • with a successful application to have their details withheld
  • that paid back the whole grant before the list was produced
  • that claimed but were not paid the grant

We cannot provide further details of employer claims.

Report fraud to HMRC

HMRC will check claims. We may withhold payments, or you may need to pay us back if we find a claim to be fraudulent or based on incorrect information.

You can report fraud to HMRC if you have evidence to suggest an employer on the list is abusing the scheme.
