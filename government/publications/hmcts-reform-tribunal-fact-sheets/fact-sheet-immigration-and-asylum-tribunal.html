The immigration and asylum tribunal is delivering an efficient and transparent service that’s simple, fair and accessible for all users. The project allows cases to be resolved at an early stage and where appropriate, without the need for a hearing. This saves judicial time and resources.

Important statistics

The immigration and asylum tribunal project has achieved several main outcomes so far, including:

  • more than 30,200 appeals received since the public beta service was launched in January 2020
  • 14,282 digital applications received by HMCTS from legal representatives as of 30 June 2021
  • 27.5% of cases in 2020 to 2021 did not need a resource intensive hearing due to the service redesign
  • 24 weeks average clearance time for cases - almost half the time of the previous system

1. Main outcomes

We launched a private beta pilot in January 2019 at 2 hearing centres. This meant that certain asylum appeals could be received digitally from legally represented appellants. We  expanded this later that year to include all other eligible hearing centres UK-wide.

We launched a public beta in January 2020, widening the service to legal firms that were not part of the private beta testing. We brought all other in-country appeal types into public beta in May 2020.

In March 2021 we released a function for legally represented appellants outside of the UK to submit appeals online.

We released Payment by Account in July 2021. This allows legal representatives to pay by direct debit when submitting an appeal.

We released a number of technical features in the first part of 2021, including:

  • Share a Case - allowing legal representatives to share appeals with more than one user within the same firm
  • Notice of Change - allowing legal representatives to make a change in an online case

We released Work Allocation for tribunal caseworkers so tasks could be automatically sent to the right place with due dates monitored and tracked.

How the immigration and asylum tribunal project helps legal professionals make asylum appeals

Before we introduced the service, making an asylum appeal was complicated and paper-based. By moving the process to MyHMCTS, the system is now simpler and more accessible. Users can manage their cases on the service.  Evidence, legal arguments and judges’ decisions can all be given electronically.

Looking ahead to 2022

By the end of 2022, we plan to:

  • deliver an end-to-end appellant in person online service
  • provide additional fees and pay functions for both represented and unrepresented appellants
  • develop a digital service for immigration bail applications
