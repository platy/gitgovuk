3. Contacting Official Receivers offices about other matters

For questions about a case, you can email or call the office that is managing the case if you:

  • are currently bankrupt

  • have recently been made bankrupt

  • are the director of a company that has recently been wound up

  • are a creditor in a bankruptcy or company liquidation

When you contact us, have the BKT or LQD reference with you as you may be asked to provide it. Include this reference in any emails you send to us.

We have guidance online about how to register as a creditor in a bankruptcy or liquidation.

If you do not know which official receiver is managing the case, you can find their details on:

  • the Individual Insolvency Register if it’s an individual bankruptcy

  • the Companies House website if it’s a company liquidation

Alternatively, you can contact the Insolvency Service helpline.

We have guidance with information about processes administered or regulated by the Insolvency Service, including bankruptcy, debt relief orders and company liquidations.

You can contact the Insolvency Service helpline using our online contact form or phoning 0300 678 0015. The helpline is open 9am to 5pm Monday to Thursday, and 9am to 3pm on Friday.

4. Mail sent to the Official Receiver

We can process physical mail, but we have limited access to paper documents and forms sent in to our offices. To make it easier for you to correspond with us, we now allow copies of paper documents and forms to be submitted by email.

Read our guidance on how to send copies of paper documents and forms to us during the current pandemic.

We will aim to deal with emails and calls promptly, but please bear with us as we expect remote working to result in an increase in messages requiring a response. If you need to send us attachments to emails please ensure you take steps to minimise the file size, for example by zipping the documents.

5. Scheduled interviews with an Official Receiver

You should only attend at our offices with a pre-arranged appointment. Please contact the Official Receiver dealing with your case and arrangements will be made for you to supply information, usually this will be electronically or over the telephone.

If you have been made bankrupt or are director of a company that is in liquidation and have not yet spoken to someone at the Insolvency Service it is important that you contact us without delay.

6. Reports to creditors

Reports to creditors are being issued by e-mail and post. If you are a creditor and urgently require a report to creditors please contact the Official Receiver dealing with the case and we will make provision to send this to you electronically.
