Details

This chapter contains provisions thatâ€¯support increased growth in digital trade between New Zealand and the UK. It commits to increasing opportunities for digital trade for all businesses and consumers. The chapter ensures the free flow of trusted data, the prevention of unjustified localisation of data and world-leading standards for personal data protection.

The FTA will reduce unjustifiable barriers to digital trade and promote interoperability of digital trading systems, making trade cheaper, faster and easier. This includes ensuring the legal recognition of electronic contracts and electronic authentication.

Additionally, innovation and emerging technologies will be supported through cooperation between the countries and through commitments to make government information available in digital formats.

The agreement is not yet in force. Both the UK and New Zealand are required to complete their respective domestic procedures for the agreement to come into effect. Once approved by both parliaments, businesses will be able to trade under its terms.
