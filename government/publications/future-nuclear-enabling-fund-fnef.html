Details

BEIS is providing up to £120 million in funding to help mature potential nuclear projects ahead of the expected government process to select the next nuclear projects. The bid window for applications is now open.

The aim of the FNEF is to help industry reduce project risks, so they are better positioned for future investment decisions. The FNEF will be targeted at applicants that could be in a position to take a Final Investment Decision (FID) within the next parliament, subject to Value for Money and all relevant approvals.

We expect the fund to support a limited number of nuclear projects through grant funding to be awarded following a competitive process.

How to apply

Read the Future Nuclear Enabling Fund Guidance Notes on this page for further information about the competition.

Submit any questions by 3pm on 16 September 2022. We will publish here responses to any queries of material significance in the week beginning 26 September 2022 so you have time to review them before submitting your application.

We aim to hold a launch event in the week beginning 5 September 2022. Email FNEF@beis.gov.uk to express your interest in attending.

Register your intention to submit an application by 3pm on 14 October 2022. Registering early will ensure you get competition updates. Once you have registered your intention to apply, we will send you a link to the application portal.

Submit all necessary documents and supporting information as outlined in the guidance by 3pm on 21 October 2022.

We will not accept late applications or applications which are not submitted via the online application portal.

Background

The FNEF was announced in the government’s 2021 Net Zero Strategy: Build Back Greener.

It is the first in a series of government interventions as set out in the 2022 British Energy Security Strategy.
