July 2022 Newsletter

July will see the deployment of EX IRON STORM in the first 3 weeks of July. This is an armoured exercise, and is a free flow exercise. It will see lots of wheeled and armoured vehicles moving both day and night about the area. During the rest of July the area will have lots of other activity both on foot and vehicles.

Dangerous trees

The recent extreme weather events have had an adverse effect on lots of trees across the training area, and although there are moves to deal with the dangerous trees, there are still some in situ. The byelaws do not allow the public into wood blocks, but if a Public Rights of Way (PROW) goes through or runs alongside a treeline, please be careful. If there are piles of logs on the training area, due to the harvest of trees affected by Ash Dieback, please stop your children climbing over these piles of logs, it is dangerous.

SPTA West

A reminder that the public are not allowed to enter past the red line in SPTA West. There has been an increase in the number of members of the public being found inside the red line, but they have no authority to be there, whether the flags are flying or not. On the ground, that red line is denoted by signs informing the public of the danger and to keep out.

Byelaw governance: antisocial behaviour

Parish members are encouraged to report all illegal activity on Salisbury Plain including: illegal off-roading, illegal motor biking, drone use, poaching, hare coursing, petty theft, camping on MoD land, kite flying to the Ministry of Defence Police on 01371 854444.

Wildfire

A wildfire is an uncontrolled blaze triggered by a heat source fuelled by weather, wind, dry underbrush and vegetation. Wildfires can burn acres of land and consume everything in their paths in minutes. Approximately 80% of wildfires are started by human interaction and SPTA is no different in this respect. A number of wildfires have already been extinguished this year that were a direct result of recreational activity on the Plain. This must serve as timely reminder that especially while we experience some otherwise favourable temperatures and glorious sunshine, setting any kind of fire or using a BBQ on SPTA is banned. The type of grassland on SPTA ignites incredibly easily and burns furiously in these conditions, getting to an uncontrollable state extremely quickly. Operation (Op) Aston

Op Aston is a joint operation between SPTA core staff, Ministry Of Defence Police  (MDP) and the Wiltshire Police Force.  Primarily, the Police attendees are from Rural Crime, whereas SPTA are stakeholders within the Rural Crime Partnership.  The operation was conducted on Sunday 1 May 2022 between 10am to 4pm, but due to commitments Wilts Police could not attend. The aims of the operation: 1. To stop and engage with all off-road activity, inform and educate on good practice and deal with Byelaw infringements effectively. 2. To show a presence in order to operate as an effective deterrent to any criminal activity across SPTA.

During this Op, there were minimum military units training and SPTA was overall quieter than normal with regards to weekend activities.  This may have been due to the presence of so many authority personnel, which is an achievement of one of the aims. In addition, the reduction in illegal activity could be down to the efforts of the staff, the Police and members of the public reporting this type of activity and themselves complying with the Byelaws. The Op is regarded as a success and there are more users educated on the rights and wrongs of using SPTA as a playground.

Ash dieback on the MOD Estate & Salisbury Plain Training Area

Some roadside works to remove dangerous and dying trees will commence in and around Everleigh on 16 to 29 March 2022. Traffic management and diversions will be in place. The details are on the one network website - search for Everleigh, Marlborough and filter for next two weeks. Any alterations or amendments will be published here.

The felling of ash trees in Everleigh Ashes is also continuing and this will continue to be an active forestry site for the next few weeks even after felling has stopped. People should avoid this area.

In the meantime, if you have any questions or require any further information, please direct them to Iain Perkins, who will route them to the correct person. The following Helpline number can also be used for queries. Landmarc: 0800 0223 334
