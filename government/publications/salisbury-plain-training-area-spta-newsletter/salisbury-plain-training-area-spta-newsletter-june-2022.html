1. June 2022 Newsletter

There will be a lot of mounted and dismounted activity throughout June. Both the Training Schools and units catch up on training and prepare themselves for operations. Across SPTA you are likely to see  a variety of armoured and wheeled vehicles both day and night.  EX IRON STORM, a major Armoured exercise, will deploy on SPTA at the end of June in preparation for its manoeuvre phases in July.

A last-minute urgent live firing requirement has resulted in a significant increase in activity on SPTA Centre and a requirement for flags to remain up from 6 Jun to 1 Jul.

2. Dangerous trees

The recent extreme weather events have had an adverse effect on lots of trees across the training area, and although there are moves to deal with the dangerous trees, there are still some in situ. The byelaws do not allow the public into wood blocks, but if a Public Rights of Way (PROW) goes through or runs alongside a treeline, please be careful. If there are piles of logs on the training area, due to the harvest of trees affected by Ash Dieback, please stop your children climbing over these piles of logs, it is dangerous.

3. SPTA West

A reminder that the public are not allowed to enter past the red line in SPTA West. There has been an increase in the number of members of the public being found inside the red line, but they have no authority to be there, whether the flags are flying or not. On the ground, that red line is denoted by signs informing the public of the danger and to keep out.

7. Imber ranges

7.1 Platinum Jubilee - Public Access to Imber Village.

Imber Village and the routes running through it, will be open to the public between 1800hrs Wednesday 01 June 2022 to 0800 hrs Monday 06 June 2022 inclusive. The public are reminded that they are to obey all safety signs. They are not permitted into any buildings in Imber Village except the Church when it is open and they are not to leave the carriageway either on foot or in their vehicle.

Please read the leaflet on public access to Imber in advance of any visit.

8. Byelaw governance: antisocial behaviour

Parish members are encouraged to report all illegal activity on Salisbury Plain including: illegal off-roading, illegal motor biking, drone use, poaching, hare coursing, petty theft, camping on MoD land, kite flying to the Ministry of Defence Police on 01371 854444.

If serious crime is experienced 999 should be dialled. Once reported please inform range control on the Plain watch number: 01980 674951 or email PLAINWATCH@landmarc.mod.uk.

9. Access

Salisbury Plain Military Lands, Bulford, Larkhill and Imber Range Byelaws regulate access to SPTA. Always comply with local signs and flags. Rights of way in Range Danger Areas are closed when red flags are flying or when the signage restricts unauthorised access. This newsletter is published up to 6 weeks before the event and changes may occur.

Access timings for the next week can be found on the SPTA Walks Line: (01980) 674763 or on the MOD Byelaws Wiltshire website.

10. Ministry of Defence Police (MDP) Salisbury Plain

The MDP will continue to enforce the current government guidelines for COVID-19.

There has been an upturn in night-time hare coursing on and around the training area and a vehicle suspected to be used in this offence has recently been seized by MDP officers.

If anyone sees or suspects that hare coursing or similar offences are taking place during the night please contact Wiltshire Police on 101 or if urgent 999.

The speed limit on the plain is 30mph.

Please report any incidents or crimes to the MDP control room 24-hours on 01371 854444. Please act responsibly on Salisbury Plain and follow the Government guidelines.

In an emergency always, call 999

11. Ash dieback on the MOD Estate & Salisbury Plain Training Area

Some roadside works to remove dangerous and dying trees will commence in and around Everleigh on 16 to 29 March 2022. Traffic management and diversions will be in place. The details are on the one network website - search for Everleigh, Marlborough and filter for next two weeks. Any alterations or amendments will be published here.

The felling of ash trees in Everleigh Ashes is also continuing and this will continue to be an active forestry site for the next few weeks even after felling has stopped. People should avoid this area.

In the meantime, if you have any questions or require any further information, please direct them to Iain Perkins, who will route them to the correct person. The following Helpline number can also be used for queries. Landmarc: 0800 0223 334

Read more about ash dieback, access to SPTA during felling works and road closures.
