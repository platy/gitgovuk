1. 1. Important statistics

The OCMC service has:

  • issued more than 305,000 claims since its introduction in March 2018
  • achieved a 95% user satisfaction rating
  • settled 53% of the 7,984 mediation appointments made in 2021
  • issued 81,715 claims in 2021
  • achieved an average of 24 days to reach a settlement

2. 2. Main outcomes

By March 2023, the OCMC project will provide a service which is digital from start to finish for people issuing and responding to claims. We have achieved several main outcomes so far, including:

  • reducing the amount of time it takes for a case to transfer to a local court
  • getting more claims through mediation to achieve higher settlement rates
  • settling a total of 26,082 claims in 2021
  • allowing legal advisers to make case management directions in defended claims up to £1,000 - freeing up judicial time, resulting in cases being dealt with faster
  • improving access to the online service for claimants on low income, who can apply for help with paying court fees

3. 3. How the OCMC project makes issuing or responding to a claim quicker and easier

Before the project was introduced, issuing or responding to a claim was done on paper, or by using the heritage Money Claims Online Service (MCOL). This made the process slow and difficult for some users to understand. It could take more than 3 months using our non-reformed services.

The project has made the process quicker, with a settlement reached in 24 calendar days on average. The service is available 24/7 and users can log on at any time to check the progress of their case.

More cases than ever before are now going through mediation. This allows higher settlement rates to be achieved.

4. 4. Looking ahead to 2022

By the end of 2022, we plan to:

  • continue to develop and test the service, as part of a ‘public beta’ testing phase
  • expand to allow legal representatives to issue and respond to claims up to £25,000 in value
  • allow multiple users to issue and respond to claims (1 Claimant v 2 Defendant, 2v1 and 2v2)
  • include the ability to issue default judgements (Crown Court Judgments)
  • include the issuing of General Applications, including the Order or outcome
