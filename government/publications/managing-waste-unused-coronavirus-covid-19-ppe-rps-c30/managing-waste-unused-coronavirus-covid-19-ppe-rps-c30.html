When this COVID-19 RPS applies

You can only use this regulatory position statement (RPS) for storing, manually de-packaging and dismantling unused waste personal protective equipment (PPE), and if it is:

  • from the Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC) at facilities the DHSC has designated
  • coded 15 02 03

If your activity meets this description, the Environment Agency will not normally take enforcement action if:

  • you meet the conditions in this COVID-19 RPS
  • your activity does not, and is not likely to, cause environmental pollution or harm human health

This COVID-19 RPS does not change your legal obligations under any relevant legislation. Normally, you will commit an offence if you accept waste at a site that requires a waste permit but that does not have one.

Conditions of this RPS

Condition 1

You must make sure your activities do not endanger human health or the environment.

You must not:

  • cause a risk to water, air, soil, plants or animals
  • cause a nuisance through noise or odours
  • adversely affect the countryside or places of special interest

Places of special interest include, but are not limited to:

  • European sites (Special Areas of Conservation and Special Protection Areas)
  • Ramsar sites
  • Sites of Special Scientific Interest
  • National and local nature reserves
  • Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty
  • Marine Conservation Zones
  • national parks

Condition 2

You must:

  • email wastetreatment@environment-agency.gov.uk with the name and address of each site you propose to operate under this RPS
  • not operate at the site until you have received written agreement from the Environment Agency

Condition 3

For all unused waste COVID-19 PPE you accept at your site, you must record the:

  • date of arrival
  • type of waste, including the European waste catalogue (EWC) code
  • quantity received

You must keep the records required by this COVID-19 RPS for 2 years after this RPS has expired, including any extensions to it. You must make them available to the Environment Agency on request.

Enforcement

A COVID-19 RPS means that the Environment Agency will not normally take enforcement action against you provided:

  • your activity meets the description set out in this COVID-19 RPS
  • you comply with the conditions set out in this COVID-19 RPS
  • your activity does not, and is not likely to, cause environmental pollution or harm human health

If you operate under this COVID-19 RPS, but think you may no longer be able to comply with its conditions, you must email us at wastetreatment@environment-agency.gov.uk immediately.

When to check back

The Environment Agency will withdraw this COVID-19 RPS on 30 June 2023, unless they extend it.

After this date your site must have a permit that authorises the acceptance of unused COVID-19 PPE waste or you must stop accepting it.

Contact the Environment Agency

You must email wastetreatment@environment-agency.gov.uk to get written agreement before you use this COVID-19 RPS.
