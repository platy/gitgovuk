Details of the application

Bittadon Farms Limited has applied to the Environment Agency for a licence.

The Environment Agency is giving notice of this application in accordance with Section 37 of the Water Resources Act 1991 and Regulation 6 of the Water Resources (Abstraction and Impounding) Regulations 2006.

The application is for a licence to impede the flow of water in an un-named tributary of the Colam Stream located at National Grid Reference SS 54589 41203, Church Farm at Bittadon, Barnstable.

Water will be impeded to slow the flow of the watercourse and impound the stream to allow for abstraction for the purpose of agriculture linked to a dairy farm. The associated abstraction licence SW/050/0008/018 is also currently being varied to increase abstraction quantities, and this was advertised on 14th September 2021 under application reference NPS/WR/035219.

The applicant has calculated the volume of the reservoir to be 41.28 m3. Water discharges from the impounded section of the watercourse either through the overtopping of the structure or via an outflow pipe. The applicant has provided further information on the outflow pipe, with their calculations suggesting a flow of 35 litres/second will pass downstream via the outflow.

Please note that the structure has already been constructed without the appropriate licence. Therefore, this proposal is to determine whether the existing structure can be approved as built.
