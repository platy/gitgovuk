Hypothermia First Aid treatment guidelines

What to look for

Moderate Hypothermia

  • the casualty may say they feel very cold
  • feel cold to the touch
  • loss of manual dexterity (clumsiness)
  • loss of insight, denying having any problem
  • un-controlled shivering
  • cold, pale hands and feet
  • mild confusion, disorientation or irritability
  • may reject help, could be difficult to treat.

Severe Hypothermia

  • slurred speech
  • lips may turn blue
  • slow and or irrgualr pulse
  • casualty is unreponsive, breathing and pulse will be faint or even undetectable and they may look dead
  • apathetic, confused, irrational and clumsy
  • consciousness may be reduced
  • shivering has stopped.

Pause Activity

  • individual commerce treatment
  • group risk assess others, them recommence activity
  • a single case is a warning that other personnel at risk, the commander is to carry out a dynamic risk assessment of the activity and is to consider other control measures including stopping from the activity
  • remove the individual from immediate danger and prevent any further causalities from occurring
  • initial assessment should always address airway, breathing and circulation problems first.

Is the casualty conscious?

  • it is important to handle the casualty with hypothermia gently and carefully
  • move the casualty indoors or somewhere warm
  • if you cannot move the casualty indoors find something for them to lie on to insulate them from the ground
  • if casualty’s clothes are wet, change them into dry clothes make sure their head is covered
  • put the casualty in a sleeping bag and cover them with blankets
  • encourage the casualty to shiver if they are capable of doing so

Yes

  • gentle exercise if able
  • give the casualty warm drinks (not alcohol) or high energy foods

No

  • place the casualty in the recovery position

Evacuate to medical care as quickly as possible. Now report it.
