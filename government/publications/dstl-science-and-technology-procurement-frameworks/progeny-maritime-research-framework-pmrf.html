The Progeny Maritime Research Framework (PMRF) enables the UK government to quickly and efficiently place contracts for scientific and technical research and development.

It covers a broad range of maritime-related (above and under water) research, science and technology, to inform decision-making and de-risk the procurement of future capability, predominantly for the Royal Navy.

Key features of the Progeny Maritime Research Framework

The current Progeny framework is available until December 2023 with a maximum ceiling of £152 million.

Progeny uses a large selection of suppliers, the Progeny community of interest, to create multidisciplinary teams to explore ideas and potential solutions.

Technical experts help shape customer requirements, provide coherence and assure the technical quality of outputs.

This covers everything from low Technology Readiness Level (TRL) ‘exploratory’ activities through to high TRL activities to de-risk solutions for potential future procurement programmes.

It includes delivery of science and technology (S&T) for all maritime-related military capabilities both above and under water, including:

  • anti-submarine warfare
  • anti-surface warfare
  • geospatial intelligence
  • integrated air defence
  • littoral manoeuvre
  • mine countermeasures
  • submarine capability
  • maritime security operations

The Progeny Framework is designed to de-risk technologies, concepts and processes which enable the UK to deliver tasks from and within the maritime environment; and to develop understanding and models which support the UK in delivering tasks from and within the maritime environment.

Customers: using the Progeny framework to deliver your S&T requirements

Progeny can be used by any UK government department including the MOD frontline commands.

Costs will include effort required for technical work, support from the programme management office to support the shaping of your requirement, identifying the team to carry out the work, and management and technical oversight.

Framework management

The prime (main contractor) is QinetiQ. Contact QinetiQ by emailing Progeny@qinetiq.com and get more information on the QinetiQ website.

Dstl, as MOD category manager for science and technology (S&T), manages the Progeny Framework Agreement.
