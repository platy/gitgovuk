The electronic warfare and cyber (EW&C) framework enables Dstl, MOD and the front line commands to quickly and efficiently place contracts for scientific and technical research and development.

The framework is divided into 4 lots, each led by a separate prime (main contractor). Each lot covers a different specialist area:

  • Lot 1: electronic warfare engineering design, manufacture and assessment
  • Lot 2: cyber, software and modelling
  • Lot 3: sensor, sensor system and signature measurement
  • Lot 4: trials and capability operation

Key features of the EW&C framework

This framework can be used:

  • at all classifications including UK SECRET
  • for the full range of technical readiness levels (TRL)
  • across the full intellectual property (IP) spectrum, from early concept onwards

The current EW&C framework ends on 30 November 2022.

After the natural expiry date of the EW&C framework we plan to use other existing routes to market to access the UK’s exceptional electronic warfare and cyber talent pool, including but not limited to R-Cloud and Serapis.

Suppliers: how to work with us through the EW&C framework

If you are a supplier interested in carrying out work for Dstl, MOD and the front line commands, through any of the EW&C framework lots, please contact the prime contractors directly.

You may also want to look for EW&C opportunities on the Defence Sourcing Portal.

Guidance on how to sell to or work with Dstl.

Customers: using the EW&C framework to deliver your S&T requirements

Contact Dstl commercial services at DstlEWCFramework@dstl.gov.uk and we will provide you with the information you need to task the prime contractors directly.

Commissioning through the EW&C framework gives you access to:

  • Technical expertise: the prime contractor teams include technical experts who work with our customers to help shape requirements, provide coherence and assure the technical quality of outputs.
  • Collaborative working: we encourage our suppliers to work collaboratively, as part of your team in your offices, or remotely with frequent and regular communication. In all cases, Dstl will provide an appropriate level of oversight and will be here to sort out any issues that may arise.

Costs will include effort required for technical work, support from the programme management office to support the shaping of your requirement, identifying the team to carry out the work, and management and technical oversight.

Lot 1: electronic warfare engineering design, manufacture and assessment

Prime contractor: Leonardo

Covers:

  • engineering design, manufacture and integration
  • technology development
  • systems engineering and integration
  • characterisation, evaluation, fault finding and repair of systems

Contact: EWandCyberFramework@leonardocompany.com

Lot 2: cyber, software and modelling

Prime contractor: BAE AI

Covers:

  • modelling (including real time models)
  • software
  • cyber environment and electronic warfare scene representation
  • algorithm development and digital signal processing (DSP)
  • cyber system security

Contact: ewnc.lot2@baesystems.com

Lot 3: sensor, sensor system and signature measurement

Prime contractor: ATLAS ELEKTRONIK UK

Covers:

  • sensor and sensor array performance characterisation including associated mechanical and electrical systems
  • signature measurements of equipment including but not limited to the following types of signature: radio frequency, electro optic and underwater

Contact: EWC@uk.atlas-elektronik.com

Lot 4: trials and capability operation

Prime contractor: QinetiQ

Covers:

  • laboratory and associated equipment capability operation and maintenance tasks for facilities, laboratories and equipment, including facilities on 3rd party sites
  • conduct trials and trials related sub tasks and analysis and reporting of trial results
  • operation of complex electro-mechanical systems in a laboratory test environment

Contact: EW&C@QinetiQ.com

Framework management

Dstl, as MOD category manager for science and technology (S&T), manages the EW&C Framework Agreement. The Dstl Framework Commercial Team can be contacted at DstlEWCFramework@dstl.gov.uk
