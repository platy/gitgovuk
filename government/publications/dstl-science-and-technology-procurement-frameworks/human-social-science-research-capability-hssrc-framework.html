The Human Social Science Research Capability (HSSRC) framework enables the UK government to quickly and efficiently place contracts for scientific and technical research and development.

It covers research and advice relating to human capability in defence and security, including:

  • personnel
  • training and education
  • humans in systems
  • human performance
  • understanding and influencing human behaviour
  • health, wellbeing and enhancing medical systems and capabilities

Key features of the HSSRC framework

This framework can be used at all classifications up to UK SECRET.

HSSRC provides:

  • Diverse supplier network comprising academia and companies of all sizes, including small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and micro-enterprise
  • Prime contractor (BAE) with experience working with MOD and wider government
  • Rigorous competition and proposal process to ensure the chosen supplier will be the most suitable
  • Common set of terms and conditions, saving time when entering contracts
  • Active encouragement of partnering and collaboration between suppliers
  • Constant improvements to ensure the framework meets customer needs
  • Breadth and depth of skills across human, social and behavioural sciences
  • Expert Research Theme Leads who assure high quality outputs, identify innovative new suppliers and provide customers with support to write clear statements of requirement
  • Emphasis on value for money, assured through competition, negotiations and benchmarking

The current HSSRC framework ends in March 2023, with the potential to extend for a further 3 years.

Suppliers: how to work with us through the HSSRC framework

If you are a supplier interested in carrying out work for the UK government through the HSSRC framework, please contact the prime (main contractor) BAE Systems directly: HSSRC@baesystems.com

You may also want to look for HSSRC opportunities on the Defence Sourcing Portal.

Guidance on how to sell to or work with Dstl.

Customers: using the HSSRC framework to deliver your S&T requirements

Dstl owns this framework but it can also be used by the rest of MOD and other government departments to place work. Departments who can use this framework include:

  • Cabinet Office
  • College of Policing
  • Department for Education
  • Department for International Trade
  • Department for Transport
  • Department for Work and Pensions
  • Home Office
  • Ministry of Justice

If your department is not on this list, Dstl can place work on your behalf, or you can contact DstlHSSRC@dstl.gov.uk to be added as a direct customer.

Costs will include effort required for technical work, support from the programme management office to support the shaping of your requirement, identifying the team to carry out the work, and management and technical oversight.

Framework management

The prime (main contractor) is BAE Systems. Contact BAE by emailing HSSRC@baesystems.com and get more information about HSSRC on the BAE website.

Dstl, as MOD category manager for science and technology (S&T), manages the HSSRC Framework Agreement. The Dstl Framework Commercial Team can be contacted at DstlHSSRC@dstl.gov.uk
