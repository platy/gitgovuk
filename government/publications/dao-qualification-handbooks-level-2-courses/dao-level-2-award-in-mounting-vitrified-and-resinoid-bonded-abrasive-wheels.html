The Qualification

Overall Objective for the Qualifications

This handbook relates to the following qualification:

DAO Level 2 Award in Mounting Vitrified and Resinoid bonded Abrasive Wheels

This qualification provides the standards that must be achieved by individuals that attend the one day Abrasive Wheels course.

Pre-entry Requirements

There are no pre entry requirements or restrictions for this course.

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of one mandatory unit and no optional units. To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 1 credit from the mandatory unit as shown in the table below.

Mandatory Units

URN Unit Title Level Credit Value GLH TQT
A/617/3626 Mount Vitrified and Resinoid bonded abrasive wheels 2 1 8 9
    Total 1 8 9

Age Restriction

This qualification is available to learners aged 18 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

This qualification is considered to be professional development or an embedded element in a variety of trade courses.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified.

Credit Transfer

Credits from identical RQF units that have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred. None identified.

Learning outcomes and Assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Comply with current legislation applicable to abrasive wheels 1.1 Adhere to the current Health & Safety regulations applicable to the use of abrasive wheels  
  1.2 Adhere to the legal requirement for a person to mount an abrasive wheel  
2. Select appropriate abrasive wheel for a task 2.1 State the different types of grinding machines  
  2.2 Define the term “Abrasive Wheel”  
  2.3 State the different types of abrasive  
  2.4 State the purpose of the different types of abrasive wheels  
  2.5 State the conditions for storing abrasive wheels  
  2.6 State the purpose of the different types of abrasive wheels  
  2.7 State the different classes of abrasive wheels  
  2.8 Interpret the abrasive wheel marking system  
3. Mount abrasive wheels and grinding machines 3.1 List the component parts required to secure an abrasive wheel  
  3.2 Inspect the grinding machine and the component parts required to secure the abrasive wheel  
  3.3 Carry out the correct isolation procedure before changing an abrasive wheel  
  3.4 Inspect an abrasive wheel for defects before mounting  
  3.5 Describe the procedure for changing an abrasive wheel  
  3.6 Carry out the procedure for changing an abrasive wheel  
4. Dress vitrified abrasive wheels 4.1 Explain the term ‘dressing’ an abrasive wheel  
  4.2 Explain the reasons to dress an abrasive wheel  
  4.3 Describe the equipment used for dressing abrasive wheels  
  4.4 Describe the procedure for dressing abrasive wheels  
  4.5 Demonstrate the procedure for dressing abrasive wheels  

Additional information about the unit

Unit purpose and aim(s)

On completion of this unit candidates will be able to mount and dress vitrified abrasive wheels on static machines and portable machines. It must be noted that this unit DOES NOT provide Training and Assessment in the competence to USE the equipment. Candidates must be trained in the safe use of particular models of equipment separately. For the avoidance of doubt, this unit applies to:

Abrasive Wheels Types as specified in HSG 17 and BS EN 12413

  • Type 1

  • Type 2

  • Type 27

  • Type 41

  • Type 42

Candidates will be able to select a suitable abrasive wheel for an application by interpreting the British Standard marking system for abrasive wheels.
