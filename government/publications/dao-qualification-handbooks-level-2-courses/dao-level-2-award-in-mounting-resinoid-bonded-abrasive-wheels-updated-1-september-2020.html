The Qualification

Overall Objective for the Qualifications

This handbook relates to the following qualification:

DAO Level 2 Award in Mounting Resinoid bonded abrasive wheels

This qualification provides the standards that must be achieved by individuals that attend the one day Abrasive Wheels course.

Pre-entry Requirements

There are no pre entry requirements or restrictions for this course.

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of one mandatory units and no optional units.

To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 1 credit from the mandatory unit as shown in the table below.

Mandatory Units

URN Unit Title Level Credit Value GLH TQT
F/617/3627 Mount Resinoid bonded abrasive wheels 2 1 7 9
    Total 1 7 9

Age Restriction

This qualification is available to learners aged 18 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

This qualification is considered to be professional development or an embedded element in a variety of trade courses.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified.

Credit Transfer

Credits from identical RQF units that have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred. None identified.

Learning outcomes and Assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1 Comply with current legislation applicable to abrasive wheels 1.1 Adhere to the current Health & Safety regulations applicable to the use of abrasive wheels 1.2 Adhere to the legal requirement for a person to mount an abrasive wheel
2 Select appropriate abrasive wheel for a task 2.1 State the different types of grinding machines 2.2 Define the term “Abrasive Wheel” 2.3 State the different types of abrasive 2.4 State the purpose of the different types of abrasive wheels 2.5 State the conditions for storing abrasive wheels 2.6 State the purpose of the different types of abrasive wheels 2.7 State the different classes of abrasive wheels 2.8 Interpret the abrasive wheel marking system
3 Mount abrasive wheels on portable equipment 3.1 List the component parts required to secure an abrasive wheel to a portable grinding machine spindle 3.2 Inspect the portable grinding machine and the component parts required to secure the abrasive wheel to the grinding machine spindle 3.3 Carry out the correct electrical or mechanical isolation procedure before changing an abrasive wheel on a portable grinding machine 3.4 Inspect an abrasive wheel for defects before mounting to the portable grinding machine spindle 3.5 Describe the procedure for changing an abrasive wheel on a portable grinding machine 3.6 Carry out the procedure for changing an abrasive wheel on a portable grinding machine

Additional information about the unit
Unit purpose and aim(s) On completion of this unit candidates will be able to mount Resinoid Reinforced abrasive wheels on portable equipment. It must be noted that this unit DOES NOT provide Training and Assessment in the competence to USE the portable equipment.Candidates must be separately trained in the safe use of particular models of equipment. For the avoidance of doubt, this unit applies to: Abrasive Wheels Types as specified in HSG 17 and BS EN 12413 * Type 27 * Type 41 * Type 42 They will be able to select a suitable abrasive wheel for an application by interpreting the British Standard marking system for abrasive.
Unit expiry date 30/11/2023
