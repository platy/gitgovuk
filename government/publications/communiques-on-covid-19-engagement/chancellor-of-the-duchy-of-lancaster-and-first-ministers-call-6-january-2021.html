Communiqué: 7 January 2021

This communiqué summarises the meeting chaired by the Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster on 6 January 2021.

The Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster chaired a meeting with the First Ministers of Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland to discuss the ongoing COVID-19 response on 6 January. The meeting took place via video conference.

The attending Ministers were:

For the UK Government:

  • The Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster, Rt Hon Michael Gove MP
  • The Secretary of State for Wales, Rt Hon Simon Hart MP
  • The Secretary of State for Northern Ireland, Rt Hon Brandon Lewis MP

For the Scottish Government:

  • First Minister, Rt Hon Nicola Sturgeon MSP

For the Welsh Government:

  • First Minister, Rt Hon Mark Drakeford MS

For the Northern Ireland Executive:

  • First Minister, Rt Hon Arlene Foster MLA
  • Minister of Health, Robin Swann MLA
  • Junior Minister, Declan Kearney MLA
  • Junior Minister, Gordon Lyons MLA

The group discussed the COVID-19 response, including the approach taken by the UK Government and the devolved administrations to international travel, vaccine deployment, and priority areas for continued joint working.

The participants agreed that ministers from the UK Government and devolved administrations will meet again today to discuss international travel, and that the Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster will continue to chair regular engagement via videoconference with the First Ministers of Scotland and Wales and the First Minister and deputy First Minister of Northern Ireland.
