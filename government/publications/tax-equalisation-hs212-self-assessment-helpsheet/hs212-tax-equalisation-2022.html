This helpsheet is intended to assist professional advisers and employers who are preparing tax returns for tax equalised individuals. The following assumptions have been made:

  • the tax returns of tax equalised individuals will be prepared by the employer or advisers engaged by the employer
  • advisers or employers have experience of preparing tax computations for foreign national employees working in the United Kingdom (UK)

This helpsheet does:

  • briefly describe tax equalisation
  • provide amended instructions for certain boxes on the ‘Employment’ and ‘Additional information’ pages
  • set out agreements reached between tax practitioners and us regarding the treatment of tax equalised foreign national employees working in the UK

This helpsheet does not:

  • explain how to ‘gross up’ from a net earnings position
  • apportion earnings between UK and overseas duties
  • allocate earnings to different charging provisions
  • calculate the figure for box 12 of the ‘Share schemes and employment lump sums etc’ section, on page Ai 2 of the ‘Additional information pages’

Definition of tax equalisation

Tax equalisation takes various forms and is therefore difficult to define precisely. The term is generally used in this helpsheet to describe the arrangement between an employer and a foreign national employee who comes to the UK to work. However, we recognise that the amended instructions in this helpsheet may be applied to other equalised scenarios.

A tax equalisation arrangement involves an agreement under which the employee is entitled to specified net cash earnings and/or non-cash benefits. The employer undertakes to meet UK Income Tax arising from the earnings and/or benefits and to provide a professional adviser or in-house specialist to deal with the individual’s UK tax affairs.

Boxes 4 and 5

The ‘Employment’ page — amended instructions for tax equalised individuals

For individuals assigned to perform duties for a UK employer while remaining employed by an overseas employer, enter details of the:

  • UK company which is benefiting from their services in boxes 4 and 5
  • off-shore principal employer in the ‘Any other information’ box, box 19, on their tax return

For employees of UK employers, complete these boxes as advised in the ‘Employment notes’.

Box 1

Pay from this employment — the total from your P45 or P60

Ignore the descriptor for this box on page E1 of the ‘Employment’ pages. Enter the:

  • aggregate of cash earnings
  • cash equivalent of non-cash benefits to which PAYE has been applied on a grossed-up basis
  • grossed-up tax on cash and non-cash benefits

The figure reported may not be the same as the P60 pay figure. You must provide a full analysis of the figure entered in box 1 as a schedule with the tax return.

Box 2

UK tax taken off pay in box 1

Enter all tax paid under PAYE.

Box 3

Tips and other payments not on your P60

Ignore the descriptor for this box on page E1. In box 3 enter any earnings paid in an earlier year which are only liable to Income Tax if the remittance basis applies (see the ‘Residence, remittance basis etc notes’) and in 2021 to 2022 there were remittances to the UK.

Boxes 9 to 16 Benefits from your employment section for benefits or expenses where the employer has paid tax on a grossed-up basis under PAYE (including under a modified PAYE arrangement):

  • enter a nil figure (0) or leave blank if, exceptionally, your software does not allow nil entries in these boxes. In these circumstances, it’s accepted that the corresponding entries on form P11D will also be nil (0) — see the note on form P11D below
  • provide a full analysis of the figure in box 1 as a schedule with the tax return

This schedule should include details of the cash equivalent of all non-cash benefits and expenses payments that are not subject to a dispensation or a PAYE Settlement Agreement and for which a nil (0) entry has been made or the box left blank. The schedule should also report amounts made good by the employee and the grossed-up tax attributable to non-cash benefits and expenses payments.

For expenses or benefits where the employer has not paid tax on a grossed-up basis under PAYE, complete these boxes as advised on pages EN1 to EN3 of the ‘Employment notes’. The reported figures should reflect the corresponding entries on form P11D.

Employer’s return of expenses and benefits on form P11D

For expenses or benefits where the employer has paid tax on a grossed-up basis under PAYE (including under a modified PAYE arrangement):

  • deduct an amount equal to the cost or value of the item concerned, where there’s a box for entering the amount from which tax has been deducted, to give a net taxable amount of nil (0)
  • enter the cash equivalent or taxable amount of any other expense or benefit under ‘Other Items’ in section M and make the equivalent deduction to give a net taxable amount of nil (0)

For expenses or benefits where the employer has not paid tax on a grossed-up basis under PAYE, complete the relevant boxes on the form P11D in accordance with the P11D Guide and the P11D Working Sheets.

Following these amended instructions does not alter or otherwise affect an employer’s obligation to make an accurate return of Class 1A National Insurance contributions due on form P11D(b) and to pay those contributions on or before the statutory due date.

Foreign earnings not taxable in the UK in the year to 5 April 2022

In box 12 on page Ai 2 of the ‘Additional Information’ pages enter the aggregate of earnings:

  • outside the scope of UK tax
  • potentially chargeable under the remittance basis (see the ‘Residence, remittance basis etc notes’) which were not remitted to the UK in 2021 to 2022

Box 20

This may be used to claim relief for ‘corresponding payments’. Do not include contributions that an employee pays to an overseas pension scheme that is not UK registered. Claim relief for an employee’s contributions to an overseas pension scheme that is not UK registered by entering the amount of contributions eligible for relief in box 4 in the ‘Tax reliefs’ section of the tax return (page TR 4).

Any other information

Please note the ‘Any other information’ box, box 19, on the tax return to the effect that:

  • the tax return has been completed in accordance with this helpsheet
  • PAYE has been operated under a modified arrangement in accordance with the PAYE Manual (PAYE82002) (where applicable)

Where there’s an entry at box 4 of the Tax reliefs section on page TR 4 of the tax return or at box 14 of the ‘Shares schemes and employment lump sums.’ section on page Ai 2 of the ‘Additional information’ pages, use this box to provide the following information the:

  • name of the Qualifying Overseas Pension Scheme (QOPS) and its QOPS reference
  • name and SF74 reference of the scheme recognised as ‘corresponding’
  • Double Taxation Agreement (DTA) under which you are claiming relief

You should also provide confirmation that the scheme has been accepted by Pension Schemes Services as corresponding, or, in the case of the UK/USA DTA, confirmation that the US scheme is of a type specified in the Exchange of Notes of 24 July 2001 as being a pension scheme under Article 3.1(o). In the case of the UK/Ireland DTA you should instead provide confirmation that the Irish scheme is tax-approved or registered for tax approval in Ireland.

Agreements

Continuation of full in-year gross-up

Full in-year gross-up computations may be prepared and used on the tax return for individuals who are contractually entitled to net cash and/or non-cash earnings and to have UK tax liability met by the employer.

P11D filing date

Where an employer has entered into an agreement with us in accordance with PAYE Manual ‘PAYE82002’ to operate a Modified PAYE arrangement, forms P11D need not be filed until 31 January of the year following the tax return year, subject to the agreement of the employees.

Schedules of P11D information

An employer may agree with us to submit P11D information in schedule form. The schedules should follow the layout of the form P11D and use the same box numbers. A certificate should be provided stating that the particulars are fully and truly stated according to the best of their knowledge and belief. We will not accept a copy of boxes 9 to 16 of the ‘Employment’ page as a substitute P11D.

Performance bonuses

We have accepted that an officer of HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) may enter into an operational agreement with a taxpayer or adviser to:

  • accept cash or receipts basis for the treatment of bonuses, as opposed to apportioning bonuses over the performance period
  • accept accounting period bonuses or calendar year bonuses as being coterminous with a fiscal year
  • apportion only years of arrival and departure, with mid-assignment years being taxed on the receipts basis Agreement is conditional upon whatever basis is agreed being applied consistently.

Global settlements

We no longer operate arrangements which allow an employer to offset employees’ under and overpayments and send us a single balancing payment.

However, where a number of employees are all underpaid, the employer may send us a single schedule showing the Unique Taxpayer References of the employees who are to be credited in advance of the payment of a single sum by CHAPS.

Payments on account

We have agreed that employees covered by a Modified PAYE arrangement, under which the employer deducts and accounts for PAYE on a gross-up of both cash and non-cash benefits in accordance with PAYE82002, need not make payments on account. But in other circumstances there may be payments on account due for a year. Where, in a tax equalisation case, an employer pays the payment on account due for any year on behalf of the employee, and full in-year gross-up is used (see notes for box 1 on page 2 of this helpsheet) then:

  • there are no PAYE implications where the employer pays the payments on account
  • the payments on account made for the year should not be entered at box 13 or box 15 of the ‘Employment’ page and grossed-up tax on the payments on account should not be entered at box 1
  • but the payments on account made for the year should be set off at box A279 of the working sheet in the ‘Tax calculation summary notes’

Double Taxation exemption

Claims for Double Taxation exemption should be made using the forms attached to Helpsheet 302 Dual residents or Helpsheet 304 Non-residents - relief under Double Taxation Agreement, as appropriate. The exempt amount should not be entered on the tax return or supplementary pages. Helpsheets 302 and 304 need a declaration of the amount of income which is claimed to be treaty exempt. We’ve agreed to accept a best estimate of exempt income. However, where a claim is made for a student’s or business apprentice’s article, precise figures are required so that a comparison can be made with the accepted levels published in Statement of Practice SP4/86.

Associated employments

An individual who holds a number of employments which are not ‘associated’ for the purposes of section 24(5) ITEPA 2003 and section 416 ICTA 1988, but is taxable under the remittance basis (see the ‘Residence, remittance basis etc notes’), need only complete ‘Employment’ pages for employments from which they:

  • receive earnings in the year and send them to the United Kingdom (UK)
  • remit earnings to the UK which were received in an earlier year

However, where employments are ‘associated’, ‘Employment’ pages and, where relevant, ‘Additional information’ pages should be completed for all of them although the amount of detail required may vary. If this applies to you, you should contact us for advice.

Tax return for years of arrival and departure

In the tax year of arrival in the UK or departure from the UK, an assignee may be entitled to split year treatment. If so, the employee is a resident in the UK for the full year but the year is split into the overseas and UK parts of the year. You need to consider the income for the UK and overseas parts of the tax year separately. Only report earnings arising in the UK part on the ‘Employment’ page.

The following exceptions apply if in the tax year of:

  • arrival, but during the overseas part, the foreign national has worked in the UK, earnings arising from those UK duties should be included on the ‘Employment’ page
  • departure, but during the overseas part, the individual works in the UK, earnings arising from those UK duties should be included on the ‘Employment’ page

Associated points

Foreign tax credit

Where a claim for foreign tax credit is made for a state whose fiscal year is different from that of the UK, you should apportion the overseas income and the foreign tax to arrive at the amounts falling into the UK reporting year.

Cessation of employment or permanent emigration

Where an employee completes their assignment and permanently leaves the UK during the reporting year, form P85 should be sent to us. You may ask for a tax return so that an in-year settlement can be computed; it will not be computed automatically.

If you do not ask for an in-year tax return, we will send you a tax return on 6 April following the date of departure and deal with any repayment due when you send the tax return back to us. If tax returns for the year are not available, a copy of the previous year’s form, annotated with the correct dates, may be filed. Updated computer generated forms may be used. There’s no statutory obligation for the tax return to be sent back before the normal filing date for the year.

Contact with us

Agreement should be reached with the HMRC expatriate team that deals with the tax affairs of the relevant tax equalised employees before tax returns prepared on the basis described above are filed. Any technical view taken or the basis used in the tax return which diverges from that set out in the appropriate ‘notes’, should be described in the ‘Any other information’ box, box 19, on page TR 7 of your tax return.
