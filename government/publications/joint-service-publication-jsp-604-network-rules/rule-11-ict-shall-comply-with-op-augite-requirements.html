1. Rule rationale

The 2010 National Security Strategy rated cyber attacks as a Tier 1 threat to the UK.

J3 Cyber Defence, acting on behalf of the Network Operating Authority (NOA), utilises various CND toolsets to monitor and assure MOD networks in accordance with Network Operating Authority requirements.

To ensure that any new or existing ICT does not adversely affect its neighbours, or the wider MOD network, NOA Cyber Compliance need to ascertain the scope and possible attack vectors associated with the system.

This assessment is based upon, but not limited to:

  • The mechanism of connection to the wider MOD networks

  • Other onward connections

  • Patch and update policy

  • User base

  • Business Impact

2. Rule 11 - ICT shall comply with Network Operating Authority requirements

ICT shall comply with Network Operating Authority requirements. Projects shall engage with Defensive Cyber Operations (MOD Corsham) to determine applicability and, where required, implement the following measures:

  • 11.1 Installation of the MOD mandated asset management software
  • 11.2 AV Policy
  • 11.3 Cyber Network Defence (CND) monitoring and blocking
  • 11.4 Network scanning
  • 11.5 Log analysis
  • 11.6 Assessment of risk
  • 11.7 Vendor support

Important: Provision and testing of new CND measures will be funded by the project. System designs which inhibit the use of MOD CND measures will be refused connection to MOD networks.

3. Asset Management: 11.1

Initial gate

  • Projects engage with NOA Cyber Compliance to identify and agree the requirement for the installation of the J3 Cyber Defence mandated IT Asset Management software in their draft SRD

  • projects will be required to fund the Test and Integration of the software as well as any implementation costs incurred during the update life-cycle of the software

Main gate

  • Projects engage with NOA Cyber Compliance to identify and agree the requirement for the installation of the J3 Cyber Defence mandated IT Asset Management software in their SRD

  • projects engage with NOA Cyber Compliance to agree a mechanism where changes to and disposals of assets can be managed within the back-end IT Asset Management database
