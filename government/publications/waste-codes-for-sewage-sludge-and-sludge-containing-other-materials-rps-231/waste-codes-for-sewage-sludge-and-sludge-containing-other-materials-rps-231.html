This regulatory position statement (RPS) is about using an S3 or T21 waste exemption or standard rules permit for the storage or treatment of additional sewage sludges, and the associated land spreading of these sludges, under the Environmental Permitting (England and Wales) Regulations (EPR) and the Sludge (Use in Agriculture) Regulations (SUiAR).

If you follow the conditions in this RPS you can:

  • use additional waste codes and descriptions under an S3 or T21 waste exemption
  • use additional waste codes and descriptions under standard rules permits

Conditions you must comply with

You must:

  • only use the waste codes and descriptions listed in this RPS
  • have registered an S3 or T21 waste exemption for the storage or treatment of sewage sludge and comply with all requirements of that exemption
  • hold a storage or land spreading standard rules permit listed in this RPS and comply with all requirements of that permit
  • comply with all the other requirements of EPR or SUiAR
  • use the waste classification technical guidance (WM3) to classify waste
  • only use the waste code 19 08 05 to describe raw untreated sludge from waste water treatment plants

If you’re operating under a bespoke permit you must contact the Environment Agency for advice.

Sludge has the same meaning as described in SUiAR:

Sludge means residual sludge from sewage plants treating domestic or urban waste waters and from other sewage plants treating waste waters of a composition similar to domestic and urban waste waters.

This RPS does not apply to any other activity, even if it is under the same legislation. You may still need other permits or licences for other activities you carry out.

You must keep records for 2 years that show you have complied with this RPS. You must make these records available to the Environment Agency on request.

You must not:

  • cause a risk to water, air, soil, plants or animals
  • cause a nuisance through noise or odours
  • adversely affect the countryside or places of special interest

Use of sludge that contains other materials

  • use these types of waste treatment and the resulting waste codes to supply or spread to agricultural land under the SUiAR
  • store these wastes under an S3 exemption or permit such as SR2010 No 17
Type of waste treatment Resulting waste code you must use

Stabilisation of sludge using non-waste lime 19 02 06 sludges from physico-chemical treatment other than those mentioned in 19 02 05 (sewage sludge only).
Sludge mixed with non-waste straw, non-waste wood or other non-waste plant tissue material 19 02 06 sludges from physico-chemical treatment other than those mentioned in 19 02 05 (sewage sludge only).
Sludge dewatering and storage with the use of chemical additives to aid dewatering 19 02 06 sludges from physico-chemical treatment other than those mentioned in 19 02 05 (sewage sludge only).

Enforcement

A RPS means that the Environment Agency will not normally take enforcement action against you if:

  • your activity meets the description set out in this RPS
  • you comply with the conditions set out in this RPS
  • your activity does not, and is not likely to, cause environmental pollution or harm human health

When to check back

This RPS will be reviewed by 31 January 2024. You will need to check back then to see if it still applies.
