The app includes a number of features to protect you, including alerts letting you know if you may have been exposed to coronavirus (COVID-19), the latest advice based on your circumstances, a symptoms checker and general information on COVID-19.

Alerts

The NHS COVID-19 app will send alerts in the following situations.

  1. You’ve been in close contact with someone who has reported testing positive for COVID-19.
  2. National policy has changed.
  3. Information about your local area has changed.
  4. You have paused contact tracing and set a reminder to turn it back on.
  5. Our guidance to you has changed. For example, your countdown timer has ended.
  6. You are advised to update your app to the latest version.
  7. You did not select ‘Notify others’ or ‘Do not notify others’ and over 24 hours have passed since entering a positive test result.

Close contact alerts

Your phone will tell you when you have been in close contact with someone who later tested positive for COVID-19.

The alert will say:

Close contact notification. You have been in close contact with someone who has COVID-19. Tap here for more details.

Read the latest advice for England

Read the latest advice for Wales

Latest information

You can access the relevant websites about COVID-19 and support available through the ‘COVID-19 guidance’ button on the home screen of the app.

You may receive a notification when national information changes, or if you live in an area where new restrictions, testing opportunities, or extra precautions are in place. You can read more by selecting the notification, or visiting the app homescreen.
