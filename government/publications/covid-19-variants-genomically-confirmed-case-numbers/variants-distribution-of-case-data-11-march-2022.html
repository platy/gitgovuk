Surveillance overview

From 31 January 2022, the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) has reported episodes of infection in its coronavirus (COVID-19) surveillance. Current surveillance of COVID-19 in England is based on positive samples from the same person grouped together, so that the person is only counted once and reported using the date of the first positive result.

From 31 January 2022, an ‘episode’ definition is introduced, grouping positive results up to and including 90 days apart into episodes. Positive test results which are more than 90 days apart are classed as separate episodes.

Information pertaining to VUI-21MAR-01, B1.324.1 with E484K has been removed as this variant has been reclassified as provisionally extinct. Variants VUI-21FEB-01 (A.23.1 with E484K), and VOC-21FEB-02 (Alpha with E484K) are provisionally extinct (not detected for 12 weeks in the UK or in GISAID) therefore are no longer being included in the publication since 12 November 2021. Variant VUI-21MAY-01, AV.1 has been removed as of 3 December 2021 as it has been classified as provisionally extinct. Variant VUI-21JUL-01 (Mu) has been removed as of 17 February 2022 as it has been classified as provisionally extinct.

Differences between a variant of concern and a variant under investigation

SARS-CoV-2 variants, if considered to have concerning epidemiological, immunological or pathogenic properties, are raised for formal investigation. At this point they are designated variant under investigation (VUI) with a year, month, and number. Following a risk assessment with the relevant expert committee, they may be designated variant of concern (VOC).

Changes in nomenclature

The previous variant names frequently include similar numbers with similar or repeating patterns. This makes referring to them verbally challenging. To overcome this challenge, a revised nomenclature has been agreed involving dropping the first 2 numbers of the year and replacing the 2-month numbers with a 3-letter abbreviation. This shortens the name and reduces the degree of repeated sequences of numbers. The table containing surveillance overview ‘UK total distribution’ contains a list of new variant names alongside previous or other names in use for VOCs and VUIs.

Note on Omicron (VOC-21NOV-01 and VUI-22JAN-01)

As genotyping assays cannot distinguish between BA.1 and BA.2 genomes, Omicron (VOC-21NOV-01) includes cases of BA.1 and BA.2 genomes where those genomes were identified by genotyping only and not confirmed by genome sequencing.
