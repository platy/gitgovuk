Introduction

The purpose of this Industry Security Notice (ISN) is to inform suppliers to the MOD of the update to the Risk Assessment (RA) and Supplier Assurance Questionnaire (SAQ) questions on the Supplier Cyber Protection Service. This ISN applies to all contracts containing DEFCON 658 (or a narrative equivalent) and relates to the existing annual renewal process. Please also refer to ISN 2019-02.

Issue

The Defence Cyber Protection Partnership’s (DCPP) Cyber Security Model (CSM) has been mandated across all new contracts placed with the MOD since April 2017. It is a risk-based proportionate approach to protecting MOD Identifiable Information (MODII) through the supply chain. Further information can be found on the defence cyber protection partnership page.

Status

In November 2019 the Risk Assessment (RA) and Supplier Assurance Questionnaire (SAQ) questions were updated. This change was originally rolled out for new RAs and SAQs only. As from 14th September 2020 these new versions will be applied to RAs and SAQs that are following the existing annual review process.

Action by industry

The new SAQ question set can be viewed. In summary, the changes are:

a. Cyber Essentials / Cyber Essentials Plus certification is to be renewed annually.

b. Three additional controls at ‘Moderate’ and one additional control at ‘Low’.

c. Suppliers using a MOD accredited network – Official (up to the Sensitive caveat) will be deemed to meet the requirements for ‘Low’. Suppliers using a MOD accredited network – Secret or Top Secret will be deemed to meet the requirements for ‘High’. This question will be asked in the SAQ before any of the Cyber Risk Profile specific questions. However, please note that complying with the Cyber Risk Profile (CRP) requirements does not provide MOD accreditation.

  1. Changes to the Risk Assessment and Supplier Assurance Questionnaire could mean:

a. The CRP has increased; or

b. There are additional or modified cyber controls required within the current CRP.

  1. Renewal of the MOD Risk Assessment will result in a new Risk Assessment Reference (RAR). On receipt of the new RAR from MOD, please log into the Supplier Cyber Protection Tool to renew the Supplier Assurance Questionnaire.

  2. If the outcome of the renewed SAQ is ‘Not Met’ (either because the CRP has increased, or because there are additional controls for the existing CRP), you will need to complete and submit a Cyber Implementation Plan (CIP). This must detail how the cyber risk is being mitigated through equivalent measures (if applicable), or what actions are required to become compliant. The CIP process is explained further in Def Stan 05-138 and the DCPP CSM Industry Buyer and Supplier Guide.

  3. Changes to the CRP or the introduction of a new CIP will be subject to formal contract amendment.

  4. For information, you can view the new RA workflow question set. In summary, there are now only six questions which impact on the Cyber Risk Profile (CRP). These relate to MOD Identifiable Information (MODII), classification, personal data and access required to MOD networks.

Validity/Expiry Date

  1. This notice is valid with immediate effect and remains so until further notice.
