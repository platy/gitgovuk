This page provides a list of authorised endorsing bodies that can issue endorsements for Start-up visas, as well as information provided by the endorsing bodies. An organisation can only issue an endorsement if it is on the list.

If you have been offered an endorsement by an organisation that is not on the list, you can report it on GOV.UK.

Before contacting an endorsing body

Before you contact an endorsing body, you should consider the content of the information provided below and visit their website. You should only contact an endorsing body if you are applying for an endorsement from them.

For more general queries regarding the Start-up route, applicants and immigration advisors should refer to the information on gov.uk. if you need further information, applicants should contact UKVI.

Immigration advisors can contact the Home Office business helpdesk.

Neither the endorsing bodies nor the business helpdesk will answer hypothetical or speculative enquiries.

Brookes Enterprise Support Department offers ongoing mentoring and guidance to all start-ups via their new Start-Up Visa accelerator programme (launching Summer 2021). It also offers some office incubation space where required and a range of free events, funding, scale-up programmes and networking opportunities that endorsees can take part in.

  • sectors: The product/service should be a digital or tech solution at its core
  • endorses businesses that: are able to make a proactive contribution to the entrepreneurial community in Oxfordshire and Oxford Brookes University
  • intake on applications: applications open three times a year, in line with student/ graduate visa route (April, September and January)
  • restrictions on applications: currently only their alumni with the exception of non-Brookes applicants who are key members and co-founders of an Brookes team
