The Offer

UK

  • England: 15 hours for children aged 3 and 4; 30 hours for working parents
  • Scotland: at least 600 funded hours per year (up to 1140 hours in some local authority areas)
  • Wales: 30 hours free early education/ childcare for working parents for 48 weeks of the year
  • Northern Ireland: 12.5 hours per week of free preschool education.

Overseas

In MOD Schools/settings, 15 and 30 hours free childcare can be accessed free of charge. Parents to apply via relevant nursery setting.

In Non-MOD schools/settings parents can claim a refund by means of Overseas Nursery Authority, ONA DIN accessible via Defence Intranet.

What parents need to do

  • register early with CEAS if posted abroad with children
  • if moving within the UK, check out what the local offer is prior to moving to avoid missing out on free hours
  • engage with nurseries early.

Eligibility

Every parent with children aged 3 and 4 years old is eligible for the basic free childcare entitlement from the term following their child’s 3rd birthday.

Additional hours:

In England and Wales, the additional hours are only available to working parents who can answer “yes” to the following:

  • do both you and your partner work 16 hours per week or more (NMW/NLW)?
  • is your individual annual employment income below £100,000?
  • are you a single parent?
  • are you on paid maternity leave?
  • are you a registered carer?

In Scotland, visit Parent Club or mygov.scot for information about early learning and childcare (ELC).

Any issues applying online, call Childcare Service Helpline on 0300 123 4097 to request a manual code.

In overseas locations, very reasonably practicable to do so, MOD will mirror entitlements which parents would have access to in England to ensure they are not disadvantaged. Please read 30 hours free childcare DIN for full details.
