Ukraine is the fastest-growing refugee crisis since the Second World War. Approximately 6.65 million people inside Ukraine have been displaced since the conflict started, and 5.8 million people are registered as refugees across Europe [footnote 1].

In response to the continuing humanitarian crisis in Ukraine, the UK government is providing humanitarian assistance to Ukraine and neighbouring countries.

1. Scale of humanitarian need

The United Nations (UN) estimates that 17.7 million people need humanitarian support within Ukraine, with around a third of the population having been forced to flee their homes.

Food, medicine and basic hygiene items are scarce in the worst-hit areas. Ensuring humanitarian aid gets in and people can get out of these areas is a major challenge.

2. UK government humanitarian objectives

The Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office, working with other UK government departments, aims to:

  • provide humanitarian aid in Ukraine and to people seeking refuge in the region. This includes protection and life-saving assistance, with a focus on the most vulnerable, including women and children
  • work with our allies to ensure that the international humanitarian system delivers a well-coordinated and well-funded response
  • press for respect for International Humanitarian Law (IHL) and call for unimpeded humanitarian access to ensure humanitarian aid gets to those who need it most and enable people to leave the worst affected areas safely

3. What the UK government is doing

An overview of UK aid to Ukraine, 3 June 2022

The UK government quickly committed large-scale humanitarian funding, sending experts to the region, and delivering life-saving supplies to Ukraine, including medical and food aid.

The UK is providing £220 million of humanitarian assistance. This £220 million pledge includes up to £145 million to UN and Red Cross Agencies and £25 million in matched funding to the Disasters Emergency Committee Appeal (DEC) – our largest ever aid match contribution. The DEC website explains how donations to this appeal are helping people fleeing conflict in Ukraine.

The priority for UK funding is to support the most vulnerable people, including women and children, the elderly and disabled, in Ukraine and in the region around Ukraine.

4. UK relief work inside Ukraine

The UK government:

  • is providing £15 million in funding to support up to 200,000 of the most vulnerable impacted by Russia’s invasion in Ukraine. This funding covers both Ukraine and Poland, where most Ukrainians who have fled the conflict have travelled
  • delivered a total of 11.07 million medical items, including 6.8 million doses of medicine, 250,000 wound care packs, as well as specialist brain and spinal injury equipment
  • pledged up to 20 NHS ambulances to the government of Ukraine and later 22 more ambulances, medical equipment and 40 fire engines
  • is supporting frontline medical aid charity UK-Med through:
    • up to £300,000 funding to help train Ukrainian health workers to deal with mass casualties and set up mobile health clinics for the most vulnerable civilians in Ukraine
    • donating £300,000 worth of medicines and pharmaceutical supplies to UK-Med, which could support a hospital for up to 6 weeks
  • brought 21 children with cancer to the UK for treatment with their families
  • delivered 21 trucks of food and water to the government of Ukraine
  • pledged over 856 portable generators to support access to power for essential services including hospitals and shelters
  • delivered UK supplies, including inflatable structures and heaters for 6 reception centres in and around Lviv to the International Organisation for Migration, to provide people who have been forced to flee their homes with basic services and information
  • pledged a fleet of 13 armoured vehicles to evacuate civilians from besieged areas
  • supported the international humanitarian system, through the UN, the Red Cross and non-governmental organisations (NGOs), providing aid for the most vulnerable and hard to reach
  • is the largest donor to the UN Ukraine Humanitarian Fund (UHF) which is providing immediate life-saving assistance, including healthcare, food, shelter, and water and sanitation. The UHF is also helping tackle sexual and gender based violence through targeted services, legal support and crisis accommodation. Our funding is already helping to reach over 2 million people with lifesaving aid

5. UK relief work in the region around Ukraine

Our humanitarian field teams in the region provide logistical support and advice and coordinate with governments and the UN in neighbouring countries.

We have provided £25 million to the UN Refugee Agency as they support host governments in neighbouring countries to receive and care for refugees. UK support will reach over 140,000 refugees in the region, providing protection, shelter and cash for the most vulnerable groups and for people with specific needs.

In partnership with Australia, we have delivered blankets, kitchen sets, solar lanterns, and hygiene kits to the UN Refugee Agency for use in Ukraine and Moldova.

We have delivered 9,468 blankets and hygiene kits to the government of Moldova, to support refugees.

We have provided tents, generators, lighting, tables and chairs, that could equip 8 reception centres, to the UN Refugee Agency to use in Ukraine and Moldova.
