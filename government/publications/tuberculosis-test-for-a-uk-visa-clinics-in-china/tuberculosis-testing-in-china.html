You must be tested for tuberculosis if you are coming to the UK for more than 6 months and are a resident of China.

Your test must be from a clinic approved by the Home Office. We will not accept a tuberculosis test certificate from a clinic we have not approved.

A full list of approved clinics can be found on the main page of Tuberculosis testing in China.

1. Attending the clinic

You can contact any clinic to book an appointment either by telephone or email. You should make it clear you need the test for a UK visa application.

You can expect to get an appointment a few days after you request one. We cannot arrange or prioritise appointments for you.

If you need to cancel your appointment, you should contact the clinic as soon as possible.

2. What you should bring with you to your appointment

  • valid identification which contains your photograph such as a passport, national ID card or Refugee Asylum Seeker Card
  • the tuberculosis test fee
  • if you have had tuberculosis in the past or have any other lung disease you should provide details of your previous screening and, if possible, your medical notes and x-ray results

If you would like an appointment with a female clinician, please ask the clinic when you schedule your appointment.

3. Test fees

The tuberculosis screening test fee is determined by the clinic. You will pay the clinic directly. The cost of the test is in addition to the visa application fee.

The test fee does not cover treatment if you have tuberculosis.
