The UK can restrict the import of endangered species from countries, for example where their trade is considered unsustainable.

The Species+ tool lists the restricted specimens where the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) is unlikely to issue an import permit.

The UK has also formed import suspensions (which are also known as negative opinions).

Before you apply for a CITES import permit you should check if it’s:

  • listed on Species+ as being restricted
  • subject to a UK import suspension

Search Species+ for restricted species

  1. Open the Species+ tool.

  2. Search for a species, higher taxon, synonym or common name.

  3. Check the EU decisions section for any current suspensions or opinions.

We are using EU decisions as our baseline position, unless superseded by UK import suspensions.

Get help

Contact APHA if you have checked Species+ and the UK’s import suspensions and are still unsure whether your specimen is restricted. You can also contact APHA for advice on whether you should apply for a permit.
