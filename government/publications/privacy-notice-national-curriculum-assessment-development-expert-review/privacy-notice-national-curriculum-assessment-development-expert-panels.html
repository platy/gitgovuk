1. Who we are

The Standards and Testing Agency (STA) is an executive agency of the Department for Education (DfE), responsible for assessment policy and the development and delivery of assessments for children in education between reception and the end of key stage 3. For the purpose of data protection legislation, DfE is the data controller for the personal data processed as part of assessment development work.

2. How we will use your information

We receive your personal data from you and use it to seek your professional opinion on materials in development for national curriculum assessments. More information about national curriculum assessments is available here.

3. The personal data we will be using

The categories of your personal data that we will be using for this project are:

  • full name
  • email address
  • telephone number
  • occupation
  • workplace location
  • professional qualifications and experience

We use information about your professional expertise and experience, where relevant to our work (for example, teaching qualifications and subjects taught), to ensure that appropriately qualified professionals take part in the review.

We will also use the following special category data:

  • gender
  • ethnic group

We use information on gender, ethnicity and geographical location to ensure that the panels are representative of our diverse society, as far as is possible within a small group.

4. Why our use of your personal data is lawful

For our use of your personal data to be lawful, we need to meet one (or more) conditions in data protection legislation. For this project, the relevant conditions are:

  • Article 6(1)(e) UK GDPR: to perform a public task as part of our function as a department, that is, the requirement on DfE to provide an effective and robust testing, assessment and moderation system to measure and monitor pupilsâ progress through primary school from reception to the end of key stage 3. Specifically, processing your data to invite you to take part in expert review panels to review our assessment materials enables us to develop and deliver statutory national curriculum assessments on behalf of the Secretary of State
  • Article 9(2)(a) UK GDPR: that you have given explicit consent to the processing of your personal data for one or more specified purposes. Specifically, we use information on your gender and ethnic group to ensure that, as far as is possible, panels are representative of our diverse society

5. Who we will make your personal data available to

We will not make your personal data available to other organisations.

6. How long we will keep your personal data

The DfE will keep your personal data as part of the assessment development expert review process for five years, after which it will be securely destroyed.
