1. How to use the indicator

Enter a country, percentage of cover, drawdown period and payment period to the model.

The model will provide you with a Country Risk Category (as set by the OECD or UKEF as appropriate), and an upfront indicative premium rate.

However, if UKEF is off-cover, or has restricted cover for the selected country, or is required to perform Market Benchmarking for pricing (see below), then you are advised to contact us directly.

1.1 Assumptions we make in providing indicative premium rates

To simplify the calculator, UKEF makes some assumptions in the calculation of indicative premium rates. These assumptions are:

  • premium is payable upfront
  • a semi-annual repayment profile
  • premium is not financed from the loan
  • product is a standard buyer credit or direct lending facility
  • drawdown and payment periods are in the ranges that are typically seen in UKEF transactions

1.2 What is and isn’t included in the indication tool

The tool is currently restricted to providing indicative premium rates for sovereign risk entities only. We expect to be able to meet the OECD minimum premium rates for all sovereign risk entities in countries where we have cover without restrictions. For Market Benchmarking Countries (“Cat 0”) we need to follow market benchmarking rules to determine the premium rate and therefore they are treated more on a case-by-case basis.

For these reasons, you should contact us for indicative premium rates for countries where we have restricted cover availability or in OECD Risk Category 0.

For non-sovereign risk entities or projects, we expect to meet OECD minimum premium rates in the large majority of cases (as per our historical experience). The specific premium rate will depend on the risk rating, and other aspects of our risk assessment. Contact us if you want to get an indication of a premium rate for a non-sovereign risk entity or project so that we can discuss the details of the potential transaction before providing an indicative premium rate.

As the OECD Arrangement does not cover short-term credit insurance products and our pricing approach is relatively bespoke, the pricing indicator tool does not yet cover those products.

2. What to do once you have an indicative rate

Once you have calculated an indicative premium rate please contact us if you wish to further discuss your case.

3. How to obtain an indicative rate for parameters or inputs not covered by the tool

Please contact us to request an indicative premium rate for any case not covered by the premium indicator tool.

4. Further development

This indication tool and this guidance note are our first steps in the development of tools to assist customers by providing pricing information for our products. We welcome feedback on this tool, and will be exploring ways to improve the service, including expanding the coverage of the tool and making improvements in the platform and delivery.

Please contact us with any feedback relating to this tool.

5. List of definitions

5.1 Sovereign Risk Entity

Sovereign risk entities are defined as entities explicitly legally mandated to enter into a debt payment obligation on behalf of the Sovereign State. These are typically a Ministry of Finance or Central Bank.

5.2 Medium/Long-term

A medium or long-term transaction is defined as a transaction where the credit terms, also known as the payment period, are longer than 2 years. Our premium indicator is limited to transactions with a payment period between 3 and 10 years.

5.3 Drawdown period

The drawdown period is the period during which a borrower may draw down the loan.

5.4 Payment period

The payment period is the time between the starting point of credit of the loan, at the end of the drawdown period, and its maturity (final payment).

5.5 Repayment profile

The repayment profile is the structure given to the periodic repayments of the loan. We have assumed a regular semi-annual repayment profile with equal payments of loan principal.

5.6 Product quality

The product quality depends on whether the product is a guarantee, direct loan or insurance, and the risks that are covered. There are three categories defined in the OECD Arrangement, with minimum premium rates increased for higher quality products that provide more comprehensive cover. Our buyer credit and direct lending facilities are priced as Above standard quality (the highest of the three categories).
