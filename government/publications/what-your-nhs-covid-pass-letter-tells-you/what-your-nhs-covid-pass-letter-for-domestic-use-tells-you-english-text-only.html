Data protection

The Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC) is the data controller and is responsible for processing your personal data for the purposes of the NHS COVID Pass programme. To find out more you can access the DHSC privacy notice. The NHS COVID Pass privacy notice is available within the guidance.

On the last page of the letter

Questions you may have

Who can get a Domestic NHS COVID Pass?

You can get a Domestic NHS COVID Pass if any of the following apply:

  • you are fully vaccinated with eligible COVID-19 vaccines
  • you are medically exempt from COVID-19 vaccination (including those exempt from vaccination and testing)
  • you are taking part in an official COVID-19 vaccine clinical trial

When and where could I be asked to show my Domestic NHS COVID Pass?

You may be asked to show this Domestic NHS COVID Pass to confirm your COVID-19 status at places that are using the service. You cannot use this pass for international travel.

Is there a digital alternative?

You can access your Domestic NHS COVID Pass using the NHS App or through the NHS website.

What should I do when my Domestic NHS COVID Pass document expires?

Check the expiry date of this Domestic NHS COVID Pass. It will not be valid after this date.

Find information on what to do next on the GOV.UK website. You can also call 119 (NHS COVID Pass service) free of charge, 7 days a week.

What should I do if I lose this Domestic NHS COVID Pass?

If you lose this Domestic NHS COVID Pass, you can still access and share your status using the NHS App or the NHS website. Or please call 119 (NHS COVID Pass service) free of charge, 7 days a week.

Is this information available in other languages, large type or braille?

You can get this information in alternative languages and formats. Call 119 (NHS COVID Pass service) free of charge, 7 days a week.
