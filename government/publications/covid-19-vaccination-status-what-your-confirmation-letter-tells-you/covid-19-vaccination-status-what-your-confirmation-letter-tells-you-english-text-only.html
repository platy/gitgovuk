About this letter

This letter is from the NHS confirming your coronavirus (COVID-19) vaccination.

This document is important. Keep it safe. It proves that you have been vaccinated.

You should keep the original English language version as you may be asked to show it if you travel abroad.

What is on the front of the letter

The front of the letter contains:

  • your name
  • your address
  • your date of birth
  • what vaccine you had
  • how many doses you had
  • when and where you had each dose

At the top of the letter is your unique reference. This is to confirm your vaccination record.

What is on the back of the letter

Questions you may have

Why have I received this letter now?

The NHS has sent you this letter because you have received 2 doses of the COVID-19 vaccine.

When and where could I be asked to show this letter?

This will depend on the rules being applied at the time. You may be asked to show this letter when you travel abroad.

Is there a digital alternative?

You can use the NHS App to display your COVID-19 vaccination record securely.

What should I do if I lose this letter?

This letter is important. Keep it safe. It proves you have been vaccinated against COVID-19. If you lose it you can get information on what to do on the NHS website: www.nhs.uk/coronavirus or please call 119 free of charge, 7 days a week.

Is this information available in other languages, large type or braille?

You can get this information in alternative languages and formats. Please call 119 free of charge, 7 days a week.

You should keep this original English-language version as you may be asked to show it if you travel abroad.
