Details

This document explains the provisions of the UK-New Zealand Free Trade Agreement in relation to customs and rules of origin.

Provisions around customs include:

  • fair and equal treatment
  • rapid and efficient clearance
  • fast decisions from customs on advance rulings
  • empowering small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)
  • no need for customs brokers
  • simplifying customs processes

Provisions around rules of origin include:

  • modern and flexible rules of origin
  • supporting UK supply chains
  • rules that safeguard and safeguard food producers

The agreement is not yet in force. Both the UK and New Zealand are required to complete their respective domestic procedures for the agreement to come into effect. Once approved by both parliaments, businesses will be able to trade under its terms.
