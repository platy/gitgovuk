1. Purpose

This operations note is to be used to determine the appropriate works within Woodland Supplement WS1 – Deer Control and Management. WS1 is only eligible to be used in conjunction (on the same area) with Countryside Stewardship (CS) Woodland Improvement Option WD2 and will be used to support the increased use of lethal control of deer. Effective use of supplement will increase resilience within the woodland, support and help protect biodiversity and structure of the site. Wider environmental benefits are likely to occur beyond the site boundary because of this option.

2. What you need to do

Guidance for each step of the process is provided for each of the mandatory requirements which are:

  • demonstrable increases or maintenance of acceptable levels of deer culling from the start of your Countryside Stewardship Agreement
  • draft Deer Management Plan (DMP) to be submitted and agreed with the Woodland Officer (WO) and Deer Officer (DO) at the time of application. Use the Deer Management Plan Guide
  • full DMP agreed with the WO and DO by the end of year 1
  • completion and submission of cull data annually to the Forestry Commission inclusive of a detailed record of deer culled, and time spent culling (effort)
  • a Habitat Impact and Deer Activity Survey is required by completing a Deer Habitat Activity Record and summarised to the Forestry Commission at the end of Year 1, 3 and 5 using a survey report template and the survey report guide
  • Deer Exclosures will be erected prior to the spring growing period in year 1, with reporting occurring towards the end of years 1, 3 and 5. Recording of changes in vegetation growth (through survey and photographs) will be completed as part of the habitat impact and deer activity survey. The number and approximate location of the exclosures will be by agreement with the Woodland/Deer Officer. These will be available as separate capital items as in FG11. Use the Deer Exclosure Guide and record form

Applicant supporting information:

  • annual training and awareness days will be available in all FC areas to provide support with deer management plan writing and the completion of monitoring requirements. Your local Forestry Commission office will have details

3. Sources of further advice

  • Best Practice Guidance on Deer Management can be found in the comprehensive suite of guides on The Deer Initiative website
  • further guidance on deer management can be found on GOV.UK
  • Forestry Commission Deer Officers can be contacted through your local Forestry Commission office
  • the Grants and Regulations website contains all the information you need to apply for grant support. Alternatively, you can request this information from your local Forestry Commission office
