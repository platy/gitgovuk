The Qualification

Overall Objective for the Qualification

This handbook relates to the following qualification:

  • Level 4 Award in Information Management

On successful completion of this qualification, learners will have gained the knowledge, skills and techniques required to effectively manage information within an organisation where they are responsible for ensuring that information is being captured, stored, distributed, used, retained, and eventually disposed of, in accordance with legal requirements and an organisation’s policy.

Pre-entry Requirements

There are no pre-entry requirements.

Unit Content and Rules of Combination

This qualification is made up of a total of one mandatory unit. To be awarded this qualification the candidate must achieve a total of 3 credits as shown in the table below.

Mandatory Units

Unit Reference Number Unit Title GLH TQT RQF Level Credit Value
L/618/5733 Information Management 21 30 4 3

The detailed content of the unit is included in Section 5 of this handbook.

Age Restriction

This qualification is available to learners aged 18 years and over.

Opportunities for Progression

There are a number of progression opportunities available to holders of this qualification such as the Professional Diploma in Information Management and Technology offered by the Open University.

Exemption

No exemptions have been identified.

Credit Transfer

Credits from similar regulated units which have already been achieved by the learner may be transferred.

Learning Outcomes and assessment criteria

The learner will The learner can
1. Understand the importance of information management (IM) to an organisation. 1.1 define the principles, concepts, responsibilities and practice of IM
  1.2 define the reasons why records are maintained
  1.3 explain the principles and practice of information risk management
2. Understand the importance of effective record keeping. 2.1 demonstrate how information is managed in context
  2.2 describe approaches to effective file storage and management
  2.3 explain the roles and responsibilities relating to record keeping
3. Be able to exploit information for the benefit of an organisation. 3.1 show how information flow is managed in an organisation
  3.2 evaluate an organisation’s information management to identify strengths, weaknesses and areas for improvement
  3.3 show how to comply with relevant rules and regulations
