Studland Bay MCZ

Studland Bay Marine Conservation Zone (MCZ) lies within the 6 nautical mile limit in English waters, on the Dorset coast. Studland Bay MCZ was formally designated on 31 May 2019. The site has four designated features: intertidal coarse sediment, long-snouted seahorse (Hippocampus guttulatus), subtidal sand and seagrass beds.

Management Approach

The Studland Bay MCZ Habitat Protection Strategy provides an overview of MMO’s management approach for marine non-licensable activities in Studland Bay Marine Conservation Zone. In addition, guidance is provided regarding installation of moorings.

Anchoring

MMO is introducing a phased voluntary approach for the management of anchoring in Studland Bay MCZ over 2021 and 2022.

Phase 1 commenced on 17 December 2021. An interim voluntary no anchor zone has been introduced. Boat users are requested not to anchor in the zoned area to protect the designated habitats and species which are sensitive to damage from anchoring.

Phase 2 will commence on 1 June 2022. In phase 2, the interim zone will be extended to protect a larger area of seagrass habitat.

Please see the Studland Bay MCZ Habitat Protection Strategy for maps displaying these zones and further information.

Other marine non-licensable activities

A monitoring and control plan will be put in place for all the other marine non-licensable activities which were taken forward for assessment. No further management is being introduced for other activities at this time.

Mooring

The installation of moorings is managed through the MMO marine licensing process. The installation, maintenance and removal of both temporary seasonal moorings and permanent moorings would require an application to MMO for determination. The Studland Bay MCZ Habitat Protection Strategy provides guidance to those interested in applying for moorings in Studland Bay and advises the use of advanced mooring systems to protect the seagrass beds in the site.

Stakeholder Engagement

Between 28 October and 15 December 2020, MMO held a call for evidence to seek views on the draft assessment of the impacts of marine non-licensable activities in Studland Bay MCZ. A number of management options were proposed for each activity due to the potential impacts that they may have on the designated features of the site. Stakeholders were invited to comment on these proposals. Following a review of all feedback received from the call for evidence, it was concluded that management measures would be developed for anchoring due to the pressure it causes at current levels towards the designated features of the MCZ.

In March 2021, Dorset Coast Forum facilitated a formal engagement period for MMO to gain further input from stakeholders about three draft anchoring management options. The purpose of this was to inform the approach to managing anchoring in Studland Bay MCZ and to ensure the necessary level of environmental protection while minimising impacts on users of the sea. A summary of the engagement can be found on the Dorset Coast Have Your Say website. At the engagement events, it was communicated that a survey would be released to gather further input. However, MMO felt that sufficient feedback had been received from stakeholders through the call for evidence and engagement events, so a survey did not go ahead.

In September 2021, MMO announced the voluntary approach to stakeholders. MMO engaged with representatives from key stakeholder groups to share information and invite feedback.

In the production of the Studland Bay MCZ Habitat Protection Strategy, MMO considered all feedback provided by stakeholders throughout the process. The Studland Bay MCZ Decision Document details feedback received and how MMO has responded.
