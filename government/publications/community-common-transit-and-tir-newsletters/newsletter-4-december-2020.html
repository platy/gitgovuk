Euro Exchange Rates

Traders and officers at UK offices of departure who wish to calculate the value of a and Common and Union Transit guarantee must use the official exchange rate when converting sterling and other national currencies into or from the euro (Article 22, Paragraph 1 of Appendix II to the Convention on a Common Transit Procedure refers).

This rate is used by offices of departure for the purpose of calculating the amount of duty and other charges covered by the standard euro 10 000 voucher in non-euro currencies.

The European Commission have published the following exchange rates between national currencies and the euro. These shall be applied with effect from 1 January 2021.

One Euro is equal to:

  • 7,4421 Danish krone DKK
  • 0,90723 Pound sterling GBP
  • 10,4853 Swedish krona SEK
  • 1,09050 Swiss franc CHF
  • 162,20 Iceland króna ISK
  • 10,9073 Norwegian krone NOK
  • 1,9558 Bulgarian lev BGN
  • 26,918 Czech koruna CZK
  • 359,89 Hungarian forint HUF
  • 61,6948 N. Macedonian denar MKD
  • 4,4935 Polish zloty PLN
  • 4,8740 Romanian leu RON
  • 9,0539 Turkish lira TRY
  • 7,5630 Croatian kuna HRK
  • 117,5746 Serbian dinar RSD
