Action to take in preparation for closure of the XML and email channel to access the New Computerised Transit Service (NCTS)

It is our intention to close the legacy XML and email channel on 28 February 2022 as highlighted in previous correspondence Newsletter 13 - November 2021, Newsletter 9 – September 2021 and Newsletter - 7 July 2021.

To enable HMRC to manage the expected increase in messages to the NCTS between the UK and the EU we will be closing the legacy XML and email channel from 28 February 2022, as these channels will no longer be able to manage predicted volumes.

This date represents the end point for submission to either channel.

After 28 February 2022 you will need to use either:

  • software compatible with the new XML Application Programming Interface (API) channel
  • the free-to-use HMRC online portal

This will be the case for all Great Britain (England, Wales and Scotland) and Northern Ireland movements.

HMRC continues to monitor our data on the use of all channels and we may contact you separately if necessary based on your current volume of use of the legacy channels.

You must take action if your software currently uses the legacy XML or email channel to send messages.

Steps to take now if you are using the XML or email channel to access the NCTS

You need to check with your software provider now, what these changes mean for you and what action they need to take to ensure you are ready for this change before 28 February 2022.

If you are not currently registered to use NCTS you will need to allow sufficient time for your registration to be processed. This can take up to 2 weeks.

You should also check with your software provider to ensure you have all the relevant information you may need to log in, for example, Government Gateway credentials.

It is vital that you have any necessary software upgrades and will be ready to use the new software package, as you will not be able to make any declarations using the current legacy XML and email channel after 28 February 2022.

Moving forward the only alternative to the new XML API channel is the free-to-use HMRC online portal.
