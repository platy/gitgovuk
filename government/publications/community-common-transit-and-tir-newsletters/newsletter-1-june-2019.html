Offices of Transit on NCTS declarations

Offices of transit occur at borders between the European Union, a Common Transit country, or a third country, when goods leave and/or enter the EU during the course of a transit movement.

Business are reminded that when sending goods to or via a Common Transit Convention country, they must state the appropriate Offices of Transit in their NCTS declaration.

The EU Delegated Regulation 2015/2446, Annex B, states that the declarant must give the identifying number of the office of transit in the country that the vehicle is entering.

For example, if the vehicle is going from France to Switzerland, the identifying number of the office on the Swiss side of the frontier must be given. For the Swiss office of Basel this would be CH001801 (Basel Weil Autobahn).

The identifying number of each office of transit is given in the European Commission’s Customs Office List.

When the UK exits the EU, Office of Transit procedures will be required at either the first office on entry to the territory of the EU or the first office on entry to the territory of the UK.
