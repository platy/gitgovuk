Errors in stating offices of transit on New Computerised Transit System (NCTS) declarations

Business are reminded that when sending goods to or through a Common Transit Convention country, they must give the appropriate offices of transit in their NCTS declaration.

The EU Delegated Regulation 2015/2446, Annex B, states that the declarant must give the identifying number of the office of transit in the country that the vehicle is entering.

The Swiss authorities have raised the issue that the incorrect offices of transit are being used. For transit movements from the UK to Switzerland, the French transit office FR004050, St Louis Autoroute is being given in error. The correct information to use is: CH001841 Basel, St.Louis Autobahn (or CH001801 Basel Weil Autobahn).

The identifying number of each office of transit is given in the European Commission’s Customs Office list.

When the UK leaves the EU, office of transit procedures will be needed at either the first office on entry to the territory of the EU or the first office on entry to the territory of the UK.
