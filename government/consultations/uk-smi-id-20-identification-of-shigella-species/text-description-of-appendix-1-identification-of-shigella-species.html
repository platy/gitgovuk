This page provides an accessible text description of algorithm Appendix 1: identification of Shigella species by culture from the UK Standards for Microbiology Investigations document ‘UK SMI ID 20: identification of Shigella species’. To view and download the full document, visit UK SMI ID 20 identification of shigella species. For the full draft document, see the consultation page.

Suspect colonies on a primary plate can follow the Gram stain pathway. Gram Stain should be done on pure cultures. Gram negative rods are arranged singly in pairs and in chains.

Results for Biochemical tests pathway

These showed:

  • specimens should have the Oxidase test (TP 26) and Urease test (TP 36)
  • shigella species are oxidase negative – oxidase positive samples are non-shigella species
  • shigella species are urease negative – urease positive samples are non-shigella species
  • shigella species that are oxidase and urease negative should follow the Carbohydrate fermentation test (Manitol)
  • a negative Carbohydrate fermentation test (Manitol) indicates Shigella dysenteriae. Agglutination with polyvalent antiserum S. dysenteriae must be performed. If there is no agglutination, this indicates a negative result and the sample is not S. dysenteriae. If the sample is agglutination positive, the specimens should be sent to the reference laboratory for further identification or if clinically indicated
  • a positive test indicates the presence of Shigella flexneri, Shigella boydii and Shigella sonnei. Agglutination should be performed with polyvalent antiserum for each species. A positive and negative result must be indicated by a pure culture with specific agglutination with monovalent antisera. Specimens should be sent to the reference laboratory for further identification or if clinically indicated

Results for MALDI-TOF MS pathway

Specimens should be sent to the reference laboratory for further identification or if clinically indicated.

Results for whole genome sequencing and molecular tests, including PCR pathway

Specimens should be sent to the reference laboratory for further identification or if clinically indicated.
