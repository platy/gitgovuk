This page provides an accessible text description of algorithm ‘identification of Corynebacterium species’ by culture from the UK Standards for Microbiology Investigations document ‘UK SMI ID 2: identification of Corynebacterium species’. For the draft document, see the consultation page.

UK SMI ID 2 issue 5: Appendix text description (identification of Corynebacterium species)

Suspect colonies

Suspect colonies on a primary plate grow as grey or black colonies and Gram stain should be done on pure cultures. Gram positive rods are arranged singly or in pairs often in a ‘V’ formation. Identification by automated methods can be undertaken from suspect colonies. If, the laboratory does not have an automated method, follow the biochemical test pathway.

Results for Biochemical tests pathway:

Rapid 4hr tests should be performed for nitrate reduction test, catalase (TP 8), pyrazinamidase and urease test (TP 36).

A positive nitrate test indicates presence of C. diphtheriae biotype biovar gravis, C. diphtheriae biotype biovar mitis, C. diphtheriae biotype biovar intermedius, C. striatum and C. pseudo-tuberculosis.

A negative nitrate test indicates presence of C. diphtheriae biotype biovar belfanti, C. ulcerans, C. pseudo-tuberculosis, C. striatum and C. jeikeium.

A positive catalase test indicates presence of Corynebacterium specie as all potentially toxigenic corynebacteria are catalase positive and for non-toxigenic Corynebacterium species, the catalase test results are varied.

Negative catalse test indicates the presence of Gardnerella vaginalis.

A positive pyrazinamidase test indicates presence of C. striatum and C. jeikeium.

A negative pyrazinamidase test indicates presence of C. diphtheriae, C. ulcerans and C. pseudotuberculosis as all potentially toxigenic corynebacteria are pyrazinamidase negative.

A positive urease test indicates presence of C. ulcerans and C. pseudotuberculosis.

A negative urease test indicates presence of C. diphtheriae biotype biovar mitis, C. diphtheriae biotype biovar gravis, C. diphtheriae biotype biovar intermedius, C. diphtheriae biotype biovar belfanti, C. striatum and C. jeikeium.

Sub-culture for purity pathway:

All suspected isolates of C. diphtheriae or other potentially toxigenic Corynebacterium species should be sub-cultured to a blood agar plate for purity at the time that the above tests are set up for possible referral to a reference laboratory.

For further identification, if clinically indicated, refer isolate to the Reference Laboratory.
