This consultation ran from
6pm on 6 July 2022 to 11:45pm on 1 September 2022

Consultation description

The UK Government has introduced a set of amendments to the High Speed Rail (Crewe – Manchester) Bill, known as Additional Provision 1 (AP1).

Alongside it, an Additional Provision 1 Environmental Statement (AP1 ES) has been produced, which reports on the likely significant environmental effects of the proposed amendments.

In addition, a Supplementary Environmental Statement (SES1) has been produced, reporting the likely significant environmental effects of changes within the existing limits and powers of the Bill

This consultation asked for your views on these documents, which can be find at:

View the documents.

How to respond

This consultation closes at 11:45pm on 1 September 2022. You can respond:

Confidentiality and data protection

Consultation responses will be published on a publicly accessible website. Individuals’ names, addresses or signatures will not be published. However, please do not include information in your response that could identify you, unless you are happy for it to be made public.

If you do not want any of your response to be published, you should clearly mark it as ‘confidential’ in the subject line of the email or at the top of your letter. Responses marked ‘confidential’ will still be passed to the independent assessor for consideration, but your personal information will not be mentioned in the report.

Information provided in response to this consultation, including personal information, may be subject to publication or disclosure in accordance with the access to information regimes. These are primarily the Freedom of Information Act 2000 (FOIA), the Environmental Information Regulations (EIR) 2004, the Data Protection Act (DPA) 2018, and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Please be aware that, under the FOIA and the EIR, there is a statutory Code of Practice with which public authorities must comply and which deals with, among other things, obligations of confidence.

In view of this, it would be helpful if you could explain to us why you regard the information you have provided as confidential. If we receive a request for disclosure of the information we will take full account of your explanation, but we cannot give an assurance that confidentiality can be maintained in all circumstances. An automatic confidentiality disclaimer generated by your IT system will not, in itself, be regarded as binding.
