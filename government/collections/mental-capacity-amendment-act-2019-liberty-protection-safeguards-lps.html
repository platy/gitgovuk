The Liberty Protection Safeguards (LPS) were introduced in the Mental Capacity (Amendment) Act 2019. LPS will provide protection for people aged 16 and above who are or who need to be deprived of their liberty in order to enable their care or treatment and lack the mental capacity to consent to their arrangements, in England and Wales.

The government has committed to bringing LPS into force to replace the Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards (DoLS).

In December 2021, we announced a delay to the implementation of LPS. See the letter to the LPS steering group below.

Consultation on the MCA Code of Practice, including the LPS

The government has opened a public consultation on proposed changes to the Mental Capacity Act Code of Practice, which includes guidance on the new LPS system. The consultation will run for 16 weeks from 17 March 2022.

The consultation is available in Welsh and easy read (Welsh).
