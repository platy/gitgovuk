Applications, changes and cancellations for consideration under the UK geographical indications (GI) schemes, how to object and appeal against decisions.

Each geographical indication (GI) scheme will publish a list of geographical product names with applications to register for these UK schemes:

  • Protected food, drink and agricultural product names
  • Spirit drinks names
  • Wine and aromatised wine names
  • Traditional terms for wine products

Defra publishes eligible product names. You can object to a request to:

  • add a product name
  • make a substantial change to a product specification
  • cancel the registration of a product name

You can appeal against decisions.

Defra will publish a notice of its decision to a minor change to a product specification, but you cannot object to a minor change.

Substantial change requests

Substantial changes to UK registered products are reconsidered for protected status. You have the opportunity to object to a substantial change request.

There are currently no cases.

  • Object to an application to register, change or cancel a geographical food or drink name
    • 8 September 2021
    • Guidance

Cancellation requests

You have the opportunity to object to requests to cancel products registered on the UK register. Follow the guidance on how to object to a cancellation.

There are currently no cases.
